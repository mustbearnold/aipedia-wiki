# Midjourney

Midjourney is the AI research lab behind the [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) image generator, one of the most recognized text-to-image tools and a benchmark for aesthetic quality. Founded in 2022 by David Holz, co-founder of Leap Motion, the company is notable for staying self-funded: it reached profitability without venture capital and reportedly grew revenue well above $200 million with a very small team.

## What They Do

Midjourney builds generative image models and delivers them through a fast, opinionated product known for painterly, cinematic output. Historically run through Discord and now its own web app, it focuses on a tight creative loop and strong default aesthetics rather than enterprise tooling or broad model APIs. The company has begun extending toward video generation and has signaled hardware ambitions.

What makes Midjourney unusual is its business model. With no outside funding, a tiny headcount, and high revenue, it is one of the leanest companies in AI, which lets it prioritize product taste and independence over growth-at-all-costs. That independence also means less of the enterprise sales, compliance, and indemnification apparatus that rivals like Adobe emphasize.

## Current Flagship Products

- [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/): The company's flagship text-to-image generator, known for leading aesthetic quality, with subscription tiers and a web app.

## Strategic Position

Midjourney's moat is taste and brand: it set the bar for image aesthetics and retains a loyal creative base, funded entirely by its own revenue. Its risks are competition and rights. Bundled generators (GPT Image in ChatGPT, Imagen in Gemini) lower the floor for casual users, open-weight models like Flux pressure the middle, and Adobe competes on commercial safety, an area where a lean, self-funded lab invests less.

For AIpedia readers, Midjourney remains the go-to for pure image quality and creative exploration. For commercial pipelines that need rights clarity, integrated editing, or enterprise controls, Adobe and others may fit better.
