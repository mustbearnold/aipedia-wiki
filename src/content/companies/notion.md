# Notion

Notion is the connected-workspace company whose docs, wiki, and project tools are used by more than 100 million people, and whose [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) layer brings assistance and now agents into that workspace. Founded in 2013 and led by CEO Ivan Zhao, Notion was valued at about $11 billion in a January 2026 tender on roughly $600 million of revenue, having raised about $418 million in total.

## What They Do

Notion builds an all-in-one workspace that merges notes, documents, wikis, databases, and project management, and Notion AI embeds generative assistance throughout: writing, summarizing, answering questions across a workspace, and increasingly running background tasks as AI agents that create documents and automate workflows. The pitch is that AI is most useful where a team's knowledge and work already live.

The strategy is to convert a large, sticky productivity base into AI revenue by making Notion AI a natural upgrade rather than a separate product. The shift from AI assistant to AI teammate, agents that take multi-step actions inside the workspace, is Notion's bet to stay central as work software becomes agentic.

## Current Flagship Products

- [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/): The AI layer inside Notion's workspace, providing writing help, Q&A across content, and AI agents for document creation and automation.

## Strategic Position

Notion's moat is its large, engaged user base and the breadth of its workspace, which gives Notion AI rich context that standalone assistants lack. Its challenge is that every productivity suite (Microsoft 365 Copilot, Google Workspace with Gemini) now ships AI, and dedicated tools compete on specific jobs. Notion competes on flexibility, design, and being the place teams already organize knowledge.

For AIpedia readers, Notion matters when AI should live inside a connected docs-and-database workspace, weighed against the Copilot and Gemini features bundled into the big office suites.
