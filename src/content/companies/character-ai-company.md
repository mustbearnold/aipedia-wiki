# Character.AI

Character.AI is the AI companion and roleplay company behind [Character.AI](/tools/character-ai/), where users chat with virtual characters or build their own AI personas. Founded in 2021 by former Google researchers Noam Shazeer and Daniel De Freitas, it became one of the most-used consumer AI apps before a $2.7 billion Google deal in August 2024 returned its founders to Google DeepMind and licensed its technology, after which the company continued under new leadership.

## What They Do

Character.AI runs a consumer platform for conversational AI personas: users talk to characters (fictional, historical, or original) and create their own, driving very high engagement, especially among younger users. Its strength has always been immersive, personality-driven chat rather than productivity, and it built one of the largest consumer AI audiences by time spent.

The defining event is the 2024 Google deal: Google paid about $2.7 billion to license Character.AI's technology and bring back Shazeer (who became a key Google AI leader) and De Freitas along with research staff. The startup continues to operate its app under new leadership, but the deal reshaped its trajectory, valuation, and independence, and it operates amid scrutiny over safety for younger users.

## Current Flagship Products

- [Character.AI](/tools/character-ai/): The consumer app for chatting with and creating AI characters, on free and paid (c.ai+) tiers.

## Strategic Position

Character.AI's moat is a large, highly-engaged consumer base and a distinctive companion-chat product. Its challenges are significant: the founder and talent exit to Google, a sharply lower valuation, safety and well-being scrutiny around companion AI and minors, and competition from general assistants and other companion apps. Its path forward is monetizing engagement while navigating safety expectations.

For AIpedia readers, Character.AI matters as the leading companion and roleplay chat product, distinct from productivity assistants, with the founder-departure and safety context as key caveats.
