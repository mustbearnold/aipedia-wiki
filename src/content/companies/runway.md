# Runway

Runway is an applied AI research company and one of the pioneers of AI video generation, behind the [Runway](/tools/runway/) creative suite and its Gen-series video models. Founded in 2018 in New York by Cristobal Valenzuela, Anastasis Germanidis, and Alejandro Matamala-Ortiz, it has raised about $860 million in total, most recently a $315 million Series E in February 2026 at a $5.3 billion valuation, with investors including General Atlantic, Nvidia, and Adobe Ventures.

## What They Do

Runway builds generative video models and a creative tooling suite around them, letting users create and edit video from text prompts and images. Its Gen-series models advanced the field on coherence, character consistency, and controllability, and Runway pairs them with editing and production features aimed at filmmakers, agencies, and creators.

The company frames itself as an applied research lab pursuing world models for universal simulation, positioning video generation as a step toward broader simulation rather than an end in itself. That research framing, plus strategic investors like Nvidia and Adobe, signals ambitions beyond a single product.

## Current Flagship Products

- [Runway](/tools/runway/): The company's AI video generation and editing suite, built on its Gen-series models, with creator and enterprise plans.

## Strategic Position

Runway's moat is its head start and research depth in AI video plus a real creative-tooling product, not just a raw model. Its challenge is fierce, well-funded competition: frontier video models from Google (Veo), OpenAI (Sora), Kling, and others are advancing fast, and the category is capital-intensive. Runway competes on production features, control, and its world-model research bet.

For AIpedia readers, Runway is a leading choice for AI video creation with professional editing, weighed against the newest frontier video models on quality, length, and cost for a given project.
