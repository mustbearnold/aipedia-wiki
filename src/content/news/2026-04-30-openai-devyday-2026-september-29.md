OpenAI's annual developer conference now has a date.

On April 30, 2026, The Verge reported that OpenAI's DevDay 2026 will take place on September 29. Last year, the company used DevDay 2025 to launch "apps" inside ChatGPT, expanding the platform from a single chat interface to a multi-application ecosystem.

## What to expect

OpenAI typically uses DevDay to reset developer expectations for the year ahead. Last year's focus on apps signaled a strategic shift toward platform play, turning ChatGPT from a product into an ecosystem.

This year, the likely themes include deeper agent capabilities, expanded tool integrations, improved API ergonomics for multi-step workflows, and possibly the next major model release cadence. The company faces growing competitive pressure from Anthropic's agent-focused roadmap, Google's Gemini platform, and open-source alternatives. DevDay is the natural venue to answer those threats.

## Why it matters

For developers building on OpenAI's platform, DevDay sets the API roadmap for the next 6-12 months. New endpoints, pricing changes, capability expansions, and deprecations are typically announced at the event.

For buyers evaluating AI platforms, the September date is important because it likely falls before major enterprise budgeting cycles. Products announced at DevDay will influence procurement decisions in Q4 2026 and Q1 2027.

## Buyer takeaway

Do not make final platform decisions that depend on OpenAI-specific features or pricing before September 29. The event is likely to change the competitive landscape in areas like agent pricing, app marketplace fees, and multi-model orchestration.

For now, the smart play is to build with flexible abstractions that can map to whatever OpenAI announces, or switch if the announcement does not favor your use case.
