AI coding startup **Cursor** (Anysphere) is in advanced talks to raise **$2 billion or more at a $50 billion valuation**, multiple sources confirm. The round nearly **doubles** the $29.3B post-money valuation Cursor received just six months ago. Already oversubscribed. Terms not final.

## The round at a glance

**Co-leads:**
- **Thrive Capital** (existing investor, leads)
- **Andreessen Horowitz** (existing investor, co-leads)

**Also participating:**
- **Nvidia** (strategic)
- **Battery Ventures** (new investor)

## Revenue trajectory (unprecedented for B2B SaaS)

| Date | Annualised Revenue Run-Rate |
|---|---|
| January 2025 | $100M |
| June 2025 | $500M |
| November 2025 | $1B |
| February 2026 | **$2B** |

Zero to $2B ARR in roughly three years. **The fastest-scaling B2B software company on record.** Faster than Slack, Zoom, or Snowflake at equivalent stages.

## Why the $50B matters

The $50B valuation at $2B ARR puts Cursor at roughly **25× revenue multiple**, inside the historical range for category leaders at hypergrowth. By comparison:
- [GitHub Copilot](/tools/github-copilot/) (inside Microsoft) is not independently valued but likely contributes ~$500M ARR to Microsoft's $250B+ AI business
- [Windsurf](/tools/windsurf/) was acquired by Cognition AI after the Google licensing deal and OpenAI acquisition collapsed, valuation undisclosed
- [Replit](https://replit.com) raised at ~$3B in 2024, lower ARR

Cursor's market lead is real and the cap table pile-on confirms investor conviction.

## Why investors are stacking in

**Three things drive the premium valuation:**

1. **Category-defining product.** Cursor is the default AI IDE in April 2026. The Agents Window shift in [Cursor 3 (April 2)](/news/2026-04-02-cursor-3-agent-first-release/) made the AI agent the primary interface; traditional editor view moved to background.

2. **Model-provider agnostic.** Cursor supports Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and local models in one tool. That flexibility insulates it from any single provider's pricing or policy shifts.

3. **Enterprise adoption.** Fortune 500 enterprise usage has accelerated hard since Cursor Business ($40/user/mo). Engineering teams standardising on Cursor as a vendor-approved tool drives the B2B ARR engine.

## Competitive context

The AI coding IDE category is the most crowded and highest-valued AI sub-category in 2026:

| Tool | Primary angle | 2026 status |
|---|---|---|
| **Cursor** | AI-native IDE, model-agnostic | $50B valuation (pending), $2B ARR |
| **GitHub Copilot** | MS-backed, deep VS Code + JetBrains integration | Embedded in MS $250B AI biz |
| **Windsurf** | Cognition AI-owned, Cascade agent | Private, post-acquisition |
| **Antigravity** | Google-backed, Manager view, Gemini 3.1 Pro default | Folded into Google AI Pro / Ultra |
| **Claude Code** | CLI-native, Anthropic-backed | Bundled with Claude Pro+ |
| **Devin** | Fully autonomous agent | Cognition AI, $500/mo Teams |

## What this means

**For the category:** Cursor's $50B valuation is the clearest signal that capital believes AI coding will be a $100B+ annual market. That puts every surrounding category (Claude Code, Windsurf, Antigravity, Copilot) under pressure to accelerate or consolidate.

**For Cursor:** $2B+ of fresh capital means aggressive expansion. Expect enterprise sales hiring, international expansion, and possibly acquisitions in adjacent categories (code review, CI, DevOps AI).

**For developers:** Short term, nothing changes. Medium term, expect Cursor to push harder on team features, admin controls, and enterprise-specific capabilities that compete directly with Microsoft's enterprise GitHub Copilot motion.
