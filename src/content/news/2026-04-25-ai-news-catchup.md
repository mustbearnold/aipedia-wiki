This is the **April 25, 2026 aipedia.wiki desk update**. The source events below were published on April 24, but they were found and added during the April 25 catch-up pass.

## GPT-5.5 API is available

[OpenAI's GPT-5.5 release page](/news/2026-04-23-openai-gpt-55-release/) now lists API access as available as of April 24.

Why it matters: this changes GPT-5.5 from a ChatGPT/Codex product launch into a developer-platform event. Buyers can now compare the model directly against Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek on API price, latency, context, and task completion quality.

Tool pages reviewed: [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) and [Codex](/tools/codex/).

## GPT-5.5 reaches GitHub Copilot

[GitHub Copilot added GPT-5.5](/news/2026-04-24-github-copilot-gpt-55/) for Pro+, Business, and Enterprise users.

Why it matters: Copilot now has both OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and Anthropic's Opus 4.7 in the same paid model picker. The tool gets stronger, but premium-request budgeting becomes a bigger part of the buying decision.

Tool page updated: [GitHub Copilot](/tools/github-copilot/).

## Anthropic publishes Project Deal

[Anthropic published Project Deal](/news/2026-04-24-anthropic-project-deal/), a one-week internal marketplace experiment where Claude agents represented employees as buyers and sellers.

Why it matters: agent quality produced measurable economic differences. Opus agents got better objective bargaining outcomes than Haiku agents, and participants did not reliably notice the gap.

Tool page reviewed: [Claude](/tools/claude/).

## Google reportedly prepares up to $40B for Anthropic

[Google reportedly plans up to $40B in Anthropic cash and compute investment](/news/2026-04-24-google-anthropic-40b-investment/), according to Bloomberg reporting carried by TechCrunch and Reuters syndication.

Why it matters: Anthropic's Claude roadmap is increasingly backed by a multi-cloud capacity race across AWS, Google TPUs, and enterprise distribution partners.

Tool page updated: [Claude](/tools/claude/).

## Cohere moves toward Aleph Alpha combination

[Cohere is moving to acquire or merge with Aleph Alpha](/news/2026-04-24-cohere-aleph-alpha/), according to TechCrunch reporting.

Why it matters: Cohere's enterprise RAG and private-deployment pitch gets a stronger European and sovereign-AI angle.

Tool page updated: [Cohere](/tools/cohere/).
