Z.ai dropped GLM-5.1 this week as an open-source model built for long-running autonomous work. The company says it can stay aligned on a single task for up to eight hours and sustain thousands of tool calls without degradation. In benchmarks, GLM-5.1 outperformed several leading Western models on SWE-Bench Pro and showed major gains in coding, reasoning, and agentic tasks. The performance gap between US and Chinese models has narrowed dramatically, GLM-5.1 scores 1,530 versus Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking at 1,548. Z.ai paired the open-source release with paid developer plans and API pricing, signaling a hybrid commercial strategy. This matters because open access plus strong performance increases competitive pressure on existing AI vendors and lowers costs for AI-enabled work.
