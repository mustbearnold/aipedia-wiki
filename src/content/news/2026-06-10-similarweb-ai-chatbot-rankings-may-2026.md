# Similarweb's May 2026 AI chatbot rankings show ChatGPT still first, with Gemini and Claude close behind

Similarweb's May 2026 **AI Chatbots and Tools** ranking keeps **chatgpt.com** in the top spot, followed by **gemini.google.com** and **claude.ai**. For AI buyers, the signal is not that traffic equals quality. The signal is that the assistant market is no longer a one-tool default.

AiPedia verified Similarweb's ranking page and 2026 generative AI traffic analysis on June 10, 2026.

## What the ranking says

Similarweb lists the top five AI Chatbots and Tools websites for May 2026 as:

- `chatgpt.com`
- `gemini.google.com`
- `claude.ai`
- `chat.deepseek.com`
- `grok.com`

The next five in the visible ranking are `openai.com`, `character.ai`, `perplexity.ai`, `copilot.microsoft.com`, and `polybuzz.ai`.

Similarweb's engagement data is useful context. In the May 2026 table, Gemini and Claude both show higher pages per visit and lower bounce rates than ChatGPT, while Grok and Character.AI show much longer average visit durations. That does not prove they are better products. It does show that different AI tools now anchor different user behaviors.

## Why it matters

AI tool buyers often start with the biggest brand. That is understandable, but it is no longer enough.

[ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) still has the broadest mindshare and remains the default general-purpose assistant for many users. [Gemini](/tools/gemini/) is now deeply tied to Google and Apple developer-platform stories. [Claude](/tools/claude/) is leaning into deep work, coding, research, and delegated workflows. [DeepSeek](/tools/deepseek/), [Grok](/tools/grok/), and [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) occupy different lanes around cost, social/search behavior, reasoning style, and real-time information discovery.

The buying implication: choose by workflow, not leaderboard position.

## What marketers and publishers should notice

Similarweb's broader generative AI analysis also tracks AI referral behavior. Its ChatGPT referral analysis matters for publishers, SaaS teams, and ecommerce brands because AI assistants are becoming discovery surfaces, not just productivity tools.

That means AI visibility now has two jobs:

- Help human buyers understand your product on your own site.
- Help AI answer engines cite, summarize, and route users accurately.

This is why AiPedia keeps pushing source-backed pages, clear verdicts, current pricing, and visible verification dates. AI referrals reward pages that can be understood, trusted, and cited cleanly.

## Buyer action

Use the ranking as a shortlist, not a decision:

- Pilot at least two general assistants if your team does serious writing, research, coding, or analysis.
- Match the tool to the workflow: search, coding, document reasoning, creative drafting, customer support, or operations.
- Track adoption, cost, accuracy, review burden, and data-safety incidents.
- Keep a fallback assistant so one provider's outage, policy change, or pricing shift does not block work.
- Review enterprise controls before allowing sensitive context into any high-traffic consumer assistant.

## Watch-outs

Web traffic rankings miss important usage. They do not fully capture mobile app use, embedded enterprise deployments, API usage, IDE usage, workplace integrations, or private deployments. They also do not measure answer quality, privacy fit, model safety, or total cost of ownership.

Do not buy an AI assistant because it ranks high. Buy it because it performs your job with evidence.
