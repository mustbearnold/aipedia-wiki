Meta's AI push is showing up in business messaging and ad creation.

On Meta's Q1 2026 earnings call, executives said business AIs now facilitate more than **10 million conversations each week**, up from 1 million at the start of the year. TechCrunch covered the figures on April 30, 2026.

## What changed

Meta said it expanded business AIs on WhatsApp to SMBs across Latin America and Indonesia, and on Messenger in Asia Pacific. The company also said more than **8 million advertisers** are using at least one of its GenAI ad creative tools.

Meta also introduced **Meta Ads AI Connectors** in open beta. The connectors let advertisers connect a Meta ad account directly to an AI agent, extending existing Marketing API patterns into agent workflows.

## Why it matters

Business messaging is one of Meta's clearest paths to monetizing AI outside the consumer Meta AI assistant. Small businesses already use WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, and Facebook to handle sales and support. If Meta can place AI agents directly inside those channels, the adoption curve is very different from asking a business to buy a separate chatbot platform.

The ad-creative numbers matter for the tool market. Native AI creative inside Meta Ads makes standalone ad-generation tools less obviously necessary for basic Meta campaigns.

## Tool impact

For **AdCreative.ai**, this is competitive pressure from the platform layer. Meta's native GenAI tools can reduce the need for a separate creative generator when the campaign only runs on Facebook, Instagram, or WhatsApp.

Standalone tools still matter for cross-platform workflows, brand-kit management, agency reporting, and generating variants across Meta, Google, LinkedIn, and TikTok. But the bar rises when Meta's own tool is free or bundled into the ad-buying workflow.

## Buyer takeaway

If your ads run mostly on Meta, test Meta's native GenAI creative tools before adding another subscription. If you need multi-platform output, cross-brand governance, or agency workflows, standalone tools still have a role.

## What to watch

Watch whether Meta starts charging for business AIs or bundles them into paid messaging and ad products. Zuckerberg signaled that the tools are currently free for most businesses, but that Meta expects to work toward a longer-term monetization model.
