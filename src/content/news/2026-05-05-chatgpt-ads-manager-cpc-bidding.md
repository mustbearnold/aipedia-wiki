# OpenAI opens ChatGPT ads to self-serve buying and CPC bidding

OpenAI announced on May 5, 2026, that its ChatGPT ads pilot is expanding beyond direct sales and agency partners. A beta self-serve Ads Manager is beginning to roll out in the United States, allowing advertisers to register, add payment details, set budgets and bids, upload creative, launch campaigns, and view performance from a portal.

The company is also adding cost-per-click bidding, alongside earlier CPM buying. That shifts part of the pilot toward performance advertising, where advertisers pay only when a user clicks. OpenAI says it is also rolling out Conversions API and pixel-based measurement while keeping individual conversations private from advertisers.

OpenAI names agency partners including Dentsu, Omnicom, Publicis, and WPP, plus ad-tech partners including Adobe, Criteo, Kargo, Pacvue, and StackAdapt. OpenAI says its own ads system controls delivery decisions even when partners help with budgeting, bidding, and creative.

## Why this matters

Ads are now a visible part of ChatGPT's business model, not just a small experiment. That affects buyer perception, especially for Free and lower-cost tiers where ad exposure may become part of the value equation.

The CPC and conversion-measurement pieces matter because they move ChatGPT closer to a normal performance-marketing platform. The product is still early, but the operating model is no longer just brand testing inside conversations.

## Buyer take

Consumer users should watch whether ads remain clearly separated from answers and whether lower-priced plans carry more ad inventory over time.

Businesses evaluating ChatGPT should separate two questions: whether employees see ads in their own product experience, and whether the company wants to buy ads inside ChatGPT to reach users during decision-oriented conversations.

## What is still unclear

OpenAI has not published stable long-term ad formats, targeting limits, pricing floors, or global availability. Treat the beta as directional, not a settled media-buying product.
