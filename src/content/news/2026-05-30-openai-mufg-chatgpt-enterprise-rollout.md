# MUFG rolls ChatGPT Enterprise to 35,000 bank employees with OpenAI

OpenAI published a May 28 customer story saying **MUFG is rolling out ChatGPT Enterprise to approximately 35,000 employees at Mitsubishi UFJ Bank**. AiPedia is covering it in the May 30 missed-news queue because this is the kind of deployment that matters more than another small chatbot feature: a major bank is turning enterprise AI adoption into operations, training, and customer-service design.

MUFG began working with OpenAI in October 2024 and started a phased ChatGPT Enterprise rollout in 2026. OpenAI says the program includes operational planning, product education, all-employee training, custom GPT workshops, executive study sessions, and AI champions inside departments.

## What changed

The most important detail is not the seat count by itself. It is the adoption machinery around it.

OpenAI says employees receiving accounts reached 100 percent training participation through mandatory e-learning. After OpenAI-led custom GPT training, employees created more than 1,800 custom GPTs in four months. MUFG also reports selected research tasks, such as AI trend tracking and executive updates, seeing 20 to 30 percent workload reductions.

That is a more useful enterprise-AI story than vague "AI transformation" language. It shows the blocking issue: employees need rules, examples, permission boundaries, and confidence before a large model rollout becomes daily work.

## Buyer read

For enterprise buyers evaluating ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude, Gemini, Microsoft 365 Copilot, or internal model gateways, MUFG's rollout points to the work behind the subscription:

- mandatory AI-use training before access;
- explicit information-management rules;
- approval routes and usage policies;
- custom GPT education;
- executive sponsorship;
- departmental AI champions;
- examples tied to actual banking workflows;
- measurement beyond seat activation.

The model is only part of the purchase. The operating model determines whether people use it safely and whether they use it for work that matters.

## Customer-facing finance is next

OpenAI also says MUFG is exploring customer-facing AI experiences. One example is Moneytree, where users could ask financial questions from within ChatGPT and view information such as account balances and transaction details. MUFG also plans AI-powered experiences around WealthNavi and emutt, including an AI concierge and personalized recommendations through a Money Advisory Platform.

Those examples are still subject to launch details and governance. But they show a larger pattern: banks are no longer only buying AI for employee productivity. They are testing AI as a new front door for financial guidance, account context, and personalized recommendations.

## Watch-outs

Financial institutions should not treat this as proof that a general enterprise chatbot is automatically ready for sensitive customer work.

The serious questions are:

- what data can employees paste or upload;
- what custom GPTs are allowed to access;
- how outputs are reviewed before customer use;
- how customer-facing recommendations are explained;
- how audit trails are retained;
- whether AI guidance is advisory, transactional, or both;
- where human approvals remain mandatory.

## AiPedia verdict

This is a **major enterprise ChatGPT adoption story**. MUFG's rollout matters because it pairs ChatGPT Enterprise access with training, champions, governance, custom GPT enablement, and early measurement.

The buyer lesson is practical: do not buy enterprise AI as a seat count. Buy it as a change-management system with secure defaults, repeatable training, workflow examples, and clear boundaries for customer-facing use.
