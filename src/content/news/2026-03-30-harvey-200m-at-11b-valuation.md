Harvey closed a $200 million round at an $11 billion valuation on March 30. The round signals continued institutional appetite for vertical AI platforms targeting high-ACV enterprise segments.

Harvey's flagship features are Vault (document-grounded drafting and research that treats the firm's own matter files as the retrieval corpus) and Workflows (pre-built automations for recurring legal tasks like contract review, second-request response, and deposition prep). Assistant handles general legal chat. Custom-model fine-tuning for specific firms was announced in 2024 and shipped through 2025.

The customer list is unusually public for an enterprise product: A&O Shearman, Reed Smith, O'Melveny, Vinson and Elkins, CMS, Dentons, DLA Piper, and BakerHostetler on the law-firm side; Deutsche Telekom, HSBC, and NBCUniversal on the in-house corporate side. Harvey reports deals typically running $1,000-$1,200 per lawyer per month.

The ACV math explains the valuation. An AmLaw-200 firm with 800 lawyers at $1,100 per seat per month is a $10.5M annual contract. Half a dozen of those plus a handful of corporate deals gets Harvey to nine-figure ARR quickly.
