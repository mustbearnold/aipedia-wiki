One-day snapshot of AI and AI-tools news for **April 24, 2026**, with catch-up items from late April 22-23 that were not yet in the archive.

## GPT-5.5 enters GitHub Copilot

[GitHub made GPT-5.5 generally available in Copilot](/news/2026-04-24-github-copilot-gpt-55/) for Pro+, Business, and Enterprise users. The model consumes premium requests at a 7.5x multiplier.

Why it matters: Copilot now has OpenAI's newest coding-capable model and Claude Opus 4.7 in the same paid model picker. The tool gets stronger, but the premium-request budgeting question gets sharper.

## Google reportedly prepares up to $40B for Anthropic

[Google is reportedly planning up to $40B in Anthropic cash and compute investment](/news/2026-04-24-google-anthropic-40b-investment/), according to Bloomberg reporting carried by TechCrunch and Reuters syndication.

Why it matters: Anthropic is building a multi-cloud capital stack around Claude: Amazon, Google TPU capacity, NEC distribution, and now a reported Google mega-commitment.

## Cohere moves toward Aleph Alpha combination

[Cohere is moving to acquire or merge with Aleph Alpha](/news/2026-04-24-cohere-aleph-alpha/), according to TechCrunch reporting.

Why it matters: Cohere's enterprise and sovereign-AI positioning gets stronger in Europe, especially for regulated buyers who already care about private deployment and multilingual retrieval.

## DeepSeek V4 preview lands

[DeepSeek launched V4 preview models](/news/2026-04-24-deepseek-v4-preview-release/) with pro and flash variants. AP reports improvements in knowledge, reasoning, and agentic capabilities.

Why it matters: OpenAI pushed GPT-5.5 upmarket; DeepSeek is pushing the open/low-cost frontier forward again.

## Anthropic and NEC form Japan partnership

[Anthropic and NEC announced a strategic collaboration](/news/2026-04-24-anthropic-nec-ai-engineering-workforce/) to deploy Claude to about 30,000 NEC Group employees worldwide and build secure Japan-market AI products for finance, manufacturing, government, and cybersecurity.

Why it matters: Claude gets a major Japan-based global partner and a distribution path through NEC BluStellar.

## GitHub Copilot data policy takes effect

[GitHub Copilot's interaction-data training policy](/news/2026-04-24-github-copilot-interaction-data-training/) starts April 24. Copilot Free, Pro, and Pro+ interaction data can be used for model training unless users opt out.

Why it matters: developers need to check Copilot privacy settings, especially when using individual Copilot plans on sensitive code.

## xAI ships Grok Voice Think Fast 1.0

[xAI launched Grok Voice Think Fast 1.0](/news/2026-04-23-xai-grok-voice-think-fast-10/), a real-time voice-agent model for customer support, sales, and enterprise workflows. xAI says it is already used by Starlink.

Why it matters: Grok now has a stronger enterprise voice-agent story, not just a consumer/X chat story.

## OpenAI catch-up: GPT-5.5 and infrastructure

OpenAI's late April 22-23 stack was dense:

- [GPT-5.5 released for ChatGPT and Codex](/news/2026-04-23-openai-gpt-55-release/)
- GPT-5.5 API access became available on April 24 at the published launch pricing
- [GPT-5.5 system card and Bio Bug Bounty published](/news/2026-04-23-openai-gpt-55-system-card-bio-bounty/)
- [OpenAI Privacy Filter released](/news/2026-04-22-openai-privacy-filter/)
- [ChatGPT for Clinicians and HealthBench Professional launched](/news/2026-04-22-chatgpt-for-clinicians-healthbench/)
- [Responses API WebSockets announced for faster agent loops](/news/2026-04-22-openai-responses-api-websockets/)

Why it matters: OpenAI is not just shipping one flagship model. It is filling in the operating system around it: privacy filters, clinician verticals, agent transport, safety evals, and Codex distribution.
