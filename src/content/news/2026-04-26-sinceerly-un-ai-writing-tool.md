Mashable SEA covered **Sinceerly**, a tool positioned around making AI-generated writing sound less machine-generated.

The premise is not new, but the demand is real. As more content is drafted by ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and writing assistants, users want editing tools that remove generic phrasing, flatten obvious templates, and restore a more human voice.

## Why it matters

This category sits in an uncomfortable place. It can help legitimate writers revise rough AI drafts. It can also be used to hide synthetic output from detection systems.
