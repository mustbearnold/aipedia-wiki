The European Union's regulatory machinery is turning its attention to AI platform bundling.

On April 30, 2026, Ars Technica reported that the European Commission has ordered Google to open Android to third-party AI services and assistants. The EU argues that Google's tight integration of Gemini into Android, including default assistant status, locked-in system-level permissions, and preferential placement in the Android setup flow, constitutes anti-competitive bundling.

Google responded by calling the order "unwarranted intervention" and is expected to challenge it.

## What happened

The specific grievance is that Google bundles Gemini as the default AI assistant on Android devices, giving it advantages that third-party AI services like ChatGPT, Claude, and others cannot match. That includes deep OS integration, voice activation from lock screen, system-level access to context, and default placement in the setup wizard.

The EU's Digital Markets Act, which designated Google as a "gatekeeper" for Android, gives regulators the authority to require interoperability and choice screens. The AI order appears to be an expansion of existing DMA enforcement into the AI assistant category.

Google argues that Gemini is a feature of Android, not a separate product, and that requiring it to be open to competitors would degrade the user experience and security model.

## Why it matters

This is the first major regulatory action targeting AI assistant bundling on mobile platforms. It sets a precedent that could:

- Force Google to show a choice screen for AI assistants on new Android devices
- Require Google to give third-party AI assistants the same system-level access as Gemini
- Allow users to set ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI assistants as the default on Android
- Require interoperability APIs that let third-party assistants access context and permissions

If the EU prevails, it would be the most significant opening of a mobile AI platform since the DMA forced Apple to allow third-party app stores and browser engines. The difference is that AI assistants are still a nascent category, so the order could shape the market before it is fully formed.

## Tool impact

For **ChatGPT** and **Claude**, the EU order is an opportunity. Both would gain default-assistant placement on billions of Android devices, a distribution channel they cannot currently access. OpenAI and Anthropic should be preparing Android-first assistant builds.

For **Gemini**, the order is a regulatory risk. Google may need to redesign how Android integrates AI, potentially reducing Gemini's default advantage and competing on quality alone.

For **Apple**, the order is a warning. If the EU targets AI assistant bundling on Android, iOS is likely next. Apple Intelligence integration into Siri and the iPhone could face similar scrutiny.

## Buyer takeaway

This is policy news, not a product change. No Android user will see a choice screen tomorrow. But if the order holds through legal challenges, expect to see AI assistant choice screens on Android within 1-2 years.

For enterprise mobile strategies, the ruling could mean more AI assistant options on the same device. If your organization standardizes on a particular AI assistant, watch for multi-assistant Android support.

## What to watch

- Google's legal response and whether it seeks an injunction
- Whether OpenAI and Anthropic prepare Android-first assistant builds
- Apple's AI bundling strategy in response to the Android precedent
- Parallel EU investigations into AI assistant bundling on iOS
