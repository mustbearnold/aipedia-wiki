# OpenAI and Anthropic back a $500M AI workforce push

Axios and the Wall Street Journal reported on June 25 that RAISE US has launched as a $500 million effort to prepare workers, employers, and states for AI-driven labor disruption. Axios says the initiative is supported by Anthropic and the OpenAI Foundation alongside companies and institutions including Amazon, IBM, Microsoft, Bank of America, and Eli Lilly.

This is not a product launch for [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or [Claude](/tools/claude/). It is still buyer-relevant because it changes the context around enterprise AI rollout. The biggest AI vendors are acknowledging that adoption is no longer only a software decision. It is also a workforce-transition decision.

## What changed

- RAISE US launched as a reported $500 million AI workforce initiative.
- Axios says the program is backed by Anthropic and the OpenAI Foundation among other corporate, philanthropic, education, and state partners.
- Reported ideas include wage insurance, retraining incentives, AI career coaching, short-term credentials, and state pilots.
- The story follows weeks of sharper debate over whether AI will replace entry-level white-collar work or mainly augment it.

## Buyer value

For executives, this is the governance side of AI ROI. If a company deploys AI only as a cost-cutting tool, it may save money in the short term and create trust, retention, legal, and public-relations problems later. If it deploys AI with role redesign, training, and clear accountability, adoption is more likely to stick.

When buying AI tools, ask vendors and internal owners:

- which roles are changed by the rollout;
- what training happens before the tool reaches production;
- which tasks remain human-owned;
- how productivity gains are measured without hiding quality loss;
- whether workers can challenge unsafe or low-quality AI output;
- whether savings fund retraining, redeployment, or better service levels.

## What to do

Treat major AI rollouts like operating-model changes, not SaaS installs. Map affected workflows, write a human-review policy, measure output quality before and after, and create a retraining path for roles most exposed to automation.

For software buyers, the practical decision is not "ChatGPT or Claude?" It is "which tool gives our team leverage without silently deleting institutional knowledge?"

## AiPedia take

RAISE US is a market signal because it moves AI labor disruption from op-ed debate into funded implementation. Buyers should welcome practical retraining work while staying skeptical of generic AI-upskilling theater. The best AI deployments save time without wasting people.
