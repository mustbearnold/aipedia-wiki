OpenAI may be looking beyond apps and standalone AI gadgets.

Android Authority and AJU Press reported that TF International Securities analyst **Ming-Chi Kuo** says OpenAI is exploring an AI-focused smartphone, with mass production projected for **2028**. The reports say OpenAI is working with **MediaTek** and **Qualcomm** on smartphone processors and that **Luxshare** is expected to be involved in system design and manufacturing.

None of OpenAI, Qualcomm, MediaTek, or Luxshare has officially confirmed the reported phone plan.

## Why it matters

The strategic question is not whether OpenAI can make another slab phone.

The question is whether ChatGPT can move from being an app on someone else's operating system to becoming the default agent layer on a device that understands location, calendar context, communications, and task state.

That would put OpenAI closer to the data and interface layer that Apple, Google, and Samsung currently control.

## Tool impact

For ChatGPT users, nothing changes today.

For developers, a real OpenAI phone would raise a different set of platform questions: local model APIs, agent permissions, app handoff, privacy boundaries, default assistant access, and whether third-party tools can become callable actions inside the device agent.

## What to watch

Treat this as a supply-chain report, not a product announcement.

The hard signals will be confirmed silicon partners, developer APIs, a real operating system story, privacy architecture, carrier or retail distribution, and whether OpenAI can avoid the failure pattern that hit earlier AI gadget launches.
