Anthropic hit the brakes on releasing Claude Mythos this week after discovering the model has unprecedented hacking capabilities. The company identified thousands of vulnerabilities across operating systems, browsers, and critical software during internal testing. Rather than release it publicly, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, a collaboration with Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Google, and Nvidia to test Mythos defensively for cybersecurity use. This is a rare move. Anthropic is essentially saying the model is too powerful to release without guardrails. The company wants to work with major tech partners to understand defensive applications before the model reaches the public. It's a responsible approach but also signals that frontier models are reaching capabilities that require careful handling before deployment.
