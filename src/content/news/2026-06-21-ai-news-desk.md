- **ChatGPT health claims.** OpenAI says GPT-5.5 Instant improved health response quality and is available to free users, subject to limits. If your team references this in content, support, education, or health-adjacent workflows, record the exact source and disclaimers.
- **ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex spend controls.** OpenAI's Global Admin Console now gives admins a credit-usage view across ChatGPT and Codex. Check default limits, group limits, individual overrides, and Cost API access before expanding advanced-model usage.
- **Connected-app permissions.** OpenAI's release notes include connected-app permission controls. Review whether users can let ChatGPT act through connected apps without enough review for your data risk.
- **Shopify AI shopping readiness.** Shopify says merchants can sell through AI channels such as ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, Gemini, and Copilot. Check product data, checkout paths, tax, discounts, returns, and channel reporting.
- **Google Cloud agent controls.** Google Cloud's release notes list workflow agents, Agent Gateway, and agent observability as major enterprise-agent surfaces. Confirm who can create agents, which tools they can reach, and where traces are stored.
- **G7 access risk.** Axios' G7 coverage makes model access and standards a vendor-risk topic. Identify fallback models for critical workflows.
- **OpenAI Bio Bounty deadline.** OpenAI's GPT-5.5 Bio Bounty application deadline is June 22, 2026. Safety, red-team, and biosecurity teams that qualify should review the requirements before the window closes.
