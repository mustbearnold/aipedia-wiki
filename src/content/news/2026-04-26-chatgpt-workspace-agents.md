OpenAI introduced **workspace agents in ChatGPT**.

The important shift is product shape. ChatGPT is not just a personal assistant if agents can live inside a workspace and participate in team-level workflows.

OpenAI describes workspace agents as shared agents for complex tasks and long-running workflows that operate within an organization's permissions and controls. They are powered by Codex and are positioned as an evolution of GPTs for teams.

## Why it matters

Team agents raise different questions than individual chat. They need permission boundaries, shared memory rules, audit logs, integration scopes, and clear ownership.

The launch also changes the competitive set. ChatGPT is moving closer to internal workflow platforms, not just chat interfaces. OpenAI says agents can be used across workplace tools, shared within an organization, and improved over time, with availability in research preview for ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, Edu, and Teachers plans.

## Tool impact

For [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/), workspace agents make the product more directly competitive with enterprise workflow tools and coding agents. The buyer question is no longer just model quality; it is whether the workspace controls are strong enough for real team use.

Teams evaluating the feature should look for:

- Admin controls for who can build, publish, and run agents.
- Clear logs and ownership for long-running work.
- Integration scopes for tools like Slack, Drive, calendars, and internal systems.
- Review steps before agents send messages, change records, or trigger workflows.
- Pricing and credit rules after any preview period ends.
