ByteDance's Trae IDE revamped pricing on February 18, dropping from a loss-leader free-Pro model to a five-tier structure: Free $0, Lite $3, Pro $10 (includes SOLO autonomous agent mode), Pro+ $30, and Ultra $100 per month.

The Pro tier at $10/month is the competitive anchor. It includes access to Claude 3.7 Sonnet, GPT-4o, DeepSeek R1, and Gemini 2.5 Pro via Trae's unified coding surface, plus the SOLO autonomous agent. Cursor's equivalent tier sits at $20/month and Windsurf at $15/month, both without native SOLO-style agent control.

Trae remains the cheapest mainstream path to frontier coding models inside a VS Code fork. The trade-off is less mature tooling for air-gapped or regulated environments, where Tabnine and Cody lead, and smaller long-context memory than Claude Code for whole-codebase work.

Adoption through 2025 was driven by the free tier. The February revamp formalises a sustainable structure without losing the entry-level offering. ByteDance's depth of funding means the sub-Cursor pricing is likely sustainable for years.
