Anthropic has received investor offers to fund at an **$800 billion valuation**, Bloomberg reported April 14, 2026. That is **more than double** the $350 billion pre-money valuation attached to its February $30 billion raise. The company is resisting the round for now.

## The numbers

| Metric | February 2026 | April 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-money valuation | $350B | ~$800B (offered) |
| Annualised revenue | ~$12B | **$30B+** |
| Revenue YoY growth | n/a | ~**1,400%** |

Anthropic crossed **$30 billion in annualised revenue** in early April. That revenue growth is what's dragging investors back to the table two months after the last round.

## Why investors are re-engaging so fast

**Three forces driving the offer:**

1. **Claude Opus 4.7 and Claude Design shipped this week.** Anthropic answered Opus 4.6's limits with a 4.7 that matches or beats Gemini 3.1 Pro on real-world eval. Then it shipped Claude Design as a direct competitor to Figma / Framer AI inside 48 hours of Opus 4.7.

2. **Enterprise revenue is compounding.** Claude for Work + Claude Code Business are the fastest-growing B2B lines in AI. Opus remains the default for Fortune 500 safety-conscious deployments.

3. **IPO window is narrowing.** With OpenAI reportedly eyeing late-2026 / early-2027 listing and Cursor's $2B+ round closing this week, anchor public-market demand for the next big AI IPO is concentrated.

## IPO talks with Goldman, JPM, Morgan Stanley

Anthropic is in **early IPO talks** with Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and Morgan Stanley. An IPO as early as **October 2026** is on the table. Projected raise: **$60B+**.

If Anthropic IPOs at $800B+ with a $60B+ raise, it becomes the largest tech IPO in history.

## Why Anthropic is resisting the round

**Three plausible reasons:**

- **Runway is not the problem.** With $30B ARR, Anthropic is self-funding a significant share of its compute. More dilution without clear deployment urgency is harder to justify.
- **IPO market wants clean cap tables.** Taking another mega-round at $800B pre-money muddies the path to a public float.
- **Strategic leverage.** Sitting on the offer without accepting lets Anthropic negotiate with multiple bidders including possibly strategic partners like Amazon, Google, or Nvidia.

## Competitive landscape

| Company | Latest valuation | ARR | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| OpenAI | ~$852B (Series D, Mar 31) | ~$24B+ | Pre-IPO, possibly late 2026 |
| **Anthropic** | **~$800B (offered)** | **$30B** | Resisting round, IPO talks early |
| xAI | ~$200B (Mar 2026) | ~$3B | Grok 4.3 beta in rollout |
| Cursor | $50B (pending) | $2B | Round closing, already oversubscribed |
| Google DeepMind | (inside Alphabet) | n/a | Gemini 3.1 Pro shipped |

**Anthropic is now the second most valuable private AI company by reported offer**. If the round closes, it would be roughly equal to OpenAI on an ARR-multiple basis but with a cleaner governance model.

## What this means

**For the AI market:** A $30B-to-$800B pop in two months confirms capital is still chasing category leaders hard. The ceiling on "how much is an AI lab worth" has not yet been found.

**For Anthropic users:** No immediate change to [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) pricing. Medium-term, expect continued model-capability investment and enterprise feature acceleration.

**For the IPO window:** If Anthropic IPOs first at $800B+, OpenAI's own listing gets dragged forward. Expect 2026 to be the year the two category leaders both hit public markets.
