OpenAI published a new principles post on April 26, 2026, signed by Sam Altman.

The five principles are **democratization**, **empowerment**, **universal prosperity**, **resilience**, and **adaptability**. OpenAI says they will guide its work as systems become more capable and harder to predict.

## Why it matters

This is a governance story, not a feature launch.

The post gives OpenAI a public framework for future tradeoffs: wider access versus safety limits, user autonomy versus harm prevention, infrastructure expansion versus cost, and product speed versus society-level coordination.

For anyone building on ChatGPT, Codex, or the OpenAI API, those tradeoffs eventually show up as model access rules, safety behavior, rate limits, system cards, enterprise controls, and policy changes.

## What changed

Business Insider compared the new post with OpenAI's 2018 charter and highlighted a shift away from a document centered tightly on AGI competition and lab-to-lab commitments.

The new version speaks more broadly about AI deployment, infrastructure, democratic decision-making, and OpenAI's own need to update its principles as it learns.

## Tool impact

Developers should treat this as a policy signal for future releases.

Expect OpenAI to keep using iterative deployment: limited launches, monitored rollouts, more safety gates for higher-risk capabilities, and occasional changes in product boundaries as evidence changes.

## What to watch

The real test is translation. Watch how these principles appear in the Model Spec, API usage policy, system cards, enterprise admin controls, and future model release notes.
