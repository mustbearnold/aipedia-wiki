Meta released Muse Spark this week, positioning itself back in the frontier model race. The model powers Meta's entire ecosystem, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and smart glasses. It handles multimodal input and coordinates multiple sub-agents to tackle complex queries. The architecture includes both fast-response and deeper reasoning modes, letting developers choose speed or accuracy depending on the task. This is significant because Meta had largely stepped back from competing with OpenAI and Anthropic on core models. Muse Spark signals the company is serious about embedding AI across its platforms and devices. The multi-agent coordination capability suggests Meta is betting on agent-based workflows as the future of AI interaction.
