The **2026 Beijing E-Town Humanoid Robot Half Marathon** kicks off today, April 19, 2026. Global teams fielded humanoid and quadruped robots running alongside human participants. Headlining the event: **Alibaba Amap** publicly debuts its first embodied robot, a quadruped from the company's new embodied-intelligence division.

## What's new

**Alibaba Amap's embodied-AI push:**

- **Embodied-intelligence division** established January 2026
- **February 2026:** released two models: **ABot-M0** (general-purpose manipulation) and **ABot-N0** (full-stack navigation)
- **April 19, 2026:** public debut of the first ABot quadruped at the Beijing Half Marathon
- **ABot-World** (Amap's in-house embodied world model) currently ranks #1 on two international benchmarks: **AGIbot World Challenge** and **World Arena**. Outperforms Google, Nvidia, and leading Chinese research teams.

The quadruped is the first physical robot to ship from Alibaba's broader embodied AI program. Amap, best known for its mapping business, is now anchoring Alibaba's robotics ambitions alongside the Qwen model family.

## Why a robot marathon is not a stunt

**Marathons stress four things robots historically fail at:**

1. **Sustained locomotion** over hours (battery, motor thermal management, gait stability)
2. **Real-world terrain** (uneven pavement, turns, inclines)
3. **Dynamic obstacle navigation** (human runners, water stations, camera crews)
4. **Autonomy at the edge** (can't tether to a lab network over 13+ miles)

Last year's inaugural humanoid marathon in Beijing (April 2025) saw most participants fail within the first few kilometers. The 2026 edition is the direct readout on **China's humanoid hardware + embodied AI progress**.

## Field context

**Participating robot makers include:**

- **Alibaba Amap** (first embodied robot debut, ABot quadruped)
- **Tiangong** (Beijing Innovation Center for Human-Machine Interaction)
- **Unitree** (quadrupeds + H1 humanoid)
- **Fourier Intelligence** (GR1 humanoid)
- **AgiBot** (humanoid, OpenAI-rumored partner)
- International teams (undisclosed at time of writing)

## Why this matters

**For Alibaba:** Moving from Qwen foundation models into embodied AI means Alibaba now has the full stack: cloud compute, foundation models ([Qwen 3.6-Plus](/tools/qwen/), Qwen 3.5 Omni), and physical robots. That matches what OpenAI lacks (no first-party robot) and Google partially has (Gemini + partner robotics).

**For China's humanoid race:** The marathon is the clearest public-facing benchmark. A successful finish by multiple teams validates that embodied world models + on-device inference + battery density are all at usable thresholds for multi-hour deployment.

**For the US side:** Physical Intelligence just shipped [π0.7](/news/2026-04-16-physical-intelligence-pi-0-7/) which demonstrated compositional generalization in the lab. The Beijing Marathon is the complementary "does it work outdoors for hours" test. Both angles (generalization + endurance) are needed for robots to matter commercially.

**For Amap users:** Short term, nothing changes. Medium term, expect Amap-delivered tasks (navigation, local search, merchant 3D tours) to integrate with Amap-deployed physical robots for delivery, in-store service, and autonomous local logistics.

## Open questions

- **Completion rate.** How many robots actually finish vs drop out? Fuller signal on 2025 → 2026 capability gain.
- **Autonomy level.** Teleoperated vs fully autonomous vs hybrid. Autonomous finishing is the real milestone.
- **Commercial timeline.** Alibaba hasn't disclosed when the ABot quadruped ships to customers or for what use cases.
- **Western response.** Nvidia (via [Figure AI](https://www.figure.ai)) and Google (via Gemini Robotics) haven't yet responded with equivalent outdoor-marathon demos.
