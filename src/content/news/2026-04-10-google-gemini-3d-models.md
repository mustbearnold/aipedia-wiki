Google quietly shipped one of its most useful Gemini updates this week, interactive 3D models and simulations. Instead of static images, Gemini now generates dynamic visualizations users can manipulate. You can rotate orbital systems, adjust physics parameters, and explore concepts through hands-on interaction. This moves beyond explanation into exploration. The feature aligns with broader industry shifts toward multimodal, interactive AI outputs. Competitors are adding similar capabilities, signaling that static text and images aren't enough anymore. Users expect to interact with AI-generated content. The practical applications are obvious: education, engineering, design, science communication. This is a feature that actually changes how people use the tool rather than a marginal improvement.
