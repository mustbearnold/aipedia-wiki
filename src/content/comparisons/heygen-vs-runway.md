# HeyGen vs Runway

[HeyGen](/tools/heygen/) and [Runway](/tools/runway/) are both AI video tools, but they solve different production jobs. HeyGen is for avatar-led business video. Runway is a production video workspace with generative models, editing, third-party model access, storage, teams, and an API.

**June 5, 2026 verdict:** choose **HeyGen** when the asset needs a presenter. Choose **Runway** when the asset needs generated footage, editing, model switching, or a production pipeline.

## Fast Pick

**Pick HeyGen for:**

- digital-twin sales clips, product explainers, training, localization, and repeatable presenter videos;
- Creator/Pro/Business app plans plus separate Avatar IV & V API usage;
- teams that need the shortest route from script to speaker-led video.

**Pick Runway for:**

- text-to-video, image-to-video, generated B-roll, creative editing, model testing, and video production;
- Gen-4.5, Gen-4 Turbo, Aleph, Act-Two, Veo 3.1, Seedance 2.0, Kling 3.0 Pro, and API routes;
- agencies, creators, and product teams that need to ship edited video, not just render a talking head.

## Pricing Reality

HeyGen's current pricing page shows Free, Creator at $29/month, Pro at $49/month, Business at $149/month plus seats, and Enterprise custom. Its developer docs list Avatar IV & V output-duration pricing, so automated avatar generation should be budgeted separately from the app subscription.

Runway's current pricing page shows Free, Standard at $12/user/month billed annually, Pro at $28/user/month, Max at $76/user/month, and Enterprise custom. The June 5 check also found a meaningful plan-name change: the old Unlimited language has been replaced by Max with 9,500 monthly credits and one-month credit rollover. API credits are purchased separately at $0.01/credit.

## Where HeyGen Wins

HeyGen wins when the message and presenter are the deliverable. A sales team, founder, product marketer, or trainer can start with a script and end with a localized speaker-led video without managing model selection, shots, edits, and timelines.

It is also easier for non-editors. The practical plan path is Creator for recurring solo avatar video, Pro for heavier usage and 4K, Business for seats/SCORM/LMS/collaboration, and Enterprise for governance and custom terms.

## Where Runway Wins

Runway wins when the work is production, not only presentation. The official pricing page lists Gen-4.5, Aleph, Gen-4, Act-Two, Veo 3.1/Veo 3, third-party video models including Seedance 2.0 and Kling 3.0 Pro, all-video-model upscaling, watermark removal on paid plans, assets, editor projects, and team features.

The API docs also matter. Runway lists video models such as `seedance2`, `aleph2`, `gen4.5`, `gen4_turbo`, `gen4_aleph`, `act_two`, `veo3`, `veo3.1`, and `veo3.1_fast`, with per-model credit costs. That makes Runway the stronger shortlist for product teams building media workflows.

## Buyer Route

If the video is a person explaining something, trial HeyGen first. If the brief includes generated scenes, visual edits, character movement, cinematic clips, or multiple model tests, trial Runway first. Use both when the final asset needs a presenter plus generated supporting footage.

## FAQ

**Is Runway better than HeyGen?**
For generated footage, editing, API video, and production workflow, yes. For avatar presenter videos, HeyGen is usually faster.

**Which is cheaper?**
It depends on the asset. HeyGen costs center on avatar app/API usage. Runway costs center on credits, models, storage, and iteration volume.

**Can HeyGen replace Runway?**
No. HeyGen does not replace Runway for cinematic generation, editing, or multi-model production.

**Can Runway replace HeyGen?**
Not cleanly for business-avatar workflows. Use HeyGen or Synthesia when the presenter is the core output.
