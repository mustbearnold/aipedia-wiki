# ElevenLabs vs HeyGen

[ElevenLabs](../tools/elevenlabs.md) and [HeyGen](../tools/heygen.md) overlap around synthetic speech, but the output is different. ElevenLabs is an audio and voice platform for text-to-speech, cloning, dubbing, narration, and voice agents. HeyGen is a video platform for avatar-led clips, translated videos, lip-sync, and script-to-video workflows.

## Quick Answer

Choose ElevenLabs when the deliverable is audio. Choose HeyGen when the deliverable is a video with a presenter, avatar, or translated talking-head format.

## Where ElevenLabs Wins

- Better for voiceovers, narration, podcasts, audiobooks, dubbing audio, and app-integrated speech.
- Stronger fit when voice quality, voice library, cloning, or low-latency audio is the main evaluation.
- Avoids video-production overhead when the audience will only hear the output.
- Easier to embed in products that need speech generation rather than rendered video.
- More flexible for teams producing audio assets across many channels.

## Where HeyGen Wins

- Produces complete avatar videos rather than only audio tracks.
- Better for sales explainers, onboarding videos, translated talking-head clips, and marketing demos.
- Lip-sync and presenter visuals matter when the audience expects a human-like video format.
- Templates and script-to-video workflows reduce the need for a separate editor.
- More useful when localization includes both the voice and the face on screen.

## Key Differences

The choice should start with the final asset. If the output is a voice file, narration layer, agent response, or dubbing track, HeyGen's avatar layer is unnecessary. If the output is a video people will watch, ElevenLabs alone will leave you assembling visuals, captions, timing, and editing elsewhere.

Rights and consent matter for both tools. Voice cloning and avatar-style video can create brand, speaker, and disclosure risk. Teams should confirm authorization, review policies, and avoid using either tool to imitate real people without permission.

## Practical Scenarios

Use ElevenLabs for:

- A product that needs spoken responses from an AI agent.
- Podcast intros, explainer narration, audiobooks, or voiceover tracks.
- Localized audio where the visual layer will be edited in another tool.
- Testing several voices before committing to a final narrator.
- Developer workflows where API behavior matters more than a video editor.

Use HeyGen for:

- A sales or onboarding video that needs a presenter on screen.
- A translated founder, trainer, or spokesperson clip.
- Customer education videos where templates and avatars speed production.
- Social or landing-page videos where visual presence matters.
- Teams that want script, avatar, voice, subtitles, and export in one workflow.

The combined workflow is useful when voice quality needs more attention than the avatar tool provides by default. In that case, generate or refine speech separately, then bring the audio into the video production path with careful timing and review.

## Who should choose ElevenLabs

Choose ElevenLabs for TTS, voice cloning, narration, dubbing audio, audiobook-style output, and voice-agent speech.

## Who should choose HeyGen

Choose HeyGen for avatar videos, localized talking-head clips, sales demos, training videos, and script-to-video production.

## Bottom Line

ElevenLabs is the audio pick. HeyGen is the avatar-video pick. Use the format of the final deliverable to decide, then test one real script end to end before buying a larger plan.

## FAQ

Yes. Some teams generate or refine voice assets separately, then use video tools for presentation, localization, and layout.
