# ElevenLabs vs HeyGen

[ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/) and [HeyGen](/tools/heygen/) both create synthetic media, but they solve different jobs. ElevenLabs is the voice platform: TTS, voice cloning, dubbing, speech-to-text, agents, music, sound effects, and API audio. HeyGen is the avatar-video platform: digital twins, avatar presenters, translation, video localization, SCORM/LMS workflows, and API video generation.

## Quick Answer

Choose ElevenLabs when the voice is the asset. Choose HeyGen when the viewer needs to see a presenter, digital twin, or avatar-led business video. Use both only when a production workflow needs ElevenLabs-level voice control inside a HeyGen-style presenter video.

The practical split is simple: if the audience mainly hears the output, start with ElevenLabs. If the audience watches a person-like presenter, start with HeyGen.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Premium narration or voiceover:** ElevenLabs, because Eleven v3 and Multilingual v2 are voice-first models.
- **Low-latency voice agent:** ElevenLabs, because Flash v2.5 and ElevenAgents map directly to conversational audio output.
- **Digital twin sales video:** HeyGen, because the presenter, face, video template, and avatar export are the product.
- **Localized talking-head clip:** HeyGen, because video translation, lip sync, avatars, and business templates stay together.
- **Audio-only podcast, audiobook, or product voice:** ElevenLabs, because HeyGen adds unnecessary video workflow.
- **Avatar video with a refined external voice:** Use both, but test sync, licensing, and export before committing to volume.

## Where ElevenLabs Wins

ElevenLabs wins when audio quality, voice identity, or API speech is the buying reason. Current ElevenLabs model help identifies Eleven v3 as its advanced expressive speech model and Flash v2.5 as the ultra-low-latency model for speed-sensitive developer use. Current API pricing lists Flash/Turbo TTS at $0.05 per 1K characters and Multilingual v2/v3 at $0.10 per 1K characters.

The broader platform also matters. ElevenLabs covers voice cloning, dubbing, Scribe speech-to-text, voice agents, sound effects, music, and creative production surfaces. HeyGen has voice features, but it is not the cleaner choice for a standalone voice product, voice app, audiobook, podcast, or agent speech layer.

## Where HeyGen Wins

HeyGen wins when the final deliverable is a video with a visible presenter. Current public pricing lists Free, Creator, Pro, Business, and Enterprise routes. Business is positioned for teams, with 1,500 credits, 4K export, SAML/SSO, collaboration, SCORM export, LMS integrations, and workflow integrations.

HeyGen API pricing is also a separate buyer surface. The current developer pricing page says self-serve API usage is billed in USD by output duration. Avatar IV and V API generation starts at $0.05 per second for Photo Avatars at 720p/1080p, with higher rates for Digital Twin, Studio Avatar, and 4K output. That is a different budget from the app-plan buying decision.

## Pricing And Procurement

For ElevenLabs, estimate characters, minutes, voice clones, dubbing, agent traffic, speech-to-text, and concurrency. The main risk is credit or API usage rising after retries, long scripts, or live agent traffic.

For HeyGen, estimate credits, avatar type, resolution, video length, translation mode, seats, SCORM/LMS needs, API usage, and whether LiveAvatar or Enterprise controls are required. The main risk is assuming app-plan credits, API wallet usage, and enterprise/live-avatar budgets behave the same way.

## Who Should Choose ElevenLabs

Choose ElevenLabs for TTS, narration, voiceovers, dubbing audio, audiobooks, podcasts, game dialogue, voice cloning, sound effects, music, speech-to-text, and product voice agents.

Skip ElevenLabs as the primary workflow if stakeholders expect a finished presenter video with avatars, video templates, comments, LMS handoff, or digital twins.

## Who Should Choose HeyGen

Choose HeyGen for avatar-led sales videos, customer education, internal training, onboarding, localized talking-head clips, digital twins, video agents, SCORM/LMS delivery, and script-to-video business workflows.

Skip HeyGen if the output is audio-only, embedded speech, a phone/voice agent, or a high-volume narration pipeline.

## Bottom Line

ElevenLabs is the voice pick. HeyGen is the avatar-video pick. Buy ElevenLabs when sound quality and voice infrastructure define success. Buy HeyGen when the deliverable needs a presenter on screen.

## FAQ

**Is ElevenLabs better than HeyGen?**
For voice quality, TTS, dubbing, and voice agents, yes. For avatar-led presenter videos, HeyGen is the better first test.

**Does HeyGen have an API?**
Yes. Current developer pricing lists self-serve API usage billed by output duration, including Avatar IV and V video generation.

**Can HeyGen replace ElevenLabs for voice?**
Not for voice-first production. HeyGen includes speech and cloning features, but ElevenLabs is stronger when voice is the product.

**Can I use ElevenLabs voices in HeyGen videos?**
Often, but test synchronization, rights, pronunciation, export workflow, and any consent constraints before production.
