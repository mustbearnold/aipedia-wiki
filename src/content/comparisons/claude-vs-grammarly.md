# Claude vs Grammarly

[Claude](../tools/claude.md) provides general AI assistance for tasks like writing, coding, and analysis. [Grammarly](../tools/grammarly.md) focuses on writing improvement through grammar checks, style suggestions, and tone adjustments. This comparison uses flagship versions and pricing as of April 2026.

## Quick Answer

Claude suits complex reasoning and long-form content creation. Grammarly fits quick writing edits in daily workflows.

## Where Claude Wins

- Handles multi-step reasoning and large datasets with 1M token context window.[1]
- Tops benchmarks for expert-level tasks like code writing and document analysis.[1]
- Processes entire documents or hours of content in one session.[2]
- Integrates into workflows for automation and research synthesis.[4]
- Free tier offers real value for basic use.[2]

## Where Grammarly Wins

- Provides real-time grammar, spelling, and punctuation corrections across apps.[7]
- Suggests style and tone adjustments for clarity and engagement.
- Integrates directly into browsers, email, and docs for seamless editing.
- Tracks writing patterns over time for personalized improvements.
- Lower cost for focused writing support without general AI needs.

## Key Differences

Claude Opus 4.7 excels in broad tasks with a 1M token context window for handling large inputs like full reports or codebases.[1] It leads benchmarks for reasoning and analysis.[1][3] Grammarly Premium targets writing refinement, offering inline suggestions in tools like Google Docs or email without needing a separate chat interface. Claude requires a Pro subscription at $20/month for full access, while Grammarly Premium costs $12/month.[1] Claude supports multimodal inputs in advanced setups, but Grammarly sticks to text processing.[2]

## Who should choose Claude

Users needing analysis of long documents, code generation, or multi-step tasks benefit from Claude's context and reasoning.[1][5]

## Who should choose Grammarly

Writers focused on polishing emails, reports, or posts find Grammarly's integrations and suggestions efficient for everyday edits.

## Bottom Line

Choose Claude for versatile AI tasks beyond writing. Pick Grammarly for targeted improvements in prose. Many combine both: Claude for drafting, Grammarly for final checks.
