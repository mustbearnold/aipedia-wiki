# Connected Papers vs nanochat

[Connected Papers](../tools/connected-papers.md) and [nanochat](../tools/nanochat.md) are tools in the research category as of April 2026. Connected Papers visualizes paper connections via graphs; nanochat provides conversational AI research assistance.

## Quick Answer

Connected Papers suits visual paper discovery; nanochat fits interactive querying. Choice depends on preference for graphs versus chat.

## At a Glance

| | Connected Papers | nanochat |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Graph-based visualization (v1.0) | GPT-5.4 integration |
| **Price** | Free basic; $3/month pro | Free / $20/month Plus |
| **Best For** | Paper similarity graphs | Conversational research |

## Where Connected Papers Wins

- Displays papers as interactive graphs to reveal connections missed in lists.
- Free tier covers core graph features for most users.
- Focuses solely on academic literature without distractions.
- Exports graphs for presentations.
- Handles large paper sets via similarity clustering.

## Where nanochat Wins

- Supports natural language queries across topics, not just papers.
- Integrates real-time web data and document analysis.
- Offers multimodal input like images or audio for research.
- Scales to complex tasks with 2M token context.
- Includes voice mode for hands-free use.

## Key Differences

Connected Papers builds static graphs from seed papers to show similarity clusters, clusters, aiding discovery of related work without reading abstracts. nanochat uses conversational AI (GPT-5.4) for dynamic queries, summarizing findings with citations, and handling diverse formats like datasets or videos. Pricing differs: Connected Papers pro at $3/month unlocks unlimited graphs; nanochat Plus at $20/month adds priority access and longer contexts. Output: graphs versus text summaries.

## Who should choose Connected Papers

Literature reviewers or citation mappers who need visual overviews. Teams preparing paper reviews benefit from shareable graphs.

## Who should choose nanochat

General researchers querying across sources or needing synthesis. Users in Google Workspace gain from multimodal integration.

## Bottom Line

Use Connected Papers for paper-centric visualization at low cost. Switch to nanochat for broader, interactive research with current AI models. Many combine both for comprehensive workflows.
