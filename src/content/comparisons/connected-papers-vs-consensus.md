# Connected Papers vs Consensus

[Connected Papers](../tools/connected-papers.md) and [Consensus](../tools/consensus.md) are two tools for academic research discovery as of April 2026. Connected Papers visualizes paper connections via similarity graphs; Consensus uses AI to summarize and extract insights from research papers.

## Quick Answer

Consensus fits workflows needing direct answers and evidence extraction from papers. Connected Papers suits visual exploration of related literature.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Connected Papers | Consensus |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Graph-based similarity visualization (v1.1.3) | AI research agent with evidence extraction (powered by GPT-5.3, Claude Opus 4.7) |
| **Price** | Free basic; Pro $3/mo or $36/yr | Free basic; Plus $8.99/mo or $96/yr; Pro $22.99/mo |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | Unlimited graph nodes; 200 papers max per graph | 2M token context; extracts key findings, P-values, study sizes |
| **Best For** | Visual literature mapping | Synthesizing answers from 200M+ papers |

## Where Connected Papers Wins

- Builds interactive graphs showing paper similarity based on citations and content, aiding broad topic exploration.
- Free tier allows unlimited searches and 10 graphs/month, sufficient for occasional use.
- Focuses on discovering prior and derivative works without AI summarization bias.
- Simple interface loads graphs quickly for seminars or grant prep.
- Exports graphs as images or lists for presentations.

## Where Consensus Wins

- Answers specific questions by searching 200M papers and citing evidence with study details.
- Extracts metrics like effect sizes and P-values directly from full texts.
- Integrates models like GPT-5.3 for synthesis across conflicting studies.
- Pro plan includes unlimited queries and team features for collaborative research.
- Tracks paper agreement levels (e.g., 80% studies support X).

## Key Differences

Connected Papers emphasizes visual networks to uncover hidden paper relationships through algorithms matching titles, abstracts, and references. Consensus acts as a search engine with AI agents that parse full texts for answers, providing verdict summaries like "Yes, with strong evidence" alongside citations. Pricing reflects this: Connected Papers Pro at $3/month supports more graphs; Consensus Plus at $8.99/month unlocks advanced synthesis. Output from Connected Papers is static graphs; Consensus delivers prose answers with extracted data.

## Who should choose Connected Papers

Researchers mapping literature visually for reviews or classes. Teams needing citation graphs without text analysis.

## Who should choose Consensus

Users querying specific research questions needing evidence pulls. Groups synthesizing meta-insights from large paper sets.

## Bottom Line

Choose Connected Papers for graph-based discovery at low cost. Opt for Consensus when extracting answers and metrics from papers matters more. Many combine both for comprehensive workflows.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Connected Papers Pro at $3/month undercuts Consensus Plus at $8.99/month; both have capable free tiers.

**Which has better output quality?**  
Consensus provides cited answers with extracted data; Connected Papers offers reliable similarity graphs without interpretation.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, start with Connected Papers graphs to find papers, then query Consensus for insights.
