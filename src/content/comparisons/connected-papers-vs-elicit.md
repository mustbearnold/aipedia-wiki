# Connected Papers vs Elicit

[Connected Papers](/tools/connected-papers/) and [Elicit](/tools/elicit/) both help researchers move faster, but they live at different stages of the review workflow. Connected Papers maps related work from a seed paper. Elicit searches 138M+ papers, supports screening and extraction, and packages a systematic-review workflow for teams that need repeatable evidence tables.

## Quick Answer

Use **Connected Papers** for the first hour of field mapping. Use **Elicit** when the job becomes screening papers, extracting fields, exporting tables, or producing a systematic-review-style report. If you only need to find adjacent papers, Connected Papers is simpler. If you need structured evidence, Elicit is the serious workflow tool.

## Decision Snapshot

| | Connected Papers | Elicit |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary job** | Visual paper graph from a seed work | Literature search, screening, extraction, reports, systematic reviews |
| **Best buyer** | Researcher entering an unfamiliar topic | Researcher, lab, policy team, or evidence-synthesis group |
| **Current pricing signal** | Free limited graph tier plus paid annual plans; verify graph limits at checkout | Basic free; Plus/Pro/Scale paid; Enterprise custom; billing cadence changes the visible price |
| **Best output** | Graph, Prior Works, Derivative Works, paper list | Evidence table, extraction columns, exports, research report |
| **Main risk** | Discovery without synthesis or methods review | AI extraction still needs human quality checks |

## Where Connected Papers Wins

Connected Papers is the lighter tool. It gives a fast visual map without asking the buyer to design a review protocol, extraction schema, or report workflow.

Choose Connected Papers when:

- one known paper should reveal adjacent work
- the user needs a field map before defining search terms
- the output is a paper list or related-work scan
- a low-friction visual workflow matters more than extraction
- the buyer is early in a thesis, grant, or technical landscape scan

## Where Elicit Wins

Elicit wins when the research job needs structure. Its pricing page and help documentation position it around Research Agent access, paper search, automated reports, systematic reviews, screening, extraction columns, exports, API access, collaboration, and enterprise controls.

Choose Elicit when:

- the review requires inclusion/exclusion criteria
- the buyer needs extraction columns for sample size, population, outcomes, or methods
- exports to RIS, CSV, BIB, PDF, DOCX, or API workflows matter
- the team needs collaboration, alerts, templates, or admin controls
- the output will inform a formal review, policy brief, evidence map, or report

## Key Differences

Connected Papers is a map. Elicit is a workflow. Connected Papers can show that a paper belongs in a cluster, but it will not screen the cluster or extract comparable fields. Elicit can produce structured tables, but it is heavier than needed when the buyer only wants a visual path from one landmark paper.

Pricing should be read through workload. Connected Papers can be enough for occasional visual mapping. Elicit Pro or Scale makes sense only when systematic review, extraction capacity, exports, or collaboration save real researcher time.

## Who Should Choose Connected Papers

Choose Connected Papers if the user is still asking "what is the literature around this paper?" It is ideal for visual learners, students, grant writers, and analysts who need fast orientation before deeper reading.

## Who Should Choose Elicit

Choose Elicit if the user is asking "which papers meet my criteria, what data do they contain, and how do I export the evidence?" It is the stronger pick for systematic reviews, scoping reviews, meta-analysis prep, policy research, and repeatable evidence workflows.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. Start with Connected Papers to discover clusters and foundational works. Move promising papers into Elicit for search expansion, screening, extraction, and export. Then inspect original PDFs before citing.

## Bottom Line

Pick **Connected Papers** for graph-based discovery. Pick **Elicit** for structured evidence work. If the output is a map, Connected Papers wins. If the output is a table, report, or review protocol, Elicit wins.

## FAQ

**Is Elicit overkill for casual research?**
Often, yes. Elicit shines when extraction and screening matter. For casual field mapping, Connected Papers or Semantic Scholar may be faster.

**Can Connected Papers do systematic reviews?**
No. It can help find related papers, but it does not manage protocol setup, screening, extraction, or PRISMA-style workflow.

**Which should a student buy first?**
Start with the free tiers. Pay for Connected Papers if you need more graphs. Pay for Elicit when exports, reports, or extraction tables become necessary.
