# Connected Papers vs Elicit

[Connected Papers](../tools/connected-papers.md) and [Elicit](../tools/elicit.md) are two tools for academic literature discovery and analysis as of April 2026. Connected Papers visualizes paper connections via similarity graphs; Elicit uses AI for search, extraction, and summarization across millions of papers.

## Quick Answer

Elicit suits workflows needing AI-driven extraction and synthesis from papers. Connected Papers fits visual exploration of citation networks.

## At a Glance

| | Connected Papers | Elicit |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Graph-based similarity visualization (v1.4) | Elicit 3.0 with GPT-5.3 integration |
| **Price** | Free basic; Plus $3/mo; Pro $12/mo | Free basic; Plus $12/mo; Enterprise $50+/user/mo |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | Unlimited graph nodes; 5 free PDFs/mo | 200k tokens; 200 papers/query; table/CSV export |
| **Best For** | Visual paper discovery | AI-assisted extraction and workflows |

## Where Connected Papers Wins

- Builds interactive graphs from seed papers to reveal similar works by content overlap, not just citations.
- Free tier allows 5 graphs/month with unlimited browsing of public graphs.
- Simple upload of one PDF generates a map instantly without login for basics.
- Focuses on forward/backward citation chains for quick literature mapping.
- No AI hallucinations; relies on Semantic Scholar embeddings for accuracy.

## Where Elicit Wins

- Searches 200M+ papers with natural language queries and extracts data into tables (e.g., methods, results).
- Automates workflows like systematic reviews with brainstorming, screening, and synthesis steps.
- Integrates LLMs like GPT-5.3 for summarization, comparison of 8+ papers at once.
- Exports to CSV, PDF, BibTeX; supports team collaboration in paid tiers.
- Tracks full audit trails for reproducibility in research pipelines.

## Key Differences

Connected Papers emphasizes visual graphs for discovering related papers through vector similarity, making it direct for exploratory browsing without text queries. Elicit acts as an AI research assistant, handling semantic search, data extraction from full texts, and multi-step tasks like evidence grading or meta-analysis. Pricing starts similar at $12/mo for pro features, but Elicit scales to enterprise with higher limits (e.g., 400 credits/mo vs Connected Papers' graph/PDF caps). Output from Connected Papers stays visual/static; Elicit generates structured tables, notes, and exports for further analysis.

## Who should choose Connected Papers

Researchers starting from a known paper who want quick visual overviews of the field. It works well for solo explorers avoiding AI-generated text.

## Who should choose Elicit

Teams conducting literature reviews, evidence synthesis, or data extraction at scale. It fits systematic workflows needing automation and collaboration.

## Bottom Line

Neither tool fully replaces manual reading; both draw from Semantic Scholar. Pick Connected Papers for graph-based discovery, Elicit for query-driven extraction. Many users combine them: graph in Connected Papers, then extract in Elicit.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Connected Papers Pro at $12/mo matches Elicit's Plus tier; free tiers differ (5 graphs vs 5,000 credits).

**Which has better output quality?**  
Elicit for structured data extraction (LLM-powered); Connected Papers for citation-agnostic similarity graphs.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes; start with Connected Papers graphs for discovery, move to Elicit for analysis.
