# Bolt.new vs Canva AI

[Bolt.new](/tools/bolt/) and [Canva](/tools/canva/) are both marketed to people who want to make things faster with AI, but they produce different artifacts. Bolt is a browser-native AI app builder from StackBlitz for generating, running, debugging, hosting, and iterating JavaScript web apps. Canva is a broad visual design platform for presentations, social posts, docs, brand assets, video, templates, and AI-assisted design workflows.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Bolt.new** when the desired output is a working web app, prototype, MVP, or code-backed experience. Choose **Canva AI** when the desired output is a polished visual asset, brand-safe design, deck, social campaign, marketing graphic, or lightweight content package.

## Decision Snapshot

| | Bolt.new | Canva AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Best buyer** | Founders, developers, product teams, students, and agencies building browser apps | Marketers, creators, sales teams, educators, agencies, and non-designers shipping visual content |
| **Core job** | Prompt-to-app generation, files, preview, WebContainers, Bolt Cloud, and publishing | Templates, Magic Studio/Canva AI, brand kits, design collaboration, presentations, video, docs, and visual publishing |
| **June 2026 pricing anchor** | Free tier; Pro $25/mo; Teams $30/member/mo; Enterprise custom | Free; Pro and Business pricing are region-rendered; Enterprise custom; AI Pass may add cost for Pro/Business buyers |
| **Primary artifact** | Running JavaScript app or prototype | Visual design asset, deck, page, video, document, or brand template |
| **Avoid if** | You mostly need graphics, brand assets, or social content | You need generated source code, app runtime, database path, or deployable web app |

## Where Bolt.new Wins

- **Real app output.** Bolt can create a runnable project with files, preview, debugging, hosting, databases, auth paths, secrets, logs, and publishing rather than a static design.
- **No local setup.** WebContainers make it useful when installing Node, configuring a dev environment, or handing off a demo would slow the team down.
- **Founder/MVP speed.** If the job is "make this product idea clickable," Bolt is closer to the final artifact than Canva.
- **Code ownership path.** Teams can inspect and harden generated code instead of being locked into a design canvas.
- **Clearer app-builder economics.** Token usage still matters, but the buyer is paying for app-building workflow, not a general design suite.

## Where Canva AI Wins

- **Visual production.** Canva is better for social graphics, decks, ads, brand templates, docs, video, and fast marketing assets.
- **Non-technical adoption.** Most users can ship useful work without understanding components, package errors, or deployment logs.
- **Brand governance.** Brand kits, templates, approval workflows, and shared assets matter more than code when the output is customer-facing creative.
- **Team breadth.** Canva fits many departments at once: marketing, sales, HR, education, support, and operations.
- **Lower-risk daily workflow.** A flawed Canva draft is usually an edit problem; a flawed app-builder draft can become a security, data, dependency, or deploy problem.

## Pricing reality check

Do not compare Canva's region-rendered design pricing with Bolt's token-based app-builder pricing as if they buy the same thing. Bolt spend should be modeled around projects, token burn, hosting needs, and whether the prototype becomes production code. Canva spend should be modeled around seats, brand controls, AI access, templates, approvals, and how many teams rely on it for content output.

## Bottom Line

Bolt is for app creation. Canva is for visual communication. If the output needs to run, pick Bolt. If the output needs to look polished and ship through a brand/content workflow, pick Canva. Many teams use both: Canva for launch assets and decks, Bolt for the product prototype behind the campaign.

## FAQ

**Is Bolt.new better than Canva AI?**
Only for web apps and prototypes. Canva is better for design, brand, social, deck, and marketing workflows.

**Which is cheaper?**
It depends on the job. Canva can be cheaper for visual content teams; Bolt can be cheaper than hiring engineering time for early app prototypes, but token burn and production hardening must be budgeted.

**Can Canva replace Bolt?**
No. Canva can mock up ideas and publish visual assets, but it does not replace a code-backed app builder.
