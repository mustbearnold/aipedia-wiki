# Figma vs Google Stitch

[Figma](/tools/figma/) and [Google Stitch](/tools/google-stitch/) both help teams move from idea to interface, but they should not be bought for the same reason in June 2026. Figma is the durable product-design workspace: components, libraries, prototypes, Dev Mode, comments, governance, Figma Make, Sites, and AI credits. Google Stitch is the faster AI-native canvas for exploring high-fidelity UI ideas from natural language, images, text, code context, voice, DESIGN.md, MCP/SDK, and exports into developer tools.

The practical buyer rule is simple: **use Stitch to explore directions; use Figma to make the chosen direction governable.** Stitch is exciting because it compresses the first draft. Figma still wins when the artifact must survive design critique, accessibility review, component discipline, stakeholder comments, and engineering handoff.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Figma** if the output needs to become a real product-design source of truth: a shared file, prototype, component library, design-system artifact, or Dev Mode handoff that designers, PMs, engineers, and stakeholders can inspect together.

Choose **Google Stitch** if the output is early UI exploration: multiple directions, quick app-screen ideation, voice-guided critique, interactive flow sketches, or design-system experiments before a team commits production design time.

There is no single winner. The best workflow is often Stitch for divergent exploration and Figma for convergent product design.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Production product design:** Figma wins because files, components, libraries, comments, versions, prototypes, variables, Dev Mode, and admin controls are the product workflow.
- **Fast UI ideation:** Google Stitch wins because Google describes it as an AI-native software design canvas for natural-language high-fidelity UI creation and rapid iteration.
- **Design-system governance:** Figma wins because Organization and Enterprise plans are built around shared libraries, branching, SCIM, workspaces, and controls.
- **Founder concept sketching:** Google Stitch wins when the founder needs to see many app-screen directions before hiring design help or building.
- **Engineering handoff:** Figma wins for design-to-engineering handoff. Stitch can export to developer tools, but the output still needs review before production reliance.
- **Lowest procurement risk:** Figma wins because pricing, seats, credits, and enterprise controls are public. Stitch is still a Labs product surface with volatile access, quota, and commercial terms.
- **AI design critique and voice exploration:** Google Stitch wins for the first-pass collaborative AI canvas experience described in Google's March and May 2026 updates.

## Pricing Reality

Figma has published buyer math. Its June 5 pricing check shows Starter free, Professional Full seat at $16/month, Organization Full seat at $55/month billed annually, and Enterprise Full seat at $90/month billed annually. Starter includes 150 AI credits per day up to 500 per month. Professional Full seats include 3,000 AI credits per month, Organization Full seats include 3,500, and Enterprise Full seats include 4,250. Dev and Collab seats are cheaper but have different product access and credit allowances.

Google Stitch is not a normal procurement line item yet. Google's public Stitch surfaces describe the product, canvas, design agent, DESIGN.md, MCP/SDK, voice interaction, AI Studio/Antigravity export paths, and web publishing/developer handoff direction. They do not publish a stable public paid plan, quota table, enterprise SLA, or long-term commercial package. Treat any third-party quota or future paid-tier claim as unverified unless Google publishes it.

The buying implication:

- **Figma cost scales by seats and AI-credit usage.** Budget by who needs Full seats, who only needs Dev or Collab access, and how much Make/Sites/AI work the team will run.
- **Stitch cost risk is uncertainty.** It may be cheap to test, but teams should not plan a procurement workflow around unpublished commercial terms.
- **The cheapest test is Stitch plus Figma Starter or Professional.** Explore in Stitch, then move only useful directions into the Figma workflow that actually needs review and handoff.

## Workflow Fit

Figma is where design becomes organizational memory. A screen can turn into a component, component variant, design-system decision, prototype, design token, comment thread, or Dev Mode reference. Figma Make adds prompt-to-functional-prototype and web-app generation, but the strongest reason to buy Figma remains the shared design process around the artifact.

Google Stitch is where a blank idea becomes visible quickly. Google's March update describes an AI-native infinite canvas, design agents, design-system context through DESIGN.md, instant interactive prototypes, voice critique, MCP/SDK, and exports to developer tools. Google's May 19 update adds real-time design with the Stitch Agent, text or voice input, existing codebase/design-file context, shareable links through Google AI Studio, export to Google Antigravity, and Netlify publishing.

That makes Stitch a front-of-funnel design accelerator. It can help product people, founders, and designers create more options before committing. It should not be treated as a replacement for Figma's production design system until the team has tested editability, accessibility, brand fit, export quality, and governance.

## Best Plan Guidance

Start with **Figma Professional Full seat at $16/month** for a designer or product owner who needs unlimited files, team libraries, advanced prototyping, Dev Mode inspection, and 3,000 monthly AI credits. Move to Organization or Enterprise when cross-team libraries, branch/merge, centralized administration, SSO, SCIM, security, or procurement controls are the reason.

Start with **Google Stitch as an experiment**, not a committed procurement platform. Use it for workshops, founder ideation, early product directions, and quick UI alternatives. Before relying on it for client or enterprise work, verify current access, export behavior, privacy terms, quota limits, and downstream developer-tool quality in the live product.

Do not buy Figma just to generate a first rough UI. Do not trust Stitch as the final source of truth for a serious product. They sit at different stages of the same workflow.

## Who Should Choose Figma

Choose Figma if the buyer is a product designer, design lead, product manager, engineering lead, design-system owner, or company that already treats interface decisions as team infrastructure. Figma is the stronger choice when the artifact needs components, variants, responsive states, comments, inspectable details, variables, Dev Mode, governance, and long-term maintainability.

Figma is also the safer default when the buyer needs commercial predictability. Pricing, AI-credit allowances, seat types, and enterprise controls are public enough to model.

## Who Should Choose Google Stitch

Choose Google Stitch if the buyer wants to explore UI directions quickly from intent rather than specifications. It is especially useful for founders, hackathon teams, PMs, designers doing broad exploration, and teams that want AI critique or voice-driven iteration before settling into a production design tool.

Stitch is not the right final stop when the buyer needs component governance, production design QA, a mature accessibility workflow, or stable procurement terms.

## FAQ

**Is Figma or Google Stitch better for product design?**

Figma is better for production product design because it is built around files, components, prototypes, design systems, comments, Dev Mode, and organization controls. Stitch is better for early UI exploration.

**Is Google Stitch free?**

Google's public Stitch pages do not publish a durable paid plan or quota table. Treat access, quotas, and future billing as volatile Labs facts and verify in the live product before planning around it.

**Can Google Stitch replace Figma?**

Not for most teams. Stitch can accelerate first drafts and flow exploration. Figma remains the safer system of record for product teams that need governed design files, reusable components, review, and handoff.

**Can I use both?**

Yes. Use Stitch to generate and critique several directions, then recreate or refine the winning direction in Figma for design-system alignment and engineering handoff.

**Which is better for developers?**

Neither is a full coding IDE. Figma is better for design specs and Dev Mode handoff. Stitch is better for exporting early design ideas into developer-tool workflows, but the output should still be reviewed.
