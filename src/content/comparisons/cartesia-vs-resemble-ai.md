# Cartesia vs Resemble AI

[Cartesia](../tools/cartesia.md) and [Resemble AI](../tools/resemble-ai.md) compete in the AI voice synthesis space as of April 2026. Cartesia focuses on low-latency streaming TTS for real-time applications, while Resemble AI emphasizes multilingual voice cloning and custom model training.

## Quick Answer

Cartesia suits low-latency needs like live agents at $0.004 per 1k chars; Resemble AI fits custom cloning projects at $0.015 per 1k chars. Choice depends on latency priority versus voice customization depth.

## At a Glance

| | Cartesia | Resemble AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Sonic 1.2 Turbo | Resemble 3.0 |
| **Price** | $0.004/1k chars | $0.015/1k chars |
| **Best For** | Real-time streaming | Multilingual cloning |

## Where Cartesia Wins

- 150ms end-to-end latency enables live conversational AI[1].
- Sonic model generates 200k+ hours daily with consistent quality[2].
- API supports 100+ languages in streaming mode[3].
- Pay-per-char pricing scales for high-volume production[4].
- Integrates directly with telephony systems without buffering[5].

## Where Resemble AI Wins

- One-shot voice cloning from 10s audio samples[6].
- 70+ languages with regional accents in flagship model[7].
- Custom model training via dashboard for brands.
- Built-in watermarking and compliance tools.
- Enterprise plans include dedicated support and SLAs.

## Key Differences

Cartesia's **Sonic 1.2 Turbo** prioritizes speed with 150ms latency for applications like voice assistants, priced at $0.004 per 1k characters on pay-as-you-go[1][2]. Resemble AI's **Resemble 3.0** excels in cloning accuracy across 70+ languages, at $0.015 per 1k characters, with options for fine-tuned models[6][7]. Cartesia handles higher throughput for real-time use; Resemble offers deeper customization for branded voices.

## Who should choose Cartesia

Teams building live voice agents or games where sub-200ms latency matters. Its pricing supports scaling to millions of interactions monthly.

## Who should choose Resemble AI

Content creators or enterprises needing precise voice replicas in multiple languages. Custom training fits ongoing production with compliance needs.

## Bottom Line

Use Cartesia for speed-critical apps; select Resemble AI for cloning versatility. Test both APIs with your prompts, as output varies by use case. Most users combine them for hybrid workflows.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Cartesia at $0.004/1k chars beats Resemble AI's $0.015/1k chars for volume use[1][6].

**Which has better output quality?**  
Resemble AI leads in cloning fidelity; Cartesia matches in naturalness for generic voices[2][7].

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, route low-latency tasks to Cartesia and cloning to Resemble AI via API orchestration[3].
