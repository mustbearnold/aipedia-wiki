# Elicit vs nanochat

[Elicit](../tools/elicit.md) and [nanochat](../tools/nanochat.md) are AI tools for research tasks as of April 2026. Elicit focuses on literature review and paper analysis; nanochat provides general conversational research support.

## Quick Answer

Elicit suits structured academic workflows with paper extraction and synthesis. Nanochat fits quick, flexible queries across topics.

## At a Glance

| | Elicit | nanochat |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | GPT-5.4 integration | Grok 4.20 Beta 2 |
| **Price** | Free / Plus $12/mo / Team $25/user/mo | Free / Pro $20/mo |
| **Context Window** | 2M tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Academic literature review | General research chats |

## Where Elicit Wins

- Extracts data tables from PDFs with high accuracy for systematic reviews.
- Searches 125M+ academic papers, filters by methodology and study design.
- Generates summaries, synthesizes findings across 100+ papers.
- Tracks citation networks and impact over time.
- Exports to RIS, BibTeX for reference managers.

## Where nanochat Wins

- Handles multi-step reasoning and agentic tasks with multi-agent verification.
- Supports real-time web browsing, code execution in chats.
- Processes images, audio alongside text for broader research.
- Lower latency for iterative querying.
- Customizable modes for reasoning or creativity.

## Key Differences

Elicit specializes in academic databases, pulling structured data like PICO elements (population, intervention, comparison, outcome) from papers, while nanochat uses general models like Grok 4.20 Beta 2 for open-ended research across web sources.[1][2] Elicit pricing starts at $12/mo for Plus with unlimited searches; nanochat offers Pro at $20/mo with higher limits.[3] Context handling favors Elicit's 2M token window for long papers versus nanochat's 1M for conversations.

## Who should choose Elicit

Researchers in medicine, social sciences need paper-specific tools. Teams benefit from collaborative features and export options.

## Who should choose nanochat

Users want one interface for diverse research, including non-academic topics. Solo workflows gain from speed and multimodality.

## Bottom Line

Choice depends on needs: Elicit for academic depth, nanochat for versatility. Test free tiers; upgrade based on query volume. Both integrate top models like GPT-5.4 or Grok 4.20.[1][3]

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Elicit Plus at $12/mo undercuts nanochat Pro at $20/mo for core features.[3]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Elicit excels in paper accuracy; nanochat leads in reasoning benchmarks like GDPval-AA.[2]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, combine Elicit for literature with nanochat for synthesis and web checks.
