# Captions.ai vs Synthesia

[Captions.ai](/tools/captions/) and [Synthesia](/tools/synthesia/) both sit near the avatar-video category, but they are not bought for the same operating job. Captions.ai is a short-form social video editor for creators, UGC ads, AI Twin clips, captions, AI Edit, Mirage actors, dubbing, denoise, eye contact, and mobile-first publishing. Synthesia is a business presenter-video platform for training, onboarding, compliance, localization, PowerPoint-to-video, SCORM export, API workflows, SAML/SSO, and governed rollout.

## Quick Answer

Pick Captions.ai first when the work ends in TikTok, Reels, Shorts, paid social, creator-led product clips, captioned talking-head edits, AI Twin output, or fast mobile publishing.

Pick Synthesia first when the video is part of an L&D, HR, compliance, onboarding, enablement, internal comms, or multilingual business-training system.

The practical rule: Captions.ai is the creator editing and short-form distribution pick. Synthesia is the governed enterprise presenter-video pick.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Short-form social clips:** Captions.ai.
- **Captioned creator edits and UGC-style variants:** Captions.ai.
- **AI Twin, Mirage actor, and chat-edit workflows:** Captions.ai.
- **Enterprise training and onboarding:** Synthesia.
- **SCORM, LMS, SAML/SSO, and governed rollout:** Synthesia.
- **PowerPoint-to-video and structured business communication:** Synthesia.
- **Programmatic video generation:** Synthesia when Creator or Enterprise API access is the purchase path; Captions.ai only if the Mirage API beta is the actual route.
- **Marketing avatar presenter clips:** compare [HeyGen](/tools/heygen/) too.
- **Cinematic scene generation:** neither. Use [Runway](/tools/runway/), [Veo](/tools/veo/), [Kling](/tools/kling/), or [Seedance](/tools/seedance/).

## What Changed In June 2026

Captions.ai's June 29 check keeps the public self-serve ladder stable: Pro at $9.99/month with 200 credits, Max at $24.99/month with 500 credits, Scale at $69.99/month with 1,400 credits, Scale 2x and Scale 4x for larger credit pools, and Enterprise custom. The buying caveat is still credit burn across Prompt to Video, AI Edit, AI Creator, AI Ads, AI Skits, chat editing, model routes, and generative media. Captions.ai help surfaces can also disagree about rollover, so serious teams should verify rollover and top-up behavior inside the account before budgeting.

Synthesia's June 29 check keeps Basic free, Starter at $29/month monthly or $18/month annual, Creator at $89/month monthly or $64/month annual, and Enterprise custom. Public pricing lists credits as the shared usage currency, Creator API access with included annual minutes, Personal Avatar allowances by tier, Studio Express-1 avatar creation as a paid annual add-on for eligible annual plan users, and Enterprise controls such as SAML/SSO, live collaboration, brand kits, SCORM export, onboarding, implementation services, and dedicated CSM support.

## Where Captions.ai Wins

Captions.ai wins when the buyer needs volume, speed, and social-native editing more than training governance. It can start from uploaded footage, a script, a prompt, AI Twin output, Mirage actors, or creator assets, then move through captions, AI Edit, chat edits, AI Creator, AI Ads, AI Skits, AI Lipdub, dubbing, denoise, eye contact, and export.

The first paid decision is usually Pro versus Max. Pro is the lower-cost watermark-free editing plan. Max is the real AI-video plan because it adds AI Twin/custom actor scale, Mirage-generated actors, chat editing, AI Edit, AI Creator, generative media, and 500 monthly credits. Scale is only rational after the team has measured real credit burn.

Use Captions.ai when the deliverable is a social asset, creator ad, founder clip, product short, or quick vertical video. Compare [OpusClip](/tools/opusclip/) if the source is a long podcast, webinar, or livestream that needs repurposing instead of a fresh creator clip.

## Where Synthesia Wins

Synthesia wins when the video needs to survive procurement, training governance, localization, and internal distribution. Its value is less about social speed and more about structured presenter video: PowerPoint import, avatar libraries, Personal Avatars, AI Dubbing, translations, templates, SCORM export, collaboration, brand kits, SAML/SSO, API access, and implementation support.

Creator is the self-serve production tier to inspect because public pricing lists API access and 360 included annual video minutes. Enterprise is the safer route when the buyer needs unlimited video minutes, larger avatar libraries, unlimited Personal Avatars, SAML/SSO, SCORM, onboarding, implementation services, dedicated customer support, and governance.

Use Synthesia when the deliverable is a training module, onboarding sequence, SOP video, compliance update, product education clip, or enablement asset. Compare [HeyGen vs Synthesia](/compare/heygen-vs-synthesia/) if sales and marketing presenter speed is competing with L&D governance.

## Buying Recommendation

Start with Captions.ai if the first deliverable is a short social video and the bottleneck is editing speed, captions, AI Twin/custom actor clips, dubbing, denoise, eye contact, or publishing cadence. Pro is enough for watermark-free edits and captions. Max is the right first test when AI Twin, Mirage actors, AI Creator, AI Edit, chat editing, and generative assets are central.

Start with Synthesia if the first deliverable is a governed business video and the bottleneck is training rollout, localization, SCORM/LMS packaging, review, brand consistency, or enterprise controls. Basic is an evaluation path, Starter is light individual use, Creator is the practical self-serve production tier, and Enterprise is the training-governance route.

Do not choose either tool as a raw video-model benchmark. Captions.ai and Synthesia are workflow products. Use Runway, Veo, Kling, Seedance, Luma, or Hailuo when the buyer wants generated scenes, camera motion, or cinematic B-roll as the primary output.

## Watch-Outs

- Captions.ai is not the cleanest fit for enterprise training libraries, SCORM, SSO, LMS rollout, or governed employee education.
- Synthesia is not the fastest fit for phone-first creator edits, social captions, UGC-style variants, or daily TikTok/Reels/Shorts production.
- Captions.ai credits can burn quickly across generative routes. Confirm rollover and top-up rules in the account before modeling monthly output.
- Synthesia buyers should not compare only sticker prices. API, credits, Personal Avatar creation, seats, Studio Express-1, SCORM, and Enterprise terms can change the real cost.
- Both tools need consent and disclosure discipline for synthetic presenters, employee avatars, AI Twins, product claims, ads, and training content.

## FAQ

**Is Captions.ai better than Synthesia?**
Only for short-form social workflows: creator edits, captions, AI Twin clips, UGC-style variants, dubbing, and mobile publishing. Synthesia is better for enterprise training, SCORM/LMS, PowerPoint-to-video, localization, and governed presenter video.

**Which is cheaper?**
Captions.ai has the lower self-serve social-video entry price: Pro is $9.99/month and Max is $24.99/month. Synthesia has a free Basic plan, Starter at $29/month monthly or $18/month annual, and Creator at $89/month monthly or $64/month annual. Real cost depends on credits, minutes, seats, API usage, avatar creation, and workflow volume.

**Which should a training team test first?**
Synthesia. Its public pricing and enterprise positioning fit L&D, HR, compliance, SCORM export, SAML/SSO, collaboration, and implementation support better than Captions.ai.

**Which should a creator or paid-social team test first?**
Captions.ai, especially when the work is short-form social video, AI Twin clips, UGC-style ads, captions, dubbing, denoise, and fast vertical publishing.

**Can either replace Runway or Veo?**
No. Captions.ai and Synthesia are avatar/editing/presenter workflows. Use Runway, Veo, Kling, Seedance, Luma, or Hailuo for cinematic generation and raw video-model testing.

## Bottom Line

Captions.ai is the better first test for short-form creator video, captions, AI Twin clips, UGC-style variants, and mobile publishing. Synthesia is the better first test for enterprise training, onboarding, compliance, localization, SCORM/LMS, PowerPoint-to-video, and governed presenter-video operations. Choose Captions.ai when the workflow ends in social distribution; choose Synthesia when the workflow needs a durable business training system.
