# Pitch vs Presentations.AI

[Pitch](../tools/pitch.md) and [Presentations.AI](../tools/presentations-ai.md) are AI presentation tools that generate slides from prompts or documents as of April 2026. Pitch uses a custom fine-tuned model based on Gemini 3.1 Pro for slide layouts and content; Presentations.AI integrates Claude Sonnet 4.6 for text generation and design automation.[1][3]

## Quick Answer

Presentations.AI edges out for teams needing fast, editable slides from complex outlines due to its 1M token context window. Pitch suits collaborative design workflows with real-time editing in Google Workspace.

## At a Glance

| | Pitch | Presentations.AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Custom Gemini 3.1 Pro | Claude Sonnet 4.6 |
| **Price** | Free / Pro $20/month | Free / Pro $25/month / Enterprise $50/user/month |
| **Context Window** | 2M tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Google Workspace teams, real-time collaboration | Long-form outlines, enterprise branding |

## Where Pitch Wins

- Integrates directly with Google Workspace for slide creation inside Docs and Sheets.[3][4]
- Real-time multiplayer editing supports team reviews without version conflicts.
- Free tier includes unlimited presentations with basic AI generation.
- Exports to PowerPoint and PDF maintain layout fidelity.
- Custom brand kits apply consistent themes across decks.

## Where Presentations.AI Wins

- Handles large documents up to 1M tokens for summarizing reports into slides.[1]
- Brand sync automatically applies logos and colors from uploaded assets.
- Remix feature regenerates specific slides while keeping others intact.
- Enterprise plan includes SOC 2 compliance and team analytics.
- One-click analytics track viewer engagement on shared links.

## Key Differences

Pitch focuses on collaborative workflows within Google tools, using Gemini 3.1 Pro's 2M token window to process datasets or videos into visuals.[1][3] Presentations.AI prioritizes automation from long text inputs via Claude Sonnet 4.6, with stronger brand controls and slide remixing for iterative design.[1] Pitch Pro costs $20/month for unlimited AI generations; Presentations.AI Pro is $25/month but adds enterprise options at $50/user/month.[2][3]

## Who should choose Pitch

Choose Pitch for teams in Google Workspace who edit decks together and need video or data imports. Its free tier and $20/month Pro plan fit small teams building pitch materials.

## Who should choose Presentations.AI

Choose Presentations.AI for sales or marketing groups converting reports to branded slides. The $25/month Pro tier supports larger inputs; enterprise at $50/user/month adds compliance.

## Bottom Line

Both tools produce professional slides from prompts, but Presentations.AI fits document-heavy workflows better with its context handling. Pitch works for collaborative Google users at a lower Pro price. Test free tiers to match your input types and team size.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Pitch Pro at $20/month undercuts Presentations.AI Pro at $25/month; both have usable free tiers.[2][3]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Presentations.AI yields higher fidelity from long docs via Claude Sonnet 4.6; Pitch excels in multimodal inputs with Gemini 3.1 Pro.[1][3]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, export PPTX from one to import into the other for hybrid workflows.
