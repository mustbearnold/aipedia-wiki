# HyperWrite vs Sudowrite

[HyperWrite](/tools/hyperwrite/) and [Sudowrite](/tools/sudowrite/) should be bought for different writing lives. HyperWrite is a general productivity writing assistant for Chrome: drafting, TypeAhead autocomplete, citations with real-time info, personas, and a Personal Assistant browser agent. Sudowrite is a fiction-first workspace for authors: Muse, Story Engine, Write, Describe, Expand, Rewrite, Brainstorm, Feedback, Canvas, and long-form manuscript work.

## Quick Answer

Choose HyperWrite when the buyer writes emails, posts, notes, web copy, research drafts, reports, or everyday professional text inside Chrome.

Choose Sudowrite when the buyer is writing fiction and needs story-specific help with scenes, characters, sensory detail, prose expansion, story continuity, and manuscript development.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best for daily productivity writing:** HyperWrite.
- **Best Chrome extension writing layer:** HyperWrite.
- **Best browser-agent add-on:** HyperWrite Personal Assistant.
- **Best for fiction drafting:** Sudowrite.
- **Best for novel workflow:** Sudowrite Story Engine and Canvas.
- **Best fiction model/workflow:** Sudowrite Muse plus prose modes.
- **Best first paid tier for general writing:** HyperWrite Premium at $19.99/month.
- **Best first paid tier for working novelists:** Sudowrite Professional at $22/month on annual billing.

## Where HyperWrite Wins

- It follows the buyer into Gmail, Google Docs, LinkedIn, and browser text fields.
- TypeAhead helps while composing, which Sudowrite is not built to do.
- Citations with real-time info are more useful for lightweight factual drafting than Sudowrite's fiction workspace.
- Custom personas are better for repeated professional voice and daily snippets.
- Personal Assistant can help with supervised online tasks beyond writing.

## Where Sudowrite Wins

- It is purpose-built for fiction instead of generic productivity writing.
- Muse and prose modes are tuned around creative storytelling, not email or reports.
- Story Engine and Canvas handle long-form project structure better than a browser extension.
- Describe, Expand, Rewrite, Brainstorm, and Feedback map to actual author workflows.
- Credit tiers make more sense for serious manuscript work than HyperWrite's AI-message cap.

## Buying Recommendation

Use HyperWrite if the buyer needs a personal writing assistant for professional, academic-adjacent, or web-based writing. Premium is the practical daily tier; Ultra is for heavy AI-message users and people who want experimental writing or agent features first.

Use Sudowrite if the buyer is writing fiction. Hobby & Student is a low-cost trial path for casual writers. Professional is the realistic tier for serious novel or screenplay work. Max is for heavy drafters who publish or revise at volume.

Do not buy Sudowrite for business copy just because it writes fluent prose. Do not buy HyperWrite for a novel just because it can generate text. The workflow matters more than the model name.

## Watch-Outs

- HyperWrite browser-agent tasks need human supervision around forms, accounts, purchases, and private data.
- HyperWrite Premium's 250 AI-message cap can be tight for heavy daily drafting.
- Sudowrite credits are consumed by AI generation, not by user-typed words.
- Sudowrite outputs still need author revision, continuity checks, originality review, and rights review.
- Publishers, contests, schools, and clients may have AI-assistance disclosure rules.

## FAQ

**Is Sudowrite better than HyperWrite for fiction?**
Yes. Sudowrite is built for scenes, characters, sensory detail, Story Engine, Canvas, and fiction-specific drafting. HyperWrite is better for everyday web and productivity writing.

**Which is cheaper?**
HyperWrite Premium is cheaper than Sudowrite Professional on monthly billing, but Sudowrite Hobby & Student is cheaper on annual billing. Compare based on workflow and volume, not just entry price.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Use Sudowrite for manuscript work and HyperWrite for emails, author newsletters, pitches, bios, and Chrome-based daily writing.
