# DeepSeek vs Poe

[DeepSeek](/tools/deepseek/) and [Poe](/tools/poe/) approach AI access from opposite ends in June 2026. DeepSeek is one lab's models offered as cheaply as possible: a free consumer chat plus an API priced far below US frontier models. Poe is Quora's aggregator: one paid subscription that routes to many models (GPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, DeepSeek, and thousands of bots) through a shared points system.

Notably, Poe includes DeepSeek as one of its bots, so the real question is whether you want DeepSeek as cheaply as possible on its own, or DeepSeek alongside many other models in one paid app.

## Quick Answer

Choose **DeepSeek** if you mostly want a strong, cheap model and you do not need many providers: the chat is free and the API is exceptionally low cost. Choose **Poe** if you want breadth, comparing DeepSeek against GPT, Claude, and Gemini in one place, under a single subscription.

The split is **cheapest single source versus paid breadth**. DeepSeek wins on raw price and openness. Poe wins on one-account access to many models and bots.

## Where DeepSeek Wins

- **Cost.** The chat is free, and the API (V4-Flash from about $0.14 per million input tokens) undercuts US frontier models by a wide margin, with deep cache-hit discounts.
- **Reasoning value.** Strong reasoning and coding for the price, with a 1M-token context window on V4 models.
- **Openness.** Open-weight models can be self-hosted and fine-tuned.
- **No subscription.** Individuals get full chat access for free; developers pay only per token.
- **Direct fidelity.** Using DeepSeek directly avoids any aggregator point costs or feature gaps.

## Where Poe Wins

- **Model breadth.** Reach DeepSeek plus GPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, open-weight models, and media bots without separate accounts.
- **Comparison.** Run the same prompt across models to see which wins on your task.
- **One subscription.** Cheaper than stacking several provider plans if you use many models.
- **Custom and creator bots.** Build, share, and monetize bots through Poe's creator program.
- **Unified UI.** All models and bots live in one clean chat app across platforms.

## Plan Guidance

DeepSeek is simple: the **chat is free** for individuals, and production users go to the **API**, where V4-Flash is the cheap default and V4-Pro is the higher-capability option, both with cache-friendly discounts and a free token grant for new developers. There is no consumer subscription.

Poe is **points-based**: a free tier for casual use, a low-cost entry tier, a first serious monthly bucket roughly in the $20/month range, and premium tiers for very heavy use. Each bot charges a per-message point cost, so reaching DeepSeek through Poe spends points where direct DeepSeek use is free or near-free.

Do not buy either on the headline price alone. For DeepSeek specifically, going direct is essentially always cheaper than reaching it through Poe. Poe earns its subscription only when you genuinely use several models or bots, not just DeepSeek.

## Watch-Outs

DeepSeek is one lab's models, text-first, with no native image, video, or voice, and as a China-based lab it raises data-governance questions for some buyers (self-hosting the open weights is the mitigation). Poe adds breadth but at a cost: reaching any model through Poe spends points, expensive bots burn points quickly, and provider-native features are not always replicated. If DeepSeek is the only model you want, Poe adds cost without adding value for you.

## Who Should Choose DeepSeek

Choose DeepSeek if you want a strong model as cheaply as possible, a free consumer chat, cheap high-volume API access, or open weights you can self-host, and you do not need many providers.

## Who Should Choose Poe

Choose Poe if you want DeepSeek alongside many other models and bots in one paid app, you compare models regularly, or you build and share custom bots.

## Bottom Line

Pick DeepSeek to use a capable model as cheaply as possible, including free chat and a rock-bottom API. Pick Poe when you want DeepSeek plus many other models and bots under one subscription. If DeepSeek alone covers your needs, use it directly; if you want breadth, Poe is the single-bill path.

## FAQ

**Is DeepSeek available on Poe?**

Yes. Poe includes DeepSeek as a bot, subject to per-message point costs. Using DeepSeek directly is cheaper, since its chat is free and its API is very low cost.

**Which is cheaper?**

DeepSeek, clearly, if DeepSeek is the model you want. Its chat is free and its API undercuts everything here. Poe only makes sense financially when you use several models, not just DeepSeek.

**Can I self-host DeepSeek?**

Yes. DeepSeek's open-weight models can run in your own environment. Poe is a hosted aggregator.

**Can I use both together?**

Yes. Some people use DeepSeek directly for cheap bulk work and a low Poe tier to compare it against other models occasionally.
