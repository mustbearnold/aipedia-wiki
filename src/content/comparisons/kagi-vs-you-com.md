# Kagi vs You.com

[Kagi](/tools/kagi/) and [You.com](/tools/you-com/) are no longer close substitutes. Kagi is a paid, ad-free search engine for humans. You.com has completed its pivot into a developer-API platform for Search, Contents, Research, and Finance Research calls.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Kagi** if you want a private daily search engine with ranked results, Lenses, ranking controls, and optional Assistant modes. Choose **You.com** if you are building an AI product or agent that needs fresh web grounding, page extraction, or multi-step research through an API.

## Buyer Snapshot

**Kagi is a subscription search product.** Trial users get 100 searches. Starter is $5/month for 300 searches, Professional is $10/month for unlimited search, and Ultimate is $25/month for premium AI features including Research mode and flagship model access.

**You.com is an API product.** The current pricing page shows Search API at $5/1K calls, Contents API at $1/1K pages, Research API from $12/1K calls, Finance Research from $110/1K calls, $100 free credit, and custom enterprise terms.

**The practical split:** Kagi belongs in a browser search bar. You.com belongs behind an app, agent, workflow, or RAG pipeline.

## Where Kagi Wins

- Human-facing search UX with ranked results, Lenses, blocks, boosts, and pins.
- Privacy-first subscription economics: no ads, no behavioral ad profile.
- Clear consumer pricing at $5, $10, and $25 per month.
- Better fit for individuals who want to search, inspect, and decide themselves.
- No engineering work required.

## Where You.com Wins

- Programmatic web grounding through Search API.
- Bulk URL-to-Markdown or raw HTML extraction through Contents API.
- Multi-step cited research through Research API and Finance Research API.
- $100 free credit for prototypes before usage-based billing starts.
- Enterprise controls such as SOC 2, zero-retention options, DPA-ready infrastructure, custom QPS, and annual or volume discounts.

## Who Should Pick Kagi

Pick Kagi if the buyer is a person frustrated with ad-supported search. It is a strong fit for privacy-sensitive search, domain control, cleaner research sessions, and paid search without building any software.

## Who Should Pick You.com

Pick You.com if the buyer is an engineering or product team. It is the better fit when search results, content extraction, or research reports need to flow into an agent, LLM app, internal workflow, or customer-facing product.

## Watch-Outs

Do not buy Kagi expecting a developer retrieval API. It is primarily a human search product.

Do not buy You.com expecting the old consumer YouPro chat plan. The current public pricing is API-first, and consumer subscriptions are no longer the visible offer.

## Bottom Line

Kagi wins for **private paid search**. You.com wins for **grounding and research APIs**. They can coexist, but they should not be evaluated as if both are consumer AI chat/search subscriptions.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**
For a person, Kagi is the cheaper and clearer product. For software teams, You.com is usage-based, starting with $100 free credit and API prices from $1 to $110+ per 1K calls or pages depending on product.

**Can You.com replace Kagi?**
Not for normal users. You.com can power an app that searches or researches the web, but it is not currently positioned as a simple paid personal search engine.

**Can Kagi replace You.com APIs?**
No. Kagi is not the right choice if the requirement is production web-grounding infrastructure for agents or LLM apps.
