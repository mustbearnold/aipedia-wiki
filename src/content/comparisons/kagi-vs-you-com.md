# Kagi vs You.com

[Kagi](../tools/kagi.md) and [You.com](../tools/you-com.md) are AI search engines that integrate large language models for query processing and response generation as of April 2026. Kagi uses a custom search index with its own LLM, while You.com combines multiple LLMs with web results.

## Quick Answer

Kagi suits users seeking ad-free, privacy-focused search with fast personal results; You.com fits those needing free access to multiple AI models and app integrations.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Kagi | You.com |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Kagi AI (custom model, 128K context) | YouPro with OpenAI models / Gemini 3.1 Pro / Claude Sonnet 4.6 |
| **Price** | $10/mo (Pro), $25/mo (Ultimate), $50/mo (Lifetime limited) | Free / $15/mo (YouPro), $200/mo (YouPro Enterprise) |
| **Context Window** | 128K tokens | Up to 2M tokens (Gemini 3.1 Pro) |
| **Best For** | Privacy, speed, personalization | Multi-model access, integrations, free tier |

## Where Kagi Wins

- Ad-free experience with no tracking or data sales.[Kagi site]
- Personalized results via "Lenses" that users customize over time.[Kagi blog]
- Fast response times due to proprietary index and edge caching.[Kagi docs]
- Strong privacy controls, including full data deletion options.[Kagi privacy policy]
- Bang commands for direct site search (e.g., !w for Wikipedia).[Kagi features]

## Where You.com Wins

- Free tier with access to leading models from OpenAI and Gemini 3.1 Pro.[2]
- YouPro mode switches between multiple LLMs for diverse outputs.[You.com pricing]
- Built-in apps for tasks like writing, coding, and image generation.[You.com apps]
- 2M token context via Gemini 3.1 Pro for long documents.[2]
- Enterprise plan at $200/mo with team features and API access.[You.com enterprise]

## Key Differences

Kagi focuses on a unified, user-controlled search experience without ads or external model dependencies, pricing at $10/mo for Pro access to its 128K context AI. You.com offers broader model selection including OpenAI frontier models, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and Gemini 3.1 Pro (2M context), with a robust free tier but ads in basic use; YouPro costs $15/mo.[1][2] Kagi excels in speed and privacy via its index, while You.com provides app ecosystem and multimodal inputs.[Kagi site][You.com site]

## Who should choose Kagi

Power users and privacy advocates who pay for clean, fast, personalized search without model switching.

## Who should choose You.com

Casual users or teams wanting free multi-model AI search with integrations into productivity apps.

## Bottom Line

Choose Kagi for premium, ad-free search tailored to individual needs at $10/mo. Opt for You.com if free access to OpenAI and Gemini model options, plus apps, meets your requirements; upgrade to YouPro for $15/mo if needed.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**
You.com free tier costs $0; Kagi starts at $10/mo. YouPro ($15/mo) undercuts Kagi Ultimate ($25/mo).[Kagi pricing][You.com pricing]

**Which has better output quality?**
You.com edges out with access to benchmark leaders like Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4.6; Kagi's custom model matches for search tasks.[1][2]

**Can I use both?**
Yes, Kagi for daily privacy-focused search, You.com for model experimentation and apps.
