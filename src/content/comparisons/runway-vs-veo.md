# Runway vs Google Veo 3.1

[Runway](/tools/runway/) and [Google Veo 3.1](/tools/veo/) are often compared as AI video generators, but the better framing is product vs model route. Runway is a production AI video workspace and API platform. Veo 3.1 is Google's frontier video model family across Flow, Gemini API, Vertex AI, Google Vids, and Gemini app surfaces.

AiPedia's answer On June 6, 2026: **Runway wins for most production workflows; Veo 3.1 wins when the buyer specifically needs Google's model, Gemini API, Vertex AI, native vertical output, or Google provenance.**

## Quick Answer

Runway leads for most creators, agencies, and teams trying to ship finished video. Its pricing page lists Gen-4.5, Gen-4, Aleph video editing, Act-Two performance capture, Veo 3.1, Veo 3, video apps, third-party video models including Seedance 2.0 and Kling 3.0 Pro, editor projects, storage, watermark removal, and team/enterprise paths. That makes Runway the better buying route when the job is broader than one model call.

Veo wins when the buyer is explicitly Google-native. The Gemini API docs and pricing pages give model IDs, paid-tier per-second pricing, text/image input, video-with-audio output, 9:16 aspect ratio configuration, 4K configuration, first/last frame control, reference images, and scene extension. If the output needs to live inside Google Cloud, Gemini API, Vertex AI, Flow, Google Vids, or a Google-approved procurement path, Veo is the cleaner answer.

## Where Runway Wins

Runway wins when video is a workflow. The buyer does not just need one generated clip; they need revisions, storage, export, editing, team review, performance capture, model switching, and sometimes API automation. Runway's current pricing page shows why it is strategically different from a single-model route: Standard includes Gen-4.5 text-to-video, Gen-4 image-to-video, Aleph, Act-Two, Veo 3.1, Veo 3, video apps, and third-party video models.

That is the conversion case for Runway. A creator can test Runway's own models, use Veo inside Runway if available, switch to other third-party models, edit clips, and keep assets in one product. Agencies and teams also get a cleaner path toward storage, collaboration, enterprise controls, and support.

Runway also has a public developer API. Its API docs list video models including `gen4.5`, `gen4_turbo`, `gen4_aleph`, `act_two`, `veo3`, `veo3.1`, and `veo3.1_fast`, with credits purchased at $0.01/credit and per-model costs listed in the API pricing docs. For teams building media products, that matters.

## Where Veo 3.1 Wins

Veo wins when the buyer wants Google's model directly. The Gemini API route is especially useful for teams already on Google Cloud or Vertex AI. The docs expose model names and supported behavior in a way that makes internal planning easier than relying on a UI-only creator app.

Veo also has a specific mobile-first advantage. Google's January 2026 update says Veo 3.1 Ingredients to Video supports native 9:16 vertical output and 1080p/4K options across Flow, Gemini API, Vertex AI, and Google Vids. If the brief is Shorts, Reels, TikTok, or mobile-first paid social, Veo deserves a direct test.

The key distinction: Veo may be available inside Runway, but direct Veo through Gemini API/Vertex AI is still the cleaner route when procurement, data handling, Google governance, or exact model pricing are the main decision factors.

## Pricing And Plan Guidance

Runway's first serious paid tier is Standard at $12/user/month billed annually with 625 monthly credits. Pro is $28/user/month billed annually with 2,250 monthly credits. Max is $76/user/month billed annually with 9,500 monthly credits, one-month credit rollover, and relaxed Explore Mode generation. Enterprise is custom. Those prices buy a workspace, not only model seconds.

Veo's clearest pricing is Gemini API per-second pricing. As of June 6, 2026, the pricing page lists Veo 3.1 Standard at $0.40/sec for 720p and 1080p, $0.60/sec for 4K; Fast at $0.10/sec for 720p, $0.12/sec for 1080p, $0.30/sec for 4K; and Lite at $0.05/sec for 720p and $0.08/sec for 1080p. That is easier to model for developers, but long iteration loops can get expensive fast.

## Who Should Choose Runway

Choose Runway if you need to make and ship finished AI video: ads, campaigns, client clips, creator packages, product videos, or experimental shorts. It is the better default when multiple people, models, assets, edits, exports, and approvals are part of the job.

Do not choose Runway only because one model inside it is attractive. If your only requirement is direct Google model access, compare the Gemini API or Vertex AI route first.

## Who Should Choose Veo

Choose Veo if your team is Google-native, API-first, governance-heavy, or specifically evaluating Google's video model. It is the better route for Gemini API, Vertex AI, Google procurement, vertical output, and transparent per-second pricing.

Do not choose Veo as a full production suite. You may still need Runway, Premiere, Resolve, or another editing/workflow layer around it.

## Bottom Line

**Runway is the better production workspace. Veo 3.1 is the better direct Google model route.** If you need to ship finished video, start with Runway. If you need Google's model, API, pricing, or governance, start with Veo.

For AiPedia revenue and trust, this page should prevent a common buyer mistake: treating Runway and Veo as interchangeable "generators." One is a workflow platform; the other is a model/API route.

## FAQ

**Is Runway better than Veo 3.1?**

For production workflow, yes. For direct Google API or Vertex AI deployment, Veo is the better route.

**Does Runway include Veo 3.1?**

Runway's pricing and API docs list Veo 3.1/Veo 3 among available model routes. Availability can still depend on plan, workspace, API version, and account status.

**Which is cheaper?**

It depends on workload. Runway subscriptions buy workspace credits and features. Veo API pricing is per second and easier to model, but high iteration volume can become expensive.

**Which should an agency buy first?**

Runway first if the agency needs editing, exports, teams, and model switching. Veo first if the agency is building around Google Cloud or needs direct Gemini API/Vertex AI access.

**Can I use both?**

Yes. A team can use Veo through Google for governed model calls and Runway for creative workflow, edits, and delivery.
