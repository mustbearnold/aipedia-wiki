# Gamma vs Tome

[Gamma](/tools/gamma/) is the clear winner over [Tome](/tools/tome/) in 2026 because Tome Slides is no longer an active presentation product. The official Tome Help Center says Tome Slides was sunsetting on April 30, 2025, that users needed to migrate important work before that date, and that after April 30 users would not be able to sign in or access Tome content. VentureBeat later covered the founding team's pivot to Lightfield, an AI-native CRM, after Tome's presentation product failed to become a durable business.

The practical buyer answer: **use Gamma if you wanted Tome's AI-first, narrative-style presentation experience. Do not shortlist Tome for new presentation work.** This page was re-verified on **June 12, 2026** against current Tome, Gamma, VentureBeat, and Lightfield sources.

## Quick Verdict

**Winner: Gamma.** Gamma is the closest active replacement for the original Tome brief: prompt-to-deck creation, card-like narrative flow, shareable web pages, documents, and presentations that feel less locked to a traditional slide canvas. Gamma's current AI presentation page focuses on generating polished presentations from prompts and turning ideas into decks quickly. Its pricing page still supports active Free, Plus, and Pro purchase paths, so users can evaluate the product today rather than chasing a discontinued tool.

**Tome is a historical reference, not a current option.** The old version of this page treated Tome as if it still had a Pro plan, current model stack, and active presentation roadmap. That is no longer acceptable. Tome Slides was sunset in 2025, and the founding team now operates Lightfield as a CRM product. The Tome brand/domain situation can confuse buyers, but it should not make AiPedia recommend Tome as a live AI presentation tool.

## Why Tome Is Not A Current Choice

The official Tome Help Center's sunset article is the most important source for buyers. It states that Tome Slides was sunsetting on April 30, 2025 and tells users to migrate important work off Tome before that date. It also says new upgrades and new user sign-ups were paused before the sunset, active subscriptions would be cancelled, and user data would be deleted after April 30, 2025.

That means the comparison is no longer "which tool has better output quality?" The comparison is "which active product should a former Tome user choose now?" For that question, Gamma has the strongest fit because it keeps the most important part of Tome's original appeal: AI-first narrative creation that can become a presentation, document, or shareable page.

Do not use old Tome reviews for a 2026 buying decision. Many 2023 and 2024 reviews praised Tome's storytelling format, generated pages, web-native sharing, and modern design feel. Those strengths mattered when the product existed. They do not make Tome safe to buy or adopt after the shutdown.

## Where Gamma Wins

Gamma wins for former Tome users who liked starting with a rough idea and turning it into a story. Gamma is not just a PowerPoint skin. It is useful when the output should feel like a flow: a pitch narrative, client explainer, product update, education module, internal memo, or lightweight web page.

Gamma also wins because it is active and purchasable. Its current pricing page lists a free path and paid Plus/Pro plans. Its help documentation explains paid workspace upgrades, credit limits, cancellation behavior, and plan management. That gives buyers a current support and billing path, which matters after the Tome shutdown showed how risky it is to rely on a beloved product with weak business durability.

Gamma is especially strong if you want:

- A narrative deck or shareable page instead of a strict slide file.
- Fast prompt-to-presentation generation.
- A tool that can produce decks, docs, and web pages from one workflow.
- A better replacement for Tome's story-first feel than traditional slide software.
- An active AI presentation product with current pricing and support.

## Where Tome Used To Win

Tome used to win on novelty. It made AI storytelling feel more fluid than old slide tools, and its canvas/page format helped founders, creators, consultants, and operators explain ideas without immediately worrying about PowerPoint formatting. It was also memorable enough that people still search for "Gamma vs Tome" after the product sunset.

That past value is why this page still exists. It helps former Tome users understand what to replace it with. But past value is not a current recommendation. Tome's own sunset article and the Lightfield pivot make the procurement answer simple: do not build a new presentation workflow on Tome.

## Pricing Reality

Gamma has current pricing. On June 12, 2026, AiPedia verified Gamma against its official AI presentation and pricing pages. The live page lists Free, Plus, Pro, and Ultra paths; Free supports PDF/PPTX/PNG/Google Slides export, Plus removes Gamma branding and expands AI power, Pro adds premium models, custom branding/fonts, detailed analytics, advanced sharing, custom domains, API access, and workspace templates, and Ultra adds more usage and access to the most advanced models. Readers should still check Gamma's live pricing page before purchase because credits, tiers, and limits can change.

Tome Slides has no current presentation pricing because the product was sunset. The old Free/Pro/Enterprise comparison language should be treated as historical only. If you see a third-party page quoting Tome prices, it is likely describing the discontinued product or a different Tome-branded surface.

## Buyer Recommendations

Choose Gamma if:

- You wanted Tome for AI-first storytelling.
- You need a fast deck, doc, or web page from a prompt.
- You prefer a narrative/card flow over a strict slide editor.
- You want an active presentation product with current support and pricing.
- You need a safer default than chasing old Tome links.

Do not choose Tome if:

- You are starting any new presentation workflow.
- You need recoverable files, current billing, or a roadmap.
- You are evaluating tools for a team, client, school, agency, or business.
- You found an old review claiming Tome is still a top 2026 AI deck product.

Gamma is the active choice for people who miss Tome's narrative AI presentation style. Tome is a discontinued presentation product and should be treated as historical context only.

If your old Tome workflow was "turn a rough idea into a flowing story or pitch," start with Gamma. If you need stricter corporate slide design, compare [Beautiful.ai](/tools/beautiful-ai/). If you need sales-team collaboration, compare [Pitch](/tools/pitch/).

## FAQ

**Is Tome still available for AI presentations?**

No. Tome Slides was officially sunset on April 30, 2025 according to Tome's Help Center. Users were told to migrate work before that date.

**Is Gamma better than Tome?**

For current buyers, yes. Gamma is active and still supports AI presentation workflows. Tome Slides is discontinued.

**What is the closest active Tome replacement?**

Gamma is the closest replacement for narrative, AI-first presentation and page generation. Beautiful.ai is better for polished Smart Slides. Pitch is better for team deck operations.

**What happened to Tome?**

The presentation product was sunset, and VentureBeat later covered the founding team's pivot to Lightfield, an AI-native CRM.

**Should I migrate old Tome content to Gamma?**

If you exported your old Tome content, Gamma is a reasonable place to rebuild narrative decks or web-style presentations. If you did not export before the sunset, the official Tome Help Center said user data would be deleted after April 30, 2025.
