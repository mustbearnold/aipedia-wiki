# Gamma vs Tome

[Gamma](../tools/gamma.md) and [Tome](../tools/tome.md) are AI presentation tools that generate slides, documents, and webpages from text prompts or notes as of April 2026. Gamma focuses on converting notes into presentations and webpages, while Tome emphasizes narrative-driven decks with embedded media.[2]

## Quick Answer

Gamma suits users needing quick webpage and document exports from notes; Tome fits teams building interactive, story-based presentations. Choice depends on export needs versus narrative focus.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Gamma | Tome |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Gamma 2.0 (Gemini 3.1 Pro integration) | Tome 3.0 (Claude Sonnet 4.6 backbone) |
| **Price** | Free / Plus $10/mo / Pro $20/mo | Free / Pro $16/mo / Enterprise $50/user/mo |
| **Context Window** | 1M tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Note-to-presentation conversion, webpage export | Narrative decks, team collaboration |

## Where Gamma Wins

- Converts short notes into full presentations or webpages faster than Tome.[2]
- Free tier supports unlimited presentations with basic exports.[2]
- Integrates Gemini 3.1 Pro for dataset analysis in slides.[1][2]
- Exports to webpages for sharing without viewer signup.
- Lower Pro pricing at $20/mo for advanced features.

## Where Tome Wins

- Builds interactive narratives with embedded video and data visuals.[2]
- Stronger team collaboration via real-time editing and comments.
- Claude Sonnet 4.6 handles long-form story structuring better.[1]
- Enterprise plan includes custom branding and analytics at $50/user/mo.
- Higher polish on mobile-responsive decks.

## Key Differences

Gamma prioritizes speed and multi-format output (presentations, documents, webpages) using Gemini 3.1 Pro, which excels in large-context tasks at lower cost.[1][2] Tome leverages Claude Sonnet 4.6 for structured narratives and multimodal embeds, with tighter collaboration but higher Pro pricing ($16/mo vs Gamma's $10/mo).[1][2] Gamma's free tier offers more exports; Tome restricts advanced sharing to paid plans.

## Who should choose Gamma

Users converting notes or research into shareable webpages or docs. Best for solo creators needing quick multi-format output.

## Who should choose Tome

Teams crafting story-driven pitches with media embeds. Suited for collaborative workflows in sales or marketing.

## Bottom Line

Both tools use 2026 frontier models (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6) for 1M-token contexts, but Gamma edges on versatility and free-tier value while Tome leads in narrative quality.[1][2] Test free tiers to match your workflow; neither dominates all use cases.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Gamma's Plus at $10/mo undercuts Tome's Pro at $16/mo; both have usable free tiers.[2]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Tome produces more polished narratives via Claude Sonnet 4.6; Gamma matches for data-heavy slides with Gemini 3.1 Pro.[1][2]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, export from one and import to the other for hybrid workflows.
