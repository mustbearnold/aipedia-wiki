# Activepieces vs Zapier

[Activepieces](/tools/activepieces/) and [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) both automate app-to-app workflows, but they are built for different owners. The buying question is not "which one has automation?" It is whether the workflow owner values open-source control, self-hosting, and active-flow pricing more than Zapier's larger SaaS catalog, lower learning curve, and managed AI orchestration layer.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Activepieces** if the team wants MIT-licensed self-hosting, cheaper high-frequency workflows, per-active-flow cloud pricing, and MCP-first app actions that can be exposed to Claude, Cursor, or Windsurf.

Choose **Zapier** if the team needs the broadest app catalog, business-user onboarding, managed reliability, and a no-code AI automation stack across Zaps, Tables, Forms, Agents, Chatbots, Canvas, MCP, and Copilot.

For technical teams that can own reliability, **Activepieces is the better control-and-cost pick**. For non-technical teams that need automations live across many SaaS tools today, **Zapier is the safer default**.

## Decision Snapshot

- **Best for open-source control:** Activepieces. Community Edition is MIT licensed and self-hostable.
- **Best for connector breadth:** Zapier. The live apps directory says 9,000+ apps.
- **Best for high-frequency flows:** Activepieces, when a small number of flows run often. Standard bills active flows, not every task.
- **Best for non-technical operators:** Zapier. The product is designed around prompt-assisted Zap building and managed cloud automation.
- **Best MCP story:** depends. Activepieces exposes its open-source app catalog and flow tools through MCP. Zapier exposes a far larger app/action library through Zapier MCP.
- **Main risk:** Activepieces requires more ownership and may miss a long-tail SaaS connector. Zapier is cloud-only and task billing can spike on multi-step or agent-heavy workflows.

## Where Activepieces Wins

Activepieces wins when automation is part of an engineering-owned or privacy-sensitive operating layer:

- **Self-hosting and license clarity:** Community Edition is MIT licensed, self-hosted, and separate from the commercial cloud and enterprise features.
- **Active-flow pricing:** Standard starts free, includes 10 active flows, and then charges $5 per active flow per month. The current pricing page says runs are unlimited.
- **MCP-first workflow control:** The public MCP page showed 754 MCPs on June 21, 2026, and the MCP tools docs expose flow creation, duplication, renaming, status changes, deletion, publishing, and full flow building.
- **Cost predictability for repeated workflows:** A flow that runs thousands of times can be cheaper than a task-metered Zap if the connector coverage is sufficient.
- **Recent open-source velocity:** GitHub releases show 0.85.4 on June 17, 2026 after June updates for data manipulation triggers and self-host reliability fixes.

Pick Activepieces when the buyer says: "We can operate a workflow tool, our app stack is covered, and we want AI agents to act through approved tools without paying per step forever."

## Where Zapier Wins

Zapier wins when the buyer needs a managed platform with the biggest app surface:

- **App coverage:** The apps directory says Zapier supports 9,000+ app connections, which is still the central reason to choose it.
- **No-code onboarding:** Free and paid plans include Zap workflows, Tables, and Forms, while Professional unlocks multi-step Zaps, premium apps, webhooks, AI fields, and conditional form logic.
- **Team controls:** Team starts at the public $69/month annual-billing price and includes 25 users, shared workflows and folders, shared app connections, SAML SSO, and priority support.
- **AI orchestration packaging:** Zapier's AI page frames MCP and SDK access as one governed connection surface for assistants, coding agents, and custom agents.
- **MCP action breadth:** Zapier's MCP guide says its app library exposes 30,000+ actions for AI tools.

Pick Zapier when the buyer says: "The operator is in marketing, sales, support, or ops, the workflow needs many SaaS apps, and we need a managed product more than self-host control."

## Plan Guidance

Start with **Activepieces Community** if the team wants to test self-hosting or keep workflow data on controlled infrastructure. Use **Activepieces Standard** when the team wants hosted Activepieces and can model cost by active flows. Move to **Ultimate** only when SSO, audit logs, custom RBAC, piece access controls, global connections, Git sync, and support are procurement requirements.

Start with **Zapier Free** only for testing. Use **Zapier Professional** when the workflow needs multi-step Zaps, premium apps, webhooks, AI fields, or shorter polling. Use **Zapier Team** when shared folders, shared app connections, SAML SSO, and admin collaboration matter. Use **Enterprise** when annual task limits, advanced admin controls, observability, deployment options, and technical account support are part of the deal.

## Who Should Avoid Each

Avoid **Activepieces** if the team cannot operate self-hosted software, needs the broadest long-tail SaaS catalog, or wants every governance feature without a custom annual contract.

Avoid **Zapier** if data-residency rules require self-hosting, workflow volume makes per-task billing hard to forecast, or the automation owner needs code-level control over the runtime.

Avoid **both** if the work is not app automation. Use [Voiceflow](/tools/voiceflow/) for customer-facing conversational agents, [LangGraph](/tools/langgraph/) for low-level durable agent orchestration, or [Pipedream](/tools/pipedream/) for developer-first API workflows.

## Cost Model

**Free path**

- **Activepieces:** self-host Community for free, or cloud Standard with 10 active flows included.
- **Zapier:** Free plan with 100 tasks per month.

**First paid public tier**

- **Activepieces:** $5 per additional active flow per month after the free flow allowance.
- **Zapier:** Professional from $19.99/month billed annually.

**Billing unit**

- **Activepieces:** active cloud flows, while self-host shifts cost to infrastructure and operator time.
- **Zapier:** tasks, with every action and many AI/tool calls consuming allowance.

**High-frequency flow**

- **Activepieces:** often cheaper if connector coverage is enough.
- **Zapier:** can become expensive when one trigger fans out to many actions.

**Governance tier**

- **Activepieces:** Ultimate custom annual contract.
- **Zapier:** Team from $69/month annually, with Enterprise custom.

## Bottom Line

Activepieces is the better pick for teams that want open-source control, self-hosting, and MCP-first workflow ownership. Zapier is the better pick for teams that want the fastest managed path across a huge SaaS catalog.

If the workflow is small, frequent, and privacy-sensitive, test Activepieces first. If the workflow touches many business apps and will be owned by non-technical operators, start with Zapier and model the task budget before it becomes production-critical.

## FAQ

**Is Activepieces a Zapier alternative?**

Yes. Activepieces is explicitly useful as a Zapier alternative when buyers want self-hosting, open-source control, and active-flow pricing. Zapier still wins when app breadth and operator simplicity matter more.

**Which is cheaper, Activepieces or Zapier?**

It depends on workflow shape. Activepieces can be cheaper for a few flows that run often because Standard bills active flows. Zapier can be cheaper for low-volume testing, but multi-step workflows and agents can consume tasks quickly.

**Which one is better for MCP?**

Use Activepieces when MCP should expose an open-source workflow and app-action layer that the team can inspect and self-host. Use Zapier when the priority is the biggest governed action library for AI assistants and agents.

**Can Zapier be self-hosted?**

No. Zapier is a proprietary cloud platform. Use Activepieces or [n8n](/tools/n8n/) when self-hosting is a hard requirement.
