# Descript vs Fish Audio / OpenAudio S2

[Descript](/tools/descript/) and [Fish Audio](/tools/fish-audio/) are both relevant to voice workflows, but the decision is simple: Descript edits recorded media, while Fish Audio generates speech. Descript is a creator editor with transcript-based cuts, Studio Sound, captions, AI Speech, Regenerate, and Underlord. Fish Audio is an open-weight TTS stack with OpenAudio S1/S2 Pro, cloud plans, API pricing, and self-hosting options.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Descript** when the work is turning recordings into finished podcasts, courses, videos, and clips. Choose **Fish Audio** when the work is generating synthetic speech, cloning voices with permission, self-hosting TTS, or embedding speech generation into a product.

If you are a creator, Descript is usually the first purchase. If you are a developer or voice team, Fish Audio is usually the first technical evaluation.

## Where Descript Wins

- **It solves the edit.** Descript lets non-editors remove mistakes, filler words, and awkward pauses by editing the transcript.
- **It has the creator workflow.** Captions, clips, Studio Sound, Regenerate, AI Speech, Brand Studio, translation, and export all sit inside the same production surface.
- **It is easier for teams publishing media.** Comments, shared projects, media-hour allowances, and export settings matter when the final deliverable is a video, podcast, or lesson.
- **It reduces tool sprawl.** Fish Audio creates speech well, but it does not replace a media editor.

## Where Fish Audio Wins

- **Open-weight TTS is the point.** Fish Audio's OpenAudio S1/S2 Pro path is better for teams that want model-level control, self-hosting, or lower marginal cost.
- **High-volume generation can be cheaper.** API pricing is published at $15 per 1M UTF-8 bytes for s1 and s2-pro, while cloud plans scale from Plus to Max.
- **Developer workflows are stronger.** Fish Audio fits products, apps, character systems, and voice pipelines where generated speech is embedded in another surface.
- **Self-hosting changes the economics.** Teams with GPU operations can avoid a purely hosted editor-style cost model.

## Pricing and Cost Control

**Descript:** Free is for evaluation. Hobbyist is $24 monthly or $16 annual, with 10 media hours and 400 AI credits. Creator is $35 monthly or $24 annual, with 30 media hours, 800 AI credits, 4K export, full Underlord, and bonus allowances. Business is $65 monthly or $50 annual, with 40 media hours, 1500 AI credits, Brand Studio, translation/dubbing, and priority support.

**Fish Audio:** Free gives a small test allowance. Plus is $11/mo with up to 200 minutes of S1/S2 generation. Pro is $75/mo with up to 1,620 minutes, 2M credits, and 3 seats. Max is $749/mo with up to 6,250 minutes, 25M credits, and 10 seats. API pricing is pay-as-you-go at $15 per 1M UTF-8 bytes for s1 and s2-pro; transcribe-1 ASR is $0.36 per audio hour.

For long-form editing, price Descript around editor seats, media hours, and AI credits. For synthetic speech at scale, price Fish Audio around generated text volume, model choice, voice QA, and whether self-hosting offsets cloud cost.

## Common Buying Mistakes

- Do not buy Fish Audio expecting a polished full media editor. It is a voice generation stack first.
- Do not buy Descript expecting open weights or self-hosted TTS. It is proprietary creator software.
- Do not compare Descript's monthly plan price to Fish Audio's API unit price without estimating actual workload.
- Do not publish cloned or synthetic voices without speaker permission, rights review, and disclosure where required.

## Bottom Line

Descript is for editing what happened on mic or camera. Fish Audio is for generating new speech and controlling the speech model path. Many serious teams can use both: Fish Audio for generated voice assets, Descript for final edits and publication.

## FAQ

**Which is better for creators?**
Descript is better for creators who need to edit recordings. Fish Audio is better for creators whose main output is generated narration or character voices.

**Which is better for developers?**
Fish Audio. API pricing, open weights, and self-hosting are more relevant to product builders than Descript's editor workflow.

**Can Fish Audio replace Descript?**
No. Fish Audio can generate speech for a project, but Descript handles transcript editing, cleanup, captions, clips, and final export.

**Can Descript replace Fish Audio?**
Not for high-volume TTS, self-hosting, or model experimentation. Descript's AI Speech is useful inside an edit, not a Fish Audio substitute.
