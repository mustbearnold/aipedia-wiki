# Capacities vs Otter.ai

[Capacities](/tools/capacities/) and [Otter.ai](/tools/otter-ai/) both sit in AI notes, but they are not close substitutes. Capacities is for building an object-based knowledge system. Otter.ai is for capturing and collaborating on live meeting transcripts.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Capacities** if your notes need to become a long-term system of projects, people, decisions, books, sources, meetings, and daily context.

Choose **Otter.ai** if your notes start as spoken meetings and the team needs live transcription, speaker identification, AI chat across meetings, OtterPilot, meeting workflows, imports, and shared transcript history.

Use both when meetings produce important knowledge: Otter captures the call; Capacities stores the durable insights.

## Where Capacities Wins

- Custom objects help turn scattered notes into people, projects, tasks, books, meetings, and source records.
- Daily notes and backlinks make it stronger for ongoing thinking, writing, research, and project memory.
- The AI assistant can search notes, read relevant content, save AI chats as objects, and run common writing/thinking commands.
- Pro users can connect their Capacities space to external AI tools through AI Chat Connectors.
- It is better when a meeting is only one input among many.

## Where Otter.ai Wins

- Otter can capture live meetings across Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Google Meet.
- The Basic plan includes live transcription, speaker identification, AI chat within and across meetings, and 300 monthly transcription minutes.
- Pro raises work capacity with 1,200 in-app recording minutes, 10 monthly audio/video imports, up to 90 minutes per meeting, advanced search/export/playback, and unlimited storage.
- Business adds unlimited meetings and in-app recordings, custom AI workflows, unlimited audio/video imports, up to four hours per meeting, stronger admin features, and three concurrent meetings.
- Enterprise adds security, SSO/SCIM, custom integrations, HIPAA add-on, API/webhooks, and sales-focused workflows.

## Plan Guidance

Start with **Otter Basic** only to test transcript quality, meeting UX, and whether a visible assistant is acceptable in your calls. Move to Pro if an individual or small team needs more recording minutes, imports, storage, exports, and templates. Move to Business if shared team administration, longer meetings, concurrent meetings, and unlimited meetings matter.

Start with **Capacities Basic** when the work is not primarily meetings. Upgrade to Pro when the AI assistant, task actions, calendar integrations, smart queries, and media limits are active parts of your system.

For regulated industries, HR, education, healthcare, legal, and customer calls, test Otter with a consent and retention policy before rolling it out. Meeting notes are sensitive data, even when the tool is working as intended.

## Workflow Fit

|---|---|---|

| Long-term personal knowledge | Capacities | Objects and backlinks preserve context. |
| Interviews and education | Otter.ai | Live transcription and speaker identification matter. |

| Customer-success meeting follow-up | Otter.ai | Shared transcripts and action items are easier to operationalize. |

## Who Should Choose Capacities

Choose Capacities if the output you care about is a durable knowledge system. It is better for researchers, writers, founders, consultants, and operators whose notes need to stay useful long after the meeting ends.

## Who Should Choose Otter.ai

Choose Otter.ai if the output you care about is the meeting record itself. It is better for teams that need transcripts, summaries, speaker attribution, action items, meeting workflows, and searchable call history.

## Bottom Line

Capacities is the better PKM system. Otter.ai is the better meeting transcription and collaboration tool. They pair well, but one does not replace the other.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**
Capacities is easier to start for personal notes because Basic is broad. Otter.ai pricing scales with meeting usage, seats, and the plan limits your team needs.

**Does Capacities record meetings?**
No. It can store and organize meeting notes, but it does not replace OtterPilot or a live transcription workflow.

**Does Otter.ai work as a second brain?**
Not really. It can search meetings, but it is not built around typed objects, properties, backlinks, or a general PKM graph.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Export or summarize the important meeting outputs from Otter, then store decisions, customer context, and project notes in Capacities.
