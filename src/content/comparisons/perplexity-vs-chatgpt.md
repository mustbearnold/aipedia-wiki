# Perplexity vs ChatGPT

[Perplexity](../tools/perplexity.md) and [ChatGPT](../tools/chatgpt.md) both answer questions using AI, but they are built for different jobs. Perplexity is an AI search engine — its purpose is to find current, accurate information and cite every source. ChatGPT is a general-purpose AI assistant — its purpose is to help with any task, from writing to coding to conversation. Using Perplexity when you need a source-backed answer and ChatGPT when you need a flexible thinking partner is not a compromise. It is the correct tool assignment.

## Quick Answer

Use **Perplexity** when you need to research a topic, verify a claim, or get a current, sourced answer to a factual question. Use **ChatGPT** when you need to write, code, brainstorm, analyze documents, generate images, or have a flexible multi-turn conversation. If you only subscribe to one, the answer depends entirely on your primary use case.

## Core Approach

**Perplexity** is a research-first product. Every answer comes with inline citations linking to the sources used. Pro Search runs multiple parallel searches, synthesizes results, and follows up on ambiguities before answering — more like a research assistant than a search engine. The focus model selection (GPT-4o, Claude, Sonar) and real-time web access mean answers reflect current information, not training data that may be months old.

**ChatGPT** is a general-purpose assistant. It answers questions using training data (with optional web browsing), generates text, writes code, creates images via DALL-E 3, runs Python via its code interpreter, and supports real-time voice conversations. The breadth of what ChatGPT does is unmatched — but it does not make source citations a core feature the way Perplexity does. When ChatGPT uses web browsing, it cites sources, but this is optional and less systematic.

## Who Should Use Perplexity

- Researchers, journalists, and analysts who cite sources
- Anyone who regularly fact-checks information or needs to verify claims
- People frustrated by AI answers that sound confident but can't be traced to sources
- Students and academics who need cited answers for papers or notes
- Business users who track market trends, competitor information, or industry news

## Who Should Use ChatGPT

- Writers, marketers, and communicators who need a flexible drafting assistant
- Developers who want AI-assisted coding, debugging, and code explanation
- Anyone who needs image generation, voice interaction, or document analysis
- Users who want to build custom AI workflows via the GPT Store
- General users who want one tool for everything rather than a specialized research tool

## Verdict

The framing of "Perplexity vs ChatGPT" implies you need to choose. For research tasks with factual stakes — finding current pricing, understanding recent developments, verifying a claim before publishing — Perplexity is better. For everything else, ChatGPT's breadth wins.

If forced to choose one subscription: most people do more general-purpose AI tasks than pure research, so **ChatGPT Plus** is the safer default. If your primary use is research, journalism, or staying current in a fast-moving field, **Perplexity Pro** at the same price delivers more per dollar for that specific use case.

## FAQ

**Is Perplexity more accurate than ChatGPT?**
For current factual information with sources, yes — Perplexity's always-on web search and systematic citation mean its factual claims are more traceable and often more current than ChatGPT's training data. For tasks that don't require the latest information (writing, coding, analysis), ChatGPT's reasoning quality is comparable or better.

**Can ChatGPT replace Perplexity?**
For casual research, ChatGPT with browsing enabled covers most of what Perplexity does. For serious research where you need every claim cited and traceable, Perplexity's systematic approach is harder to replicate with ChatGPT's manual browsing mode.
