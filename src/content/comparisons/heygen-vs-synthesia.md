# HeyGen vs Synthesia

[HeyGen](../tools/heygen.md) and [Synthesia](../tools/synthesia.md) both turn scripts into presenter-led AI video, but they are not interchangeable buying decisions. HeyGen is the better default for most self-serve marketing, sales, localization, and creator teams because it exposes a stronger free-to-paid path, custom digital twins on lower tiers, LiveAvatar workflows, broad language coverage, and direct business-video features at public prices. Synthesia is the safer enterprise training pick when SCORM export, PowerPoint-to-video, brand kits, SAML/SSO, implementation support, and L&D governance matter more than fast campaign production.

The short version: **choose HeyGen if the buyer is trying to make avatar videos quickly and repeatedly. Choose Synthesia if the buyer is buying a corporate training system.** That split matters because the wrong purchase can look fine in a demo and still fail in production. A marketing team may overpay for training governance it does not need; an L&D team may save money up front and then miss LMS, collaboration, or rollout controls.

## Quick Answer

For most buyers, [HeyGen](../tools/heygen.md) wins. Its current public pricing page lists a free plan, Creator at $29/month, Pro at $99/month, Business at $149/month plus $20 per added seat, and Enterprise custom pricing. Creator includes 30-minute videos, one Custom Digital Twin, unlimited Photo Avatars, 700+ stock video avatars, voice cloning, 175+ languages and dialects, 1080p export, brand kit, watermark removal, Avatar IV generation, Video Agent access, translation with lip sync, generated avatar looks, and advanced image/video models.

Choose [Synthesia](../tools/synthesia.md) instead when the business case is training and enablement at scale. Its pricing page lists Basic free, Starter at $29/month monthly or $18/month annual, Creator at $89/month monthly or $64/month annual, and Enterprise custom pricing. Enterprise adds unlimited video minutes, 1-click translations into 80+ languages, 240+ stock AI avatars, unlimited Personal Avatars, SAML/SSO, live team collaboration, brand kits, SCORM export, onboarding, implementation services, and a dedicated customer success manager.

## Where HeyGen Wins

HeyGen wins the general-purpose avatar-video purchase because it is easier to start, easier to understand as a creator/marketing tool, and better aligned with repeatable commercial video output. Its free plan is now a tighter evaluation lane: it lists 1 video per month, videos up to 3 minutes, one Custom Digital Twin, 500+ stock photo avatars, 720p export, share/download support, and trial access to premium features. That is enough to test the workflow shape, but serious campaign output should be evaluated on Creator or above.

The Creator tier is the key self-serve upgrade. At $29/month, it unlocks longer videos, 1080p export, watermark removal, brand kit, voice cloning, unlimited Photo Avatars, 700+ stock video avatars, 175+ languages and dialects, and access to premium generation features. That is the strongest reason HeyGen should remain the default recommendation for marketers, agencies, sales teams, and founders who need a practical avatar video pipeline.

HeyGen also has the stronger real-time story. Its LiveAvatar help page describes responsive LiveAvatars, Full and Lite integration modes, knowledge-base prompts, and voice chat through the Streaming SDK. That does not mean every buyer needs LiveAvatar, and it should not be confused with ordinary rendered-video subscriptions. But if the product vision includes an on-site guide, sales avatar, or app-embedded conversational presenter, HeyGen is the more relevant shortlist.

## Where Synthesia Wins

Synthesia wins when the buyer is not really buying "avatar video" as a campaign asset. It wins when the buyer is buying a repeatable training and enablement system. The Enterprise plan is built for that world: unlimited video minutes, 240+ stock avatars, unlimited Personal Avatars, 1-click translations, SAML/SSO, live team collaboration, brand kits, SCORM export, onboarding, implementation services, and dedicated CSM support.

That matters because enterprise training teams often care less about the cheapest way to render a single avatar clip and more about repeatable governance. They need consistent brand presentation, controlled access, localization, LMS packaging, stakeholder review, and support during rollout. Synthesia's pricing and security posture make more sense for that buying motion than for a solo creator comparing one subscription against another.

Personal Avatars are also a serious enterprise advantage. Synthesia's docs describe live consent recording, ownership handling, voice/language support, and Personal Avatars created from photo or video. For companies that need avatar identity governance, consent, and internal enablement workflows, those details are not cosmetic. They are procurement blockers.

## Pricing and Plan Guidance

Buy HeyGen Creator when one person or a small team needs recurring marketing, sales, or localization videos and can live with one Custom Digital Twin. Upgrade to Pro when 4K export, more premium usage, faster processing, or heavier translation proofreading matters. Business becomes relevant when team seats, SAML/SSO, collaboration, SCORM export, LMS integrations, and workflow integrations matter. Enterprise is the procurement path when the organization needs unlimited videos, no duration maximum, SCIM, MFA, role/access controls, audit logs, commercial terms, and dedicated success support.

Buy Synthesia Starter only if light individual video creation is enough. Creator is the better production tier because public pricing lists more stock avatars, larger usage allowances, API access, and production features. Enterprise is the main reason to choose Synthesia over HeyGen: unlimited minutes, governance, collaboration, SCORM, onboarding, and implementation support are the value.

The money trap is comparing only first paid prices. Both start at $29/month on public monthly pricing, while Synthesia discounts Starter to $18/month and Creator to $64/month on annual billing. The real cost still depends on video volume, avatars, credits, API use, premium generation, dubbing, seats, and enterprise governance. Before paying, check whether the exact workflow needs rendered avatar videos, interactive training modules, API automation, live avatars, or LMS-ready packages. Those are different purchases.

## Trust, Security, and Data Handling

Both tools now have credible enterprise claims, but they emphasize different workflows. HeyGen's security page describes tenant data separation, least-privilege access, MFA for internal production access, SSO, SCIM provisioning, audit logs, TLS 1.2+ in transit, AES-256 or equivalent encryption at rest, AWS US hosting, customer data deletion after the contracted retention period or request, and enterprise customer data excluded from AI model training by default.

Synthesia's security practices describe logical separation of customer data, customer-controlled SSO, encryption at rest with AES-256, TLS 1.2+ in transit, EU/Ireland data storage, export support after termination, and deletion of customer data within up to 90 days after request or account termination. That makes Synthesia easier to justify for regulated training programs where deletion windows, SSO, and data-region language matter during procurement.

## Bottom Line

HeyGen is the better answer for most people landing on this comparison because most visitors are trying to create avatar videos, demos, sales clips, localization assets, or digital-twin marketing content without buying an enterprise training platform. Synthesia is not worse; it is pointed at a different buyer. It becomes the better choice when the purchase is owned by L&D, compliance, HR, or enablement teams that need SCORM, collaboration, governance, implementation support, and rollout controls.

If the next click is a paid plan, pick based on workflow rather than brand. **HeyGen Creator is the default first paid plan for self-serve avatar video. Synthesia Enterprise is the strategic pick for training systems.** Anything in between should be tested with a real script, real brand assets, real export requirements, and the exact language/localization workflow before committing.

## FAQ

**Is HeyGen better than Synthesia?**

For most self-serve creator, marketing, sales, and localization workflows, yes. HeyGen has the stronger default path because Creator includes custom avatar and commercial video features at $29/month. Synthesia is better when the organization needs enterprise training controls, SCORM export, implementation support, and L&D governance.

**Which is cheaper, HeyGen or Synthesia?**

Both list a free plan and both list a $29/month first paid plan on public monthly pricing as of May 8, 2026. Synthesia also lists annual discounts from $18/month for Starter and $64/month for Creator. The real price depends on video volume, seats, API usage, credits, avatars, dubbing, and enterprise requirements. Do not compare only sticker price.

**Which is better for corporate training?**

Synthesia is usually better for corporate training because its Enterprise plan is built around unlimited video minutes, Personal Avatars, brand kits, SCORM export, live team collaboration, onboarding, implementation services, and dedicated customer success.

**Which is better for real-time avatars?**

HeyGen is the better shortlist if real-time avatar interaction matters. Its LiveAvatar documentation covers responsive avatars and voice chat through the Streaming SDK. Synthesia is stronger for generated business videos and training workflows, not live conversational avatars.

**Should I buy both?**

Only if the organization has two separate workflows: marketing/sales avatar content and enterprise training. Most smaller teams should start with HeyGen. Enterprise L&D teams should evaluate Synthesia first and compare HeyGen only if campaign-style avatar production is also in scope.
