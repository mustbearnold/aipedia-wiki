# HeyGen vs Synthesia

[HeyGen](/tools/heygen/) and [Synthesia](/tools/synthesia/) both make presenter-led AI video, but the right buyer split is sharper than "which avatar looks better?"

**June 5, 2026 verdict:** choose **HeyGen** for self-serve marketing, sales, localization, digital-twin, and fast business-video production. Choose **Synthesia** when the buyer is L&D, HR, compliance, or enablement and needs SCORM, LMS packaging, governance, collaboration, implementation support, and training rollout controls.

## Quick Answer
**Pick HeyGen if:**

- you need campaign, sales, founder, product, or localization videos quickly;
- Creator at $29/month is enough to start, or Pro at $49/month is needed for heavier usage and 4K;
- API or LiveAvatar workflows are part of the roadmap.

**Pick Synthesia if:**

- the output is corporate training, onboarding, compliance, SOPs, or enablement;
- SCORM export, SAML/SSO, live collaboration, brand kits, Personal Avatars, and implementation services matter;
- the budget owner wants enterprise training governance more than fast campaign production.

## Pricing Reality

HeyGen's current public pricing page shows Free, Creator at $29/month, Pro at $49/month, Business at $149/month plus $20/seat, and Enterprise custom. Its developer docs list separate Avatar IV & V API pricing, so automated generation should not be budgeted from the app table alone.

Synthesia's current pricing page shows Basic free, Starter at $29/month monthly or $18/month annual, Creator at $89/month monthly or $64/month annual, and Enterprise custom. It also lists credits as the shared usage currency, Creator API access, Veo 3.1/Sora 2 generated asset integrations, and Enterprise controls such as SCORM export, SAML/SSO, live collaboration, brand kits, onboarding, implementation services, and dedicated CSM support.

## Where HeyGen Wins

HeyGen wins most self-serve avatar-video decisions. Its buying path is simpler for marketing and sales teams: start with a script, create or choose an avatar, localize, export, and repeat. The current Pro price correction matters: older AiPedia copy that said Pro was $99/month is stale against the live pricing page.

HeyGen is also the more relevant shortlist when the roadmap includes real-time or programmatic avatar workflows. Its API docs now price Avatar IV & V output directly, and its product positioning keeps app plans, API usage, and enterprise/video-agent needs as separate surfaces.

## Where Synthesia Wins

Synthesia wins when the purchase is a training system. Its Enterprise pitch is not just avatars; it is unlimited video minutes, 1-click translations, 240+ stock AI avatars, unlimited Personal Avatars, SAML/SSO, live team collaboration, brand kits, SCORM export, AI Dubbing add-ons, onboarding, implementation services, and dedicated CSM support.

Personal Avatar and security documentation also matter for enterprise buyers. Synthesia documents personal-avatar creation routes from photo or video, and its security practices cover customer controls, encryption, application security, and data handling expectations. That is useful procurement evidence for L&D and compliance teams.

## Buyer Route

If the team is making campaign videos this week, start with HeyGen Creator and compare Pro only when 4K, usage, or speed blocks production. If the team is rolling out training at company scale, start with Synthesia Enterprise and compare HeyGen Business only if marketing/localization speed is also in scope.

Do not compare only the first paid sticker price. The real cost depends on minutes, credits, seats, avatars, dubbing, API usage, collaboration, SCORM/LMS needs, and governance.

## FAQ

**Is HeyGen better than Synthesia?**
For most self-serve avatar-video, sales, marketing, and localization workflows, yes. For enterprise training and L&D governance, Synthesia is often the safer first test.

**Which is cheaper?**
HeyGen Creator is $29/month and Pro is currently $49/month. Synthesia Starter is $29/month or $18/month annually, and Creator is $89/month or $64/month annually. Real cost depends on usage, credits, seats, avatars, and workflow requirements.

**Which is better for corporate training?**
Synthesia, especially when SCORM, LMS workflows, SAML/SSO, collaboration, onboarding, implementation, and dedicated CSM support matter.

**Which is better for real-time avatars?**
HeyGen is the stronger shortlist for real-time or programmatic avatar-video paths. Synthesia is stronger for generated business training videos.

## Bottom Line

HeyGen is the better first test for fast self-serve avatar videos, marketing clips, sales messages, localization, and programmatic avatar workflows. Synthesia is the better first test when the video is part of training, L&D, HR, compliance, SCORM, LMS rollout, or enterprise governance. Pick HeyGen for speed and campaign production; pick Synthesia for controlled training operations.
