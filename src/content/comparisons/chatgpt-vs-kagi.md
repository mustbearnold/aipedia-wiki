# ChatGPT vs Kagi

[ChatGPT](../tools/chatgpt.md) and [Kagi](../tools/kagi.md) both help users find and work with information, but they start from different assumptions. ChatGPT is a general AI assistant for writing, coding, analysis, images, voice, files, and synthesis. Kagi is a paid search engine with privacy, ranking controls, and AI answer features layered onto search.

## Quick Answer

Choose ChatGPT if you need an assistant that can create, reason, and transform work. Choose Kagi if you want a cleaner, private search experience with source-first discovery.

## Where ChatGPT Wins

- Better for drafting, rewriting, coding, summarizing files, brainstorming, and multimodal work.
- More useful when the output is a document, plan, script, analysis, or generated asset.
- Stronger as a general workplace assistant across many tasks.
- Can synthesize sources after research and turn them into finished deliverables.
- Better for users who want conversation, iteration, and content creation in one place.

## Where Kagi Wins

- Better for search-first users who want ranked web results, source control, and less ad-driven noise.
- Privacy and user-controlled ranking are central features, not side benefits.
- Useful when the task is discovery, comparison, or verification rather than content generation.
- Encourages inspection of sources instead of accepting a single generated response.
- Better replacement for a traditional search engine than ChatGPT is.

## Key Differences

ChatGPT is an answer-and-creation environment. Kagi is a search environment. ChatGPT can browse and cite, but it is not primarily a search engine. Kagi can summarize and answer, but it is not primarily a general assistant.

The strongest combined workflow is Kagi for discovery and source inspection, then ChatGPT for synthesis, drafting, coding, or next-step execution.

## Practical Workflow

Use Kagi first when:

- You need to inspect multiple sources directly.
- Search privacy and ad-free results are part of the value.
- You are comparing vendors, documentation, news, or public claims.
- You want a better daily web-search experience.
- You need to avoid over-trusting a single generated answer.

Use ChatGPT first when:

- You need to write, revise, code, analyze, or plan.
- You already have source material and need synthesis.
- The task benefits from back-and-forth refinement.
- You need outputs in a specific format.
- You are creating something new rather than searching the web.

For high-stakes research, use Kagi or another search tool to inspect sources, then ask ChatGPT to work only from the evidence you provide.

## Who should choose ChatGPT

Choose ChatGPT if your work spans creation, analysis, coding, files, writing, and iterative reasoning.

## Who should choose Kagi

Choose Kagi if search quality, privacy, source inspection, and less noisy web discovery are the main goals.

## Bottom Line

ChatGPT is the broader assistant. Kagi is the better search-first product. Use Kagi to find and verify; use ChatGPT to create and synthesize.

## FAQ

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Kagi can handle source discovery while ChatGPT turns researched material into finished work.

**Which is cheaper?**
Use the generated fact table and vendor pages for current pricing. The choice is mostly about search-first versus assistant-first workflow.

**Which one should I pick first?**
Start with ChatGPT if you need creation and analysis. Start with Kagi if search quality and privacy are the pain points.
