# ChatGPT vs Kagi

[ChatGPT](../tools/chatgpt.md) provides general-purpose conversational AI with multimodal features. [Kagi](../tools/kagi.md) focuses on search with AI summaries and privacy. This comparison covers their flagship versions, pricing, and use cases as of April 2026[1][2].

## Quick Answer

ChatGPT suits broad tasks like text generation, coding, and image creation in one interface. Kagi excels in fast, private search with direct answers and source citations.


## Where ChatGPT Wins

- Handles text, images, voice, browsing, and code in unified interface[2].
- Pro tier at $200/mo unlocks highest performance for complex reasoning[2].
- Plugin ecosystem supports custom workflows[2].
- Real-time voice for conversations and debugging[6].
- Free tier delivers value for casual use[2].

## Where Kagi Wins

- Privacy-focused search without tracking or ads[7].
- Direct answers with source links reduce follow-up queries[7].
- Lower entry price at $10/mo for core search features.
- Integrates latest models like GPT-5.3 without separate subscriptions[1].
- Faster responses for research due to search-first design[7].

## Key Differences

ChatGPT acts as a general AI platform, processing uploads like documents or images alongside text prompts for outputs in multiple formats. Its pricing scales to $200/mo for Pro access to GPT-5.5, targeting users needing one tool for diverse tasks[2]. Kagi prioritizes search, delivering AI-summarized results from the web with citations and no data retention, starting at $10/mo. While ChatGPT leads in versatility, Kagi offers specialized speed and privacy for information retrieval[1][2][7].

## Who should choose ChatGPT

Users handling mixed workflows like writing, coding, and media creation benefit from its all-in-one access. Teams needing enterprise integrations pick the Pro plan for scale[2].

## Who should choose Kagi

Individuals or teams focused on research value its cited answers and privacy. Power users select higher tiers for increased queries[7].

## Bottom Line

Choose ChatGPT for broad AI capabilities across tasks. Opt for Kagi when search speed, privacy, and citations matter most. Many combine both for complementary strengths[2][7].

## FAQ

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, ChatGPT covers generation while Kagi handles discovery; no overlap in core functions[2][7].

**Which is cheaper?**  
Kagi starts lower at $10/mo versus ChatGPT Plus at $20/mo, but ChatGPT free tier suits light use[2].

**Which one should I pick first?**  
Start with ChatGPT for general needs or Kagi if search drives your workflow[2][7].
