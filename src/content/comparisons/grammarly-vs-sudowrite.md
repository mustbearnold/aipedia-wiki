# Grammarly vs Sudowrite

[Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) and [Sudowrite](/tools/sudowrite/) should almost never be bought for the same primary job. Grammarly is an inline editor for grammar, clarity, tone, fluency, and workplace communication. Sudowrite is a fiction-first writing workspace built around Muse, Story Engine, Write, Describe, Expand, Rewrite, Brainstorm, Feedback, and manuscript workflow.

## Quick Answer

Choose Grammarly when the buyer needs a writing-quality layer for emails, documents, school work, support replies, marketing drafts, and professional communication.

Choose Sudowrite when the buyer is writing fiction and needs help with scenes, characters, sensory detail, dialogue, story momentum, prose expansion, and long-form creative revision.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best for grammar, spelling, clarity, and tone:** Grammarly.
- **Best for workplace and student polish:** Grammarly.
- **Best for team writing consistency:** Grammarly.
- **Best for fiction drafting:** Sudowrite.
- **Best for scene expansion and sensory detail:** Sudowrite.
- **Best fiction model/workflow:** Sudowrite Muse plus Story Engine.
- **Best first Sudowrite paid tier:** Professional at $22/month annual for working novelists.
- **Best final-pass fiction polish:** Use Grammarly after Sudowrite export.

## What Changed In June 2026

Grammarly's current pricing support page positions Grammarly Pro inside the Superhuman suite and lists monthly, quarterly, and annual Pro pricing. Grammarly remains the line-edit and communication guardrail, not a novel-writing environment.

Sudowrite's current pricing page lists Hobby & Student at $10/month on annual billing with 225,000 credits/month, Professional at $22/month annual with 1,000,000 annual-billing credits/month or 450,000 monthly-billing credits/month, and Max at $44/month annual with 2,000,000 credits/month plus rollover. The vendor docs describe Muse as Sudowrite's fiction-specific model, with Muse 1.5 listed as the current flagship prose mode and Muse powering Draft, Write, Expand, and Story Bible synopsis generation.

The editorial correction is simple: Grammarly is not "less creative Sudowrite," and Sudowrite is not "Grammarly for fiction." One polishes text; the other helps create and reshape story material.

## Where Grammarly Wins

- It is faster for final-pass grammar, clarity, tone, and fluency checks.
- It works where daily writing happens rather than asking authors to move into a separate fiction workspace.
- It is useful for fiction-adjacent work such as query letters, author bios, newsletters, blurbs, and emails.
- It is better for school, workplace, and professional writing where correctness matters more than imagination.
- Enterprise and Pro controls matter for teams; Sudowrite is primarily an author tool.

## Where Sudowrite Wins

- It is purpose-built for fiction rather than generic business or academic prose.
- Muse is designed for creative storytelling and is the default for Draft and Write, while also powering Expand and Story Bible synopsis work.
- Story Engine and Canvas organize long-form projects better than an inline grammar checker can.
- Describe, Expand, Rewrite, Brainstorm, and Feedback match actual author workflows.
- It handles fiction themes and creative directions that general writing assistants may flatten or refuse.

## Buying Recommendation

Use Grammarly when the manuscript, email, essay, or business document already exists and the buyer wants it cleaner. It is a polish layer.

Use Sudowrite when the fiction project is still being made: premise, outline, chapter, scene, paragraph, sensory detail, or rewrite pass. Hobby & Student is enough for casual experimentation. Professional is the realistic pick for serious novel work. Max is for heavy drafters publishing or revising at volume.

The best author workflow is not either/or. Draft and explore in Sudowrite, then run exported prose through Grammarly for mechanical polish. Keep final voice, continuity, originality, and rights decisions human-led.

## Pricing Snapshot

- **Grammarly Free:** core writing suggestions and limited AI help.
- **Grammarly Pro:** support pricing lists $30/member/month, $60/member/quarter, or $144/member/year.
- **Sudowrite free trial:** 10,000 credits with full feature access.
- **Sudowrite Hobby & Student:** $10/month annual or $19 monthly, with 225,000 credits/month.
- **Sudowrite Professional:** $22/month annual or $29 monthly, with 1,000,000 credits/month on annual billing or 450,000 on monthly billing.
- **Sudowrite Max:** $44/month annual or $59 monthly, with 2,000,000 credits/month and rollover.

## Watch-Outs

- Grammarly can make prose correct without making the scene better.
- Sudowrite can generate more story material without making the story structurally stronger.
- Sudowrite credits are consumed by AI generation. Long sessions can burn credits quickly.
- Publishers, contests, and schools may have AI-assistance policies. Check before submitting work.
- Neither tool replaces author judgment, fact-checking, originality review, or rights review.

## FAQ

**Is Sudowrite better than Grammarly for fiction?**
For drafting and developing fiction, yes. For final grammar, clarity, and typo cleanup, Grammarly is better.

**Can Grammarly write a novel?**
Not as its core job. It can help polish sections, but Sudowrite is built for story workflow and manuscript development.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Draft and expand in Sudowrite, then polish exported chapters with Grammarly.
