# Grammarly vs Sudowrite

[Grammarly](../tools/grammarly.md) and [Sudowrite](../tools/sudowrite.md) are two options in the writing category as of April 2026. Grammarly focuses on editing and grammar checks across documents; Sudowrite targets creative fiction generation. This comparison covers flagship versions, pricing, and workflow fit.

## Quick Answer

Grammarly suits general writing, editing, and professional documents; Sudowrite fits fiction authors needing story generation and expansion. Choice depends on editing needs versus creative drafting.

## At a Glance

| | Grammarly | Sudowrite |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Grammarly Premium with GPT-5.4 integration | Sudowrite Pro with Claude Opus 4.7 |
| **Price** | Free / Premium $12/mo (annual) / Business $15/user/mo | Pro $29/mo / Max $99/mo (annual discounts apply) |
| **Context Window / Output Specs** | 128K tokens input; tone, clarity, plagiarism checks | 1M tokens (Claude); story beats, character arcs, 4K+ word outputs |
| **Best For** | Emails, reports, academic papers | Novels, scripts, creative prose |

## Where Grammarly Wins

- Real-time editing in browsers, apps like Google Docs, Microsoft Word.
- Plagiarism detection scans against 16B web pages, academic papers.
- Tone adjustment for professional, concise, persuasive styles.
- Team plans include style guides, brand consistency for businesses.
- Free tier covers basic grammar, spelling for casual users.

## Where Sudowrite Wins

- Generates plot twists, character backstories, dialogue from prompts.
- Rewrite tool refines prose for pacing, show/dont-tell rules.
- Canvas mode brainstorms scenes, arcs visually.
- Fiction-specific models handle long narratives without repetition.
- Export to Scrivener, Google Docs for author workflows.

## Key Differences

Grammarly processes existing text for corrections, suggestions on clarity, engagement; it integrates everywhere but generates little original content. Sudowrite starts from outlines or snippets to produce full chapters, using creative models like Claude Opus 4.7 for narrative flow; it lacks broad editing but excels in drafting. Grammarly pricing starts lower for individuals; Sudowrite scales credits for heavy generation.

## Who should choose Grammarly

Students, professionals, businesses handling emails, reports, web copy. Those needing quick fixes without switching apps.

## Who should choose Sudowrite

Fiction writers, novelists, screenwriters building stories from scratch. Authors prioritizing generation over polishing.

## Bottom Line

Pick Grammarly for everyday editing across platforms; it handles professional workflows reliably. Choose Sudowrite for creative writing where story expansion matters most. Many authors use both: Sudowrite to draft, Grammarly to refine.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Grammarly Premium at $12/mo beats Sudowrite Pro at $29/mo for basic use; Sudowrite Max $99/mo suits high-volume generation.

**Which has better output quality?**  
Grammarly excels in error-free professional text; Sudowrite produces coherent fiction narratives per user reviews.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes; export Sudowrite drafts to Grammarly for final edits in shared docs.
