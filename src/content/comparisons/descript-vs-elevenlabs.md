# Descript vs ElevenLabs

[Descript](/tools/descript/) and [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/) both touch AI voice, but they are not substitutes for the same buyer. Descript is a transcript-first audio and video editor for cleaning, cutting, captioning, and publishing existing media. ElevenLabs is a voice-generation platform for text-to-speech, cloning, dubbing, speech-to-text, and voice agents.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Descript** if the work starts with a recording and ends with a polished podcast, lesson, talking-head video, or social clip. Choose **ElevenLabs** if the work starts with a script, product workflow, cloned voice, multilingual narration, or real-time voice-agent loop.

The safest default for most creators is: record and edit in Descript, generate premium narration or cloned voice lines in ElevenLabs, then assemble the final media in Descript.

## Where Descript Wins

- **Transcript editing is the product.** Descript lets editors cut spoken media by editing text, which is faster than waveform editing for interviews, lessons, podcasts, and talking-head videos.
- **Post-production tools live together.** Studio Sound, filler-word removal, captions, clips, Regenerate, AI Speech, AI avatars, and Underlord sit in the same creator workflow.
- **It is better after recording.** If the source material already exists and needs cleanup, edits, captions, export, or social repurposing, Descript is the more complete tool.
- **Plan allowances map to editor work.** Current Descript pricing uses media hours and AI credits rather than pure character generation, so it fits teams budgeting by editing workload.
- **Non-developers can use it immediately.** ElevenLabs has a strong UI, but Descript is closer to a full media editor for a producer who does not want to wire tools together.

## Where ElevenLabs Wins

- **Voice quality and range.** Eleven v3 is the stronger default for expressive narration, character work, and multilingual voice generation, with 70+ languages in the current model docs.
- **Real-time and API use.** Flash v2.5 is documented around ~75ms latency, while Scribe v2 Realtime targets ~150ms live transcription. Descript does not replace a voice-agent API stack.
- **Cloning and voice production.** Instant and Professional Voice Cloning are core ElevenLabs surfaces; Descript AI Speech is better framed as a fix-a-flub feature inside editing.
- **Clear API pricing.** ElevenLabs publishes TTS rates for Flash/Turbo and Multilingual/v3, plus Scribe v2 and Scribe v2 Realtime speech-to-text rates.
- **More complete voice platform.** ElevenLabs covers TTS, speech-to-text, dubbing, Studio, voice agents, sound effects, music, and newer image/video surfaces.

## Pricing and Plan Guidance

**Descript:** Free is for evaluation. Hobbyist is $24 monthly or $16 monthly on annual billing, with 10 media hours and 400 AI credits. Creator is $35 monthly or $24 annual, with 30 media hours, 800 AI credits, 4K export, full Underlord access, and extra bonus allowances. Business is $65 monthly or $50 annual, with 40 media hours, 1500 AI credits, Brand Studio, translation/dubbing, and priority support. Enterprise is custom.

**ElevenLabs:** Free includes 10K credits and no commercial rights. Starter is $6/mo and unlocks commercial use plus Instant Voice Cloning. Creator is $22/mo with 121K credits and Professional Voice Cloning. Pro is $99/mo with 600K credits and 44.1kHz PCM audio output via API. Scale is $299/mo, Business is $990/mo, and Enterprise is custom.

For a creator making edited videos or podcasts, start with Descript Creator before buying ElevenLabs Pro. For a developer building voice into a product, start with ElevenLabs API pricing and use Descript only if you also need editorial post-production.

## Common Buying Mistakes

- Do not buy Descript expecting it to be the highest-fidelity standalone TTS platform. It is an editor with AI voice tools.
- Do not buy ElevenLabs expecting a full transcript-based video editor. It generates and manages voice; it does not replace Descript's post-production workflow.
- Do not compare plan prices without units. Descript is constrained by media hours and AI credits; ElevenLabs is constrained by credits, characters, minutes, quality tier, and API product.
- Do not clone voices without consent. ElevenLabs and Descript both create voice-rights obligations for teams publishing synthetic speech.

## Bottom Line

Descript is the editor. ElevenLabs is the voice engine. If you only choose one, choose based on the starting point: existing recordings point to Descript; scripts, cloned voices, APIs, or agents point to ElevenLabs.

## FAQ

**Which is better for podcasts?**
Descript is better for podcast editing because transcription, cuts, cleanup, captions, and clips happen in one place. ElevenLabs is better when the podcast needs premium generated narration or cloned voice inserts.

**Which is cheaper?**
For basic commercial voice generation, ElevenLabs Starter at $6/mo is cheaper than a paid Descript editor seat. For editing real media, Descript Hobbyist or Creator can be cheaper than trying to assemble an editing workflow around generated audio tools.

**Does Descript replace ElevenLabs?**
No. Descript can generate or repair speech in an edit, but ElevenLabs is stronger for standalone TTS, voice cloning, dubbing, APIs, and agents.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Generate or clone voice in ElevenLabs, then edit and publish in Descript. That is often the highest-quality workflow.
