# Consensus vs Elicit

[Consensus](../tools/consensus.md) and [Elicit](../tools/elicit.md) are AI tools for academic and scientific research, focused on literature search, synthesis, and evidence extraction from papers as of April 2026. Consensus emphasizes consensus extraction from studies with yes/no answers and visualizations, while Elicit prioritizes workflow automation for systematic reviews and data extraction.

## Quick Answer

Elicit suits broader research workflows with extraction and summarization; Consensus excels for quick evidence checks on specific questions. Choice depends on need for speed versus depth.

## Where Consensus Wins

- Delivers yes/no/maybe answers with study citations in seconds for factual queries.[1](https://consensus.app/)
- Smart Tree View visualizes agreement across studies on topics like "Does X cause Y?".[2](https://consensus.app/blog/)
- Free tier searches 200 million papers with core features like Copilot chat.[3](https://consensus.app/pricing/)
- Lower Plus pricing at $8.99/month for unlimited searches and PDF analysis.[4](https://consensus.app/pricing/)
- Strong for clinical and biomedical questions with evidence grading.[5](https://consensus.app/home/blog/consensus-pro/)

## Where Elicit Wins

- Automates full workflows: search, screen, extract data into spreadsheets from 125M papers.[6](https://elicit.com/)
- Extracts to 40 custom columns for systematic reviews, reducing manual work by 80%.[7](https://elicit.com/pricing)
- Plus plan at $12/month includes notebook collaboration and full export options.(https://elicit.com/pricing)
- Better for complex queries needing reasoning and multi-step extraction with Claude Sonnet 4.6.(https://elicit.com/)
- Trusted by 2M+ researchers for reproducibility in meta-analyses.(https://elicit.com/about)

## Key Differences

Consensus focuses on rapid synthesis for yes/no questions, pulling distributions like "67% of studies say yes" with interactive charts from its 200 million paper index; it integrates GPT-5.3 for chat but prioritizes pre-analyzed consensus metrics over custom workflows.[1](https://consensus.app/) Elicit, powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6, handles open-ended research by searching 125 million papers, ranking by relevance, and enabling spreadsheet exports with up to 40 data fields per paper; its strength lies in reducing systematic review time from weeks to hours.[6](https://elicit.com/) Consensus free tier offers more generous search limits (unlimited basic), while Elicit gates advanced extraction behind paid plans; both support 1M token contexts for long PDFs, but Elicit adds team collaboration in notebooks.

## Who should choose Consensus

Choose Consensus for quick evidence checks, like verifying causal claims in medicine or policy, where visualizations of study agreement speed decisions. Solo researchers or clinicians benefit from its free unlimited searches and low-cost Plus plan.

## Who should choose Elicit

Choose Elicit for in-depth systematic reviews or meta-analyses needing structured data extraction and team sharing. Academic teams or grant-funded projects gain from its workflow tools that scale to hundreds of papers.

## Bottom Line

Consensus fits fast, targeted queries with its consensus metrics and affordability. Elicit handles comprehensive research pipelines better through extraction and collaboration. Most users start free on both; test for your query type.
