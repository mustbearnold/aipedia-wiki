# Cartesia vs Voxtral

[Cartesia](../tools/cartesia.md) and [Voxtral](../tools/voxtral.md) are two options in the AI voice category as of April 2026. This comparison covers flagship versions, pricing, and workflow fit based on available data.

## Quick Answer

Cartesia offers lower latency for real-time applications; Voxtral provides higher fidelity for studio production. Choice depends on speed needs versus audio detail.

## At a Glance

| | Cartesia | Voxtral |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Sonic 2.0 | VoiceForge 3.1 |
| **Price** | $0.25 per 1k chars input, $1.20 per 1k chars output | $0.40 per 1k chars input, $2.10 per 1k chars output |
| **Context window/output specs** | 400k chars context, 30s max output, <200ms latency | 1M chars context, 5min max output, 800ms latency |
| **Best For** | Real-time voice agents, telephony | Audiobooks, podcasts, dubbing |

## Where Cartesia Wins

- Lower latency suits live conversations and call centers.[1]
- Cheaper per-character rates reduce costs for high-volume use.[2]
- Smaller model size enables edge deployment on devices.[3]
- Stable API uptime above 99.9% in Q1 2026 reports.[4]
- Broader language support, 52 voices across 29 languages.[5]

## Where Voxtral Wins

- Superior MOS scores, 4.7 versus Cartesia's 4.3 on blind tests.[6]
- Longer output duration fits narrative content production.[7]
- Advanced emotion controls for expressive delivery.
- Higher sampling rates up to 48kHz for professional audio.

## Key Differences

Cartesia prioritizes speed with sub-200ms end-to-end latency, making it suitable for interactive applications where delays disrupt flow; Voxtral emphasizes quality, achieving higher mean opinion scores through detailed prosody modeling, though at higher latency and cost. Pricing reflects this: Cartesia at $0.25/$1.20 per 1k input/output characters versus Voxtral's $0.40/$2.10. Context handling favors Voxtral's 1M characters for complex scripts, while Cartesia caps at 400k but processes faster.[1][2][6]

## Who should choose Cartesia

Teams building voice assistants or customer support bots benefit from Cartesia's low latency and cost efficiency.

## Who should choose Voxtral

Content creators producing audiobooks or videos select Voxtral for its audio fidelity and extended output capabilities.

## Bottom Line

Use Cartesia for applications needing immediate response times, like telephony. Opt for Voxtral when audio realism outweighs speed, such as in media production. Test both APIs with your prompts to confirm fit.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Cartesia has lower rates at $0.25 input/$1.20 output per 1k characters; Voxtral costs $0.40/$2.10.[2]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Voxtral scores higher on MOS benchmarks at 4.7; Cartesia reaches 4.3.[6]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, combine Cartesia for real-time interaction and Voxtral for final polished audio in hybrid workflows.[1]
