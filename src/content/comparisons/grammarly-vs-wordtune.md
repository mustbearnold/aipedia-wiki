# Grammarly vs Wordtune

[Grammarly](../tools/grammarly.md) and [Wordtune](../tools/wordtune.md) are two AI writing assistants that check grammar, suggest rewrites, and edit text as of April 2026. Grammarly focuses on broad proofreading and style corrections across apps; Wordtune emphasizes rewrite suggestions and tone adjustments in real time.

## Quick Answer

Grammarly suits users needing comprehensive grammar checks and team collaboration features. Wordtune fits those who prioritize quick rewrites and creative phrasing options.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Grammarly | Wordtune |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Grammarly Premium with GPT-5.3 integration | Wordtune Pro with Claude Sonnet 4.6 |
| **Price** | Free / Premium $12/mo / Business $15/user/mo | Free / Pro $9.99/mo / Teams $12/user/mo |
| **Context Window** | 500k tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Grammar, plagiarism checks, enterprise teams | Rewrites, tone shifts, individual creators |

## Where Grammarly Wins

- Offers plagiarism detection across 16 billion web pages, which Wordtune lacks.[https://www.grammarly.com/business]
- Integrates with 500,000+ apps including Microsoft Word, Google Docs, and email clients for seamless use.[https://www.grammarly.com/]
- Provides detailed tone analysis with confidence scores and full-sentence rewrites in 30+ languages.[https://www.grammarly.com/business]
- Business plan includes admin controls, style guides, and analytics for teams of 3+ users.[https://www.grammarly.com/business/pricing]
- Free tier includes basic grammar and spelling checks unlimited for individuals.[https://www.grammarly.com/plans]

## Where Wordtune Wins

- Generates 5-10 rewrite options per sentence with one click, faster for ideation than Grammarly's single suggestions.[https://www.wordtune.com/pricing]
- Uses 1M token context for coherent edits on long documents like reports or emails.[https://www.wordtune.com/]
- Spire plan adds custom tone matching and priority support at lower per-user cost for small teams.[https://www.wordtune.com/pricing]
- Real-time browser extension rewrites sentences as you type in Gmail, LinkedIn, and Docs.[https://www.wordtune.com/features]
- Pro plan includes unlimited rewrites and image generation for visuals in writing workflows.[https://www.wordtune.com/pricing]

## Key Differences

Grammarly excels in accuracy for grammar, punctuation, and clarity with its proprietary engine plus GPT-5.3 for advanced suggestions; it processes shorter contexts but covers more use cases like citations and conciseness.[https://www.grammarly.com/] Wordtune prioritizes creativity through Claude Sonnet 4.6, offering varied phrasings and a larger 1M token window for book chapters or articles, though its grammar detection trails Grammarly in precision.[https://www.wordtune.com/] Pricing starts lower for Wordtune individuals at $9.99/mo versus Grammarly's $12/mo, but Grammarly Business scales better for enterprises with SSO and data controls.[https://www.grammarly.com/business/pricing][https://www.wordtune.com/pricing]

## Who should choose Grammarly

Students, professionals, and businesses handling reports or emails benefit from Grammarly's reliable checks and integrations. Teams over 10 users gain from its admin dashboard and compliance tools.

## Who should choose Wordtune

Freelance writers, marketers, and creators needing fast style variations pick Wordtune for its rewrite speed and long-context handling. Solo users save with the cheaper Pro plan.

## Bottom Line

Choose Grammarly for precise editing and broad compatibility in professional settings. Opt for Wordtune when rewrite variety and document length matter more than exhaustive grammar scans. Many users run both via browser extensions for complementary strengths.

## FAQ

**Which has better output quality?**  
Grammarly leads in grammar accuracy and plagiarism tools; Wordtune produces more creative rewrites with Claude Sonnet 4.6.[https://www.grammarly.com/][https://www.wordtune.com/]

**Can I use both?**
