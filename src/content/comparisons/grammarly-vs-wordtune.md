# Grammarly vs Wordtune

[Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) and [Wordtune](/tools/wordtune/) both improve text the buyer has already written. Grammarly is broader: grammar, clarity, tone, fluency, full-sentence rewrites, style consistency, and team/Enterprise controls. Wordtune is narrower: voice-preserving sentence rewrites, summaries, paraphrasing, fluency, grammar, and quick clarity help from AI21 Labs.

## Quick Answer

Choose Grammarly when the buyer needs reliable writing quality across the browser, desktop, documents, and team communication. It is the better editor.

Choose Wordtune when the buyer mostly wants cheaper sentence-level rewrites and summaries without buying a full communication-quality platform. It is the better focused rewrite layer.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best grammar and spelling layer:** Grammarly.
- **Best professional tone and clarity layer:** Grammarly.
- **Best for teams and Enterprise controls:** Grammarly.
- **Best low-cost voice-preserving rewrite plan:** Wordtune Unlimited at $9.99/month or $6.99/month annual.
- **Best for occasional sentence rewrites:** Wordtune Basic free.
- **Best for broad browser coverage:** Grammarly.
- **Best for preserving a writer's original phrasing:** Wordtune.

## What Changed In June 2026

Grammarly's current support page lists Pro at $30/member/month, $60/member/quarter, or $144/member/year and notes that Pro replaces the older Grammarly Business plan. Grammarly remains the safer choice for broad professional writing quality and team deployment.

Wordtune's current plan documentation presents Basic, Advanced, and Unlimited for individual use, with pricing varying by region. The AiPedia tool record remains anchored to Wordtune's public plan ladder: Basic free, Advanced at $6.99/month or $4.89/month annual, and Unlimited at $9.99/month or $6.99/month annual. Wordtune Business docs say Business has a free 7-day trial for accounts with 3 to 100 seats and that pricing depends on seat quantity.

The old comparison used unsupported model and context-window claims. The current buyer decision is simpler: grammar-first breadth versus rewrite-first value.

## Where Grammarly Wins

- It catches more types of writing issues before the text leaves the app.
- It is stronger for punctuation, grammar, clarity, tone, fluency, and consistency.
- It fits daily professional communication better than a rewrite-only workflow.
- It has a clearer Enterprise procurement path for security, admin, SSO, and data controls.
- It is less likely to create many alternative phrasings when the user only needs a clear correction.

## Where Wordtune Wins

- It is cheaper for buyers who mainly need rewrites and summaries.
- It stays close to the writer's original voice instead of pushing mode-heavy paraphrases.
- Basic gives occasional users a useful free rewrite/suggestion and summary path.
- Unlimited removes rewrite, suggestion, and summary caps at a low annual price.
- Business exists for 3 to 100 seats, but buyers need to confirm seat pricing and admin needs before rollout.

## Buying Recommendation

Use Grammarly if writing quality is a daily risk: work email, executive docs, sales copy, support replies, academic drafts, or team communication. Pro is easier to justify when the user writes across many apps every day.

Use Wordtune if the user already has decent grammar but wants a cheaper way to explore alternate sentence wording, summaries, clarity rewrites, and voice-preserving edits. Advanced is enough for light daily use; Unlimited is the realistic plan for serious rewrite sessions.

Use both only when the workflow warrants it: Wordtune for phrasing options, Grammarly for final correctness and tone before publishing or sending.

## Pricing Snapshot

- **Grammarly Free:** core writing suggestions and limited AI assistance.
- **Grammarly Pro:** support pricing lists $30/member/month, $60/member/quarter, or $144/member/year.
- **Grammarly Enterprise:** custom pricing for larger or more controlled deployments.
- **Wordtune Basic:** free, with daily rewrite/suggestion limits and three monthly summaries.
- **Wordtune Advanced:** $6.99/month or $4.89/month annual; more rewrites and summaries.
- **Wordtune Unlimited:** $9.99/month or $6.99/month annual; unlimited rewrites, suggestions, and summaries.
- **Wordtune Business:** free 7-day trial for 3 to 100 seats; pricing depends on seat quantity.

## Watch-Outs

- Wordtune is not a full drafting assistant. It needs source text.
- Grammarly can be too aggressive on domain-specific jargon, names, and technical text.
- Wordtune Business pricing is seat-dependent; verify admin, invoice, security, and SSO needs before rollout.
- Grammarly checkout can vary by region and account state.
- Neither tool verifies facts. Rewrites can make false claims sound cleaner.

## FAQ

**Is Wordtune better than Grammarly?**
For cheap voice-preserving sentence rewrites, Wordtune can be better. For grammar, spelling, tone, clarity, and team writing quality, Grammarly is better.

**Which is cheaper?**
Wordtune Unlimited is cheaper than Grammarly Pro on the public annual anchors. Grammarly may still be worth more if the user needs always-on correction across many apps.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Use Wordtune to explore phrasing and Grammarly for final grammar, tone, and consistency.

## Bottom Line

Grammarly is the better full writing assistant for grammar, tone, clarity, consistency, and team communication. Wordtune is the better lightweight rewrite layer when the user wants cheaper phrasing options and summaries without buying a broader communication platform. Choose Grammarly when mistakes leaving the app are the risk; choose Wordtune when the main job is sentence-level polish.
