# ChatGPT vs Elicit

[ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) and [Elicit](/tools/elicit/) both help with research, but they solve different research jobs. ChatGPT is the broad academic assistant for brainstorming, explanation, writing, coding, files, data, charts, and current web research. Elicit is the specialist for academic-paper search, systematic reviews, screening, extraction tables, evidence reports, alerts, and paper-centered workflow.

## Quick Answer

Choose **ChatGPT** if you need one flexible assistant for mixed academic work: outlining, explaining methods, summarizing uploaded material, debugging analysis code, drafting prose, and checking reasoning. Choose **Elicit** if the hard part is finding papers, screening papers, extracting structured evidence, or building a repeatable literature-review workflow.

For students and solo researchers, start with ChatGPT Free or Plus plus free academic search. For systematic reviews, scoping reviews, evidence maps, clinical literature, or policy reviews, Elicit Pro or Scale is the more relevant paid upgrade.

## Decision Snapshot

| Research need | Better fit | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Mixed academic assistant work | ChatGPT | Handles explanation, writing, file analysis, code, data, charts, current web research, and broad drafting. |
| Literature-review workflow | Elicit | Built around paper search, screening, extraction, reports, and structured evidence tables. |
| Casual study help | ChatGPT | Faster and broader when the user is learning, outlining, or asking methods questions. |
| Systematic review | Elicit | Pro and higher plans include the dedicated workflow and screening/extraction capacity. |
| Source-grounded paper extraction | Elicit | Stronger when the output is a table with fields, studies, and evidence. |
| Current non-academic research | ChatGPT | Better for product docs, companies, policy, news, and market research outside academic corpora. |

## Where ChatGPT Wins

ChatGPT wins when research is only one part of the workflow. It can explain methods, summarize uploaded notes, help write Python or R, analyze spreadsheets, draft a literature-review introduction, build an outline, generate a presentation, and search current web sources.

It is also the easier first purchase when the user does not need formal review structure. Many students and analysts need a thinking partner, not a systematic-review pipeline.

Choose ChatGPT first if you need:

- One assistant for writing, files, code, charts, images, voice, and current web work.
- Study help, methods explanations, and first-draft prose.
- Research-question framing before formal search begins.
- Help after the source set is selected.
- A lower-friction individual subscription.

## Where Elicit Wins

Elicit wins when the deliverable is evidence, not just a fluent answer. Its pricing page lists unlimited search across more than 138 million papers on Basic, plus paid tiers that add Research Agent access, exports, clinical-trials search, systematic-review workflow, extraction columns, reports, alerts, collaboration, figure interpretation, and Enterprise controls.

Elicit's Pro plan is the first serious systematic-review tier. It includes the dedicated workflow, 5,000-paper screening, 144 reports or systematic reviews per year, 20 columns at a time, custom extractions, explanations, templates, and API access. Scale adds collaboration, figure extraction, more reports, more extraction capacity, and admin usage tracking. Enterprise is the institutional route for custom deployments, SSO/SAML, 2FA, custom sources, unlimited Search API access, and no training on your data by default.

Choose Elicit first if you need:

- Academic-paper search and screening.
- Structured extraction tables.
- Systematic review, scoping review, or evidence map workflows.
- Repeatable paper alerts and evidence reports.
- Collaboration and admin controls for research teams.

## Pricing And Plan Guidance

ChatGPT's paid decision is simple for most researchers: Plus is the first upgrade when file work, deep research, image generation, data analysis, memory, projects, and heavier usage matter. Pro is only for heavy GPT-5.5 Pro, agent mode, deep research, or Codex usage.

Elicit pricing needs billing-cadence attention. As of the June 2026 source check, Basic is free. The yearly surface lists Plus at $7/user/month billed annually, Pro at $29/user/month billed annually, and Scale at $49/user/month billed annually. The monthly surface lists Pro at $49/user/month and Scale at $169/user/month. Enterprise is custom.

Do not compare these as interchangeable chatbot seats. ChatGPT Plus buys a broad assistant. Elicit Pro buys a literature-review workflow.

## Best Workflow

Use Elicit first when the source set does not exist yet:

1. Define the research question and inclusion criteria.
2. Search papers and clinical trials where relevant.
3. Screen titles and abstracts.
4. Extract fields into a table.
5. Inspect original papers before citing.

Use ChatGPT after evidence is selected:

1. Explain methods or statistical terms.
2. Critique the argument.
3. Debug analysis code.
4. Turn extraction notes into an outline.
5. Revise prose while preserving source references.

For serious work, cite original papers, not generated summaries.

## Watch-Outs

- ChatGPT can sound confident without proving the source.
- Elicit accelerates screening and extraction, but it does not replace human judgment.
- Paywalled full text, inclusion criteria, study quality, and extraction fields still need expert review.
- Institutional, journal, funder, or employer AI-use policies may require disclosure.
- Do not upload confidential research, patient data, unpublished manuscripts, or restricted interviews without checking policy and vendor terms.

## Bottom Line

ChatGPT is the better general academic assistant. Elicit is the better literature-review machine. If your work is mixed, start with ChatGPT. If the output is an evidence table, systematic review, scoping review, or paper-screening workflow, start with Elicit.

## FAQ

**Can ChatGPT replace Elicit?**

Not for formal literature-review work. ChatGPT can help frame questions, explain papers, and draft prose, but Elicit is built for paper search, screening, extraction, and review workflow.

**Can Elicit replace ChatGPT?**

No. Elicit is not a broad assistant for coding, image generation, general writing, presentations, voice, or mixed daily work.

**Which is cheaper?**

ChatGPT has a free tier and Plus. Elicit has Basic free, Plus yearly, Pro, Scale, and Enterprise. The cheaper choice depends on whether you need a broad assistant or a systematic-review workflow.

**Which is safer for citations?**

Elicit is safer for paper-centered workflows because it is built around source viewing and extraction. But neither tool makes citations safe automatically. Open the original source before citing it.

**Should researchers use both?**

Often, yes. Elicit handles paper discovery, screening, and extraction. ChatGPT helps explain methods, revise prose, debug analysis, and package the work after evidence is selected.
