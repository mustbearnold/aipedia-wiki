# Amplemarket vs Apollo

[Amplemarket](/tools/amplemarket/) and [Apollo](/tools/apollo/) are close enough to compare because both sit in the outbound sales workflow: prospect data, enrichment, sequencing, calling, CRM handoff, and AI-assisted sales work. The buying question is not "which one has AI?" It is whether the team should buy a premium AI SDR suite or start with a lower-friction GTM platform.

AiPedia verified the current public pricing and product claims on June 28, 2026. Both tools have affiliate links on AiPedia, but the recommendation stays buyer-first: annual commitment, credit limits, CRM hygiene, data quality, deliverability, and rep adoption matter more than commission.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Amplemarket** if the team has a real SDR motion, can commit annually, and wants one vendor for AI-assisted prospecting, contact-level signals, multichannel sequences, Duo Copilot, Duo Voice, Duo Inbox, Workflows automation, and Claude or ChatGPT MCP access.

Choose **Apollo** if the team wants the safer first purchase: public self-serve pricing, a free path, Basic at $49/month on annual billing or $65 month to month, Professional at $79/month on annual billing or $99 month to month, 230M+ contacts, sequences, dialer, CRM sync, AI Assistant, MCP, and enough GTM workflow coverage to learn before buying a premium suite.

For most founders and small SDR teams, **Apollo is the default first buy**. For funded outbound teams that will use Duo, Workflows, voice, inbox, and AI-agent routing together, **Amplemarket can be the better upgrade**.

## Decision Snapshot

- **Best first outbound platform:** Apollo. It is easier to test and cheaper to scale seat by seat.
- **Best premium AI SDR suite:** Amplemarket. Duo and Workflows give it a stronger packaged AI SDR story.
- **Best for founder-led sales:** Apollo, unless the founder already has high annual budget and a clear outbound process.
- **Best for a 4+ rep SDR team standardizing AI-assisted outbound:** Amplemarket deserves the sales call, especially if Duo Voice and Workflows are part of the rollout.
- **Best contact-database scale claim on current public pages:** Apollo says 230M+ contacts. Amplemarket's pricing explainer says 200M+ AI-verified contacts.
- **Main watch-out:** Apollo can become credit and data-governance work. Amplemarket can become an expensive annual commitment if reps do not actually use the full workflow.

## Where Amplemarket Wins

Amplemarket wins when the buyer wants an opinionated AI SDR operating stack rather than a lighter sales platform.

- **Duo is the product difference.** Duo Copilot, Duo Copywriter, Competitive Intelligence, Signals, Duo Voice, and Duo Inbox are designed to sit inside one outbound workflow. Apollo has AI assistance, but Amplemarket is more explicitly packaged around AI SDR behavior.
- **Workflows go beyond simple sequences.** Amplemarket's current product story includes workflow automation for follow-ups, routing, CRM ownership, external webhooks, HTTP actions, off-platform activity capture, and sequence movement.
- **MCP is sales-agent focused.** Amplemarket's MCP announcement says Claude and ChatGPT can search prospects, enrich contacts, and build lead lists. Its June 2026 product update adds sequence creation through MCP for user review inside Amplemarket.
- **Team packaging is clearer for an SDR org.** Startup includes 2 users at $600/month on an annual term. Growth includes 4 users and adds Duo Voice. Elite includes 10 users and adds Duo Inbox.
- **It forces a serious rollout.** That sounds like a downside, but it helps when the buyer is a revenue leader who wants one standardized outbound motion rather than every rep improvising.

Pick Amplemarket when the buyer says: "We already know outbound is a core motion. We want AI-assisted research, signal discovery, sequences, calling, reply handling, and workflow automation in one governed sales system."

## Where Apollo Wins

Apollo wins when the buyer needs a practical, lower-risk starting point.

- **Lower public entry cost.** Apollo's current pricing page exposes Free, Basic at $49/month on annual billing or $65 month to month, Professional at $79/month on annual billing or $99 month to month, and Enterprise custom. Amplemarket Startup is $600/month on an annual term for 2 users.
- **Broader first-platform fit.** Apollo covers prospecting, enrichment, sequences, dialer, CRM sync, deal execution, AI Assistant, MCP, integrations, Chrome extension, and workflow automation.
- **Self-serve testing is easier.** Apollo has a sign-up path and free plan. Amplemarket has a free trial, but every paid plan is an annual commitment.
- **Contact-database scale is stronger on current public pages.** Apollo's sales engagement page says 230M+ contacts. Amplemarket's pricing explainer says 200M+ AI-verified contacts.
- **It is easier to pair with specialists.** Apollo can run as the base layer while Clay handles deeper enrichment or Instantly handles high-volume cold-email infrastructure.

Pick Apollo when the buyer says: "We need prospect data plus engagement in one system, but we are still proving the outbound motion and cannot justify a premium annual suite yet."

## Pricing And Plan Guidance

Use **Apollo Free** only for testing data coverage and workflow fit. Use **Apollo Basic** when a solo founder or early sales hire needs paid prospecting access without committing to the full SDR motion. Use **Apollo Professional** when the team needs a practical outbound platform with engagement, dialer, AI, CRM, and workflow features. Treat **Enterprise** as the route for governance, custom packaging, or larger GTM operations.

Use **Amplemarket Startup** only when 2 users can justify a $600/month annual commitment through booked-meeting lift, not just nicer AI copy. Move to **Growth** when a 4-user team needs Duo Voice, higher contact volume, and sales-led support. Consider **Elite** when 10+ users need Duo Inbox, larger contact pools, and a more governed rollout.

The cost fork is simple: Apollo is the better proving ground. Amplemarket is the stronger consolidation bet after the team has proof that outbound, rep adoption, and target-market coverage are worth a larger annual commitment.

## Buyer Fit Matrix

**Solo founder testing outbound:** Apollo. It has the lower entry cost, a free path, and one platform for search and sequences.

**2-person founder-led sales team with annual budget:** Depends. Apollo is safer; Amplemarket makes sense only if Duo and Workflows are central to the rollout.

**4+ SDR team standardizing AI-assisted outbound:** Amplemarket. Growth packaging and Duo Voice fit a managed SDR motion better than a light self-serve test.

**Team already has Outreach or Salesloft:** Depends. Apollo can replace more of the stack; Amplemarket is better if AI SDR workflow is the main reason to switch.

**Need deep enrichment logic:** Neither as the only tool. Use Clay alongside the chosen sales platform.

**Need many-inbox cold email at low cost:** Neither as the only tool. Instantly is usually the sender-first choice.

**Need governed AI actions from Claude or ChatGPT:** Depends. Apollo has MCP and broader GTM operations; Amplemarket has a more sales-agent-specific MCP workflow.

## Who Should Avoid Each

Avoid **Amplemarket** if the team is under five reps, cannot commit annually, only needs a contact database, or has not proven that outbound can produce qualified meetings in its market.

Avoid **Apollo** if the team is specifically buying for a premium AI SDR rollout, wants Duo-style reply and voice workflows under one vendor, or already has budget for a guided annual implementation.

Avoid **both** if the real bottleneck is sender infrastructure, mailbox rotation, or inbox placement. In that case, evaluate [Instantly](/tools/instantly/) first. Avoid both as the only system if the real bottleneck is account research and enrichment waterfalls. In that case, evaluate [Clay](/tools/clay/).

## Bottom Line

Apollo is the better first outbound platform for most buyers. It is easier to test, easier to budget, and broad enough to cover prospect data, sequences, dialer, CRM sync, AI Assistant, and MCP workflows.

Amplemarket is the better premium AI SDR suite when the team has enough outbound volume to justify an annual commitment and enough sales-ops discipline to use Duo, Workflows, MCP, voice, inbox, and CRM routing together.

## FAQ

**Is Amplemarket better than Apollo?**

It is better for teams buying a premium AI SDR suite. Apollo is better for most teams buying their first all-in-one outbound platform.

**Which is cheaper, Amplemarket or Apollo?**

Apollo is cheaper to start. Apollo's public pricing page currently exposes Free, Basic at $49/month on annual billing or $65 month to month, and Professional at $79/month on annual billing or $99 month to month. Amplemarket Startup is $600/month on an annual term for 2 users.

**Which has the bigger database?**

Apollo's current sales engagement page says 230M+ contacts. Amplemarket's pricing explainer says 200M+ AI-verified contacts. Buyers should still test coverage in their exact market because database headline size does not prove target-list quality.

**Can Apollo replace Amplemarket?**

For many founder-led and small-team outbound workflows, yes. Apollo covers prospecting, enrichment, sequences, dialer, CRM sync, AI Assistant, and MCP. Amplemarket remains stronger when Duo, Workflows, voice, inbox, and annual SDR-team rollout are the real buying reasons.

**Can Amplemarket replace Apollo?**

Yes, if the team wants a premium all-in-one sales workflow and can accept the annual plan floor. It is not the right replacement if the buyer mainly wants cheaper prospecting, flexible monthly testing, or a base GTM platform to pair with Clay or Instantly.
