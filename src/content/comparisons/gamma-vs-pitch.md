# Gamma vs Pitch

[Gamma](../tools/gamma.md) and [Pitch](../tools/pitch.md) are AI presentation tools that generate slides from text prompts or notes as of April 2026. Gamma focuses on converting notes into presentations, documents, and webpages, while Pitch offers deck creation with design templates.[2]

## Quick Answer

Gamma suits quick note-to-slide conversion for individuals; Pitch fits teams needing collaborative deck design. Choice depends on workflow needs.

## At a Glance

| | Gamma | Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Gamma 2.0 (Claude Sonnet 4.6 integration) | Pitch 3.5 (Gemini 3.1 Pro backbone) |
| **Price** | Free; Plus $10/month; Pro $20/month | Free; Pro $20/month; Enterprise $100/user/month |
| **Best For** | Solo creators, note-based slides | Teams, branded decks |

## Where Gamma Wins

- Converts short notes directly into full presentations or webpages faster than Pitch.[2]
- Free tier supports unlimited AI generations with basic exports.[2]
- Lighter interface for one-person workflows without team overhead.
- Integrates Claude Sonnet 4.6 for structured outline generation from prompts.[1]
- Exports to PDF, PPT, or web without watermarks on paid plans.

## Where Pitch Wins

- Stronger collaboration features for real-time team editing on shared decks.
- Template library tailored for sales, pitch, and investor decks.
- Brand kit application ensures consistent styling across slides.
- Gemini 3.1 Pro handles multimodal inputs like images or data for complex visuals.[1][3]
- Enterprise plans include analytics on viewer engagement.

## Key Differences

Gamma prioritizes speed for individual users turning ideas or notes into slides via AI outlining powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6, which excels in structured content at 1M token context.[1] Pitch emphasizes team use with Gemini 3.1 Pro for multimodal processing, supporting larger datasets or visuals in collaborative environments with 2M token capacity.[3] Pricing starts free for both, but Gamma's Plus at $10/month offers more generations than Pitch's Pro at $20/month, while Pitch scales better for enterprises.[2]

## Who should choose Gamma

Solo creators or content makers who start from notes and need fast exports to PDF or web. Best for non-designers focused on outline-to-slide automation.

## Who should choose Pitch

Teams building branded sales or investor decks with shared editing. Suited for groups requiring templates, analytics, and multimodal inputs.

## Bottom Line

Use Gamma for personal, note-driven presentations at lower cost. Opt for Pitch when team collaboration and deck analytics matter more than solo speed. Test free tiers to match your workflow.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Gamma Plus at $10/month undercuts Pitch Pro at $20/month for similar generation limits.[2]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Pitch edges out for polished, branded decks; Gamma matches for simple note conversions.[2]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, Gamma for drafts, Pitch for team polish; both free tiers allow testing.
