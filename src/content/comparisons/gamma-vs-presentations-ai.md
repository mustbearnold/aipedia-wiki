# Gamma vs Presentations.AI

[Gamma](/tools/gamma/) and [Presentations.AI](/tools/presentations-ai/) both create presentations with AI, but Gamma is the stronger default for most buyers. Gamma is not only a slide generator. Its official product navigation and AI presentation page position it across presentations, websites, social media, documents, API, and graphics. Its AI presentation page says users can create a working presentation in under a minute, refine it, customize it, export it, and publish it in a format that looks good on any device. Presentations.AI is more narrowly focused on AI-generated presentations with annual pricing.

The practical rule: **choose Gamma when you want one AI workspace for decks, docs, websites, and shareable content. Choose Presentations.AI when you want a cheaper annual presentation-only drafting tool.** This page was re-verified on **May 8, 2026** against current official Gamma and Presentations.AI sources.

## Quick Verdict

**Gamma wins for most AI presentation buyers.** Its current pricing page lists a Free tier plus Plus, Pro, and Ultra tiers. The page confirms useful functional differences: Free can create up to 10 cards per prompt and supports simple presentations, docs, websites, social, images, PDF/PPTX import, and PDF/PPTX/PNG/Google Slides export. Plus adds up to 20 cards per prompt, removes Gamma branding, and adds advanced AI image models. Pro adds up to 60 cards per prompt, premium AI image models, custom branding and fonts, detailed analytics, advanced sharing, up to 10 custom domains, API access, and workspace templates. Ultra adds up to 75 cards per prompt, advanced AI models across text/image/video, up to 100 custom domains, and early access to new features.

**Presentations.AI is a lower-cost annual presentation-only option.** Its pricing page lists Starter at $0 with limited AI credits, Public beta at $40/year for up to 10 members, Pro at $198/year under early-bird pricing, and Enterprise as custom. Its product page emphasizes effortless creation, personalized design, adaptive templates, PowerPoint compatibility, Brand Sync, sharing/access control, analytics, multi-device support, and multilingual support.

If the buyer wants a flexible content surface that can become a deck, document, website, or shareable link, Gamma is the stronger choice. If the buyer only wants low-cost annual AI slide drafting, Presentations.AI can be tested.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best default for AI presentations:** Gamma.
- **Prompt, notes, or source material into a polished first draft:** Gamma.
- **Decks plus docs, websites, and shareable pages:** Gamma.
- **Custom domains, analytics, API, and workspace templates:** Gamma Pro or Ultra.
- **Lowest annual AI presentation experiment:** Presentations.AI Starter or Public beta.
- **Presentation-only workflow with Brand Sync:** Presentations.AI.
- **Teams that publish content beyond slides:** Gamma.

## Where Gamma Wins

Gamma wins on breadth and workflow depth. Its AI presentation page describes turning text into a made-to-impress presentation, remaking an entire deck style in one click, using flexible templates, publishing with analytics, and generating a working presentation quickly. The pricing page shows the same product direction: Gamma is not just slides. Even the Free tier includes presentations, docs, websites, social, images, imports, and exports. Pro and Ultra add custom branding, detailed analytics, advanced sharing, custom domains, API access, workspace templates, and broader model access.

That breadth matters because AI presentation buyers rarely stop at slides. A founder may need an investor deck, a one-page explainer, a public proposal, a website-style leave-behind, and a PDF. A consultant may need a deck and a client-facing written brief. A marketer may need a campaign deck, social assets, and a web page. Gamma makes those adjacent formats part of the same workflow.

Gamma also has clearer upgrade logic. Use Free to test. Use Plus when branding removal and more cards per prompt matter. Use Pro when custom branding, analytics, advanced sharing, custom domains, API access, and workspace templates become important. Use Ultra when the buyer wants much more usage and the most advanced model access Gamma offers.

## Where Presentations.AI Wins

Presentations.AI wins when the buyer wants a lower-cost annual presentation-only tool and does not need Gamma's broader content workspace. Its pricing page is attractive for testing: Starter is $0, Public beta is listed at $40/year for up to 10 members, Pro is listed at $198/year, and Enterprise is custom. That can be compelling for users who create occasional decks and do not want a recurring monthly subscription.

The product features are also relevant: AI-driven creation, personalized design, templates that adapt when content changes, PowerPoint compatibility, Brand Sync, sharing/access control, analytics, multi-device editing, and multilingual support. Those are enough for lightweight deck creation, class projects, early startup drafts, and internal brainstorms.

The weaker part is verifiability. The older page mentioned specific backend models, huge input capacity, and pricing that were not supported by current official sources. AiPedia removed those claims. Presentations.AI may be a useful slide generator, but the decision should be based on visible features, live pricing, and hands-on output testing.

## Pricing And Plan Guidance

Start with **Gamma Free** if you are testing prompt-to-deck workflows. Move to **Gamma Plus** if you create decks regularly and want more cards per prompt plus branding removal. Move to **Gamma Pro** if you need custom branding, fonts, analytics, advanced sharing, custom domains, API access, and workspace templates. Use **Gamma Ultra** when much higher usage and the most advanced Gamma model access matter.

Start with **Presentations.AI Starter** for basic testing. Consider **Public beta** if the $40/year offer is still live and you want a low-cost annual tool for up to 10 members. Use **Pro** if you need additional credits, Pro templates, custom fonts/colors, analytics, PDF export, and advanced features. Use Enterprise for custom admin, compliance, templates, credits, and support needs.

Do not use exact price as the only decision. Gamma is stronger when it replaces multiple content workflows. Presentations.AI is stronger when it stays a cheap presentation-only draft tool.

## What To Avoid

- **Do not buy Presentations.AI because of unverified backend model claims.** AiPedia could not verify older model-capacity claims from official sources.
- **Do not buy Gamma if you only need one cheap annual deck draft.** Presentations.AI may be enough for low-frequency use.
- **Do not buy Presentations.AI if you need docs, websites, API access, advanced sharing, or custom domains.** Gamma's official pricing page supports those workflows more clearly.
- **Do not ignore export and publishing.** Test PDF, PPTX, Google Slides export, link sharing, mobile rendering, and analytics before committing.
- **Do not treat AI output as finished strategy.** Both tools still need human editing for audience, proof, narrative, and call to action.

## Bottom Line

Gamma is the better AI presentation choice for most buyers because it supports the full path from prompt to deck, document, website, shareable page, analytics, export, custom domain, and API-enabled workflow. Presentations.AI is a useful cheaper annual experiment for presentation-only drafts, but it is not as strong as a broader content system.

If presentations are part of a larger content workflow, start with Gamma. If you only want a low-cost slide draft tool, test Presentations.AI.

## FAQ

**Is Gamma better than Presentations.AI?**

Gamma is better for most buyers because it supports presentations, documents, websites, social content, graphics, exports, analytics, custom domains, API access, and workspace templates. Presentations.AI is better for low-cost annual presentation-only drafting.

**Which is cheaper?**

Presentations.AI has a lower annual entry point if its Public beta offer remains live. Gamma has a Free tier and paid Plus, Pro, and Ultra tiers; check Gamma's live pricing page during checkout because exact pricing display can vary.

**Which plan should most people choose?**

Most Gamma buyers should start with Free or Plus, then upgrade to Pro when custom branding, analytics, advanced sharing, custom domains, API access, or workspace templates matter. Presentations.AI buyers should start with Starter or Public beta.

**Can I use both?**

Yes, but most teams should choose one primary system. Gamma is more likely to become the long-term workspace; Presentations.AI is more likely to be a lightweight draft tool.
