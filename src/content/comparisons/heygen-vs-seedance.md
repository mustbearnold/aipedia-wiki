# HeyGen vs Seedance 3.1

[HeyGen](../tools/heygen.md) and [Seedance 3.1](../tools/seedance.md) compete in AI video generation as of April 2026. This comparison uses data from official sites and recent reviews to cover flagship versions, pricing, and use cases.

## Quick Answer

HeyGen leads for avatar-based business videos with templates; Seedance 3.1 excels in cinematic text-to-video generation. Choice depends on whether you need talking heads or creative scenes.

## Key Differences

HeyGen focuses on realistic digital humans from scripts, with strengths in lip-sync accuracy (95%+ match rate) and multi-language support, making it suited for sales, training, and customer service videos. Seedance 3.1 prioritizes text-to-video for artistic outputs, supporting styles like realism or anime with physics-based motion, though clip lengths max at 10 seconds without editing. HeyGen's pricing scales by credits (e.g., 15 credits/minute on Creator), while Seedance uses generation limits (100/month on Pro). Both offer free tiers, but HeyGen's suits quick tests, Seedance's limits to watermarked previews.

## Who should choose HeyGen

Teams producing scripted talking-head content, such as marketing or e-learning, benefit from its avatar library and collaboration tools. Solo creators save time with one-click templates over manual prompting.

## Who should choose Seedance 3.1

Filmmakers and advertisers needing dynamic scenes from descriptions prefer its motion control and style variety. Developers integrating video gen into apps value the API and training options.

## Bottom Line

Pick HeyGen for efficient avatar videos in business settings; its templates and integrations reduce production time. Select Seedance 3.1 for higher creative control in short-form cinematic work, especially with custom prompts. Test free tiers to match your needs.
