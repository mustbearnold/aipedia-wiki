# Cartesia vs ElevenLabs

[Cartesia](/tools/cartesia/) and [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/) both generate speech, but they are not interchangeable. Cartesia is the specialist for low-latency speech inside live products. ElevenLabs is the broader voice platform for narration, dubbing, voice cloning, Studio workflows, Scribe speech-to-text, sound effects, music, Image to Video, and ElevenAgents.

## Quick Answer

Choose Cartesia when the hard requirement is real-time spoken response in a voice agent, IVR, game, or app. Choose ElevenLabs for most creator, publisher, marketing, localization, audiobook, voice-cloning, and general high-quality TTS workflows.

## Where Cartesia Wins

- **Latency-first buying.** Cartesia should be first in the test stack when a human is waiting for the next spoken response.
- **Voice-agent plumbing.** Sonic TTS, Ink-Whisper STT, and Line agent orchestration are built around live voice products.
- **Developer deployment.** Cartesia makes sense for teams embedding speech in their own app, call flow, or real-time product.
- **Traffic modeling.** The pricing page exposes agent minutes, TTS credit consumption, professional voice cloning, and phone-number minute costs that teams can model directly.
- **Narrow focus.** It is easier to evaluate if the only question is whether the live conversation feels fast and stable.

## Where ElevenLabs Wins

- **Finished speech quality.** Eleven v3 remains the stronger default for expressive narration, audiobooks, trailers, and character voiceovers.
- **Creator platform breadth.** ElevenLabs covers voice cloning, dubbing, Studio, Scribe STT, sound effects, music, Image to Video, and ElevenAgents in one account.
- **Non-developer workflow.** Teams can produce content in the UI without building a custom voice stack first.
- **Commercial creator path.** Starter unlocks commercial rights; Creator is the more natural landing tier for frequent YouTube and podcast work.
- **Voice library and localization.** It is usually the safer first test when voice variety, language coverage, dubbing, and publishing workflow matter more than the lowest possible latency.

## Key Difference

Cartesia is the sharper tool for live voice output. ElevenLabs is the broader platform for produced voice assets and a wider audio workflow.

If the user hears the voice in a live back-and-forth exchange, run Cartesia in a real call flow. If the user hears a polished asset after scripting, review, dubbing, or publishing, start with ElevenLabs.

## Who Should Choose Cartesia

Choose Cartesia if you are building a voice agent, phone automation flow, conversational product, game dialogue layer, or other experience where a slow response damages trust.

## Who Should Choose ElevenLabs

Choose ElevenLabs if you need narration, dubbing, voice cloning, creator tooling, audiobooks, Studio workflows, Scribe STT, sound effects, music, or a voice workflow non-developers can operate.

## Bottom Line

Cartesia is the real-time voice infrastructure pick. ElevenLabs is the broader AI audio platform pick. Many teams can use ElevenLabs for produced audio and Cartesia only where live latency is the deciding constraint.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**
It depends on characters, credits, generated seconds, agent minutes, concurrency, and whether the workload is live-agent traffic or produced content. Do not compare the plan prices alone.

**Which has better voice quality?**
ElevenLabs is the safer default for polished narration and voice variety. Cartesia should be judged on real-time feel, responsiveness, and voice quality inside the actual interaction.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. A common split is Cartesia for live streams or call flows and ElevenLabs for produced voice assets, dubbing, Studio projects, or pre-recorded content.
