# Claude vs Gemini

[Claude](/tools/claude/) and [Gemini](/tools/gemini/) are both frontier assistants, but they serve different defaults. Claude is strongest for long-form writing, careful reasoning, Claude Code, enterprise controls, and large-document work. Gemini is strongest for Google Workspace, Android, long-context multimodal workflows, coding-agent access across Google developer tools, and bundled image/video capabilities.

## Quick Answer

Choose Claude if you want the better writing and reasoning partner, especially for long documents, codebase analysis, and terminal-native Claude Code work. Choose Gemini if your work lives in Google products or needs bundled multimodal output such as Nano Banana image generation and Veo video generation. Claude feels like a specialist for deep text and coding judgment; Gemini feels like the AI layer for Google users.

## Where Claude Wins

Claude wins when the work is text-heavy and judgment-heavy. Opus 4.7, a 1M token context window in Claude Code and the API, Claude Code, and Anthropic's more conservative assistant style make it strong for reports, strategy documents, code review, policy analysis, and long-form editing.

Claude is also easier to trust for users who want pushback. It tends to be less eager to simply validate a premise, which matters when the task is diagnosis, critique, or planning.

## Where Gemini Wins

Gemini wins when the user is already in Google. Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive, Chrome, Android, and Workspace integration turn Gemini into an ambient productivity layer rather than a separate assistant. It also has a broader native media story: Nano Banana for images and Veo for video are central product advantages.

Gemini is the better pick for multimodal research and production work that starts in Google files or ends in Google apps. Claude can analyze uploads, but Gemini can be closer to the user's actual workspace, search layer, storage bundle, and media stack.

## Pricing and Limits

Claude has a free tier, Pro at $20/mo or $17/mo annually, Max from $100/mo, Team seats from $25/mo or $20/mo annually, and Enterprise at $20/seat plus usage at API rates. Opus 4.7 API pricing starts at $5 per 1M input tokens and $25 per 1M output tokens, with prompt caching, batch discounts, and US-only inference at 1.1x.

Google AI plans start with Free, then Google AI Plus from $7.99/mo, Google AI Pro at $19.99/mo in the US, and Google AI Ultra at $249.99/mo. AI Pro bundles 5TB of storage, higher access to Gemini 3.1 Pro, Deep Research, Gemini in Google apps, higher developer-tool limits, image/music/video generation access, and Google Home Premium Standard. Ultra adds 30TB storage, the highest access limits, Deep Think, Gemini Agent in the US/English, YouTube Premium where available, and higher coding-agent limits.

For API buyers, Google's May 2026 pricing page lists Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview at $0.45 per 1M text/image/video input tokens, $0.90 for audio input, and $2.70 per 1M output tokens including thinking tokens. That makes Gemini's API price much lower than Claude Opus 4.7, but the buying decision is not only token cost: quality, context behavior, governance, review burden, and where the work already lives matter.

## Current Product Signals

Google's current signal is Gemini 3.1 Pro plus a wider multimodal and agent stack. The official model list shows Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview, Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, Nano Banana 2, Nano Banana Pro, Gemini 3.1 Flash Live, and Veo 3.1. Google AI Pro and Ultra also surface paid access to Workspace, Deep Research, Chrome auto browse, Antigravity, Jules, Gemini CLI, and Gemini Code Assist limits.

## Best Choice by User Type

Pick Claude for writers, analysts, lawyers, researchers, and developers who value Claude Code, careful critique, and high-trust long-form work.

Pick Gemini for Google Workspace power users, Android users, teams creating images or video, students or operators who value the storage bundle, and builders already using Google Cloud or Google developer tools.

Pick both if writing quality and Google-native workflows are both central. A common setup is Claude for final reasoning, writing, and code review, with Gemini for Google-file workflows, Deep Research, image/video generation, and Android/Workspace convenience.

## Bottom Line

Claude is the better deep-work assistant for writing, critique, and terminal-agent coding. Gemini is the better Google-native multimodal assistant and bundle. The choice depends less on benchmark charts and more on where your documents, code, storage, search, and media already live.

## Evaluation Notes

The decision usually comes down to document judgment versus ecosystem gravity. Claude is the cleaner pick when the work is a long memo, policy review, research synthesis, legal-style analysis, or codebase reasoning task. Gemini is the cleaner pick when the work already lives in Google and the assistant should touch email, docs, storage, Android, images, and video.

The first evaluation test is where the user's files live. If the important files are in Google Drive and the user collaborates in Docs or Sheets, Gemini's integration can beat a stronger standalone answer. If the important work is a long argument, complex edit, or careful critique, Claude often feels more useful even without Google's distribution.

The second test is media output. Claude can analyze images, but Gemini's native image and video stack changes the buying decision for creative and marketing teams. A writer may prefer Claude; a creator working across text, images, and video may prefer Gemini.

The third test is coding style. Claude Code gives Claude a distinct developer workflow, especially for terminal-native users. Gemini can still code, and Google now packages developer access through AI Studio, Gemini CLI, Code Assist, Jules, and Antigravity, but Claude's coding-agent story is more focused for senior engineers who want one terminal agent to reason through a repo.

The fourth test is procurement. Claude is simpler when the buyer wants one premium assistant with explicit enterprise controls. Gemini is simpler when Workspace, Google Cloud, identity, storage, documents, and data controls are already under Google.

## Common Mistakes

A common mistake is choosing Gemini only because it is bundled into Google services. Convenience matters, but output quality still matters for final work. Another mistake is choosing Claude for every Google-heavy team even when the real bottleneck is workflow integration.

Another mistake is comparing only subscription sticker prices. Claude Pro and Google AI Pro are close for individuals, but the bundles differ radically: Claude buys a focused assistant and Claude Code access; Gemini buys storage, Google-app access, media generation, Deep Research, and developer-tool limits.

The best trial is not a prompt contest. Give both tools the same week of real documents, emails, code tasks, and media needs, then compare saved time and review burden.

## Buying Checklist

Before deciding on Claude vs Gemini, answer four practical questions. First, where does the source context live today: documents, code, Google files, GitHub issues, X posts, or an API pipeline? Second, who reviews the output, and how costly is a mistake? Third, does the tool need to be used by one power user, a whole team, or non-technical colleagues? Fourth, will the work happen once in a chat, or repeatedly inside a workflow that needs logging, permissions, tests, and fallback behavior?

The best choice is usually obvious after those answers. A broader assistant wins when people need a shared place to think. A specialist wins when the workflow has a fixed surface, such as an editor, repository, social feed, or model API. Price matters, but only after the workflow fit is clear. A cheaper tool that adds review burden can cost more than it saves.

## FAQ

### Is Claude better than Gemini?

Claude is better for long-form writing, critique, codebase reasoning, and Claude Code. Gemini is better for Google Workspace, Android, native image/video, Google Search/Deep Research workflows, and users who want AI bundled with storage and Google apps.

### Is Gemini cheaper than Claude?

For API use, Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview is much cheaper than Claude Opus 4.7 on the current official rate cards. For consumer subscriptions, Claude Pro is $20/mo and Google AI Pro is $19.99/mo in the US, so the question is bundle value rather than sticker price.

### Which is better for coding?

Claude is the stronger pick for terminal-native agentic coding through Claude Code. Gemini is better if the team already uses Google AI Studio, Gemini CLI, Code Assist, Jules, Antigravity, or wants coding access bundled into Google AI plans.

### Which is better for images and video?

Gemini. Nano Banana covers image generation/editing, and Veo covers video generation. Claude can analyze images but does not have native image or video generation.

### Which should a Google Workspace team buy?

Start with Gemini if Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive, Chrome, Android, and Google Cloud are already the daily workspace. Add Claude for writing, critique, policy review, or code review where answer quality matters more than app integration.
