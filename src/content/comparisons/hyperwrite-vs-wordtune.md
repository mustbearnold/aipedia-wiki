# HyperWrite vs Wordtune

[HyperWrite](/tools/hyperwrite/) and [Wordtune](/tools/wordtune/) both sit in the browser, but they enter the writing process at different moments. HyperWrite is a drafting and assistant layer with TypeAhead, AI messages, citations with real-time info, custom personas, hundreds of AI tools, and a Personal Assistant browser agent. Wordtune is AI21 Labs' focused rewrite layer for improving text that already exists through rewriting, grammar, summaries, paraphrasing, fluency, and clarity suggestions.

## Quick Answer

Choose HyperWrite if the buyer needs help creating text, expanding ideas, drafting emails, composing in Chrome, and occasionally delegating supervised browser tasks.

Choose Wordtune if the buyer already writes the core message and mostly needs cheaper sentence rewrites, tone/clarity improvements, summaries, and voice-preserving polish.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best for blank-page drafting:** HyperWrite.
- **Best while-you-type autocomplete:** HyperWrite TypeAhead.
- **Best browser-agent add-on:** HyperWrite Personal Assistant.
- **Best low-cost rewrite layer:** Wordtune.
- **Best free occasional rewrite path:** Wordtune Basic.
- **Best for preserving the user's original intent:** Wordtune.
- **Best for research-adjacent drafting:** HyperWrite, because Premium includes citations with real-time info.
- **Best for simple final-pass sentence alternatives:** Wordtune.

The old comparison was too generic. The current decision is **drafting assistant** versus **rewrite assistant**.

## Where HyperWrite Wins

- It is better when the buyer has a topic, prompt, or rough idea but not a finished paragraph.
- TypeAhead reduces the friction of writing inside Gmail, Docs, LinkedIn, and other browser fields.
- Citations with real-time info help draft lightweight research-backed text faster than a rewrite-only tool.
- Custom personas help preserve a recurring professional style across prompts.
- Personal Assistant gives a supervised browser-task lane that Wordtune does not offer.

## Where Wordtune Wins

- It is cheaper for users who mainly need rewrites and summaries.
- It stays close to the user's original meaning instead of pushing a broad generated draft.
- Basic gives occasional users a free daily rewrite/suggestion path.
- Unlimited removes caps at a low annual price compared with most writing assistants.
- It is simpler for final-pass editing: highlight text, choose a better version, move on.

## Buying Recommendation

Use HyperWrite if the buyer wants AI involved before the sentence exists. Premium is the normal daily tier; Ultra is for users who will burn through AI messages or want first access to writing and agent experiments.

Use Wordtune if the buyer already writes well enough and wants more choices, cleaner phrasing, summaries, and low-cost polish. Advanced works for light daily use. Unlimited is the practical plan for serious editing sessions because caps disappear.

Use both only when the workflow is draft-first, rewrite-second. HyperWrite can create options; Wordtune can narrow and polish them while keeping the original voice closer.

## Pricing Snapshot

- **HyperWrite Free:** limited monthly credits on basic AI.

- **HyperWrite Ultra:** $44.99/month or $29/month billed annually; unlimited AI messages, 10 custom personas, first access to experimental features, and unlimited TypeAheads.
- **Wordtune Basic:** free, with daily rewrite/suggestion limits and three monthly summaries.
- **Wordtune Advanced:** $6.99/month or $4.89/month annual; more rewrites and summaries.
- **Wordtune Unlimited:** $9.99/month or $6.99/month annual; unlimited rewrites, suggestions, and summaries.
- **Wordtune regional caveat:** support docs say prices can vary based on region or promotions.

## Watch-Outs

- HyperWrite Premium has a 250 AI-message cap, so heavy drafting can push users toward Ultra.
- HyperWrite's Personal Assistant should not run unsupervised on private accounts, forms, purchases, or irreversible actions.
- Wordtune needs source text; it is not a full blank-page content generator.
- Wordtune can make a sentence smoother without making the underlying claim true.
- Neither tool replaces fact-checking, domain review, or final editorial judgment.

## FAQ

**Is HyperWrite better than Wordtune?**
For drafting, autocomplete, citations with real-time info, and browser-agent help, yes. For low-cost sentence rewrites and summaries, Wordtune is better.

**Which is cheaper?**
Wordtune is cheaper on the public individual plan ladder. HyperWrite costs more because it bundles broader drafting, TypeAhead, personas, citations, and agent features.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Use HyperWrite to draft or expand, then Wordtune to polish phrasing and preserve the intended voice.
