# HyperWrite vs Wordtune

[HyperWrite](../tools/hyperwrite.md) and [Wordtune](../tools/wordtune.md) are AI writing assistants that edit and generate text in 2026. HyperWrite uses GPT-5.3 as its flagship model with Unlimited plan at $19.99/month; Wordtune uses Claude Sonnet 4.6 with Premium plan at $12.99/month.

## Quick Answer

Wordtune edges out for most users due to lower cost and Claude Sonnet 4.6's strength in editing tasks. HyperWrite suits those needing broader generation features from GPT-5.3.

## At a Glance

| | HyperWrite | Wordtune |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | GPT-5.3 | Claude Sonnet 4.6 |
| **Price** | $19.99/mo Unlimited | $12.99/mo Premium |
| **Context Window** | 128K tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Content generation, brainstorming | Rewriting, editing in apps |

## Where HyperWrite Wins

- Supports more generation tools like TypeAhead and AutoWrite for full drafts.
- Integrates GPT-5.3 for versatile tasks beyond editing, including research summaries.
- Offers browser extension for quick inline suggestions across sites.
- Includes unlimited AI usage on paid plans without strict output caps.
- Provides templates for emails, blogs, and social posts.

## Where Wordtune Wins

- Lower entry price at $12.99/month for 1,000 rewrites daily.
- Claude Sonnet 4.6 excels in nuanced rewriting and style matching.
- Native integrations with Google Docs, Gmail, and Microsoft Word.
- Larger 1M token context for handling long documents.
- Spire feature for fact-checking edits in real-time.

## Key Differences

HyperWrite focuses on generating new content from prompts using GPT-5.3, which scores high on creative benchmarks but has a 128K token limit. Wordtune prioritizes refining existing text with Claude Sonnet 4.6, leveraging its 1M context for coherent edits on full articles. Pricing reflects this: HyperWrite's $19.99 Unlimited allows endless generations; Wordtune's $12.99 Premium caps at usage tiers but starts cheaper for editors.

## Who should choose HyperWrite

Writers starting from scratch pick HyperWrite for its generation tools and GPT-5.3 outputs.

## Who should choose Wordtune

Editors and teams in Docs or Word select Wordtune for affordable, context-aware rewrites via Claude Sonnet 4.6.

## Bottom Line

Choose Wordtune if rewriting is your main task; its pricing and model fit editing workflows best. Opt for HyperWrite for generation-heavy use where GPT-5.3 versatility matters more than cost. Both integrate well, so test free tiers first.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Wordtune at $12.99/month vs HyperWrite's $19.99/month.

**Which has better output quality?**  
Wordtune for editing due to Claude Sonnet 4.6; HyperWrite for generation with GPT-5.3.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, via browser extensions; no conflicts reported.
