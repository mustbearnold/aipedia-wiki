# Sudowrite vs Wordtune

[Sudowrite](../tools/sudowrite.md) and [Wordtune](../tools/wordtune.md) are AI writing assistants that target different stages of the writing process as of April 2026. Sudowrite focuses on fiction and creative generation from prompts, while Wordtune emphasizes real-time editing and refinement of existing text.

## Quick Answer

Sudowrite suits fiction writers needing story expansion and scene generation; Wordtune fits nonfiction and professional writers seeking sentence rewrites and clarity improvements. Choice depends on whether generation or editing is the priority.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Sudowrite | Wordtune |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | GPT-5.3 integration | Claude Sonnet 4.6 + GPT-5.3 |
| **Price** | $29/month (Professional) | $19.99/month (Premium) |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | 200K tokens input, unlimited output | 1M tokens context, real-time browser extension |
| **Best For** | Fiction, novels, screenplays | Emails, docs, productivity editing |

## Where Sudowrite Wins

- Generates full scenes, chapters, or plot arcs from short prompts using GPT-5.3 for creative flow.[1]
- Canvas feature maps story outlines and characters with AI suggestions for plot consistency.
- Handles long-form fiction without strict length limits, ideal for novel drafts.
- Offline mode available for core generation after initial sync.
- Strong in role-playing and genre-specific styles like fantasy or sci-fi.

## Where Wordtune Wins

- Real-time rewriting in browsers, Google Docs, and Gmail with one-click options for tone or length.[3]
- Spire integration uses Claude Sonnet 4.6 for nuanced edits in professional content.
- Tracks rewrite history and allows A/B testing of phrasing for emails and reports.
- Free tier includes 10 rewrites daily, sufficient for light use.
- Focuses on conciseness and clarity, reducing word count by up to 30% without losing meaning.

## Key Differences

Sudowrite operates as a dedicated app for starting and expanding creative writing projects, leveraging GPT-5.3 for zero-to-full-draft workflows; it excels where users need inspiration from blank pages but requires more setup for nonfiction. Wordtune functions as an overlay editor across apps, powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5.3 hybrids, prioritizing polish on existing text with minimal disruption; its browser extension makes it faster for daily productivity but less suited for original long-form creation.[1][3]

## Who should choose Sudowrite

Fiction authors drafting novels or scripts benefit from its generation tools. Users comfortable with a standalone app for immersive writing sessions find it matches their process.

## Who should choose Wordtune

Professionals editing emails, reports, or web content value its inline suggestions. Those integrated into Google Workspace or needing quick refinements across tools prefer its accessibility.

## Bottom Line

Neither tool dominates universally; Sudowrite leads for creative generation, Wordtune for editing efficiency. Test free tiers to match your primary task, as both use top models like GPT-5.3 and the Claude 4 series. Combine them for full workflows if budget allows.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Wordtune Premium at $19.99/month undercuts Sudowrite's $29/month Professional plan, though Sudowrite offers a $19/month tier with fewer credits.[3]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Sudowrite produces more coherent long fiction via GPT-5.3; Wordtune delivers precise, professional edits with Claude Sonnet 4.6 nuance.[1]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, export Sudowrite drafts to Google Docs for Wordtune polishing; no direct integration exists.
