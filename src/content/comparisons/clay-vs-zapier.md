# Clay vs Zapier

[Clay](../tools/clay.md) and [Zapier](../tools/zapier.md) are two options in the ai-automation category as of April 2026. Clay focuses on AI-enriched data workflows for sales and marketing teams, while Zapier provides no-code integrations across 7000+ apps.[https://www.clay.com/pricing](https://www.clay.com/pricing)[https://zapier.com/pricing](https://zapier.com/pricing)

## Quick Answer

Zapier suits broad app integrations and simple automations; Clay excels in AI-driven data enrichment and table-based workflows. Choose based on whether you need general connectivity or targeted lead research.

## Decision Snapshot
| | Clay | Zapier |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Clay 2.0 (AI tables with 1M context via Claude Sonnet 4.6 / Gemini 3.1 Pro) | Zapier Central (AI bots with GPT-5.3 integration) |

| **Best For** | Sales enrichment, AI research tables | Multi-app zaps, no-code bots |

## Where Clay Wins

- Handles AI data enrichment across 100+ sources in table format, ideal for building lead lists with custom research prompts.[https://www.clay.com/](https://www.clay.com/)
- Integrates frontier models like Claude Sonnet 4.6 (1M context) for multi-step reasoning on datasets, outperforming basic zaps in complex analysis.[1]
- Credits-based pricing scales with AI usage; users report 5x faster pipeline building vs manual tools.[https://www.clay.com/pricing](https://www.clay.com/pricing)
- Native AI agents for enrichment chains (e.g., scrape, summarize, score leads) without external coding.[https://www.clay.com/product](https://www.clay.com/product)
- Strong for teams needing collaborative tables with real-time AI updates.

## Where Zapier Wins

- Connects 7000+ apps with pre-built zaps, covering email, CRM, and databases without AI setup.[https://zapier.com/apps](https://zapier.com/apps)
- Zapier Central bots use GPT-5.3 for natural language automation across apps, simpler for non-technical users.[https://zapier.com/central](https://zapier.com/central)
- Task-based pricing starts free at 100 tasks/mo, cheaper for low-volume general automations.[https://zapier.com/pricing](https://zapier.com/pricing)
- Enterprise features like SOC 2 compliance and team sharing suit large orgs with compliance needs.
- Paths and filters enable conditional logic in zaps, reliable for event-driven workflows.

## Key Differences

Clay centers on AI-powered tables for data workflows, using large context models (e.g., Gemini 3.1 Pro at 2M tokens) to process and enrich rows with web data, summaries, or scores; pricing ties to AI credits (e.g., $59/mo for 10k).[1][https://www.clay.com/pricing](https://www.clay.com/pricing) Zapier emphasizes app-to-app zaps and AI bots via Zapier Central, with GPT-5.3 for chat-based actions; it scales on tasks (e.g., $49/mo for 2k) and supports more integrations but less depth in AI research.[https://zapier.com/pricing](https://zapier.com/pricing) Clay fits research-heavy use; Zapier handles routine syncing.

## Who should choose Clay

Sales and marketing teams building enriched pipelines from raw data. It combines table editing with AI agents for tasks like lead scoring or competitor analysis.

## Who should choose Zapier

Operations teams automating across apps like Slack, Google Sheets, and HubSpot. Its vast integrations and simple bots work for event triggers without data modeling.

## Bottom Line

Neither dominates all use cases; Zapier leads for integration volume, Clay for AI data work. Test free tiers: Clay for enrichment prototypes, Zapier for zap chains. Most users run both for complementary strengths.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Zapier Professional at $49/mo for 2k tasks undercuts Clay Starter ($59/mo for 10k credits) for light use, but Clay costs less per AI operation.[https://zapier.com/pricing](https://zapier.com/pricing)[https://www.clay.com/pricing](https://www.clay.com/pricing)

**Which has better output quality?**  
Clay, via Claude Sonnet 4.6 / Gemini 3.1 Pro, produces higher-quality enriched data and reasoning; Zapier suits reliable transfers over deep analysis.[1]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes; integrate via webhooks or APIs for hybrid workflows, e.g., Zapier feeds data into Clay tables.
