# Clay vs Zapier

[Clay](/tools/clay/) and [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) often meet inside sales and marketing operations, but they answer different questions. Clay asks: "Which accounts and contacts should we work, and what should we know about them?" Zapier asks: "Which apps should do what after an event happens?"

## Quick Answer

Use **Clay** when the workflow depends on enriched prospect data, account research, waterfalls, Claygent, and GTM-specific logic. Use **Zapier** when non-technical teams need broad SaaS automation across 9,000+ apps, Zaps, Tables, Forms, MCP, Copilot, Agents, Chatbots, and business workflows. Clay is a specialist revenue-data machine. Zapier is a general automation platform.

## Decision Snapshot

| | Clay | Zapier |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary job** | Prospect/account enrichment and AI research | No-code SaaS automation and AI orchestration |
| **Best buyer** | RevOps, GTM engineer, outbound agency, ABM team | Non-technical operator, marketing ops, support ops, small business |
| **Current pricing signal** | Free; Launch/Growth scale by actions and data credits; Enterprise custom | Free; Professional from $19.99/month annually; Team from $69/month annually; Enterprise custom |
| **AI surface** | Claygent, AI columns, fixed or token-based model use, BYO API keys | Zapier AI, Copilot, Agents, MCP, SDK, Chatbots, AI tools across 9,000+ apps |
| **Main strength** | Better GTM data before action | Fastest path to connecting business apps |
| **Main risk** | Enrichment/action costs and workflow QA | Task billing and automation sprawl |

## Where Clay Wins

Clay wins when the business problem is lead quality. Its current pricing and integrations pages frame the product around 150+ data providers, Claygent research, data credits, actions, BYO API keys, and integrations into GTM systems. That is the right surface when outbound performance depends on the quality of the list and the specificity of each account insight.

Choose Clay when:

- sales data is incomplete or stale
- the team needs multi-provider waterfalls
- personalized outbound depends on real account triggers
- the output is a qualified lead/account table
- a GTM operator can own prompts, sources, QA, and credit usage

Zapier can connect the apps around this workflow, but it does not replace the data-enrichment logic itself.

## Where Zapier Wins

Zapier wins when the business problem is automation breadth. Its current AI page positions Zapier as an AI orchestration stack where teams connect AI to workflows, build agents, deploy chatbots, use Copilot, and plug MCP/SDK surfaces into thousands of apps. Its apps directory still publishes 9,000+ integrations, which is the main moat.

Choose Zapier when:

- non-technical operators need workflows live quickly
- long-tail SaaS app coverage matters
- the job is moving data between apps after an event
- AI agents need safe app actions through MCP or Zapier's platform
- the buyer values ease over cost efficiency at high volume

The watch-out is task billing. A multi-step Zap or agent with several tool calls can consume many tasks per run, so Zapier can be cheap for simple workflows and expensive for complex/high-frequency ones.

## Key Differences

Clay is a **pre-action intelligence layer**. Zapier is an **action-routing layer**.

In Clay, the workflow enriches and researches rows so a seller knows what to do. In Zapier, the workflow moves information between apps or lets an AI agent take controlled actions. Clay can prepare a better lead; Zapier can notify the rep, update HubSpot, create a task, post to Slack, or connect an AI assistant to an approved action.

The highest-quality GTM stack often uses both: Clay makes the data useful, then Zapier moves approved outputs into the tools where work happens.

## Who Should Choose Clay

Choose Clay if the campaign fails without better data. It is the better fit for high-value outbound, account-based marketing, custom prospect research, and revenue teams that can maintain GTM workflows.

## Who Should Choose Zapier

Choose Zapier if the company needs general business automation. It is the better fit for connecting forms, CRMs, spreadsheets, inboxes, help desks, calendars, Slack, and AI tools without asking engineering to build every integration.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. A common stack:

1. Clay enriches accounts and contacts.
2. A human or rule approves rows.
3. Zapier moves approved leads into CRM, Slack, project tools, or customer workflows.
4. Zapier MCP/Agents can expose approved actions to AI surfaces.

That stack is powerful, but it needs guardrails. Do not let AI-generated Clay outputs trigger irreversible Zapier actions without review.

## Bottom Line

Pick **Clay** for GTM enrichment. Pick **Zapier** for broad no-code automation. Use both only when enriched revenue data needs to move through many SaaS systems and the team can govern task spend and action safety.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Zapier has a cheaper self-serve entry point for simple automation. Clay can create more revenue value when better data improves outbound performance. Neither is automatically cheaper because Clay bills around actions/data credits and Zapier bills around tasks.

**Does Zapier replace Clay?**
No. Zapier can route data and call APIs, but it is not a dedicated GTM enrichment workspace with Claygent and provider waterfalls.

**Does Clay replace Zapier?**
No. Clay can integrate with GTM tools, but Zapier is broader for app-to-app workflows, MCP, Agents, and non-technical automation.

**Best first purchase?**
Buy Zapier first for general automation. Buy Clay first when lead/account data quality is the specific bottleneck.
