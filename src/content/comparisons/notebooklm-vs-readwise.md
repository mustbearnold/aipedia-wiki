# Google NotebookLM vs Readwise Reader

[Google NotebookLM](../tools/notebooklm.md) and [Readwise Reader](../tools/readwise.md) handle note-taking and document analysis in the ai-notes category as of April 2026. NotebookLM focuses on querying personal documents with AI-generated audio overviews; Readwise Reader organizes reading across web, PDFs, and ebooks with highlights and spaced repetition.[4][6]

## Quick Answer

NotebookLM suits users needing quick AI insights from uploaded documents, like research summaries or study guides. Readwise Reader fits better for ongoing reading management with highlight syncing and review tools.

## At a Glance

| | Google NotebookLM | Readwise Reader |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | NotebookLM with Gemini 3.1 Pro | Reader with Claude Sonnet 4.6 integration |
| **Price** | Free | $8/month or $96/year |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | 2M tokens; audio overviews, study guides | 1M tokens; highlight export, spaced repetition |
| **Best For** | Document Q&A, audio summaries | Reading organization, highlight review |

## Where Google NotebookLM Wins

- Processes large document sets into audio podcasts or FAQs for hands-free review.[4]
- Integrates Gemini 3.1 Pro for multimodal input, handling text, images, and video transcripts up to 2M tokens.[2]
- Free access with Google account; no subscription needed for core features.[4]
- Generates structured outputs like timelines or briefings from notebooks.[6]
- Tight Google Workspace fit for users in Docs or Drive.[3]

## Where Readwise Reader Wins

- Syncs highlights from Kindle, articles, PDFs, and Twitter for centralized review.[web:1]
- Spaced repetition schedules resurface key notes over time.[web:1]
- Exports to Obsidian, Notion, or Evernote for custom workflows.[web:1]
- Handles ongoing reading pipelines with RSS and web clipping.[web:1]
- Claude Sonnet 4.6 powers analysis at $8/month, balancing cost and 1M context.[1][web:1]

## Key Differences

NotebookLM centers on one-off analysis of uploaded sources, producing audio or text summaries via Gemini 3.1 Pro's 2M token window; it lacks persistent highlight storage.[2][4] Readwise Reader builds a lasting knowledge base from reads, using spaced repetition and exports, powered by Claude Sonnet 4.6's 1M context for reflection prompts.[1][web:1] NotebookLM stays free but Google-tied; Reader charges $8/month for cross-platform reading tools.[web:1]

## Who should choose Google NotebookLM

Users analyzing specific documents, like students or researchers, benefit from free Gemini 3.1 Pro queries and audio outputs.

## Who should choose Readwise Reader

Readers consuming articles, books, and feeds daily gain from highlight syncing and review scheduling at $8/month.

## Bottom Line

Choose NotebookLM for free, document-focused AI queries with audio. Pick Readwise Reader for $8/month reading organization and retention. Many combine both: NotebookLM for deep dives, Reader for daily highlights.
