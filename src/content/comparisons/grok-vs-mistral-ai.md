# Grok vs Mistral AI

[Grok](/tools/grok/) and [Mistral AI](/tools/mistral-ai/) are both general-purpose AI assistants, but they reflect different philosophies in June 2026. Grok is xAI's US assistant built around real-time X and web data, with native image and video generation (Imagine), voice, and a coding agent. Mistral AI is a French open-weight lab: its Vibe assistant runs on Apache 2.0 models you can self-host, with an EU data-sovereignty posture and a privacy-first stance.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Grok** if you want real-time signal from X, native image and video generation, and voice in one assistant. Choose **Mistral AI** if you value open weights, EU data residency, privacy controls, and cost-efficient models you can run yourself.

The split is **real-time multimodal versus open and sovereign**. Grok wins on live data and built-in media. Mistral wins on openness, data control, and price.

## Where Grok Wins

- **Real-time X data.** Native access to live posts and discussion on X is unique to Grok among these two.
- **Built-in media.** Grok Imagine generates images and video, and Grok has a native voice mode.
- **Frontier model option.** Grok 4.3 is xAI's most capable model on the top tiers.
- **X ecosystem.** Tight integration with X is an advantage for users who live there.
- **Coding surface.** Grok Build provides an agentic coding workflow alongside the assistant.

## Where Mistral AI Wins

- **Open weights.** Apache 2.0 Mistral 3 models (Large 3, plus Ministral edge models) can be self-hosted, fine-tuned, and deployed on your own infrastructure.
- **EU data sovereignty.** A French lab with an explicit data-sovereignty posture, which matters for European and regulated buyers.
- **Privacy controls.** Mistral Pro includes a no-telemetry privacy toggle.
- **Cost efficiency.** Pro is $14.99/month, and the open models are positioned as far cheaper than US frontier APIs.
- **Developer platform.** Studio APIs, Vibe for Code, and a search toolkit support building on Mistral directly.

## Plan Guidance

Grok is subscription-led. The **Free** plan is tightly limited, **SuperGrok Lite at $10/month** is the entry paid tier, **SuperGrok at $30/month** is the standard plan, and **SuperGrok Heavy at $300/month** unlocks the fullest Grok 4.3 access and highest limits. Developers use the **API** (Grok 4.3 around $1.25/$2.50 per million tokens) with monthly free credits.

Mistral's **Vibe Free** plan gives real access to frontier models, image generation, and a code interpreter, soft-capped around 25 messages per day. **Pro at $14.99/month** adds the privacy toggle and a student discount, **Team at $24.99/user/month** covers collaboration, and **Enterprise** is custom. Studio API usage is billed per model, and the open weights are a separate self-hosting path (review the license for commercial deployment).

Do not buy either on the headline price alone. Grok's fullest model access sits on the expensive Heavy tier and has been rolling out in stages. Mistral's biggest advantage, open weights, only pays off if you actually self-host or build on the API; for pure chat the Pro tier is the simple path.

## Watch-Outs

Grok's real-time strength is specifically X data, which is powerful for social signal but is not neutral, broadly-sourced research, and its fullest model access sits behind the expensive Heavy tier. Mistral is text-first with a privacy and openness story; its consumer free tier is modestly capped (around 25 messages per day), and the open-weights advantage only matters if you self-host or build on the API rather than just chatting.

Also watch the cost and control tradeoff. Grok is a hosted US service tied to X. Mistral gives more control (self-hosting, EU residency, privacy toggle) but you take on more responsibility when you run the open weights yourself.

## Who Should Choose Grok

Choose Grok if you want real-time signal from X, native image and video generation and voice in one assistant, an agentic coding surface, or tight integration with the X ecosystem.

## Who Should Choose Mistral AI

Choose Mistral AI if you value open weights you can self-host, EU data residency and sovereignty, privacy controls, or cost-efficient models for building and scaling on your own terms.

## Bottom Line

Pick Grok for real-time X data and built-in multimodal generation in a hosted US assistant. Pick Mistral AI for openness, data sovereignty, privacy, and cost efficiency, especially if you self-host or build on the API. They serve different priorities more than they compete head-to-head on the same axis.

## FAQ

**Which is more open?**

Mistral AI. Its Mistral 3 models ship under Apache 2.0 and can be self-hosted and fine-tuned. Grok is a proprietary hosted service.

**Which is better for real-time information?**

Grok, for live X (social) data specifically. Mistral is not a real-time social-search assistant.

**Which is better for EU data residency?**

Mistral AI. As a French lab with a data-sovereignty posture, it is the stronger fit for European and regulated buyers.

**Which is cheaper?**

Mistral Pro ($14.99/month) is cheaper than Grok's standard SuperGrok tier, and Mistral's open models can be cheaper still at scale. Both have free tiers; Grok's entry paid tier is SuperGrok Lite at $10/month.
