# Elicit vs Perplexity

[Elicit](../tools/elicit.md) focuses on academic literature search and analysis. [Perplexity](../tools/perplexity.md) provides general research with web search and citations. This comparison uses flagship versions and pricing as of April 2026.

## Quick Answer

Perplexity suits broader research needs with real-time web access. Elicit excels for academic paper workflows.

## At a Glance

| | Elicit | Perplexity |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Elicit 3.0 (Claude Sonnet 4.6 backend) | Perplexity Pro (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Opus 4.7) |
| **Price** | Free / Plus $12/month / Team $25/user/month | Free / Pro $20/month / Enterprise custom |
| **Context Window** | 1M tokens | 2M tokens (Gemini 3.1 Pro) |
| **Best For** | Academic literature review | General research, fact-checking |

## Where Elicit Wins

- Extracts structured data like summaries, tables from PDFs of academic papers.
- Filters searches to peer-reviewed sources only.
- Tracks citations and generates bibliographies automatically.
- Handles bulk uploads of research papers for theme analysis.
- Integrates with tools like Zotero for reference management.

## Where Perplexity Wins

- Searches live web for current events and non-academic info.
- Synthesizes answers from multiple sources with inline citations.
- Supports multimodal inputs like images, videos via Gemini 3.1 Pro.
- Faster responses for quick queries due to optimized search.
- Enterprise version offers team collaboration and API access.

## Key Differences

Elicit limits searches to academic databases, making it precise for systematic reviews but missing recent preprints or web news. Perplexity indexes the full web, including news and blogs, but may include lower-quality sources without academic focus. Elicit pricing starts lower at $12/month for Plus, while Perplexity Pro at $20/month adds more models like Gemini 3.1 Pro with 2M token context. Output in Elicit emphasizes tables and extraction; Perplexity prioritizes conversational synthesis.

## Who should choose Elicit

Researchers conducting literature reviews or meta-analyses. Students and academics needing paper summaries and citation tracking.

## Who should choose Perplexity

Professionals requiring up-to-date info across web sources. Teams doing market research or fact-checking with shared access.

## Bottom Line

Choose Elicit for academic depth; Perplexity for breadth and speed. Many users combine both: Elicit for papers, Perplexity for context.

## FAQ

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, they complement each other. Use Elicit for papers, Perplexity for web synthesis.

**Which is cheaper?**  
Elicit Plus at $12/month undercuts Perplexity Pro at $20/month. Free tiers differ in search scope.

**Which one should I pick first?**  
Start with Perplexity for general use; switch to Elicit if focused on academic work.
