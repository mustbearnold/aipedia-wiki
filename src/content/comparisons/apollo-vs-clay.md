# Apollo.io vs Clay

[Apollo.io](../tools/apollo.md) and [Clay](../tools/clay.md) both sit in the modern GTM stack, but they solve different buying jobs. Apollo is the all-in-one B2B database plus sales-engagement system: prospect search, enrichment, sequencing, dialer, CRM sync, and AI-assisted outreach in one subscription. Clay is the spreadsheet-like GTM engineering layer: waterfall data providers, run AI account research, normalize messy lists, and push signal-rich records into another system.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Apollo.io** if your team wants one vendor for B2B contact discovery, enrichment, sequences, calling, and revenue-team workflow. Choose **Clay** if your team already has a sales-engagement motion and needs a flexible enrichment/research workspace that can combine many data sources and custom AI steps.

## Decision Snapshot

| | Apollo.io | Clay |
|---|---|---|
| **Best buyer** | SDR, AE, RevOps, and founder-led sales teams that need a complete outbound platform | Growth, RevOps, agency, and data-heavy GTM teams building custom enrichment pipelines |
| **Core job** | Find prospects, enrich them, sequence them, call them, and sync activity to CRM | Build tables that waterfall providers, call APIs, run AI research, and output clean lists |
| **June 2026 pricing anchor** | Free tier; paid plans scale up to $149/user/month before add-ons and consumption | Free; Launch from $167/mo annual or $185/mo monthly; Growth from $446/mo annual or $495/mo monthly; Enterprise custom |
| **Cost variable to model** | Seats, export credits, enrichment credits, engagement features, and CRM workflow needs | Actions, data credits, enrichment providers, AI usage, and workspace volume |
| **Trust posture** | Stronger if you want one accountable system for prospect data and engagement | Stronger if you need inspectable, source-by-source enrichment logic |
| **Avoid if** | You need unusual data workflows, custom research chains, or many non-sales data sources | You need an out-of-the-box dialer, native sequencer, and full sales-engagement UI |

## Where Apollo.io Wins

- **Single-stack outbound.** Apollo combines prospect search, enrichment, sequences, dialer, meeting workflows, CRM sync, and analytics. Clay usually needs another tool to send and manage outreach.
- **Lower operational friction.** A sales team can start with Apollo's own database and engagement workflows instead of designing a table, provider waterfall, and export path.
- **Seat-based buying is easier to forecast.** Apollo still has credit and export variables, but the purchase starts from familiar per-user sales-software plans.
- **Better for managers.** Apollo's value is strongest when managers need activity visibility, campaign analytics, permissions, and CRM hygiene in one place.
- **Cleaner handoff to reps.** SDRs live in a sales UI, not a data-workbench UI.

## Where Clay Wins

- **Custom enrichment depth.** Clay is the better choice when the win condition is "build the exact dataset we need," not "send more sequences from a default database."
- **Waterfalling and source control.** Teams can chain multiple providers, score confidence, and decide when to pay for expensive data.
- **AI research workflows.** Clay is stronger for account briefs, persona inference, trigger research, and structured research that feeds another GTM system.
- **Agency and RevOps fit.** If every client or segment needs a different enrichment recipe, Clay's table model is more reusable than Apollo's built-in workflow.
- **Less lock-in to one database.** Clay can pull from many sources and internal data instead of making Apollo's database the center of truth.

## Pricing reality check

Apollo is usually cheaper to pilot for a rep or small team because the entry plan is per user and includes the core sales workflow. Clay becomes rational when enrichment quality, provider choice, and repeatable research logic are worth the higher workspace spend. Do not compare only headline plan prices: Apollo costs can rise with credits and seats; Clay costs can rise with actions, data credits, and premium providers.

## Who should choose Apollo.io

Pick Apollo if your immediate problem is pipeline creation: find accounts, get verified contacts, enroll prospects, manage calls, and keep Salesforce or HubSpot updated. It is the safer default for sales teams that do not have a dedicated RevOps builder maintaining enrichment workflows.

## Who should choose Clay

Pick Clay if your immediate problem is data quality or research depth: account segmentation, multi-provider enrichment, AI-generated account notes, custom signals, territory planning, or agency-scale list building. It is the better workbench when the outbound system is already chosen.

## Bottom Line

Apollo is the GTM execution platform. Clay is the GTM data and research workbench. Many mature teams use both: Clay to produce higher-confidence, better-segmented lists; Apollo to run outbound and manage sales activity. If you can only buy one, choose Apollo for sales execution and Clay for custom enrichment quality.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Apollo is cheaper for a simple sales team pilot. Clay's Launch and Growth plans cost more, but the comparison changes if Clay replaces multiple enrichment vendors or saves RevOps labor.

**Which has better data?**
Apollo is better when you want a ready B2B contact database inside a sales tool. Clay is better when you want to combine, test, and audit multiple data sources.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes. A common stack is Clay for enrichment and account research, then Apollo for sequencing, calling, and rep workflow.
