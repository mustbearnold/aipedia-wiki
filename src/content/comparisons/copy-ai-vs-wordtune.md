# Copy.ai vs Wordtune

[Copy.ai](../tools/copy-ai.md) and [Wordtune](../tools/wordtune.md) are two AI writing tools available as of April 2026. Copy.ai focuses on generating marketing copy from prompts, while Wordtune specializes in editing and rephrasing existing text.

## Quick Answer

Wordtune suits users who edit drafts for clarity and tone; Copy.ai fits teams generating copy from scratch. Choice depends on whether rewriting or creating content is the main task.

## At a Glance

| | Copy.ai | Wordtune |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | GPT-5.3 integration | Claude Sonnet 4.6 |
| **Price** | Free; Pro $49/mo; Team $249/mo | Free; Premium $9.99/mo; Teams $14.99/user/mo |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | 128K tokens input; unlimited generations on Pro | 1M tokens; real-time browser/editor integration |
| **Best For** | Marketing copy generation | Text rewriting and polishing |

## Where Copy.ai Wins

- Generates full articles, ads, and emails from short prompts using GPT-5.3 for varied output styles [1].
- Team plans include collaboration features like shared workspaces and brand voice training.
- Higher output volume on paid tiers supports bulk content needs for agencies.
- Templates cover 90+ use cases from product descriptions to social posts.

## Where Wordtune Wins

- Edits text inline across browsers, Google Docs, and Gmail for direct workflow integration [2].
- Claude Sonnet 4.6 provides precise rewrites with tone matching and fact-checking suggestions.
- Lower pricing starts at $9.99/mo for premium features like unlimited rewrites.
- Focuses on shortening or expanding sentences while preserving original meaning.

## Key Differences

Copy.ai acts as a content generator, taking ideas and producing complete pieces powered by GPT-5.3 Codex, which excels in creative tasks but requires prompt engineering for consistency [1]. Wordtune functions as an editor, refining user-written text with Claude Sonnet 4.6's 1M token context for handling long documents and maintaining nuance [1][2]. Copy.ai pricing scales for teams at $249/mo, while Wordtune offers individual plans from $9.99/mo, making it more accessible for solo users.

## Who should choose Copy.ai

Copy.ai works for marketers and agencies needing rapid generation of sales copy, blog posts, or ad variations. Its template library speeds up repetitive tasks.

## Who should choose Wordtune

Wordtune fits writers, professionals, and students who draft content and need quick refinements for clarity, brevity, or style. Browser extensions enable use in daily apps.

## Bottom Line

Both tools handle writing tasks, but Copy.ai leads for creation volume, and Wordtune excels in editing precision. Test free tiers to match your primary need, as integration differs by workflow.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Wordtune Premium at $9.99/mo undercuts Copy.ai Pro at $49/mo for individual use; team pricing favors Copy.ai for groups over 10 users.

**Which has better output quality?**  
Wordtune's Claude Sonnet 4.6 scores higher on nuanced rewriting; Copy.ai's GPT-5.3 generates more creative variety [1].

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, combine Copy.ai for drafts and Wordtune for polishing in sequence.
