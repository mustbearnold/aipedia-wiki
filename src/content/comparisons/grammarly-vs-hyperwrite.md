# Grammarly vs HyperWrite

[Grammarly](../tools/grammarly.md) and [HyperWrite](../tools/hyperwrite.md) are two AI writing tools available in April 2026. Grammarly focuses on grammar checks, style suggestions, and tone adjustments across apps. HyperWrite emphasizes full content generation, research assistance, and workflow automation for longer writing tasks.

## Quick Answer

Grammarly suits quick edits and real-time proofreading in daily writing. HyperWrite fits users needing AI-generated drafts, outlines, and research summaries.

## At a Glance

| | Grammarly | HyperWrite |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Grammarly Premium with GPT-5.3 integration | HyperWrite Ultimate with Claude Opus 4.7 |
| **Price** | Free; Premium $12/month; Business $15/user/month | Free; Premium $19.99/month; Ultra $44.99/month |
| **Context Window** | 200K tokens | 1M tokens |
| **Best For** | Editing existing text | Generating new content |

## Where Grammarly Wins

- Real-time suggestions work in browsers, apps, and desktop for instant grammar, clarity, and tone fixes.
- Integrates with 500K+ apps including Google Docs, Microsoft Word, and email clients.
- Business plans include team style guides and plagiarism detection.
- Lower entry price for basic premium features.
- Reliable for non-native English speakers on sentence-level improvements.

## Where HyperWrite Wins

- Generates full articles, emails, and outlines from prompts using Claude Opus 4.7.
- TypeAhead predicts and auto-completes sentences in real time across apps.
- Research mode pulls and summarizes web data into writing.
- Larger 1M token context handles long documents and complex projects.
- Higher output limits on premium tiers for heavy users.

## Key Differences

Grammarly acts as an editor that refines user-written text with suggestions for grammar, style, and engagement; it processes input in real time but generates limited new content. HyperWrite functions as a writing companion that creates original text from scratch, supports multi-step research, and offers tools like infinite email response generation. Grammarly's strength lies in broad integrations and low-friction corrections, while HyperWrite provides deeper generation capabilities at a higher price point for premium features.

## Who should choose Grammarly

Students, professionals, and teams editing emails, reports, or docs benefit from its inline suggestions and app integrations. It works best for polishing existing work without full rewrites.

## Who should choose HyperWrite

Content creators, researchers, and marketers generating blogs, outlines, or summaries need its drafting and research tools. It suits workflows requiring original content from prompts.

## Bottom Line

Choose Grammarly for editing and proofreading in everyday tools. Pick HyperWrite for creating new content with research support. Many users combine both: Grammarly for polish, HyperWrite for drafts.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Grammarly Premium starts at $12/month, lower than HyperWrite Premium at $19.99/month. Free tiers exist for both with core features.

**Which has better output quality?**  
HyperWrite edges out on generation quality via Claude Opus 4.7; Grammarly excels in editing precision for style and tone.

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, they complement each other. Use HyperWrite to draft, then Grammarly to refine.
