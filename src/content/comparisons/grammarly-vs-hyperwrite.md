# Grammarly vs HyperWrite

[Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) and [HyperWrite](/tools/hyperwrite/) both live close to where people write, but they solve different jobs. Grammarly is the inline quality layer for grammar, clarity, tone, full-sentence rewrites, team communication rules, and Enterprise controls. HyperWrite is a Chrome-native personal AI assistant that combines writing tools, TypeAhead autocomplete, Scholar-style citation help, and a supervised Personal Assistant browser agent.

## Quick Answer

Choose Grammarly when the buyer already writes in Gmail, Docs, Slack, LinkedIn, CMS fields, or workplace documents and needs fewer mistakes, clearer tone, and consistent team writing. It is the safer default for everyday professional writing.

Choose HyperWrite when the buyer wants more zero-to-one drafting and light browser help inside Chrome. It is stronger for personal productivity workflows where the user can supervise generated text, research pulls, and browser-agent actions.

## Winner By Use Case

- **Best for grammar, clarity, and tone:** Grammarly.
- **Best for always-on browser writing quality:** Grammarly.
- **Best for team style and Enterprise procurement:** Grammarly.
- **Best for TypeAhead autocomplete:** HyperWrite.
- **Best for personal drafting plus citations:** HyperWrite.
- **Best for browser-agent experimentation:** HyperWrite, with supervision.
- **Best first paid plan for most editors:** Grammarly Pro annual at $144/member/year if the checkout matches the support pricing.
- **Best HyperWrite paid tier:** Premium at $19.99/month for daily writing; Ultra at $44.99/month only when unlimited AI messages matter.

## What Changed In June 2026

The same support page lists Pro at $30/member/month monthly, $60/member/three months quarterly, or $144/member/year, with up to 149 seats and regional checkout currencies.

HyperWrite's current pricing page keeps the Free, Premium, and Ultra posture. Free has limited monthly credits on basic AI. Premium unlocks more advanced AI for higher-quality writing. The AiPedia tool record remains anchored to HyperWrite's public Premium $19.99/month and Ultra $44.99/month pricing, with annual equivalents and the important Premium 250 AI-message limit from the vendor page.

The biggest editorial correction: do not compare these pages by invented model names or token windows. Grammarly does not publish a buyer-facing token context window, and HyperWrite routes third-party model capacity rather than selling a fixed named frontier model.

## Where Grammarly Wins

- It follows the user across more writing surfaces than HyperWrite, especially outside a pure Chrome workflow.
- Its core value is low-friction correction: grammar, punctuation, clarity, tone, fluency, full-sentence rewrites, and style guidance where the text already lives.
- Pro and Enterprise are more credible for teams that need seat management, style rules, security review, SSO, DLP, and writing consistency.
- It is better for non-native English writers and high-volume workplace communication.
- It avoids the agent-risk layer. Grammarly suggestions are easier to review than a browser agent clicking through accounts or forms.

## Where HyperWrite Wins

- It is more useful when the buyer wants drafting help, not just editing.
- TypeAhead can be faster than opening a chatbot for short emails, replies, and paragraph completion.
- HyperWrite's real-time research and citation-backed writing features make it more useful for personal research drafts than Grammarly.
- Personal Assistant can handle simple browser tasks, form-filling, and navigation that Grammarly does not attempt.
- Premium and Ultra are easy to understand for individuals who want a single Chrome extension for writing plus light browser assistance.

## Buying Recommendation

Use Grammarly if the job is "make everything I already write cleaner and safer." That includes email, proposals, support replies, school drafts, executive docs, and team communication. Start free, then move to Pro when tone rewrites, advanced suggestions, or team features save enough time.

Use HyperWrite if the job is "help me draft and operate inside Chrome." Premium is the practical test tier. Move to Ultra only after measuring how quickly the buyer burns through AI messages and whether Personal Assistant actually saves time on repeatable workflows.

The clean stack is HyperWrite for early drafting or Chrome tasks, then Grammarly for final polish and team-safe language. Do not let HyperWrite's browser agent submit purchases, account changes, or private forms without human review.

## Pricing Snapshot

- **Grammarly Pro:** support pricing lists $30/member/month, $60/member/quarter, or $144/member/year; public checkout can vary by region.
- **Grammarly Enterprise:** custom pricing for larger or more controlled deployments.
- **HyperWrite Free:** limited monthly credits on basic AI.
- **HyperWrite Premium:** $19.99/month monthly; annual equivalent in the tool record is $16/month, with 250 AI messages/month and unlimited TypeAheads.
- **HyperWrite Ultra:** $44.99/month monthly; annual equivalent in the tool record is $29/month, with unlimited AI messages and early experimental access.

## Watch-Outs

- Grammarly's public plans page and support pricing can show different checkout context by region, currency, or account state. Use the support page as the US anchor, then verify checkout.
- Grammarly is not a long-form drafting engine. Claude or ChatGPT still beat it for zero-to-one writing.
- HyperWrite's Personal Assistant needs supervision around logins, forms, purchases, private data, and irreversible actions.
- HyperWrite is Chrome-first. Native Word, desktop Notion, and non-browser workflows may not get the same value.
- Neither product removes the need to fact-check generated or rewritten claims before publishing.

## FAQ

**Is Grammarly better than HyperWrite?**
For editing, grammar, tone, and professional writing consistency, yes. HyperWrite is better when the buyer wants drafting, TypeAhead, citations, and light browser-agent help.

**Which is cheaper?**
It depends on billing. Grammarly Pro's support page lists $144/member/year as the annual anchor, while HyperWrite Premium is $19.99/month or about $16/month on annual billing. Grammarly is usually the better value if the buyer mainly edits.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Use HyperWrite to draft or research in Chrome, then Grammarly to polish the final copy inside the destination app.
