# Perplexity vs You.com

[Perplexity](../tools/perplexity.md) and [You.com](../tools/you-com.md) compete in the AI search category as of April 2026. Perplexity uses its Sonar Large model with API access to models like Claude Opus 4.7 and Gemini 3.1 Pro; You.com defaults to GPT-5.3 with options for Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Gemini 3.1 Pro.

## Quick Answer

Perplexity leads for cited research and decision support due to its focus on verifiable answers. You.com suits general queries with broader app integrations, but Perplexity fits most search workflows better.

| | Perplexity | You.com |

## Where Perplexity Wins

- Provides inline citations on every response for verifiable research, unlike You.com's less consistent sourcing.[4]
- Enterprise plan integrates with workflows for teams needing decision support.[4]
- Access to top models like Claude Opus 4.7 (1M context) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (2M context) via API.[1][3]
- Focuses on search accuracy with real-time web results and follow-up questions.
- Pro plan at $20/mo includes unlimited Pro Searches and file uploads.

## Where You.com Wins

- Lower Premium tier at $15/mo offers GPT-5.3 access for general tasks.[3]
- Apps feature combines search with tools like code execution and image generation in one interface.
- Free tier includes model switching between GPT, Claude, and Gemini for casual use.
- Stronger multimodal support with native image and voice handling.
- Broader ecosystem with email summaries and custom agents.

## Key Differences

Perplexity prioritizes search with citations and model-agnostic access to leaders like Claude Opus 4.7 (best for expert work, 1M context) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (top benchmarks, 2M context, $2/$12 per million tokens).[1][3] You.com emphasizes a chat interface powered by GPT-5.3 (versatile, 128K context) with Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Gemini options, plus apps for productivity.[1][3] Perplexity Pro costs $20/mo for unlimited advanced searches; You.com Premium is $15/mo but limits heavy use, with Pro at $200/mo for full access.[3]

## Who should choose Perplexity

Professionals and teams doing research or analysis benefit from its citations and large-context models. It handles document-heavy tasks reliably.

## Who should choose You.com

Casual users or those needing chat with apps and model variety prefer its lower entry price and integrations. It works for mixed daily tasks.

## Bottom Line

Choose Perplexity for research needing sources and large contexts; its Enterprise focus and model access make it stronger for work.[4] You.com fits lighter, versatile use at a cheaper base tier. Test both free versions to match your needs.
