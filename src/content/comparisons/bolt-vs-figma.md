# Bolt.new vs Figma AI

[Bolt.new](../tools/bolt.md) and [Figma AI](../tools/figma.md) are AI tools for design and prototyping tasks as of April 2026. Bolt.new focuses on prompt-based web app and prototype generation, while Figma AI integrates AI features into collaborative design workflows.[6]

## Quick Answer

Bolt.new suits quick prototypes and client demos from text prompts; Figma AI fits teams needing collaborative editing with AI assistance. Choice depends on whether you prioritize speed for solo prototyping or team-based design refinement.[6]

## Decision Snapshot
| | Bolt.new | Figma AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | GPT-5.3 Codex | Integrated Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro |
| **Price** | Free tier; Pro $20/month | Free tier; Professional $12/user/month |
| **Best For** | Rapid prototypes, client demos | Team collaboration, design refinement |

## Where Bolt.new Wins

- Generates full web prototypes from single prompts in seconds, ideal for demos.[6]
- Handles client-facing prototypes without setup, used weekly for such tasks.[6]
- Leverages GPT-5.3 Codex for code and UI generation in one interface.[1]
- Free tier supports real prototyping without payment.[3]

## Where Figma AI Wins

- Provides real-time collaboration for design teams with AI tools like auto-layout and content generation.[4]
- Integrates with Google Workspace and other ecosystems for shared workflows.[3][4]
- Uses Claude Sonnet 4.6 (1M context) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (2M context) for handling large design files.[1][3]
- Established platform with enterprise adoption for production design.[4]

## Key Differences

Bolt.new emphasizes one-shot prototype creation via prompts powered by GPT-5.3 Codex, making it faster for individual users testing ideas quickly.[1][6] Figma AI embeds models like Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Gemini 3.1 Pro into a mature design environment, supporting multi-user editing and integration with tools like Google Workspace.[1][3][4] Bolt.new's free tier covers basic use, while Figma's pricing scales per user for teams.[3]

## Who should choose Bolt.new

Solo developers or agencies needing fast prototypes for client demos. It excels when speed trumps polish.[6]

## Who should choose Figma AI

Design teams in collaborative settings or Google ecosystems requiring AI-assisted refinement. It handles shared files and production workflows.[3][4]

## Bottom Line

Use Bolt.new for prompt-to-prototype speed in solo or demo scenarios; select Figma AI for team design with AI support. Both have free tiers for testing, with paid plans starting at $12 to $20 monthly.[3][6]

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Bolt.new Pro at $20/month matches ChatGPT Plus pricing; Figma Professional at $12/user/month costs less for small teams.[3]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Figma AI offers higher polish through collaboration; Bolt.new provides quicker but rougher prototypes.[6]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, combine Bolt.new for initial prototypes and import to Figma AI for team refinement.[6]
