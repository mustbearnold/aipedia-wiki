# ChatGPT vs Make

[ChatGPT](../tools/chatgpt.md) and [Make](../tools/make.md) address fundamentally different needs. ChatGPT is a conversational AI assistant for content generation, analysis, and reasoning across text, images, and code. Make is a visual workflow automation platform that connects apps and services without coding. This comparison covers their current flagship versions, pricing, and which tool fits which workflow as of April 2026.

## Quick Answer

Choose **ChatGPT** if you need a general-purpose AI assistant for writing, analysis, coding, and research. Choose **Make** if you need to automate repetitive tasks across multiple apps and services. They solve different problems and are often used together rather than as alternatives.

## Where ChatGPT Wins

- **Versatility across domains**: Handles text generation, image creation, voice conversations, code execution, and real-time web browsing in a single interface[2]
- **Reasoning and analysis**: GPT-5.4 excels at complex problem-solving, long-form thinking, and expert-level analysis across knowledge work[5]
- **Accessibility**: Free tier delivers genuine value; Plus tier at $20/month is affordable for individual users and small teams[2]
- **Multimodal input and output**: Processes and generates text, images, audio, and video within one tool[2]
- **Plugin ecosystem**: Growing library of integrations and custom actions extends functionality without leaving the interface[2]

## Where Make Wins

- **Purpose-built automation**: Designed specifically for connecting apps and automating repetitive workflows without writing code[5]
- **Enterprise integration**: Connects hundreds of business applications (Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Google Workspace, etc.) with visual workflow builders[5]
- **Scalability for teams**: Business tier ($299/mo) supports complex multi-user automation at organizational scale[5]
- **Cost efficiency for high-volume tasks**: Once configured, automated workflows run at minimal marginal cost compared to manual labor[5]
- **No AI hallucination risk**: Executes deterministic workflows based on rules and data, not probabilistic language models[5]

## Key Differences

ChatGPT is a **reasoning and generation tool** that excels when you need creative output, analysis, or problem-solving. It requires human judgment to verify results and decide next steps. Make is a **deterministic automation platform** that executes predefined workflows reliably across apps. ChatGPT works best for tasks requiring judgment, creativity, or complex reasoning. Make works best for repetitive, rule-based processes that benefit from consistency and speed.

ChatGPT's pricing scales with usage (tokens consumed); Make's pricing scales with workflow complexity and automation volume. ChatGPT requires you to prompt it for each task; Make runs workflows on schedule or trigger without ongoing input. ChatGPT can hallucinate or produce inconsistent results; Make executes exactly as configured.

Many teams use both: ChatGPT for drafting, analysis, and ideation; Make for automating the resulting workflows and connecting outputs to other systems.

## Who Should Choose ChatGPT

Choose ChatGPT if you work primarily with text, code, or analysis. It's the default for writers, developers, researchers, and knowledge workers who need one tool to handle multiple types of work. The Pro tier ($200/mo) is justified if you need advanced reasoning, priority access, or higher usage limits.

## Who Should Choose Make

Choose Make if you manage repetitive business processes or need to connect multiple apps. It's essential for operations teams, marketing automation, data integration, and any workflow that runs the same steps repeatedly. The Standard tier ($9.99/mo) works for simple automations; Business tier ($299/mo) is necessary for enterprise-scale orchestration.

## Bottom Line

ChatGPT and Make are not competitors; they solve different problems. ChatGPT is for thinking and creating. Make is for automating and connecting. Many workflows benefit from both: use ChatGPT to generate content or analyze data, then use Make to distribute that output across your business systems automatically. Choose based on your primary need, not on which tool is "better."

## FAQ

**Can I use both together?**
Yes, and many teams do. ChatGPT can generate content or analysis that Make then distributes via email, Slack, or other apps. Make can trigger ChatGPT via API to generate content as part of a larger workflow.

**Which is cheaper for a small team?**
ChatGPT Plus at $20/mo per user is cheaper than Make Standard at $9.99/mo if you have multiple team members who each need the AI assistant. Make is cheaper if you need automation but not AI reasoning.

**Which one should I pick first?**
Start with ChatGPT if your bottleneck is creating or analyzing content. Start with Make if your bottleneck is repetitive manual tasks across multiple apps. Most teams eventually use both.
