# Canva AI vs Google Stitch

[Canva AI](../tools/canva.md) and [Google Stitch](../tools/google-stitch.md) compete in the AI design category as of April 2026. Canva Magic Studio uses Adobe Firefly integration for image generation and editing; Google Stitch leverages Gemini 3.1 Pro for design tasks within Google Workspace.[4][2]

## Quick Answer

Canva AI suits teams needing quick templates and collaborative design; Google Stitch fits Google Workspace users requiring large-context multimodal design.[2][4]

## Decision Snapshot
| | Canva AI | Google Stitch |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Magic Studio (Firefly-powered) | Gemini 3.1 Pro |
| **Price** | Free / Pro $14.99/mo | Free / Advanced $19.99/mo |
| **Context Window** | 128K tokens | 2M tokens |
| **Best For** | Fast team design production | Google ecosystem multimodal design |

## Where Canva AI Wins

- Excels in fast design production for teams with template-based workflows.[4]
- Integrates Adobe Firefly for brand-safe image generation and editing.
- Lower Pro pricing at $14.99/mo supports broader team access.
- Free tier includes core Magic Studio features for basic use.
- Strong for non-designers creating social media and marketing visuals.

## Where Google Stitch Wins

- 2M token context window processes large datasets, PDFs, videos for complex designs.[2]
- Multimodal input/output handles text, image, audio, video in one tool.[2]
- Tight integration with Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive streamlines workflows.[2][4]
- Free tier delivers real value with Gemini 3.1 Pro access.[2]
- Matches Gemini 3.1 Pro benchmarks for agentic design tasks.[1]

## Key Differences

Canva AI focuses on accessible, template-driven design via Magic Studio, ideal for quick outputs without deep expertise; it pairs with Adobe Firefly for safe creative assets but limits context to shorter inputs.[4] Google Stitch embeds Gemini 3.1 Pro's 2M context and multimodal capabilities into Workspace, enabling analysis of full documents or videos for data-driven designs, though it requires Google ecosystem familiarity.[2][4] Pricing favors Canva Pro at $14.99/mo over Gemini Advanced $19.99/mo, but Google offers superior scale for enterprise integration.[2]

## Who should choose Canva AI

Teams producing marketing visuals or social content benefit from Canva AI's templates and Firefly integration.

## Who should choose Google Stitch

Google Workspace users handling large files or multimodal projects gain from Stitch's 2M context and ecosystem ties.[2][4]

## Bottom Line

Choose Canva AI for simple, team-based design at lower cost; select Google Stitch for advanced multimodal work in Google environments. Neither dominates all use cases; fit depends on workflow and ecosystem.[2][4]

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Canva Pro at $14.99/mo undercuts Google Stitch Advanced $19.99/mo; both have capable free tiers.[2]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Google Stitch edges out with Gemini 3.1 Pro benchmarks and multimodality; Canva matches for templated designs.[1][4]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes; Canva handles templates, Google Stitch manages complex inputs without overlap issues.
