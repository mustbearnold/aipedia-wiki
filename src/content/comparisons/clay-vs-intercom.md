# Clay vs Intercom

[Clay](../tools/clay.md) and [Intercom](../tools/intercom.md) are two options in the ai-automation category as of April 2026. Clay focuses on data enrichment and sales automation with AI agents, while Intercom provides customer messaging with AI support features.

## Quick Answer

Clay suits sales teams needing data enrichment and outbound automation; Intercom fits customer support and inbound messaging teams. Choice depends on whether outbound sales or customer service is the priority.

## At a Glance

| | Clay | Intercom |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Clay 2.0 with Claude Sonnet 4.6 integration | Intercom AI with Fin AI agent (GPT-5.3 powered) |
| **Price** | $149/user/month (Pro plan) | $99/month (Essential), $179/seat/month (Expert) |
| **Best For** | Sales outreach, lead enrichment | Customer support, messaging automation |

## Where Clay Wins

- Handles multi-step data enrichment from 100+ sources like LinkedIn and company websites for personalized outreach.
- AI agents automate outbound workflows, such as finding emails and crafting messages at scale.
- Table-based interface allows quick data imports, transformations, and exports for sales pipelines.
- Integrates Claude Sonnet 4.6 for reasoning over large datasets in agentic tasks.
- Lower per-user cost for small sales teams compared to Intercom's seat-based pricing.

## Where Intercom Wins

- Real-time customer messaging across web, app, and email with AI chatbots for inbound queries.
- Fin AI agent resolves 65% of support tickets autonomously using GPT-5.3 Codex.
- Built-in analytics track customer engagement and resolution times.
- Scales for enterprise support with role-based permissions and compliance features.
- Free tier available for testing basic messaging before upgrading.

## Key Differences

Clay centers on proactive outbound automation for sales, pulling and enriching data into spreadsheets for campaigns, while Intercom emphasizes reactive inbound support through chat and tickets. Clay's strength lies in its 1M token context handling via Claude Sonnet 4.6 for complex enrichments, contrasting Intercom's focus on conversational AI with GPT-5.3 for quick resolutions. Pricing reflects this: Clay charges per sales user for enrichment tools, Intercom per support seat for messaging volume.[1][2]

## Who should choose Clay

Sales development reps and growth teams building outbound pipelines. It excels when data research and personalization drive revenue.

## Who should choose Intercom

Customer success and support managers handling inbound inquiries. It streamlines messaging at scale for retention.

## Bottom Line

Sales-focused teams pick Clay for enrichment automation; support teams select Intercom for messaging efficiency. Many combine both via integrations for full funnel coverage, as free tiers allow testing without commitment.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Clay at $149/user/month for Pro suits small sales teams; Intercom starts at $99/month flat for Essential, better for low-volume support.[1][2]

**Which has better output quality?**  
Clay leverages Claude Sonnet 4.6 for precise data tasks; Intercom's Fin uses GPT-5.3 Codex for conversational accuracy. Benchmarks favor Claude for reasoning, GPT for speed.[1]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes, via Zapier or native APIs; Clay enriches leads for Intercom messaging sequences.
