# Clay vs Intercom

[Clay](/tools/clay/) and [Intercom](/tools/intercom/) are not substitutes. Clay is an outbound GTM enrichment and account-research workspace. Intercom is an AI-first customer support platform built around Fin AI Agent, customer conversations, help centers, inbox workflows, Copilot, and outcome-based support automation.

## Quick Answer

Use **Clay** when the job is finding, enriching, scoring, and researching prospects before outbound. Use **Intercom** when the job is resolving customer questions, routing support conversations, assisting human agents, and managing chat/email support inside one platform. If the buyer says "pipeline," start with Clay. If the buyer says "tickets, chat, deflection, or help center," start with Intercom.

## Decision Snapshot

| | Clay | Intercom |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary job** | Outbound GTM enrichment and account research | Customer support automation and conversational helpdesk |
| **Best buyer** | RevOps, SDR ops, GTM engineer, outbound agency | Support leader, CX ops, SaaS onboarding/support team |
| **Current pricing signal** | Free; Launch/Growth scale by actions and data credits; Enterprise custom | Essential/Advanced/Expert seats plus $0.99 per Fin outcome; Fin can also run on existing helpdesks |
| **AI surface** | Claygent, AI columns, BYO keys, fixed or token-based AI pricing | Fin AI Agent, Copilot, Pro add-on, workflows, knowledge-based answers |
| **System of record** | Enriched prospect/account table | Inbox, help center, customer conversation history |
| **Main risk** | Bad workflows create bad lists and wasted credits | Seat + Fin outcome + Copilot/add-on spend can stack quickly |

## Where Clay Wins

Clay wins before a customer exists. It helps GTM teams research accounts, pull contact data, waterfall providers, run Claygent research, score leads, and push qualified records into CRMs or senders. Its current pricing emphasizes actions, data credits, 150+ data providers, Claygent, and BYO API keys, so the buyer should think in workflows rather than seats alone.

Choose Clay when:

- outbound targeting quality matters more than support automation
- account research needs custom signals from the web
- the team wants a spreadsheet-like GTM workflow with enrichment vendors attached
- Salesforce/HubSpot/sender handoff depends on clean enriched fields
- the team can QA prompts, sources, data, and credit usage

## Where Intercom Wins

Intercom wins after prospects or customers are already talking to the business. Its current pricing page puts Fin AI Agent inside the support platform and lists Fin at $0.99 per outcome, annual seat tiers at Essential $29, Advanced $85, and Expert $132 per seat/month, Copilot at $29 per agent/month, and add-ons like Pro and Proactive Support Plus.

Choose Intercom when:

- chat, email, help center, and in-app messages need one support workspace
- the team has enough conversation volume for Fin outcome pricing to matter
- support agents need AI answers, summaries, and handoff context
- SaaS onboarding or customer support is the workflow, not outbound prospecting
- the knowledge base is mature enough for AI deflection

## Key Differences

Clay is about **who should we contact next?** Intercom is about **how do we support the people already contacting us?**

Clay belongs in revenue operations, account-based marketing, and outbound sales. Intercom belongs in customer support, customer success, onboarding, and helpdesk operations. Clay can send enriched leads into support or CRM systems, but it does not replace a support inbox. Intercom can capture leads and route conversations, but it does not replace Clay's enrichment and research workflows.

The cost model is also different. Clay spending comes from actions, data credits, providers, AI research, and workflow volume. Intercom spending comes from support seats, Fin outcomes, Copilot, messaging channels, and add-ons. A team can be "small" in headcount and still expensive on either platform if it runs a high-volume workflow.

## Who Should Choose Clay

Choose Clay if the business needs more or better outbound opportunities. It is the better fit for B2B prospecting, enrichment, signal-based lead lists, customer lookalike research, and custom GTM operations.

## Who Should Choose Intercom

Choose Intercom if the business needs fewer unresolved support conversations and better customer communication. It is the better fit for chat-first SaaS support, AI deflection, support inboxes, help-center automation, and human-agent productivity.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes, but they meet at the CRM/customer record, not inside the same job. Clay can enrich companies and contacts before sales outreach. Intercom can later support those customers after signup. Mature teams may connect both through CRM, data warehouse, or automation tools, but the workflows should be owned by different operators.

## Bottom Line

Pick **Clay** for outbound research and enrichment. Pick **Intercom** for inbound support and AI customer service. Do not buy Intercom to solve prospecting, and do not buy Clay to solve customer support.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
There is no honest universal answer. Clay depends on actions, data credits, enrichment providers, and AI usage. Intercom depends on seats, Fin outcomes, Copilot, and add-ons.

**Does Intercom replace Clay for lead generation?**
No. Intercom can collect and route customer conversations, but Clay is the stronger tool for building enriched prospect lists and outbound workflows.

**Does Clay replace Intercom for support?**
No. Clay is not a chat-first helpdesk or support inbox. Intercom is purpose-built for customer conversations, knowledge-base answers, and AI/human handoff.

**Best first purchase?**
Buy Clay first if pipeline quality is the bottleneck. Buy Intercom first if support volume, response time, or customer deflection is the bottleneck.
