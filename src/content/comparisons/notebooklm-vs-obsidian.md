# Google NotebookLM vs Obsidian

[Google NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/) and [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) solve different parts of the notes workflow. NotebookLM is a cloud source notebook: upload PDFs, Docs, Slides, web pages, YouTube links, audio, or text, then ask grounded questions and generate study artifacts. Obsidian is a local-first Markdown knowledge base: keep notes as files, link them over years, and add AI through plugins and your own model keys.

## Quick Answer

Choose **NotebookLM** when the source pack already exists and you need cited answers, Audio/Video Overviews, study guides, reports, quizzes, flashcards, mind maps, or briefings. Choose **Obsidian** when the note system itself is the asset: private Markdown files, backlinks, graph view, Canvas, Dataview, and plugin-controlled AI.

## Decision Snapshot

| | NotebookLM | Obsidian |
|---|---|---|
| **Best job** | Source-grounded analysis of a bounded corpus | Long-term personal knowledge management |
| **Storage model** | Cloud notebooks in Google | Local plain Markdown files |
| **AI access** | Built in, Gemini-backed, source-grounded | Plugin-based, bring your own API key or local model |
| **Price floor** | Standard tier is free | Core app is free for personal use |
| **Paid decision** | Upgrade when source, chat, and Studio limits matter | Pay for Sync, Publish, or commercial support |
| **Watch-out** | Not a durable vault or open-web research engine | AI is setup-heavy and plugin quality varies |
| **Winner for most people** | NotebookLM for research packets | Obsidian for everyday notes |

## Where NotebookLM Wins

- **Cited Q&A from sources.** NotebookLM's core advantage is answering against uploaded material with citations instead of acting like a generic chatbot.
- **Fast study artifacts.** It can generate Audio Overviews, Video Overviews, reports, quizzes, flashcards, mind maps, data tables, and briefing docs from the same notebook.
- **Low setup.** A Google account is enough to start. Paid tiers mainly raise caps through Google AI Plans, Google Cloud, Workspace, or Education packaging.
- **Document-heavy work.** It is strongest for class readings, policy packs, legal summaries, research folders, board materials, and meeting transcripts.
- **Clear collaboration handoff.** Share a notebook or derived briefing without asking teammates to learn a PKM system.

## Where Obsidian Wins

- **Data ownership.** Notes remain local `.md` files that work in any text editor, backup system, or Git workflow.
- **Daily knowledge building.** Backlinks, graph view, Canvas, Dataview, templates, and community plugins are built for years of linked notes.
- **AI control.** Smart Connections, Copilot, Text Generator, and other plugins can use Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, OpenRouter, or local Ollama-style models depending on the user's setup.
- **Offline-first work.** The core vault works without a network connection and without sending content to Obsidian servers.
- **Lower platform risk.** The file format survives even if a plugin, sync provider, or hosted service changes direction.

## Key Differences

NotebookLM is a **source session**. You bring it a corpus, ask questions, check citations, and export the useful output elsewhere. It is excellent when the question is "what do these documents say?"

Obsidian is a **knowledge base**. You write, link, refactor, tag, and query notes over time. It is excellent when the question is "how do I keep my thinking, references, and drafts usable for years?"

Pricing follows that split. NotebookLM's Standard tier is free, with higher notebook/source/chat/Studio limits in paid Google AI tiers. Obsidian's core app is free for personal use; Sync is $4/month annually or $5 monthly, Publish is $8/month annually or $10 monthly, Catalyst is $25 one-time, and commercial support is $50/user/year.

## Who Should Choose NotebookLM

- Students turning lectures, slides, and reading packets into study guides.
- Analysts summarizing reports, transcripts, and source packs.
- Teams that need a one-off cited briefing without building a note system.
- Researchers who want Audio Overviews or Video Overviews from uploaded material.

## Who Should Choose Obsidian

- Writers, developers, researchers, and operators building a long-term vault.
- Privacy-conscious users who prefer local files and optional sync.
- Power users who want plugin-level control over AI, search, metadata, and exports.
- Anyone who treats notes as durable infrastructure rather than temporary research input.

## Bottom Line

Use NotebookLM for **source-grounded extraction and study artifacts**. Use Obsidian for **owning and growing a knowledge base**. The best combined workflow is simple: capture durable notes in Obsidian, send source packs or exported transcripts to NotebookLM when you need cited synthesis.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**
Both can start free. NotebookLM Standard is free with Google account access. Obsidian's core app is free for personal use; Sync and Publish are optional paid add-ons.

**Which has better AI?**
NotebookLM has better zero-config source Q&A. Obsidian has better model choice only if you are willing to configure plugins and pay separate API or local-model costs.

**Can I use both?**
Yes. Use Obsidian as the permanent vault and NotebookLM as the temporary analysis bench for PDFs, slides, videos, transcripts, and research packets.
