# Google NotebookLM vs Obsidian

[Google NotebookLM](../tools/notebooklm.md) and [Obsidian](../tools/obsidian.md) serve notes workflows differently as of April 2026. NotebookLM focuses on AI-driven analysis of personal documents, while Obsidian provides a local markdown-based knowledge base with plugins.

## Quick Answer

Obsidian suits users wanting a free, customizable, offline note-linking system. NotebookLM fits those needing AI to query and synthesize uploaded sources.

## At a Glance

| | Google NotebookLM | Obsidian |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Gemini 3.1 Pro | v1.8.0 (core) + AI plugins |
| **Price** | Free | Free (core); Sync $5/mo; Publish $8/mo; Catalyst $5+ one-time |
| **Context Window/Output Specs** | 2M tokens; audio overviews, study guides | Unlimited local files; plugin-dependent AI (e.g., 200K tokens via Copilot) |
| **Best For** | Document Q&A, research synthesis | Linked notes, personal wiki, long-term organization |

## Where Google NotebookLM Wins

- Handles large uploads like PDFs or videos for instant Q&A and summaries using Gemini 3.1 Pro.[3][4]
- Generates audio podcasts or briefings from sources, useful for on-the-go review.[4]
- Tight Google ecosystem integration for Workspace users processing Drive files.[2][3]
- No setup required; web/app access with free tier covering most personal use.[3]
- Multimodal input supports text, images, audio for comprehensive analysis.[3]

## Where Obsidian Wins

- Fully local storage with no cloud dependency or vendor lock-in.
- Graph view links notes bidirectionally for knowledge discovery over years of data.
- Thousands of free plugins extend to calendars, kanban, AI chat via any model.
- Markdown standard ensures portability; exports to PDF, HTML without limits.
- Offline-first with mobile/desktop sync optional at low cost.[Obsidian site]

## Key Differences

NotebookLM acts as an AI research assistant bound to your uploaded sources; it excels at querying dozens of documents at once but resets per notebook and lacks permanent linking. Obsidian builds a persistent, searchable graph from markdown files; AI is optional via plugins, prioritizing user control over structure. Pricing reflects this: NotebookLM stays free for core AI use, while Obsidian charges only for sync/publish. Context differs too, with NotebookLM's 2M tokens per query versus Obsidian's file-system scale.[3]

## Who should choose Google NotebookLM

Users handling project-specific research or needing quick AI insights from fresh uploads. It works for students summarizing lectures or professionals briefing teams from reports.

## Who should choose Obsidian

Long-term note-takers building a second brain with links and queries. Developers, writers, or researchers value its flexibility across devices without subscription pressure.

## Bottom Line

Choose NotebookLM for occasional AI-powered document work inside Google tools. Pick Obsidian for everyday note management with enduring structure and extensions. Many combine both: Obsidian for storage, NotebookLM for analysis.

## FAQ

**Which is cheaper?**  
Obsidian core is free forever; add-ons optional. NotebookLM remains free with no paid tiers announced.[3]

**Which has better output quality?**  
NotebookLM leverages Gemini 3.1 Pro for precise source-grounded responses. Obsidian quality depends on chosen AI plugin, often matching via Claude or GPT-5.[1][3]

**Can I use both?**  
Yes; export Obsidian markdown to NotebookLM for AI processing, or use plugins to query Obsidian vaults directly.
