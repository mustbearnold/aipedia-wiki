# Cursor vs Devin

[Cursor](../tools/cursor.md) and [Devin](../tools/devin.md) compete in the AI coding category as of April 2026. Cursor leads as the dominant AI-native IDE with $2 billion in annual recurring revenue, while Devin offers agentic capabilities for autonomous software engineering tasks.[3]

## Quick Answer

Cursor suits most developers due to its fast autocomplete and integration into daily coding workflows. Devin fits teams needing full project autonomy from natural language specs.

## At a Glance

## Where Cursor Wins

- Supermaven autocomplete delivers the fastest completions in the industry.[3]
- Background agents handle tasks autonomously without interrupting workflow.[3]
- $2 billion ARR shows strong adoption among developers.[3]
- Tight VS Code compatibility eases transition for existing users.
- Pro plan at $20/month provides high value for daily use.

## Where Devin Wins

- Builds complete projects from natural language descriptions.
- Handles multi-step agentic workflows beyond single-file edits.
- Larger 2M token context processes full codebases effectively.
- Enterprise focus with per-seat pricing for teams.
- Stronger on benchmarks for complex software engineering tasks.

## Key Differences

Cursor functions as an AI-enhanced IDE, prioritizing autocomplete speed and inline edits within familiar editors like VS Code. Devin acts as an autonomous engineer, taking high-level specs and delivering deployed applications with minimal oversight. Cursor's lower $20/month pricing targets individuals, while Devin's $50/month per seat suits teams tackling larger scopes. Context windows differ at 1M for Cursor and 2M for Devin, affecting large codebase handling.[3]

## Who should choose Cursor

Solo developers or small teams focused on accelerating code writing and debugging. Its autocomplete and agents integrate directly into existing IDEs for minimal disruption.

## Who should choose Devin

Engineering managers or startups needing rapid prototypes from specs. Its agentic system manages full project lifecycles, from planning to deployment.

## Bottom Line

Cursor dominates for hands-on coding due to speed and adoption. Devin excels in autonomous project delivery but at higher cost. Most users pick Cursor; scale to Devin for team-level automation.
