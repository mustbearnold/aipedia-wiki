# State of AI Tools: April 2026

April 2026 was a signal month. Anthropic shipped two frontier releases, Claude Desktop reached GA after a year in waiting rooms, and the agentic-coding race consolidated around two CLI tools instead of twelve GUI ones.

This report covers what changed across the 132 tools in the aipedia.wiki catalog, which scores moved and why, and what looks likely to land in May. Every claim below is linked to a primary source.

## The headline story

On April 16, 2026, Anthropic released [Claude Opus 4.7](/tools/claude/). The model narrowly retook the lead for most powerful generally-available LLM on agentic coding, scaled tool use, computer use, and financial-analysis benchmarks. API sticker price is unchanged from 4.6 at $5 in / $25 out per MTok, but a new tokenizer produces 1.0-1.35× more tokens for the same input. Budget-sensitive API workloads need to benchmark before migrating.

Nine days earlier, on April 7, Anthropic announced [Claude Mythos Preview](https://anthropic.com/glasswing), a specialised frontier model for defensive cybersecurity, developed under Project Glasswing. The consortium includes AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, and seven others. Mythos scores 83.1% on vulnerability-reproduction benchmarks versus Opus 4.6's 66.6%.

Mythos is invitation-only. No self-serve API, no Max-tier access, no public waitlist. A major US lab has pulled a second frontier model out of its research pipeline and gated it behind a defence consortium, which is a first. The closest precedent is Google's Defence Llama work with Lockheed Martin, but that was a fine-tune of a public base model. Mythos is a separate architecture.

Practical effect for anyone building on Claude: the main lineup is now Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5, all accessible through claude.ai, the API, and Claude Code. Mythos is not replacing Opus and is not coming to Pro.

The second-order question is whether this becomes a pattern. If OpenAI or Google respond with their own gated frontier models for defence or biosecurity, the top of the capability curve splits into public and private tracks. That would change how the aipedia.wiki moat score works across the lab tier. For now, one data point.

## New and noteworthy

**[Claude](/tools/claude/) Desktop and Claude Cowork reached general availability** on macOS and Windows on April 9, 2026. The desktop app is serviceable. The Cowork feature (shared Claude sessions across multiple humans) is the more interesting of the two, because it solves the "everyone has their own ChatGPT history" problem that every team using AI assistants has hit. Small teams at small companies are the audience; large orgs already run Enterprise.

**Computer Use inside [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) is in research preview** as of the week of March 30 to April 3, 2026. It is exactly as rough as a research preview suggests. On a simple "open Chrome, find this page, scroll to pricing, copy the numbers" task, it works. Latency is 2 to 4 seconds per action and error recovery is poor. Not production-ready. Teams building agentic workflows should start experimenting now.

**Claude Code hit v2.1.108** on April 14, 2026. The Monitor tool (watch a running process, act on events) and Ultraplan (auto-spin cloud environments from a natural-language description) are both in early preview. Both work well enough for production use on small projects. Ultraplan for one-off prototypes is genuinely useful.

**[Cursor](/tools/cursor/) 2.0** is the current stable release. The delta versus Cursor 1.x is noticeable for users coming off older versions, unremarkable for active daily users. It remains the leading GUI agentic IDE. Claude Code is eating its coding-hour share for multi-file sessions.

**[Qwen3.6 Plus](/tools/qwen/) with 1M context** is the open-weight story still worth watching. Released February 2026, taking longer than usual to shake out in the community because the inference costs on the MoE architecture surprised users in both directions. Cheaper than expected on small tasks, slightly more expensive than expected on very long contexts.

## Score changes

Three score movements this month. Each is editorial, not data-driven.

- **[Claude](/tools/claude/) moved from 9.5 to 9.6.** Opus 4.7 takes measurable leads on agentic coding, tool use, computer use, and financial analysis. Tokenizer-driven cost bump holds the move to 0.1 rather than 0.2.
- **[Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) held at 9.5.** Ultraplan and Monitor are useful; Computer Use preview is ahead of schedule. Sharp edges for beginners remain, and research previews do not count toward scoring. Revisit in May.
- **Sora (dead) moved to the archive with a final score of 7.2.** Cleanup from the March discontinuation. The final score reflects the product as it shipped, not a post-mortem.

No other tool in the catalog had a material change this month that would justify a score move. Scores hold until something real happens.

## What's coming in May

**Claude Sonnet 4.7.** Anthropic's cadence puts a Sonnet refresh in the 2-4 week window after the Opus release. Odds above 60% for a May ship.

**Runway Gen-5.** Roadmap points to longer clip lengths. Nothing confirmed. Competition from [Kling 3.0](/tools/kling/) is real; Runway's lead on professional workflows has narrowed year over year.

**Gemini 3.2.** Google's cadence is less predictable than Anthropic's or OpenAI's. Odds: 40% May, 60% June.

**Agentic coding consolidation.** [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) and [Aider](/tools/aider/) own the CLI agentic space. Cursor owns the GUI. The mid-market ("agentic but in VS Code as an extension") is a graveyard in slow motion. Fewer than two extension-based tools are likely to survive into 2027.
