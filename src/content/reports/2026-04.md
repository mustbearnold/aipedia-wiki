# State of AI Tools: April 2026

Most months in AI are noise. April was signal. Anthropic shipped a separate frontier model inside a defence-industry consortium, Claude Desktop finally reached GA after a year of "coming soon," and the agentic-coding race consolidated around two CLI tools instead of twelve GUI ones. This report covers what changed across the 132 tools I track, which scores moved and why, and what I expect to land in May.

If you're reading this as the first monthly report: the format is new, the voice isn't. Every claim here comes from primary sources linked inline. If I got something wrong, [tell me](mailto:eli@aipedia.wiki).

## The headline story

On April 7, 2026, Anthropic announced [Claude Mythos Preview](https://anthropic.com/glasswing), a specialised frontier model for defensive cybersecurity, developed as part of Project Glasswing. The consortium includes AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, and seven others. Mythos scores 83.1% on vulnerability reproduction benchmarks versus Opus 4.6's 66.6%.

What this actually means is more interesting than the numbers. Mythos is invitation-only. No self-serve API, no Max-tier access, no public waitlist. Anthropic has pulled a second frontier model out of its research pipeline and gated it behind a defence consortium, which is a first for a major US lab. The closest precedent is Google's Defence Llama work with Lockheed Martin, but that was a fine-tune of a public base model. Mythos is a separate architecture.

The practical effect for anyone building on [Claude](/tools/claude/): nothing changes on the main lineup. Opus 4.6, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5 are still the only models you can access through claude.ai, the API, or Claude Code. Mythos isn't replacing Opus, and it's not coming to Pro. If you work in defensive security at one of the consortium companies, you probably already have an invite. If you don't, ignore the announcement for product purposes; it's more interesting as an AI policy story than a tools story.

The second-order question is whether this becomes a pattern. If OpenAI or Google respond with their own gated frontier models for defence or biosecurity, the top of the capability curve splits into public and private tracks. That would change how aipedia.wiki scores "moat" across the lab tier. For now, one data point.

## New and noteworthy

**[Claude](/tools/claude/) desktop and Claude Cowork reached general availability** on macOS and Windows on April 9, 2026. I've been running the desktop app for a week. It's fine. The cowork feature (sharing a Claude session across multiple humans) is the more interesting of the two, because it solves the "everyone has their own ChatGPT history" problem that every team using AI assistants has hit. Small teams at small companies are the audience; big orgs already have Enterprise.

**Computer Use inside [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) is in research preview** as of the week of March 30 to April 3, 2026. It's exactly as rough as you'd expect for a research preview. I tested it on a simple "open Chrome, find this page, scroll to the pricing section, copy the numbers back" task. It works. Latency is 2 to 4 seconds per action and error recovery is poor. Don't put this in front of a customer, but if you're building agentic workflows, start experimenting now.

**Claude Code hit v2.1.108** on April 14, 2026, which is the current production CLI version. The Monitor tool (watch a running process and act on events) and Ultraplan (auto-spin cloud environments from a natural language description) are both in early preview. Both work well enough that I've used them on this site. Ultraplan for one-off prototypes is genuinely useful and I did not expect to say that. Monitor is more niche.

**[Cursor](/tools/cursor/) 2.0** is the current stable release. The difference versus Cursor 1.x is noticeable if you were on an older version but unremarkable if you've been shipping with Cursor for the last six months. I keep using it as the GUI option when I don't want to live in a terminal, but Claude Code has eaten more of my coding hours this month than Cursor has.

**[Qwen3.6 Plus](/tools/qwen/) with 1M context** is still the open-weight story worth watching. Released February 2026 but taking longer than usual to shake out in the community because the inference costs on the MoE architecture surprised people in both directions. Cheaper than expected on small tasks, slightly more expensive than expected on very long contexts.

## Score changes

Three score movements this month. Each one is editorial, not data-driven.

- **[Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) held at 9.5.** The case for raising to 9.7 is real (Ultraplan and Monitor are genuinely useful, Computer Use preview is ahead of schedule). The case for holding is that the tool still has sharp edges for beginners and I'm not rewarding a research preview. Holding this month, revisiting in May.

- **[Claude](/tools/claude/) holds at 9.5.** Mythos Preview is not a factor in the main lineup's score because it isn't a general-release product. If it ever became available through a paid tier, I would revisit.

- **[Sora](/tools/sora/) (dead) moved to the archive with a final score of 7.2.** This is cleanup from March's discontinuation. The final score reflects what the tool was when it was alive, not a post-mortem judgement.

No other tool in the catalog had a material change this month that would justify a score move. I'd rather hold scores steady and move them when something real happens than nudge them around monthly for the appearance of activity.

## Pricing shifts

No major pricing shifts confirmed across the catalog in April 2026. [Claude Pro](https://claude.com/pricing) held at $20 per month, [ChatGPT Plus](/tools/chatgpt/) held at $20, [Cursor Pro](/tools/cursor/) held at $20, [Midjourney Standard](/tools/midjourney/) held at $30, [ElevenLabs Creator](/tools/elevenlabs/) held at $22. The Claude API is still $5 input / $25 output per million tokens on Opus 4.6, $3 / $15 on Sonnet 4.6, $1 / $5 on Haiku 4.5.

If you see a pricing change I missed, [email me](mailto:eli@aipedia.wiki) and I'll verify and update.

## The graveyard

Nothing new died in April 2026 that I can confirm from primary sources. The most recent confirmed discontinuations are [Sora](/tools/sora/) (March 2026), [GPT-4o](/tools/dead/) retired by OpenAI on February 13, 2026, and [DALL-E](/tools/dead/) retired in March 2025. Inflection's Pi was already in maintenance mode before April; I'm not treating that as an April death.

A few tools I'm watching for late-month or early-May confirmation: whether any of the smaller Chinese open-weight projects stop pre-training following the pattern [01.AI set in March 2025](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/01.AI), and whether any of the "AI browser" wrapper startups from the 2024 cohort quietly shut down. No public obituaries yet.

## What I'm watching in May

**Opus 4.7.** Widely reported as in development with no announced date. The gap between Opus 4.6 (released late 2025) and now is about six months, which is the cadence Anthropic has held. I would be mildly surprised if it shipped before mid-May and genuinely surprised if it didn't ship by end of May.

**Runway Gen-5.** The Runway roadmap points to a Gen-5 release with longer clip lengths, but nothing confirmed. The competition from [Kling 3.0](/tools/kling/) is real; Runway's lead on professional workflows is not as wide as it was a year ago.

**Gemini 3.2.** Google's cadence is less predictable than Anthropic's or OpenAI's. I'd give it 40% odds of a May release, 60% odds of June.

**Agentic coding consolidation.** [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) and [Aider](/tools/aider/) are eating the CLI agentic space. Cursor is eating the GUI agentic space. The mid-market ("agentic but in VS Code as an extension") is a graveyard in slow motion. I'll be surprised if more than two extension-based tools survive into 2027.
