# State of AI Tools: April 2026

April 2026 was a signal month. Anthropic shipped Claude Opus 4.7, gated Claude Mythos Preview behind Project Glasswing, pushed Claude Cowork to general availability, and the agentic-coding race consolidated around serious IDE and CLI workflows instead of shallow wrappers.

This report preserves the April 2026 snapshot, originally produced when the AiPedia catalog covered 132 tools. The live catalog has since expanded, so the May 13 refresh focused on verifying the volatile April claims and updating the May watchlist with confirmed launches, rather than rewriting the historical issue around today's count. High-impact claims below are tied to primary or official sources where possible.

The May prediction section is updated with confirmed releases: GPT-5.5 (April 23), Gemini 3.1 Pro (April 16), Veo 3.1 (April 28), Mistral 3 family (April 28), Daybreak agent (May 11), Codex Chrome (May 7), Claude for Legal (May 12), and Gemini Intelligence / Googlebook (May 12).

## The headline story

On April 16, 2026, Anthropic released [Claude Opus 4.7](/tools/claude/). The model narrowly retook the lead for most powerful generally-available LLM on agentic coding, scaled tool use, computer use, and financial-analysis benchmarks. API sticker price is unchanged from 4.6 at $5 in / $25 out per MTok, but a new tokenizer produces 1.0-1.35× more tokens for the same input. Budget-sensitive API workloads need to benchmark before migrating.

Nine days earlier, on April 7, Anthropic announced [Claude Mythos Preview](https://www.anthropic.com/project/glasswing), a specialised frontier model for defensive cybersecurity, developed under Project Glasswing. The launch partners include AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Palo Alto Networks. Anthropic says Mythos Preview has already identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across critical infrastructure and is available only as a gated research preview.

Mythos is invitation-only. No self-serve API, no Max-tier access, no public waitlist. A major US lab has pulled a second frontier model out of its research pipeline and gated it behind a defence consortium, which is a first for this generation of commercial frontier AI deployment.

Practical effect for anyone building on Claude: the main lineup is now Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, and Haiku 4.5, all accessible through claude.ai, the API, and Claude Code. Mythos is not replacing Opus and is not coming to Pro.

The second-order question is whether this becomes a pattern. If OpenAI or Google respond with their own gated frontier models for defence or biosecurity, the top of the capability curve splits into public and private tracks. That would change how the aipedia.wiki moat score works across the lab tier. For now, one data point.

## New and noteworthy

**[Claude](/tools/claude/) Desktop and Claude Cowork reached general availability** on macOS and Windows on April 9, 2026. The desktop app is serviceable. The Cowork feature (shared Claude sessions across multiple humans) is the more interesting of the two, because it solves the "everyone has their own ChatGPT history" problem that every team using AI assistants has hit. Small teams at small companies are the audience; large orgs already run Enterprise.

**Computer Use inside [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/) is in research preview** as of the week of March 30 to April 3, 2026. It is exactly as rough as a research preview suggests. On a simple "open Chrome, find this page, scroll to pricing, copy the numbers" task, it works. Latency is 2 to 4 seconds per action and error recovery is poor. Not production-ready. Teams building agentic workflows should start experimenting now.

**Claude Code's April agent features became clearer after launch.** Anthropic's Week 15 Claude Code notes covered the Monitor tool and Ultraplan cloud planning, and the current commands documentation still lists `/ultraplan` and `/ultrareview`. The April 14 v2.1.108 package release added recap and cache-control improvements rather than being the original source of Monitor and Ultraplan.

**[Cursor](/tools/cursor/) 2.0** is the current stable release. The delta versus Cursor 1.x is noticeable for users coming off older versions, unremarkable for active daily users. It remains the leading GUI agentic IDE. Claude Code is eating its coding-hour share for multi-file sessions.

**[Qwen3.6 Plus](/tools/qwen/) with 1M context** is the open-weight story still worth watching. Released February 2026, taking longer than usual to shake out in the community because the inference costs on the MoE architecture surprised users in both directions. Cheaper than expected on small tasks, slightly more expensive than expected on very long contexts.

## What's coming in May (status as of May 13)

**Claude Sonnet 4.7.** Still unconfirmed as of May 13. Anthropic's public release notes list Opus 4.7 and Claude Design in April, and the May 9 to 12 burst was enterprise (FIS, Akamai, Claude for Legal) rather than a Sonnet point release. Keep on the watch list.

**Runway Gen-5.** Still unconfirmed as of May 13. Veo 3.1 shipped on April 28 and Kling 3.0 continues to lead on motion realism, so Runway's window to launch keeps tightening.

**Gemini 3.2.** Not released as of May 13. Google instead doubled down on the Gemini 3.1 line: Gemini 3.1 Pro (April 16) plus the May 12 launches of Gemini Intelligence (consumer assistant layer) and the Googlebook device. Treat Gemini 3.2 as speculation; the real May story is Gemini Intelligence as the distribution layer.

**Confirmed May launches not on April's radar.** Daybreak (OpenAI, May 11), Deployment Company (OpenAI, May 11), Codex Chrome (May 7), GPT-5.5-Cyber EU preview (May 11), Claude for Legal (May 12), Gemini Intelligence and Googlebook (May 12), Isomorphic Labs $2.1B Series C (May 12), Mistral 3 family with Apache 2.0 Large 3 (April 28), and the reported Anthropic $50B raise at $900B (April 30).

**Agentic coding consolidation.** [Claude Code](/tools/claude-code/), Codex (now with Chrome), and [Aider](/tools/aider/) anchor the CLI agentic space. Cursor still owns the GUI. The mid-market ("agentic but in VS Code as an extension") is a graveyard in slow motion. Fewer than two extension-based tools are likely to survive into 2027.
