# Best AI for Meeting Notes (May 2026)

The best AI meeting-note tool depends on whether you need a personal notetaker, live captions, team meeting intelligence, CRM handoff, enterprise controls, or source-grounded review after the call.

Verified May 9, 2026 against official Fathom, Fireflies, Otter, Read AI, and NotebookLM sources. AiPedia may earn from some tool links, but the ranking is editorial.

## Quick Verdict

Pick **[Fathom](/tools/fathom/)** first if you want the easiest default AI meeting notetaker. Its current pricing page lists a free individual plan with unlimited recordings and transcriptions, instant AI summaries, clips, playlists, and call search. Team and Business tiers add shared search, collaboration, CRM sync, SSO, custom data retention, coaching metrics, and AI scorecards.

Pick **[Fireflies](/tools/fireflies/)** for team meeting intelligence. Its current pricing page lists Free, Pro, Business, and Enterprise plans, with paid tiers adding unlimited transcription, unlimited AI summaries, more storage, integrations, action items, analytics, user groups, SSO/SCIM/HIPAA controls, and private storage.

Pick **[Otter.ai](/tools/otter-ai/)** when live transcription, speaker identification, calendar-meeting coverage, and collaborative meeting workflows matter more than free unlimited transcription. Otter's current pricing page lists a Basic free plan with 300 monthly transcription minutes, plus paid Pro, Business, and Enterprise tiers.

Pick **[Read AI](/tools/read-ai/)** when the buyer wants meeting reports, coaching, enterprise search, email/message summaries, and a broader productivity layer around meetings. Its current pricing page lists Free, Pro, Enterprise, and Enterprise+ plans with meeting-report limits, upload credits, Search Copilot, integrations, and workspace controls.

Pick **[NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/)** as the free post-meeting analysis companion when you already have the transcript, recording notes, PDFs, or source files and want grounded Q&A, summaries, reports, flashcards, mind maps, and source-based review.

## Best Picks by Meeting Job

- **Best default for individuals:** [Fathom](/tools/fathom/)
- **Best for team call intelligence:** [Fireflies](/tools/fireflies/)

- **Best meeting-report and coaching layer:** [Read AI](/tools/read-ai/)

## What To Buy First

Start with the meeting type:

1. If you mostly need personal notes and summaries, start with Fathom Free.
2. If the team needs searchable call history, analytics, and integrations, compare Fireflies Business with Fathom Team/Business.
3. If live transcription and collaborative note editing matter, test Otter.
4. If meeting reports, coaching, and workplace search matter, test Read AI.
5. If the transcript already exists and you need source-grounded analysis, use NotebookLM.

## Top Picks

### 1. Fathom

[Fathom](/tools/fathom/) is the best default AI meeting notetaker for most individuals because the free plan is unusually useful. The current pricing page lists unlimited recordings and transcriptions, instant AI summaries, clips, playlists, and search across calls on the free individual plan. Paid individual Premium adds advanced summaries, action items, a conversational meeting assistant, and a custom meeting bot. Team and Business add shared search, folders, comments, SSO, CRM sync, deal views, coaching metrics, AI scorecards, and data-retention controls.

**Best for:** solo operators, founders, consultants, recruiters, salespeople, and teams that want a low-risk starting point before paying.

**Watch-out:** team search, CRM sync, admin controls, coaching, and advanced collaboration live behind paid tiers. Fathom is the cleanest first test, not automatically the best enterprise standard.

### 2. Fireflies

[Fireflies](/tools/fireflies/) is the best pick when meeting notes become a team knowledge system. The current pricing page lists Free, Pro, Business, and Enterprise tiers. Free includes unlimited transcription with limited AI summaries and 800 minutes of storage per seat. Pro adds unlimited transcription and AI summaries, 8,000 minutes of storage per seat, downloads, talk-time analytics, AI Skills, action items, and unlimited integrations. Business adds unlimited storage, video recording, multi-language mode, conversation intelligence, team analytics, and user groups. Enterprise adds SSO, SCIM, HIPAA compliance, private storage, custom retention, and a dedicated account manager.

**Best for:** sales teams, customer success, management teams, recruiting teams, and organizations that want searchable meeting intelligence.

**Watch-out:** the bot-style meeting presence can be sensitive in some meetings. For confidential calls, test consent flows, recording rules, retention settings, and admin policy before broad rollout.

### 3. Otter.ai

[Otter.ai](/tools/otter-ai/) is strongest when the meeting requires live transcription and collaborative capture. Its current pricing page lists Basic free with Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet support, AI chat within/across meetings, AI meeting workflows, live transcription, speaker identification, multi-language support, mobile apps, and 300 monthly transcription minutes. Paid tiers increase recording minutes, file imports, meeting length, storage, integrations, admin features, concurrent meetings, and enterprise controls.

**Best for:** education, interviews, teams that need live captions, collaborative transcripts, and people who want a familiar dedicated transcription workflow.

**Watch-out:** the free plan is useful but capped. If the buyer records many calls each week, compare paid Otter against Fathom and Fireflies before standardizing.

### 4. Read AI

[Read AI](/tools/read-ai/) is best when meeting notes are part of a broader productivity and coaching layer. Its current site positions Read around meeting summaries, transcripts, real-time notes, playback, Search Copilot, apps, integrations, and Ada, its digital twin. The pricing page lists Free with 5 meeting transcripts per month, meeting summaries, transcription, search, meeting coach, basic integrations, and 20+ languages. Paid tiers add unlimited meeting transcripts, upload credits, premium integrations, unlimited reports/storage, video playback, highlights, enterprise search, workspace controls, and enterprise security options.

**Best for:** teams that want meeting reports, coaching trends, search across workplace context, and a wider productivity layer than raw transcription.

**Watch-out:** the free plan is narrower than Fathom's for raw meeting volume. Read AI becomes more compelling when reporting, coaching, search, and workspace controls are the reason to buy.

### 5. NotebookLM

[NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/) is not a live meeting recorder. It is the best free companion after a meeting when the transcript, notes, slide deck, source docs, or recording-derived text already exists. Google's NotebookLM upgrade help lists browser availability, free and paid plan paths, and usage limits for notebooks, sources, chats, audio overviews, video overviews, reports, flashcards, quizzes, mind maps, deep research, and sharing features.

**Best for:** students, researchers, consultants, journalists, and teams turning transcripts into grounded summaries, briefs, study materials, and follow-up questions.

**Watch-out:** NotebookLM is a post-meeting source workspace, not a replacement for Fathom, Fireflies, Otter, or Read AI when you need automatic recording and live capture.

## What Not To Do

Do not choose a meeting bot only by transcript accuracy claims. Test on your real audio: accents, interruptions, background noise, meeting size, and platform setup matter.

Do not deploy meeting recording broadly without a consent policy. Meeting tools can create trust issues if attendees do not know what is being recorded, where notes go, or how long recordings are retained.

Do not buy a team plan before testing CRM handoff, Slack/Notion/Zapier workflows, admin controls, retention, and whether summaries are actually useful to the people who read them.

Do not use NotebookLM as a live notetaker. Use it after the transcript exists.

## FAQ

**What is the best free AI meeting notes tool?**  
Fathom is the best free first test for most individuals because the free plan includes unlimited recordings and transcriptions. NotebookLM is the best free post-meeting review tool once you already have source material.

**Is Fireflies better than Fathom?**  
Fireflies is usually better for team meeting intelligence, analytics, integrations, admin controls, and searchable call history. Fathom is usually easier as the first individual notetaker.

**Is Otter still worth it?**  
Yes, especially for live transcription, speaker identification, collaborative transcripts, and teams that already like Otter's meeting workflow. Watch the free-minute cap before relying on it heavily.

**Where does Read AI fit?**  
Read AI fits when you want meeting reports, coaching, workplace search, email/message context, and a broader productivity surface around meetings.
