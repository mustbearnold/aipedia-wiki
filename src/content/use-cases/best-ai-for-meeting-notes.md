# Best AI for Meeting Notes (2026)

AI tools for meeting notes transcribe audio from Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams calls, generate summaries, extract action items, and share outputs. As of April 2026, specialized tools outperform general models like ChatGPT with OpenAI frontier models or Gemini 3.1 Pro for accuracy in real-time transcription and workflow integration[2][4].

## Quick Verdict

**Fathom** tops the list for its free tier with unlimited transcription, accurate summaries, and simple sharing. **Otter.ai** follows as runner-up for teams needing live collaboration and calendar sync. Both handle most use cases without cost barriers[4].

## Top Picks

### 1. Fathom
Fathom records meetings across Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and in-person via its app, then auto-generates transcripts, summaries, and action items. Users highlight its speed: notes ready in seconds post-call with 95% transcription accuracy for clear audio. It integrates with Slack, Notion, and email for sharing clips or full notes. No bots join calls; it uses device-level recording for privacy. As of 2026-04-15, the flagship version is Fathom 4.2, released March 2026, adding multi-language support for 20+ languages and speaker diarization[4].

**Why it wins for meeting notes:** Zero learning curve; free plan covers unlimited meetings for individuals. Summaries focus on key decisions and tasks, reducing review time by 80% per user reports.

**Pricing (2026-04-15):** Free (unlimited storage, basic sharing); Pro $19/user/month (custom templates, team spaces, API access)[4].

**Limitations:** Lacks advanced analytics like sentiment tracking; Pro needed for team features. High background noise reduces accuracy to 85%[4].

### 2. Otter.ai
Otter.ai provides real-time transcription during live calls on Zoom, Meet, or Teams, with live captions for participants. It identifies speakers, highlights keywords, and auto-pulls action items into a collaborative notepad. The 2026 flagship Otter 5.1, launched February 2026, includes OtterPilot for automated note-taking and integration with Google Calendar, Outlook, Slack, and Salesforce[2].

**Why it wins for meeting notes:** Strong for teams; real-time editing and search across past notes. Handles 50+ languages with 92% accuracy on standard audio.

**Pricing (2026-04-15):** Free (300 minutes/month, 3 lifetime uploads); Pro $10/user/month (6,000 minutes, custom vocabulary); Business $20/user/month (unlimited, analytics)[2].

**Limitations:** Free tier limits minutes; occasional speaker confusion in overlapping talk. Enterprise setup requires admin approval[2].

(172 words)

### 3. Fireflies.ai
Fireflies.ai joins calls as a bot to record, transcribe, and analyze discussions across 60+ platforms including Zoom, Teams, and Webex. It generates summaries, tracks tasks, and offers conversation intelligence like sentiment and topic trends. Flagship version Fireflies 3.5 from April 2026 adds AI Ask for querying past meetings and CRM integrations[3].

**Why it wins for meeting notes:** Analytics suit sales or managers; searchable knowledge base from all notes. 94% accuracy with noise cancellation.

**Pricing (2026-04-15):** Free (800 minutes storage); Pro $10/user/month (unlimited storage, custom domains); Enterprise $19/user/month (SSO, advanced security)[3].

**Limitations:** Bot presence can feel intrusive; storage caps on free plan. Slower processing for long calls (over 2 hours)[3].

(158 words)

### 4. NotebookLM
Google's NotebookLM turns meeting recordings or uploads into interactive notebooks with summaries, timelines, and Q&A. Powered by Gemini 3.1 Pro as of 2026, it processes audio, PDFs, or notes for grounded answers without hallucinations. Version 2.3, updated March 2026, supports video input and shares notebooks via links[2][4].

**Why it wins for meeting notes:** Free, private processing; excels at querying specifics like "What was decided on budget?" from full context.

**Pricing (2026-04-15):** Free (unlimited for personal Google accounts); Gemini Advanced $19.99/month unlocks longer contexts[2].

**Limitations:** No live transcription; requires manual upload. Best for post-meeting review, not real-time[4].
