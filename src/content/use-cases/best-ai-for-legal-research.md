# Best AI for Legal Research (Archived)

AiPedia removed the previous version of this page from the active index on May 9, 2026. This archive note was rechecked on June 6, 2026 and remains noindexed.

The old guide made legal-research recommendations using generic AI-model claims, stale pricing/version language, and weak third-party ranking sources. That is not good enough for a high-stakes legal buyer page. A trustworthy legal-research guide needs current source-backed coverage of legal databases, jurisdiction limits, citation reliability, privilege and confidentiality, hallucination risk, professional-responsibility constraints, and human lawyer review.

## June 6, 2026 Status

AiPedia found stronger legal AI signals since the old page was archived:

- Anthropic announced Claude for Legal on May 12, 2026, with 20+ MCP connectors and 12 legal workflow plugins.
- Thomson Reuters is building the next generation of CoCounsel Legal as an agentic platform grounded in Westlaw and Practical Law, with beta access and lawyer-controlled reasoning review.
- Thomson Reuters' public CoCounsel FAQ says customer content and prompts are not used to train CoCounsel or associated LLMs, and describes contractual restrictions on third-party model training.
- Spellbook's current pricing page uses custom pricing, a 7-day trial, user-count based licensing, and public security claims including SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, and CCPA.
- Harvey remains relevant as an enterprise legal/professional-services AI platform, especially where firm documents, deployment governance, and matter-specific context matter.

Those are useful rebuild inputs, but they are not enough to re-index a buyer guide. AiPedia still needs jurisdiction-specific caveats, product-by-product legal authority coverage, citation behavior checks, privilege handling, and lawyer-reviewed workflow guidance before this page can safely rank recommendations.

## Where To Go Instead

- Use [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) only for cited web research and early issue spotting, not as legal authority.
- Use [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) for document summarization and drafting support only when a qualified human checks sources and legal conclusions.
- Use [Harvey](/tools/harvey/), CoCounsel Legal, Spellbook, LexisNexis, Westlaw, or a firm-approved legal AI system when the work is privileged, client-facing, or practice-specific.
- Use [best AI tools for researchers](/guides/best-ai-tools-for-researchers/) for general research workflows that are not legal advice.
- Use [best AI for citations](/guides/best-ai-for-citations/) for citation-integrity workflows outside formal legal advice.

## Rebuild Criteria

AiPedia should only re-index this route after rebuilding it around:

- legal-specific tools and databases,
- current official vendor sources,
- jurisdiction and practice-area limits,
- privilege, confidentiality, and retention handling,
- professional-responsibility caveats,
- human lawyer review requirements,
- citation verification and authority-check workflow,
- a clear "not legal advice" warning,
- separate buyer paths for students, solo lawyers, firms, legal operations, and in-house counsel,
- explicit language on when a generic chatbot is unacceptable.

Until then, keeping this page indexed would hurt trust more than it would help traffic.
