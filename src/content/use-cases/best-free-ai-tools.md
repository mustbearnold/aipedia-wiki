# Best Free AI Tools (June 2026)

The best free AI tool for most people is still **[ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/)** because it is the easiest place to learn everyday AI. But the best free stack is not one tool. In June 2026, AiPedia recommends **ChatGPT or Gemini** as the default assistant, **Claude** for careful writing feedback, **Perplexity** for cited web research, and **NotebookLM** for work grounded in your own sources.

Start free. Upgrade only when a free limit repeatedly blocks real work, not because every model launch creates subscription FOMO.

Verified June 12, 2026 against current official OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, NotebookLM, and Perplexity sources.

## Quick Verdict

### Best free overall: ChatGPT

Pick [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) first if you want one free account for everyday questions, writing help, learning, planning, GPTs, and general AI exploration. OpenAI's Free Tier FAQ makes the key constraint clear: free access has rate limits, and upgrading can reset or improve usage access.

Upgrade only when the limits waste time or when paid tools are part of a repeat workflow. If you ask a few questions a day, stay free.

### Best free Google path: Gemini

Pick [Gemini](/tools/gemini/) if your real workflow lives in Search, Gmail, Docs, Drive, Android, YouTube, or Google accounts. Google AI Pro is the paid bundle for more capable Gemini access, Deep Research, a larger context window, Gemini in Gmail/Docs, NotebookLM benefits, and cloud storage; that makes free Gemini the right no-cost trial before a bundle decision.

Upgrade only if Google-native capacity is the bottleneck. Do not pay for Google AI Pro just because you already use Google products.

### Best free writing second opinion: Claude

Pick [Claude](/tools/claude/) for editing, critique, rewriting, summarizing, and thoughtful prose feedback. Anthropic's current plan guide still lists a Free plan for limited occasional use, with Pro and Max plans for heavier usage.

Use Claude Free as a writing second opinion. Upgrade only when you want Claude as a daily work partner.

### Best free cited research habit: Perplexity

Pick [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) when the answer needs sources. It is not the broadest creative assistant, but it teaches the right habit: trace claims back to evidence before you write, buy, or publish.

Upgrade when research volume, file use, model choice, or advanced search needs become serious.

### Best free source-grounded workspace: NotebookLM

Pick [NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/) when your own materials are the source of truth: PDFs, notes, transcripts, reports, slides, articles, and documents. Google's help page says users can sign up free with a Gmail account, while higher limits and features are available through Google AI plans, Google Cloud, or qualifying Workspace plans.

Use NotebookLM when accuracy depends on assigned or uploaded sources. Use Perplexity when accuracy depends on the open web.

## Best Free Stack by User

### Students

Use Gemini for Google-native drafting and study help, NotebookLM for assigned-source study, Perplexity for cited web research, and ChatGPT or Claude for tutoring and feedback.

Do not use any free tool to invent citations, fake reading, or bypass school policy.

### Writers

Use ChatGPT for ideation and outlines, Claude for critique and rewrites, Perplexity for source checks, and NotebookLM when writing from a defined source pack.

Upgrade only when the writing workflow is daily and the free limits slow you down.

### Researchers

Use Perplexity for open-web source discovery, NotebookLM for source packs, Gemini for Google-native workflows, and Claude for careful synthesis.

Keep primary-source inspection in the workflow. Free AI research is a discovery layer, not a citation substitute.

### Solo builders

Use ChatGPT and Claude for requirements, debugging thoughts, and architecture questions, then move to the [AI coding assistant guide](/guides/best-ai-coding-assistant/) if the actual job is IDE coding.

Free chat is not enough for serious repo work if you need tool execution, codebase context, or repeatable tests.

### Casual users

Start with ChatGPT Free. Add Gemini only if Google integration matters. Add Perplexity only when you need source-backed answers. Add NotebookLM only when you have documents to query.

## What Free Plans Are Good For

Free AI tools are excellent for:

- learning what AI can and cannot do
- drafting low-risk text
- asking everyday questions
- checking whether a paid plan would help
- light research with source inspection
- studying source packs
- comparing assistant style before buying

Free AI tools are weak for:

- guaranteed high-volume work
- sensitive company data without approved controls
- production automation
- regulated decisions
- expensive model features
- team governance
- any workflow where limits create missed deadlines

## Upgrade Triggers

Upgrade when one of these is true:

- You hit limits several times in the same real workflow.
- The paid plan replaces paid labor or saves billable time.
- You need higher limits, team controls, admin settings, or auditability.
- You need Google Workspace, NotebookLM, or storage benefits bundled with AI.
- You need consistent research, files, or model access that free tiers cannot provide.

Do not upgrade because:

- a social post says a free plan is "unlimited"
- a model name changed
- you want to collect subscriptions
- one impressive demo does not match your daily work

## Safe Free Stack

For most people, use this sequence:

1. Pick one default assistant: ChatGPT or Gemini.
2. Add one source tool: Perplexity.
3. Add one document tool: NotebookLM.
4. Add Claude when writing quality matters.
5. Pay only after a repeated limit blocks a workflow.

## FAQ

**What is the best free AI tool overall?**
ChatGPT Free for most people. It is the broadest beginner-friendly surface and the easiest way to learn AI before paying.

**What is the best free AI tool for students?**
Gemini, NotebookLM, and Perplexity. Gemini fits Google school workflows, NotebookLM works from assigned sources, and Perplexity helps with cited research.

**What is the best free AI writing tool?**
Claude Free is the best second-opinion writing assistant. ChatGPT Free is better if you also want a broader all-purpose assistant.

**Are free AI tools safe for work data?**
Not automatically. Check vendor terms, privacy settings, admin controls, and your company policy before uploading sensitive business data.

**Should I pay for ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, or Google AI Pro first?**
Use the [under-$20 AI tools guide](/guides/best-ai-tools-under-20-month/) if you are ready to pay. ChatGPT Plus is the broadest first paid subscription, Claude Pro is the writing and analysis alternative, and Google AI Pro is the Google-native bundle.
