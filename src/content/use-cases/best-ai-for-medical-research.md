# Best AI for Medical Research (Archived)

AiPedia removed the previous version of this page from the active index on May 9, 2026. This archive note was re-verified on May 13, 2026.

The old guide made high-stakes medical-research recommendations using stale April 2026 model/pricing language, generic chatbot rankings, weak third-party sources, and unsupported claims about multimodal scan analysis, clinical-trial reasoning, and medical research accuracy. That is not good enough for a medical or biomedical research buyer page.

Medical research is not a normal productivity use case. A trustworthy guide needs medical-specific evidence standards, privacy guidance, source verification, clinical/biomedical scope limits, regulatory caveats, and clear separation between research support, clinical decision support, and regulated medical-device software.

## Where To Go Instead

- Use [best AI tools for researchers](/guides/best-ai-tools-for-researchers/) for general literature-review, paper discovery, and source-backed research workflows.
- Use [best AI for citations](/guides/best-ai-for-citations/) for citation context, source trails, and evidence-checking workflows.
- Use [Elicit](/tools/elicit/) for structured literature-review workflows when the job is paper screening and extraction.
- Use [Semantic Scholar](/tools/semantic-scholar/) for free academic search and citation trails.
- Use [Scite](/tools/scite/) for citation context around medical or biomedical papers.
- Use [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) only for cited web research and early source discovery, not as medical authority.
- Use [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) only for summarization, explanation, drafting, or code support when a qualified researcher checks the sources and conclusions.

## Rebuild Criteria

AiPedia should only re-index this route after rebuilding it around:

- medical and biomedical research-specific tools,
- current official vendor sources,
- current FDA, NIH, and relevant regulator guidance where applicable,
- privacy, PHI, IRB, institutional-policy, and data-handling caveats,
- separation between research support and clinical decision support,
- explicit warning against using consumer AI tools for diagnosis or treatment decisions,
- source-backed literature-review workflows,
- citation verification and primary-source inspection,
- human expert review,
- no unsupported claims about scan interpretation, clinical outcomes, or medical accuracy.
