# Best AI Email Triage for Heavy Inboxes (June 2026)

A founder, executive, or professional with 3000+ unread emails has a specific problem: the inbox is full of mail that should not have been there. Newsletters, automated reports, transactional emails, low-priority CCs, and one-time correspondence drown out the messages that actually need attention. Discipline does not fix this. Filters partially fix it. Machine learning trained on years of your actual email behavior fixes it.

This guide is for the specific buyer: someone whose inbox has crossed the threshold where it cannot be managed manually. AiPedia verified pricing and capabilities on June 12, 2026.

The short version: **SaneBox** wins because its core feature, ML triage trained on your behavior, is the only thing that scales to heavy inboxes without changing your email client. **Superhuman** wins when you can migrate clients and speed is the dominant value. **Shortwave** wins when you want an AI assistant that drafts and summarizes within Gmail.

## Quick Verdict

Use [SaneBox](/tools/sanebox/) when you cannot or do not want to change your email client. SaneBox works on top of any IMAP inbox (Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, FastMail, custom) and routes incoming mail into focused folders based on ML trained on your specific behavior. You keep Gmail or Outlook; SaneBox sits underneath as a filter layer.

Use Superhuman when you can migrate to a new email client and value speed above all. Superhuman is a complete email client with AI features for triage, drafting, and search. Different product, different price, different commitment.

Use Shortwave when you want a Gmail-native experience with deep AI features for drafting, summarizing, and asking questions about your inbox. Built on top of Gmail, no client migration required.

## Why Heavy Inboxes Need Their Own Answer

Three reasons the generic "best email tool" guide misses this buyer:

- **Manual rules do not scale.** A heavy inbox receives mail from hundreds of distinct senders patterns. Rules cover a small fraction. ML trained on your behavior covers the rest.
- **Client migration is expensive at scale.** Switching from Gmail to Superhuman is a multi-week behavior change. For some users it pays off; for others it does not.
- **AI drafting is downstream of triage.** Drafting tools only help if you see the email. For heavy inboxes, triage is the bottleneck, not drafting.

SaneBox addresses the triage problem without forcing a client migration. That structural choice is the reason it wins this niche.

## 1. SaneBox: Best ML Triage Without Client Migration

[SaneBox](/tools/sanebox/) wins this category because of one structural decision: it works on top of your existing email client.

The mechanic: connect SaneBox to your IMAP inbox (Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, FastMail, custom). It scans the historical mail to learn patterns: which senders you reply to, which you ignore, which you read but do not respond to, which you delete unread. Then on every new email, it predicts importance and routes accordingly. Important mail stays in Inbox. Less important goes to SaneLater. Newsletters go to SaneNews. Unimportant CCs go to SaneCC.

A daily digest emails a list of what was routed away, so nothing disappears silently.

**Best plan:** Snack is the entry tier with the core triage plus three of the six features. Lunch adds three more features. Dinner is the full feature set.

**Why it wins:**

- **ML triage** trained on your specific behavior, not generic rules.
- **Works on any IMAP inbox.** No client migration. No changing your address. No changing your existing rules and filters.
- **SaneBlackHole** permanently blocks senders with one drag.
- **SaneLater** holds non-urgent mail and surfaces only the important.
- **SaneNoReply** tracks emails you sent that did not get a response.
- **SaneCC** routes CCs separately so they do not drown the main inbox.
- **SaneReminders** for follow-up automation.
- **Daily digest** so nothing routed away disappears silently.
- **No data training across users.** Your model is yours.

**Watch-outs:**

- The ML takes 1-2 weeks to settle. Early routing decisions will need correction.
- SaneBox is a filtering layer, not a drafting or summarization tool. For those, pair with another tool.
- Pricing is per inbox, not per user. Multiple inboxes (work + personal) cost extra.
- The product is mature and slightly old-fashioned in UI compared to newer AI tools. The wedge is the underlying ML, not interface polish.
- Heavy power users sometimes hit edge cases in routing. The fix is to use BlackHole or training corrections actively.

[Try SaneBox →](https://www.sanebox.com/)

## 2. Superhuman: Best for Speed-Focused Client Migration

Superhuman is a complete email client. Faster keyboard shortcuts, AI summaries, AI drafts, snippets, scheduled send, follow-up tracking.

**When it's the right pick:**

- You can commit to changing email clients.
- You value speed above all (true power users save real hours).
- AI features inside the client (drafting, summarizing) are useful.

**When SaneBox wins instead:**

- You cannot change clients (corporate Outlook, IT mandate, multi-account complexity).
- Triage is the bottleneck more than drafting speed.
- You want a lower price floor.

## 3. Shortwave: Best Gmail-Native AI Assistant

Shortwave is Gmail-native with an AI assistant layer. Summarize threads, draft replies, ask questions about your inbox, build automated workflows.

**When it's the right pick:**

- You live in Gmail.
- AI drafting and summarization are the lift you want, not triage.
- You want a slightly different client UX but staying on Gmail underneath.

**When SaneBox wins instead:**

- Triage is the bottleneck.
- You use Outlook or iCloud, not Gmail.
- Pricing matters and you do not need the AI drafting depth.

## 4. Microsoft Copilot for Outlook

If you live in Microsoft 365 and have Copilot, it is the right default. Native, bundled, drafts and summarizes inside Outlook. Lacks the deep ML triage of SaneBox but for many users covers enough.

Pair pattern: Copilot + SaneBox on the same Outlook account is fine and addresses different problems.

## Pricing Reality

Use this as buying guidance, not a fixed stack total:

- **SaneBox:** The current pricing page lists Snack at $4.99/month, Lunch at $7.99/month, and Dinner at $24.99/month. Buy it when you want filtering beneath your existing Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, FastMail, or IMAP inbox.
- **Superhuman:** The current Superhuman Suite page lists Pro at $12/member/month billed annually or $30 monthly, and Business at $33/member/month billed annually or $40 monthly. Business is the safer reference point when Mail is the reason you are buying.
- **Shortwave:** The current pricing page lists Business at $24/seat/month billed annually, Premier at $36, and Max at $100. Choose it when Gmail-native AI search, summaries, filters, and team email workflow matter more than classic inbox filtering.
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot:** Still budget as a Microsoft 365 add-on or bundled SKU decision, not a standalone inbox tool. Microsoft has July 1, 2026 SMB SKU changes coming, so verify the exact tenant price before recommending it to a team.

SaneBox still has the clearest low-cost entry for heavy-inbox triage; Superhuman and Shortwave are higher-commitment email-client decisions.

## Setup Time

- **SaneBox:** 1-2 weeks because its filtering gets better after it watches and learns your mailbox behavior.
- **Superhuman:** 1-2 weeks because migration, shortcuts, and team habits matter as much as AI features.
- **Shortwave:** About 1 hour for Gmail connection and first useful AI summaries, with deeper payoff after labels and filters settle.
- **Microsoft Copilot:** Immediate if the Microsoft 365 tenant, license, and Outlook setup are already in place.

## Failure Modes

- **Expecting SaneBox to work instantly.** The ML needs time. Correct mistakes for the first two weeks rather than abandoning.
- **Pairing SaneBox with aggressive rules.** Your existing rules and SaneBox's training can conflict. Audit rules after deploying SaneBox.
- **Using SaneBox without checking the digest.** The digest is the safety mechanism. Skip it for two weeks and you may miss real mail that got routed away.
- **Migrating to Superhuman without committing to keyboard shortcuts.** The speed payoff requires investment in learning the keystrokes. Without that, Superhuman is just an expensive Gmail.
- **Treating Shortwave drafting as ground truth.** AI-drafted email needs human review before sending, especially for important relationships.
