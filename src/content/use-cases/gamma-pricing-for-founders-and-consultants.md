# Gamma Pricing for Founders and Consultants (June 2026)

Gamma pricing is a workflow decision, not just a deck-tool subscription. A founder or consultant needs to decide whether the bottleneck is first-draft speed, branding removal, custom domains, analytics, API generation, or heavy AI-credit use before paying for Plus, Pro, or Ultra.

AiPedia rechecked Gamma's pricing page, subscription help, API help, and affiliate help on June 28, 2026. The pricing page loaded in a browser and listed Individual annual-billing prices at Free $0, Plus $9 per seat per month, Pro $18 per seat per month, and Ultra $90 per seat per month. The help center confirms Free, Plus, Pro, Ultra, Teams, and Business plans, plus plan gates for credits, cards per prompt, API access, custom domains, analytics, and paid-plan credit usage. Two current Gamma sources conflict on details: the help article says Pro generates up to 50 cards per prompt while the pricing page lists 60, and the help FAQ says Ultra is only available monthly while the pricing page displays annual Ultra pricing. Treat those points as live-checkout checks before buying.

## Quick Verdict

Use [Gamma](/tools/gamma/) when the job is to turn an outline, memo, pitch idea, discovery-call notes, PDF, or rough brief into a deck, doc, webpage, or shareable client link quickly.

Start on **Free** with one real but client-safe project. Move to **Plus** when Gamma branding, the 10-card prompt limit, or the free-credit model blocks repeat work. Move to **Pro** when the output is client-facing and custom branding, analytics, password protection, API access, custom domains, or a higher prompt card cap matter. Inspect **Ultra** only when Pro limits are already visible.

Do not buy Gamma as a brand-governance system, a strict PowerPoint replacement, or a proposal automation suite. Use [Beautiful.ai](/tools/beautiful-ai/) for controlled business slides, [Pitch](/tools/pitch/) for team deck workflow, [Canva](/tools/canva/) for broad design work, and [Decktopus](/tools/decktopus/) when the proposal link needs forms, booking, or response capture.

## Free Is The Right First Test

Gamma Free is the right starting point when the buyer has not tested a real deliverable. The current help center says Free can generate up to 10 cards in a single prompt, with more cards added manually. It also says free-plan AI credits do not refresh, although users can earn more credits through referrals and Free accounts can hold up to 2,000 credits at once per user.

Use Free to test:

- one founder pitch deck
- one consultant leave-behind
- one workshop or course outline
- one memo-to-deck conversion
- one shareable webpage version of a deck
- one PDF or PPTX import and export loop

If the output still needs a full rebuild, do not solve that by upgrading. Fix the prompt, source material, or deck structure first.

## Plus Is The Smallest Serious Upgrade

Plus is the first upgrade to inspect when the work is recurring but still solo and lightweight. Gamma's current subscription help says Plus adds 1,000 monthly credits, basic and advanced image models, Gamma branding removal, and up to 20 cards in a single prompt.

Plus makes sense when:

- the buyer sends public or client-facing links and cannot keep Gamma branding
- the 10-card prompt limit creates too much manual extension work
- image generation matters but premium models are not required
- the buyer does not need API access, custom domains, detailed analytics, or password protection

Skip Plus and test Pro if the deck is part of a measured client workflow, publication workflow, or repeatable business-development process.

## Pro Is The Practical Client-Work Plan

Pro is the plan AiPedia would inspect first for serious founder, consultant, or boutique-agency use. The current help center says Pro includes everything in Plus, plus 4,000 monthly credits, API access, custom fonts, headers and footers, password protection, premium image models, up to 50 cards per prompt, up to 10 custom domains, detailed analytics, and more advanced sharing. Gamma's pricing page currently lists Pro at up to 60 cards per prompt, so buyers who care about the exact cap should verify it in checkout or account upgrade flow.

Buy Pro when:

- client-facing Gamma pages need a custom domain
- analytics change follow-up behavior
- password protection matters for client reviews
- custom branding and fonts reduce cleanup
- a founder or consultant wants to publish repeated investor, sales, or advisory pages
- API access will generate decks, docs, webpages, or social posts from structured inputs

Do not buy Pro before testing export cleanup. Gamma is excellent for first drafts and shareable links, but a PowerPoint-first client may still require manual slide cleanup.

## Ultra Is For Proven Heavy Use

Ultra is not the default founder or consultant recommendation. It is the plan to inspect after Pro limits are real. Gamma's current help center says Ultra adds 20,000 monthly credits, access to the most advanced models, API access, up to 75 cards per prompt, basic through ultra image models, up to 100 custom domains, detailed analytics, and early feature access such as Studio Mode.

Buy Ultra only when at least one of these is true:

- Pro monthly credits are already too tight
- API generation is a real workflow, not a future idea
- the buyer publishes many Gamma sites or client portals
- long initial generation saves enough time to justify the higher tier
- ultra image models or early features materially change output quality

The current pricing page lists Ultra at $90 per seat per month when billed annually, while the help FAQ says Ultra is brand new and only available monthly. Treat that as a live-checkout caveat. Confirm the billing toggle and renewal total before standardizing on Ultra.

## Credit Rules That Matter

Gamma credits can be easy to misread. The current help center says paid-plan standard presentations and images are included, while credits are mainly used for Ultra AI models and API access. It also says paid-plan credits refresh monthly and unused credits can roll over up to double the plan size.

Model credits around actual behavior:

| Workflow | Why it changes the plan choice |
|---|---|
| Prompt-to-deck drafting only | Free or Plus may be enough if branding and card count are not blocking. |
| Client-facing share links | Plus removes branding; Pro adds protection, analytics, and custom domains. |
| Published microsites | Pro or Ultra matters when custom domains and site count become part of delivery. |
| API generation | Pro is the entry point; Ultra is safer when API volume burns credits. |
| Long initial decks | Pro raises the card cap, but current Gamma sources disagree on 50 versus 60; Ultra raises it to 75. |
| Advanced image generation | Plus, Pro, and Ultra step up image-model access, with Ultra getting ultra image models. |

The safest purchase path is to run one representative project and record what actually blocked the workflow: branding, card count, custom domain, analytics, API access, or credits.

## When Gamma Is The Wrong Purchase

Choose a different tool when:

- **The sales team needs brand lockdown:** use [Beautiful.ai](/tools/beautiful-ai/) or the [SMB sales deck guide](/guides/best-presentation-tool-for-smb-sales-teams/).
- **The deck is a team asset:** use [Pitch](/tools/pitch/) when comments, rooms, guests, versioning, and team analytics matter.
- **The buyer needs broad design work:** use [Canva](/tools/canva/) when presentations are only one part of a social, print, video, and brand asset workflow.
- **The proposal needs response capture:** use [Decktopus](/tools/decktopus/) or the [consultant proposal-deck guide](/guides/best-ai-proposal-deck-tool-for-consultants/).
- **The client requires exact PowerPoint control:** use PowerPoint or Google Slides as the source of truth and treat Gamma as a draft generator.

## Buying Checklist

Before paying for Gamma beyond a test, answer these questions:

1. Will the final deliverable be a Gamma link, a webpage, a PDF, a Google Slides file, or a PowerPoint file?
2. Does the buyer need branding removal, custom fonts, or a custom domain?
3. Does analytics change follow-up, or is it just nice to have?
4. Will one person use the account, or will multiple users need seats?
5. Is API generation already scoped with structured inputs and review owners?
6. How much cleanup does a real exported deck need?
7. Which alternative will be tested before annual or monthly renewal?

If those answers are vague, stay on Free or Plus until the workflow has proof.

## Bottom Line

Gamma is worth paying for when it reliably turns rough material into useful client-facing decks, docs, webpages, or shareable links. **Free** proves the output. **Plus** removes the obvious solo limits. **Pro** is the practical plan for recurring client-facing work. **Ultra** is a power-user plan for high-volume, API, advanced-model, and custom-domain workflows.

## FAQ

### Which Gamma plan should most founders start with?

Start with Free and one real deck. Upgrade only after the buyer knows whether the blocker is branding, card count, custom domain, analytics, API access, or credits.

### Is Gamma Pro worth it for consultants?

Gamma Pro is worth inspecting when Gamma becomes a recurring client-deliverable workflow. API access, custom domains, detailed analytics, password protection, custom branding, custom fonts, and the higher Pro prompt card cap are the main reasons to move beyond Plus.

### Should I buy Gamma Ultra?

Only after Pro limits are proven. Ultra is for heavy users who need 20,000 monthly credits, advanced models, up to 75 cards per prompt, API generation, up to 100 custom domains, or early features.

### Is Gamma better than Beautiful.ai for consulting decks?

Gamma is better for fast first drafts and shareable card-based output. Beautiful.ai is better when the story is known and the deck needs controlled, brand-consistent business-slide layout.

### Can Gamma replace PowerPoint?

Sometimes for link-first or web-first work. It is not a perfect replacement when every stakeholder expects a conventional editable PowerPoint file with strict brand and chart control.
