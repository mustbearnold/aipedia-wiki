# Best AI Tools for Lawyers (Archived)

AiPedia removed the previous version of this page from the active index on May 9, 2026, and the rebuild bar moved sharply higher on May 12, 2026 when Anthropic launched [Claude for Legal](/news/2026-05-12-anthropic-claude-for-legal-plugins-microsoft-365/).

The old guide mixed generic chatbots, coding tools, stale April 2026 product claims, and unsupported legal-fit statements. That is not acceptable for a legal buyer page. Lawyers need source-backed guidance on confidentiality, document retention, jurisdiction, practice-area fit, citation checking, legal-database coverage, client-data handling, and human review.

## What Changed On May 12, 2026

Anthropic shipped:

- 12 practice-area Claude plugins covering Commercial, Corporate (M&A diligence), Employment, Privacy, Product, Regulatory, AI Governance, IP, Litigation, plus three role-specific plugins for corporate counsel, regulatory counsel, and law students.
- 20-plus new MCP connectors linking Claude to Harvey, Relativity, Everlaw, Thomson Reuters CoCounsel, DocuSign, and Box.
- A Microsoft 365 integration that embeds Claude inside Word, Outlook, Excel, and PowerPoint as a single context-carrying agent.
- Four launch firms running live matters on the stack: Freshfields, Quinn Emanuel Urquhart & Sullivan, Holland & Knight, and Crosby Legal.

This is the first frontier-lab vertical product built specifically around regulated legal workflows, and it materially changes what a credible "best AI for lawyers" guide must cover.

## Where To Go Instead

- Use [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) for drafting and summarization only with source review and professional oversight.
- Use [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) for cited web research and source discovery, not as legal authority.
- Use [best AI for summarization](/guides/best-ai-for-summarization/) for non-legal document-summary workflows.
- Use [best AI for citations](/guides/best-ai-for-citations/) for citation and source-management workflows.

## Rebuild Criteria

This page should only return to the active index after AiPedia rebuilds it around:

- Claude for Legal plugin coverage by practice area, with current Anthropic source links,
- the Harvey, Relativity, Everlaw, CoCounsel, DocuSign, and Box connector footprint,
- Microsoft 365 (Word, Outlook, Excel, PowerPoint) integration scope and limits,
- legal-specific AI products (Harvey, CoCounsel, Lexis+ AI, Westlaw AI, Spellbook, Eve, Crosby) with current vendor documentation,
- confidentiality, matter-data, and outside-counsel guideline implications,
- jurisdiction and practice-area caveats,
- professional-responsibility constraints and hallucination risk in filings,
- citation verification workflow,
- buyer flows for solo lawyers, in-house legal, BigLaw firms, legal ops, and students,
- clear "not legal advice" language near any legal-use recommendation.

Until then, noindexing the route protects AiPedia's trust and crawl quality.
