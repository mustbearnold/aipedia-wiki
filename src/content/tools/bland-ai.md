# Bland AI

Bland AI is an enterprise platform for building AI agents that make and take phone calls, texts, and now iMessages. Unlike orchestration platforms that let you plug in a language model, voice, and transcriber of your choice, Bland builds its own voice models in-house and runs them on infrastructure it owns end to end. The pitch is that voice is a distinct domain that general-purpose model stacks were not built for, especially for long, unscripted, high-stakes conversations in healthcare, insurance, and financial services.

Founded in 2023 out of Y Combinator's S23 batch, Bland closed a $50M Series C in June 2026, taking total funding past $100M. The company says it now handles more than 3.5 million calls a week for over 250 enterprise customers, including Samsara, Kin Insurance, and CNO Financial Group.

## Quick answer

Bland AI is worth it if you are an enterprise buyer in a regulated industry who wants a single vendor to own the voice model, the infrastructure, and the compliance paperwork, and you have budget for a $299 to $499+ monthly platform fee on top of per-minute usage. It is not the right pick if you want the cheapest voice-agent option, want to choose your own LLM and TTS providers, or need latency numbers you can trust without your own testing.

## What it actually is

Bland is voice-agent infrastructure that Bland itself owns top to bottom: its own speech-to-text and text-to-speech models, its own telephony orchestration, and its own hosting, rather than a layer that routes calls through OpenAI, ElevenLabs, Deepgram, or Twilio on your behalf. The company's argument is that stitching together general-purpose providers introduces latency and reliability problems that a purpose-built voice stack avoids, and that owning the full stack is what lets it sign HIPAA BAAs and offer on-prem or VPC deployment for Enterprise customers.

**Norm**, launched March 26, 2026, is Bland's prompt-to-agent builder. Describe an agent in plain English (for example, "build me a scheduling agent and integrate with Cal.com") and Norm generates the prompt, persona, conversation pathways, validation rules, and API integrations, then runs simulated calls against the new agent to surface edge cases before it goes live. Norm is also reachable over iMessage.

**iMessage** became a supported channel in May 2026, joining voice and SMS under the same pathway and persona-memory system, so a single conversation flow can span a phone call, a text, and an iMessage thread. Enterprise customers can get a verified branded business profile for iMessage instead of an unrecognized number.

**Noise Cancellation V2** filters background noise before it reaches the model, aimed at the common failure mode where a noisy environment (call centers, cars, retail floors) degrades transcription accuracy.

Onboarding runs through a **Forward Deployed Engineer** team that builds a customer's first agent end to end, owning the pathway design, integrations, and test loop rather than leaving the customer to self-serve. Bland cites a typical two-to-six-week deployment timeline for this process.

## When to pick Bland AI

- **You are in a regulated industry.** SOC 2 Type I/II, HIPAA with signed BAA, PCI DSS v4.0, and GDPR/DPA are all listed as current certifications, and Bland positions itself explicitly as "built for complex, regulated industries."
- **You want one vendor to own the whole stack.** No assembling an LLM, TTS, STT, and telephony provider yourself; Bland's proprietary models and infrastructure are the product.
- **Your calls are long and unscripted.** Bland's own positioning leans on handling 30-45 minute conversations that wander, get interrupted, or hit questions no script anticipated, rather than short scripted IVR-style calls.
- **You want hands-on onboarding.** The Forward Deployed Engineer model suits enterprise buyers who want a vendor team building the first agent rather than a self-serve dashboard.
- **You need on-prem or VPC deployment.** Available on Enterprise, which matters for buyers who cannot send call audio to third-party cloud infrastructure at all.

## When to pick something else

- **You want to choose your own model, voice, and transcriber:** [Vapi](/tools/vapi/) is a bring-your-own-provider orchestration platform with a lower entry platform fee (about $0.05/min) and no fixed monthly minimum.
- **You want transparent pay-as-you-go with no platform fee at any tier:** [Retell AI](/tools/retell-ai/) charges purely per-minute ($0.07-$0.31/min) and per-message with $10 in free credits, and adds concurrency and features as line items rather than a flat monthly fee.
- **You are budget-constrained or a solo builder:** Bland's Build tier alone is $299/mo before any usage, which prices out small teams that Vapi or Retell can serve on pure usage pricing.
- **You need independently verified low latency today:** treat Bland's sub-400ms Turbo claim as a target, not a guarantee, and benchmark against your own call patterns before committing budget.
- **Voice cloning or narration is the core job, not phone calls:** [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/) is built for voice quality and cloning rather than call orchestration.

## Pricing

Verified July 2, 2026 directly against [bland.ai/pricing](https://www.bland.ai/pricing) and cross-checked against [docs.bland.ai/platform/billing](https://docs.bland.ai/platform/billing), which match exactly.

| Plan | Platform fee | Per-minute rate | Concurrent calls | Who it's for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Start | $0/mo | $0.14/min | 10 | Testing and low-volume pilots, no card required |
| Build | $299/mo | $0.12/min | 50 | Teams running production call volume |
| Scale | $499/mo | $0.11/min | 100 | High-volume operations needing lower per-minute cost |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Unlimited | On-prem/VPC, dedicated infrastructure, contracted volume |

Additional line items apply on top of the base rate: transfer time is billed at $0.05/min (Start), $0.04/min (Build), or $0.03/min (Scale) when using a Bland-provided number, waived entirely if you bring your own Twilio (BYOT). SMS runs $0.02 per message, web widget messages are $0.01 flat, and failed or very short outbound call attempts carry a $0.015 minimum charge. Voicemail is billed as standard call time. LLM, speech-to-text, and text-to-speech are bundled into the per-minute rate rather than billed as separate pass-through costs, which is the opposite model from Vapi and Retell AI, where those providers are billed separately or at cost.

Enterprise pricing is custom and typically includes contracted volume discounts, unlimited concurrency, unlimited calls per day, unlimited voice clones and knowledge bases, dedicated orchestration servers, and the Forward Deployed Engineer onboarding team.

## System verdict

> **Pick Bland AI if you are an enterprise buyer in a regulated industry that wants one vendor to own the model, the infrastructure, and the compliance paperwork.** SOC 2, HIPAA with a signed BAA, PCI DSS, and GDPR are covered out of the box, and the Forward Deployed Engineer team builds the first agent for you rather than leaving you to self-serve a dashboard.
>
> **Skip it if you want the cheapest option, control over your own model stack, or latency you can bank on without testing it yourself.** Build starts at $299/mo before usage, the model and voice stack is closed, and independent tests have repeatedly clocked real-world latency well above the marketed sub-400ms figure.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Start for pilots and proof-of-concept work with no monthly commitment, Build for teams running real production volume, Scale for high-volume operations that want a lower per-minute rate, Enterprise for regulated organizations that need on-prem or VPC deployment and contracted terms.

<summary>Is Bland AI HIPAA compliant?</summary>

Bland lists itself as HIPAA-eligible with a signed BAA available, alongside SOC 2 Type I and II, PCI DSS v4.0, and GDPR/DPA. Confirm current BAA terms directly with Bland before handling protected health information, since compliance scope depends on your contract tier.

<details>
<summary>How does Bland AI's latency compare to its marketing claims?</summary>

Bland markets sub-400ms latency for its Turbo mode against a stated 1,240ms industry average. Independent testing and user reports have repeatedly found real production latency landing between roughly 700ms and 1,500ms, and higher under load. Treat the 400ms figure as a best-case target and benchmark against your own call patterns before relying on it.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use my own LLM or voice provider with Bland AI?</summary>

No. Bland runs on its own proprietary voice models and infrastructure rather than letting you bring your own LLM, TTS, or STT provider. If provider choice matters more than a bundled compliance-ready stack, [Vapi](/tools/vapi/) is built around bring-your-own-provider orchestration instead.
</details>

<summary>How does Bland AI compare to Vapi and Retell AI?</summary>

[Vapi](/tools/vapi/) is a bring-your-own-provider orchestration layer with a lower platform fee and no fixed monthly minimum. [Retell AI](/tools/retell-ai/) is pure pay-as-you-go with component pricing and no platform fee at any tier. Bland trades flexibility and lower entry cost for a closed, compliance-ready, white-glove package.
