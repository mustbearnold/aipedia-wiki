# Zapier

Zapier is the original no-code automation platform developed by Zapier Inc. It connects apps together through automated workflows called "Zaps" and is primarily used for automating repetitive tasks across business tools without writing code. Its key differentiator is the broadest integration library of any automation platform, with 6,000+ app connections. As of April 2026, Zapier offers a free tier with 100 tasks per month and paid plans from $19.99 to $299+ per month. Compared to Make, Zapier is easier to learn and has far more integrations, but it costs significantly more at high volume and handles complex logic less elegantly.

## What It Does

Zapier is the leading no-code automation platform that connects 6,000+ apps through trigger-action workflows called "Zaps," supporting multi-step sequences with branching, filters, and AI-powered features including chatbots and natural language automation ([Zapier](https://zapier.com)). A trigger in one app (new email, form submission, payment received) automatically fires actions in other apps (create row in spreadsheet, send Slack message, update CRM). Recent additions include AI Copilot for building Zaps from descriptions and AI Actions for exposing integrations to LLMs. It remains the easiest automation tool for non-technical users.

## Who It's For

- **Non-technical users:** the easiest automation platform to learn and use
- **Small business owners:** connect CRM, email, invoicing, and other tools without code
- **Marketing teams:** automate lead capture, nurture sequences, and reporting
- **Operations managers:** standardize processes across departments
- **Anyone starting with automation:** lowest learning curve in the category

## Key Features

- **6,000+ integrations:** the largest app directory of any automation platform, by a wide margin ([Zapier](https://zapier.com))
- **Simple trigger-action model:** if-this-then-that logic that anyone can understand
- **Multi-step Zaps:** chain multiple actions, add filters, paths (branching), and formatters
- **AI Copilot:** describe what you want to automate in natural language, Zapier builds the Zap
- **AI Chatbots:** build AI-powered chatbots connected to your business tools
- **AI Actions:** expose Zapier's integrations to LLMs for natural language automation
- **Tables:** built-in database for storing and processing automation data
- **Interfaces:** build simple forms and pages connected to Zaps

## Limitations

- **Expensive at scale:** task-based pricing adds up fast; a busy workflow can cost hundreds per month ([Zapier Pricing](https://zapier.com/pricing))
- **Limited complexity:** paths and filters exist but complex branching, looping, and error handling are weaker than Make or n8n
- **No self-hosting:** cloud-only; data passes through Zapier's servers
- **Task counting is aggressive:** every action in a multi-step Zap counts as a separate task; a 5-step Zap uses 5 tasks per trigger
- **Vendor lock-in:** workflows are not exportable; switching platforms means rebuilding everything
- **Performance at volume:** high-frequency Zaps can hit rate limits and require expensive plan upgrades
- **AI features are add-ons:** chatbots and AI actions cost additional per-usage fees

## Bottom Line

Zapier is the best choice for non-technical users who need simple automations across a wide variety of apps, but Make wins if complex multi-step logic and cost efficiency at scale are the priority. Start with Zapier for ease of use and integration breadth, switch to Make or n8n when costs or complexity demand it.

## FAQ

**Is Zapier free?**
Yes, Zapier offers a free tier with 100 tasks per month, 5 single-step Zaps, and 15-minute trigger intervals. It is enough for basic testing but not for real workloads.

**How much does Zapier cost?**
Zapier Starter costs $19.99/month for 750 tasks. Professional costs $49/month for 2,000 tasks with paths and custom logic. Team costs $69/month. Company plans start at $299+/month for 50,000+ tasks.

**What are the best alternatives to Zapier?**
Make (from $9/month) offers more complex workflow capabilities at a lower cost per operation. n8n is open-source, self-hostable, and developer-friendly with built-in AI agent nodes. Relevance AI focuses specifically on building AI agents rather than general workflow automation.
