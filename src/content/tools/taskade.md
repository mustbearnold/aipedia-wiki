# Taskade

Taskade is a collaborative workspace where tasks, documents, mind maps, video chat, and AI agents share one real-time canvas. The platform was founded in 2017 and pivoted hard into AI starting in 2023, positioning agents and a template library as primary surfaces.

Users build custom agents that read and write project data, trigger workflows, and join the same conversation threads as teammates.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Taskade if you want AI agents living inside project views, not bolted onto them.** Agents read the same tasks, docs, and mind maps your team sees, and they act on that state. The 700+ template library shortens cold-start time. Free tier supports real work.
>
> **Skip it if you need enterprise project portfolio management, a deep knowledge graph, or pure workflow plumbing.** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) wins on knowledge density. [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) wins on long-term personal knowledge. [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) and [Claude](/tools/claude/) win on agent intelligence. [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) wins on cross-app automation breadth.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for solo use with three agents, Pro $8/user/mo for most small teams, Team $12/user/mo for collaborative workspaces, Business $20/user/mo for admin and analytics, Enterprise custom for SSO and compliance.

## What it actually is

One workspace with multiple project views layered over a shared data model. The same list of tasks can render as a checklist, kanban board, mind map, calendar, or outline. Switching view does not duplicate the data.

AI Agents are first-class objects with instructions, tools, and knowledge files. An agent can read a project, edit tasks, generate a summary, or chat with the team inside the same workspace. Users build agents through a visual editor or prompt-driven setup.

The moat is integration density, not any single specialist capability. Taskade is weaker than Notion on knowledge management, weaker than Monday on enterprise PM, weaker than ChatGPT on raw agent intelligence. The trade is that small teams get all those surfaces in one place at one price.

## When to pick Taskade

- **Small teams that want AI baked into project views.** Agents read and act on the same tasks everyone else sees. No copy-paste between chatbot and PM tool.
- **Template-driven project starts.** 700+ templates cover meetings, OKRs, sprints, content calendars, and personal workflows. Faster than building from blank.
- **Mixed-format planning.** The same list renders as outline, board, mindmap, and calendar. Useful for teams whose members think in different structures.
- **Budget-sensitive buyers.** Pro at $8/user/mo (annual) undercuts Notion AI add-ons and ClickUp Business Plus on sticker price.
- **Solo founders and small ops teams.** Free tier supports real work (3 agents, 5 projects) before any payment decision.

## When to pick something else

- **Deep knowledge base with backlinks and graph view:** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) for company wikis, or [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) for long-term personal knowledge.
- **Agent intelligence above all else:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or [Claude](/tools/claude/). Stronger underlying models, broader tool ecosystems.
- **Cross-app automation plumbing:** [Zapier](/tools/zapier/). Thousands more integrations and more mature triggers.
- **Enterprise project portfolio management:** Monday, Asana, or ClickUp. Deeper resource, reporting, and governance features at scale.
- **AI scheduling as the primary job:** Motion. Purpose-built for calendar optimization.

## Failure modes

- **Agent intelligence is mid-tier.** Taskade routes to frontier providers but cannot match a dedicated ChatGPT or Claude session for hard reasoning. Users pushing agents past project-scoped tasks will hit quality ceilings.
- **Knowledge base depth trails Notion.** No true backlinks graph, weaker database views, thinner formula language. Teams whose primary need is a wiki should pick Notion.
- **Credit consumption is variable.** 5,000 Pro credits sound generous yet deplete fast under heavy agent use. Track usage before assuming the quota fits the workload.
- **Integrations breadth is narrower than Zapier.** Native connectors cover the common apps. Edge-case SaaS typically requires a Zapier bridge.
- **Enterprise governance is still maturing.** SSO and SCIM arrive only at the custom Enterprise tier. SOC 2 and similar certifications should be verified with sales before procurement.
- **Offline mode is limited.** Mobile and desktop apps rely on active sync. Teams working in low-connectivity environments should test before committing.
- **Feature breadth can overwhelm.** The same canvas holding tasks, docs, maps, chat, video, and agents creates cognitive load for new users. Template-led onboarding mitigates but does not eliminate this.
- **Pricing jumps steep between Business and Enterprise.** $20/user/mo to custom creates a large gap. Mid-size teams needing SSO sit awkwardly in the pricing ladder.

## FAQ

**Is Taskade free?**
Yes. The free tier includes 3 AI agents, 5 projects, 1,000 monthly AI credits, and real-time collaboration. Paid plans begin at Pro $8/user/mo on annual billing ([Taskade pricing](https://www.taskade.com/pricing)).

**What are Taskade AI Agents?**
Custom agents defined by instructions, tools, and knowledge files. Agents read project data, edit tasks, generate summaries, and chat inside the same workspace. Users build them through a visual editor or a prompt-driven setup ([Taskade AI](https://www.taskade.com/ai)).

**Which models power Taskade's AI?**
Taskade routes across GPT and Claude families for most agent work. Model choice is managed by Taskade and is not user-configurable on standard plans.

**How does Taskade compare to Notion AI?**
Different strengths. Notion wins on knowledge base depth, backlinks, and database power. Taskade wins on AI agents baked into project views, multi-view canvases (list, board, mindmap, calendar), and real-time video plus chat on the same surface. Knowledge-first teams pick Notion. Action-first small teams pick Taskade.

**How does Taskade compare to ClickUp or Monday?**
ClickUp and Monday target larger teams needing enterprise PM features such as resource management, advanced reporting, and governance. Taskade targets small and mid-size teams who want AI agents and multi-format planning in one lightweight workspace. Teams over 50 seats typically outgrow Taskade on PM depth.

**Does Taskade integrate with Zapier?**
Yes. Taskade connects to Zapier and has native integrations for the common SaaS stack (Google, Slack, calendar tools). For rare or long-tail apps, Zapier remains the broader automation layer.

**Is Taskade SOC 2 compliant?**
Enterprise-grade compliance details should be confirmed with Taskade sales. SSO and SCIM sit on the Enterprise tier. Procurement teams should review the current trust page before committing.
