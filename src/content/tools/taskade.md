# Taskade

Taskade is a collaborative workspace where tasks, documents, mind maps, video chat, and AI agents share one real-time canvas. The platform was founded in 2017 and pivoted hard into AI starting in 2023, positioning agents and a template library as primary surfaces.

Users build custom agents that read and write project data, trigger workflows, and join the same conversation threads as teammates.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Taskade if you want AI agents living inside project views, not bolted onto them.** Agents read the same tasks, docs, and mind maps your team sees, and they act on that state. The 700+ template library shortens cold-start time. Free tier supports real work, and Taskade now routes across 11+ frontier models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google with per-agent model selection.
>
> **Skip it if you need enterprise project portfolio management, a deep knowledge graph, or pure workflow plumbing.** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) wins on knowledge density. [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) wins on long-term personal knowledge. [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) and [Claude](/tools/claude/) win on agent intelligence; Claude Opus 4.7 (April 16) holds the agentic-coding lead. [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) wins on cross-app automation breadth.
>
> **Who pays which tier (workspace pricing, rechecked June 9):** Free for solo testing with up to 3K one-time credits, Starter $6/mo (3 users, 10K monthly credits) for tiny teams, Pro $16/mo (10 users, 50K monthly credits) for most small teams, Business $40/mo (unlimited users, 150K monthly credits) for admin and analytics, Max $200/mo for autonomous agents and extended thinking, Enterprise from $400/mo for SSO, SAML, BYOK, and MCP integration. ## What it actually is

One workspace with multiple project views layered over a shared data model. The same list of tasks can render as a checklist, kanban board, mind map, calendar, or outline. Switching view does not duplicate the data.

AI Agents are first-class objects with instructions, custom tool schemas, persistent memory across sessions, and training files. An agent can read a project, edit tasks, generate a summary, chat with the team inside the same workspace, or be published publicly and embedded on any website with no code. Users build agents through a visual editor or prompt-driven setup, and assign a different model to each agent across 11+ frontier options from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google.

The moat is integration density and model neutrality, not any single specialist capability. Taskade is weaker than Notion on knowledge management, weaker than Monday on enterprise PM, weaker than a dedicated Claude or ChatGPT session on raw agent intelligence. The trade is that small teams get all those surfaces in one place at one price, with the Max tier opening up autonomous agents and extended thinking for power workflows, and Enterprise adding MCP integration so Taskade plugs into Claude Desktop, Cursor, and VS Code.

## When to pick Taskade

- **Small teams that want AI baked into project views.** Agents read and act on the same tasks everyone else sees. No copy-paste between chatbot and PM tool.
- **Template-driven project starts.** 700+ templates cover meetings, OKRs, sprints, content calendars, and personal workflows. Faster than building from blank.
- **Mixed-format planning.** The same list renders as outline, board, mindmap, and calendar. Useful for teams whose members think in different structures.
- **Budget-sensitive buyers.** Pro at $16/mo for up to 10 users (workspace pricing, not per seat) undercuts Notion AI add-ons and ClickUp Business Plus on total cost at small team size.
- **Solo founders and small ops teams.** Free tier supports limited testing (1 app, 1 agent, 3 automations, 3K one-time credits); Starter at $6/mo for 3 users is the realistic floor for real work.
- **Model-flexible buyers.** Per-agent model selection across 11+ providers means a team is not locked to a single frontier lab as benchmarks shift.

## When to pick something else

- **Deep knowledge base with backlinks and graph view:** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) for company wikis, or [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) for long-term personal knowledge.
- **Agent intelligence above all else:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or [Claude](/tools/claude/). Stronger underlying models, broader tool ecosystems.
- **Cross-app automation plumbing:** [Zapier](/tools/zapier/). Thousands more integrations and more mature triggers.
- **Enterprise project portfolio management:** Monday, Asana, or ClickUp. Deeper resource, reporting, and governance features at scale.
- **AI scheduling as the primary job:** Motion. Purpose-built for calendar optimization.

## Failure modes

- **Agent intelligence is mid-tier on default tasks.** Taskade routes to frontier providers but cannot match a dedicated [Claude](/tools/claude/) Opus 4.7 or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) session for hard reasoning. Max-tier extended thinking narrows the gap but does not close it.
- **Knowledge base depth trails Notion.** No true backlinks graph, weaker database views, thinner formula language. Teams whose primary need is a wiki should pick Notion.
- **Credit consumption is variable.** 50K Pro credits sound generous yet deplete fast under heavy agent or app-generation use. Unused credits do not roll over. Track usage before assuming the quota fits the workload.
- **Integrations breadth is narrower than Zapier.** 100+ native connectors cover the common apps including Stripe, Slack, GitHub, and HubSpot. Edge-case SaaS typically requires a Zapier bridge.
- **MCP and SSO sit at Enterprise from $400/mo.** Mid-size teams that need Model Context Protocol routing for Claude Desktop, Cursor, or VS Code (or SAML SSO) must commit to the Enterprise floor. SOC 2 and similar certifications should be verified with sales before procurement.
- **Pricing surfaces still conflict.** The pricing page shows $6/$16/$40, while the free-plan help-center table still lists $8/$20/$50. Treat $6/$16/$40 as the live annual-facing pricing-page values and verify the checkout quote before renewal.
- **Offline mode is limited.** Mobile and desktop apps rely on active sync. Teams working in low-connectivity environments should test before committing.
- **Feature breadth can overwhelm.** The same canvas holding tasks, docs, maps, chat, video, and agents creates cognitive load for new users. Template-led onboarding mitigates but does not eliminate this.
- **Max tier price jump is steep.** Business $40 to Max $200 is a 5x leap. Teams that need autonomous agents and extended thinking should benchmark the workload against direct Claude Opus or GPT-5.5 Pro before committing.

## FAQ

**Is Taskade free?**
Yes, but the 2026 free tier tightened. It includes 1 app, 1 agent, 3 automations, and 3,000 one-time AI credits (no monthly refresh). Paid plans now begin at Starter $6/mo for 3 users with 10K monthly credits, and Pro at $16/mo for up to 10 users with 50K monthly credits ([Taskade pricing](https://www.taskade.com/pricing)).

**What are Taskade AI Agents?**
Custom agents defined by instructions, custom tool schemas, persistent memory across sessions, and training files (uploaded docs, URLs, Google Drive, Dropbox, or project data). Agents read project data, edit tasks, generate summaries, chat inside the same workspace, and can be published publicly and embedded on any website with no code ([Taskade AI](https://www.taskade.com/ai)).

**Which models power Taskade's AI?**
Taskade routes across 11+ frontier models from OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, and Google Gemini. Users can assign a different model to each agent to avoid vendor lock-in. The Max tier exposes autonomous agents and extended thinking for harder reasoning workloads.

**How does Taskade compare to Notion AI?**
Different strengths. Notion wins on knowledge base depth, backlinks, and database power. Taskade wins on AI agents baked into project views, multi-view canvases (list, board, mindmap, calendar), and real-time video plus chat on the same surface. Knowledge-first teams pick Notion. Action-first small teams pick Taskade.

**How does Taskade compare to ClickUp or Monday?**
ClickUp and Monday target larger teams needing enterprise PM features such as resource management, advanced reporting, and governance. Taskade targets small and mid-size teams who want AI agents and multi-format planning in one lightweight workspace. Teams over 50 seats typically outgrow Taskade on PM depth, though the new workspace pricing (flat $40/mo Business with unlimited users) softens that ceiling.

**Does Taskade integrate with Zapier?**
Yes. Taskade connects to Zapier and ships 100+ native integrations across Stripe, Slack, GitHub, HubSpot, Google Workspace, and calendar tools. Durable execution auto-retries failed automations. For rare or long-tail apps, Zapier remains the broader automation layer.

**What is the MCP integration?**
The Enterprise tier exposes Model Context Protocol so Taskade can plug into Claude Desktop, Cursor, and VS Code. This is the most material 2026 enterprise feature for teams that use Claude Code or Cursor as their primary surface and want Taskade projects accessible from those editors.

**Is Taskade SOC 2 compliant?**
Enterprise-grade compliance details should be confirmed with Taskade sales. SSO, SAML, and MCP sit on the Enterprise tier from $400/mo. Procurement teams should review the current trust page before committing.
