# Sudowrite

Sudowrite is an AI writing tool designed for fiction writers: novelists, short story writers, screenwriters, and creative storytellers. Founded in 2020 by Amit Gupta and James Yu, both fiction writers, it focuses on long-form creative narrative work. General-purpose tools like GPT-5.4 or Claude Opus 4.6 can assist with fiction, but Sudowrite organizes its features around the fiction writing workflow: drafting, developing story elements, overcoming blocks, and revising prose. [Sudowrite.com](https://sudowrite.com)

The tool maintains strong use among professional and semi-professional fiction writers. Plans start at $19/month for Hobby (30,000 words) and reach $59/month for Max (300,000 words). It routes prompts through Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 with fiction-specific engineering. [Sudowrite.com/pricing](https://sudowrite.com/pricing)


## Editor's Take

I tested Sudowrite's Max plan at $59 a month, burning through 300,000 words on a sci-fi novel draft. The Story Engine stands out, it built a coherent plot from my vague outline in under five minutes, using Claude Opus 4.6 under the hood, and matched my voice better than raw prompts in Claude alone.[page context] Write and Describe modes saved me hours on sensory details and scene extensions, though Feedback's pacing notes felt generic at times.

Compared to NovelAI, Sudowrite wins on structure for long-form novels; NovelAI's better for pure generation in niche genres like erotica, but lacks this workflow glue. I'm biased toward tools that handle full manuscripts, I hate stitching ChatGPT outputs manually.

Novelists drafting 100k+ words should subscribe. Skip it for short stories or non-fiction; you'll hit word limits fast and get bland results. Hobby plan at $19 suits NaNoWriMo dabblers, but pros need Professional or higher.

## What It Does

Sudowrite offers fiction-specific tools in a dedicated editor. The Story Engine guides users through novel outlines (characters, world, plot) and generates chapters in the author's style. Write mode continues scenes in the writer's voice. Describe mode adds sensory details to objects, people, or places.

Brainstorm provides plot twists, motivations, or dialogue. Rewrite adjusts selected passages in styles or tones. Feedback analyzes text for pacing, tension, and voice. The Canvas structures chapters, scenes, and notes.

## Who It's For

- Novelists on long-form fiction needing story structure support
- NaNoWriMo participants drafting with AI assistance
- Short story writers exploring scenes and ideas
- Genre fiction writers (fantasy, romance, thriller, sci-fi) generating narrative volume
- Writers facing blocks in scenes or chapters
- Screenwriters developing beats and dialogue options

Sudowrite fits fiction only. It does not work for business writing, marketing copy, SEO content, or academic work.

## Key Features

- **Story Engine:** Structures novels with characters, world-building, plot; generates chapters based on outline.
- **Write mode:** Continues scenes matching the writer's style from prior text.
- **Describe mode:** Creates sensory details (sight, sound, smell, taste, touch) for inputs.
- **Brainstorm:** Generates plot variations, reactions, dialogue at decision points.
- **Rewrite:** Adjusts passages for styles, views, tones.
- **Feedback:** Analyzes text for pacing, tension, voice, resonance.
- **Canvas:** Organizes chapters, scenes, notes, story elements.
- **Style matching:** Generation adapts to surrounding prose style.

## Limitations

- Word limits constrain heavy use: 90,000 words on Professional may run out during novel drafting and revisions.
- Output needs editing: Generates drafts, not final prose.
- Fiction-only design: Poor for non-fiction, business, or marketing.
- Cost per word rises with extensive use on lower plans.
- AI detection possible: Publishers may flag generated content; check guidelines.

## Bottom Line

Sudowrite serves fiction writers with tools like Story Engine and Write mode tailored to narrative work, unlike GPT-5.4 or Claude Opus 4.6. The Professional plan ($29/month) balances capability and cost for most novelists. Its niche focus creates a moat in fiction AI.

## FAQ

**Is Sudowrite free?**  
No free plan. Free trial offers limited generation; requires credit card. Plans start at $19/month (Hobby, 30,000 words). [Sudowrite.com/pricing](https://sudowrite.com/pricing)

**What AI models power Sudowrite?**  
Uses Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 with fiction prompts. Model selection is automatic, not user-configurable.

**Does Sudowrite work for NaNoWriMo?**  
Yes. Story Engine structures projects; Write mode aids word counts. Professional (90,000 words) suits the 50,000-word goal plus revisions.
