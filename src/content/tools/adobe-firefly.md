# Adobe Firefly

Adobe's native AI image and video generator. **Firefly Image Model 5** is the production flagship, generating 4MP native-resolution images with prompt-based editing and layer support.

Trained only on licensed Adobe Stock and public domain content. Paid plans include IP indemnification against copyright claims. No other major image generator offers this guarantee.

## What it actually is

One AI generator across two surfaces: a standalone web app at firefly.adobe.com, and embedded features inside Photoshop (Generative Fill, Generative Expand), Illustrator (vector generation), and Premiere Pro (video clips).

Image Model 5 brings together generation and editing. The Prompt to Edit tool lets creative professionals describe edits in natural language. The model treats objects as layers and supports resize, rotate, and targeted prompts per layer.

The moat is legal, not aesthetic. Adobe's training-data sourcing creates the only mainstream image generator that indemnified enterprise users can deploy without IP risk. That positioning compounds inside Adobe-ecosystem shops.

## When to pick Adobe Firefly

- **The output goes to a paying client.** Freelancers and agencies need a defensible IP provenance. Firefly provides one; Midjourney and Flux do not.
- **Your organization runs Creative Cloud.** Firefly credits come bundled with CC All Apps at $59.99/month, and the tools live inside the apps designers already use.
- **You need Generative Fill or Generative Expand.** In-Photoshop region fill with matching lighting and perspective is best-in-class.
- **You produce commercial video at 1080p.** Firefly Pro adds video generation that sits natively in Premiere Pro.
- **Enterprise legal must sign off on AI content.** VIP programs include documented indemnification up to $10K/output.

## When to pick something else

- **Peak aesthetic quality for concept art or mood:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) V7. Still the reference for cinematic work.
- **Text-in-image reliability:** [Ideogram](/tools/ideogram/). Firefly's typography trails.
- **Open weights or LoRA training:** [Flux](/tools/flux/) or [Stable Diffusion](/tools/stable-diffusion/).
- **Image gen bundled with a chatbot:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) (GPT Image 2).
- **Stock imagery with AI generation on top:** [Freepik](/tools/freepik/). Integrated library plus multi-model gen.

## Failure modes

- **Aesthetic ceiling trails Midjourney and Flux.** The licensed training set produces clean, on-brand, commercially-safe output. It does not produce Midjourney-grade mood or Flux-grade photoreal detail.
- **Credit cost varies per operation.** A simple image might cost 2 credits; a video or high-res render can cost 100. Budgeting monthly credits requires monitoring actual consumption.
- **Free tier burns fast.** 25 credits covers about a morning of experimentation. Even the 100-credit Standard plan suits light use only.
- **No general-access public API.** Programmatic integration requires an Enterprise agreement. Developers picking on pricing alone will find the gate closed.
- **Indemnification coverage has fine print.** VIP programs cap at $10K/output and require operating inside approved workflows. Users must follow Adobe's GenAI user guidelines to stay covered.
- **Smaller prompt-sharing community.** Public prompt libraries and style references are thinner than Midjourney's. Learning the prompt grammar takes longer.
- **Locked to the Adobe ecosystem for full value.** Standalone Firefly at $9.99 is viable but underwhelming next to $15 Plus at Ideogram or $10 Basic at Midjourney. The ROI sits with Creative Cloud subscribers.

## FAQ

**Is Adobe Firefly free?**
Yes, the free tier offers 25 credits monthly via web app at firefly.adobe.com. Exports include watermarks and are non-commercial. Standard at $9.99/month unlocks commercial rights and IP indemnification.

**Is Firefly safe for commercial use?**
Yes, uniquely so among major image generators. Paid plans include IP indemnification. Adobe defends paid users against copyright claims on content generated through Firefly and integrated Creative Cloud apps ([Adobe GenAI guidelines](https://www.adobe.com/legal/licenses-terms/adobe-gen-ai-user-guidelines.html)).

**What is Firefly Image Model 5?**
Firefly's current flagship, unveiled at Adobe MAX 2025 and generally available as of early 2026. Generates 4MP native-resolution images. Supports layered editing, Prompt to Edit natural-language edits, and custom model training ([Firefly Image 5 coverage](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/28/adobe-firefly-image-5-brings-support-for-layers-will-let-creators-make-custom-models/)).

**How does Firefly compare to Midjourney?**
Midjourney V7 leads on aesthetic quality, cinematic lighting, and stylized output. Firefly leads on commercial safety, Creative Cloud integration, and video generation. Many agencies use Midjourney for ideation and Firefly for final licensed delivery.

**Does Firefly have a public API?**
No general-access API as of April 2026. Programmatic integration requires an Adobe Enterprise agreement. Developers seeking pay-per-use image APIs should look at OpenAI, Replicate, or Fal.

**How is Firefly bundled with Creative Cloud?**
Creative Cloud All Apps at $59.99/month includes 1,000+ Firefly credits and the integrated tooling inside Photoshop, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro. This is the cost-effective entry point for existing Adobe users.
