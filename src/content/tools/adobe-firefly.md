# Adobe Firefly

Adobe Firefly (Firefly 4 as of April 2026) is Adobe's native AI generator for images, videos, vectors, and text effects, trained exclusively on Adobe Stock licensed images and public domain content. Launched in March 2023, it provides commercially safe outputs for client work, advertising, and product packaging without copyright risk. It integrates directly into Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, and other Creative Cloud apps.[Adobe Firefly](https://firefly.adobe.com/)


## Editor's Take

I tested Firefly 4 last week in Photoshop and the web app. The commercial safety holds up, no IP headaches for client work, unlike Midjourney v8, where you're still rolling the dice on lawsuits. Generative Fill nails canvas expansions in under 10 seconds, and video gen on Pro hits 1080p with decent audio sync. But image quality caps out average; Midjourney's details and stylization crush it every time.

At $9.99 for Standard or $29.99 Pro, it's fair if you're already in Creative Cloud. Standalone, skip it, those 100 credits burn fast on complex prompts. I prefer Midjourney for pure aesthetics, but agencies locked into Adobe should stick here. Firefly wins on indemnity, loses on wow factor. Use it for safe B-roll and vectors; don't if you're chasing art direction.

## What It Does

Firefly generates images, videos, vectors, and text effects from text prompts or reference images. In Photoshop, it powers Generative Fill to extend or replace image parts, and Generative Expand to enlarge canvas edges. In Illustrator, it creates editable SVG vectors; in Premiere Pro, it generates video clips and edits footage with text prompts.

The web app at firefly.adobe.com includes style reference, structure reference, and people reference for precise control. Firefly 4 adds video generation with audio reactivity and 1080p output on Pro plans. It supports multimodal inputs like sketches or depth maps combined with text.

## Key Features

- Commercially safe outputs trained on licensed Adobe Stock and public domain data; Adobe indemnifies enterprises against copyright claims[Adobe Guidelines](https://www.adobe.com/legal/licenses-terms/adobe-gen-ai-user-guidelines.html)
- Photoshop Generative Fill selects regions to fill or replace with matching lighting and perspective
- Generative Expand extends images beyond original canvas
- Style, Structure, and People References control aesthetics, composition, and human poses
- Video generation in Firefly 4 creates 1080p clips with text prompts, available on Pro/Creative Cloud
- Vector generation in Illustrator produces editable SVGs from prompts
- Text Effects applies realistic textures to typography

## Limitations

- Image quality trails Midjourney v8 alpha and Flux 2 in photorealism and artistic styles due to training data limits
- 25 free credits exhaust quickly; even 100-credit Standard suits light use only
- Credit costs vary by operation (2-100 credits per gen), complicating budgeting
- No public API; enterprise agreements required for API access
- Smaller prompt-sharing community than Midjourney

## Bottom Line

Adobe Firefly excels where copyright safety matters most. Professionals publishing or licensing work choose it to avoid IP disputes that plague Midjourney v8 alpha or Flux 2. Creative Cloud users gain the most from its app integrations. For pure aesthetic quality, Midjourney or Flux 2 outperform it.

## FAQ

**Is Adobe Firefly free?**  
Yes, the free tier offers 25 credits monthly via web app at firefly.adobe.com. Exports include watermarks; credits reset monthly. Professionals typically need paid plans.

**Is Adobe Firefly safe for commercial use?**  
Yes, uniquely so. Trained only on licensed Adobe Stock/public domain content, Adobe indemnifies enterprises against copyright claims. No equivalent from Midjourney v8 alpha, Flux 2, or GPT-5.4 image tools.[Adobe Policy](https://www.adobe.com/legal/licenses-terms/adobe-gen-ai-user-guidelines.html)

**How does Firefly compare to Midjourney v8 alpha?**  
Midjourney v8 alpha leads in visual quality, lighting, and styles. Firefly prioritizes legal safety over peak aesthetics. Many use Midjourney for ideation, Firefly for final commercial assets.
