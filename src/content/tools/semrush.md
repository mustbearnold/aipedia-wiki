# Semrush

Semrush is the broadest SEO and digital marketing platform on the market, and as of **April 28, 2026** it is an **Adobe company**. Adobe completed the acquisition on that date, folding Semrush into Adobe's CX Enterprise brand-visibility stack. The suite spans 55+ tools across keyword research, backlink analysis, site audits, rank tracking, competitor intelligence, PPC research, and social.

Semrush Copilot, AI Visibility Toolkit, Semrush One, ContentShake AI, and **AI PR Toolkit** bring generative AI into core workflows. Current Semrush documentation separates the classic SEO Toolkit, Semrush One, AI Visibility Toolkit, AI PR Toolkit, Content Toolkit, and other toolkits, so buyers should price the bundle that matches their actual workflow.

## What it actually is

A digital marketing platform, now an Adobe company, that bundles 55+ tools into a single subscription. The core pitch: stop paying for separate SEO, PPC, social, content, competitive-intelligence, AI-visibility, and reporting tools when one suite can cover most of the work.

Core workflows run through Keyword Magic Tool, Backlink Analytics, Site Audit, Position Tracking, Traffic Analytics, and SEO Writing Assistant. PPC research spans keyword gap analysis, ad copy review, and display advertising insights. AI integration sits across several layers: Semrush One combines SEO and AI visibility, AI Visibility Toolkit can be evaluated separately, AI PR Toolkit extends the visibility thesis into earned media, and Content Toolkit handles topic ideas, briefs, generation, optimization, browser workflow, and CMS publishing.

The moat is surface area plus Adobe's enterprise channel. Ahrefs beats it for many backlink-first teams. Surfer beats it on focused content optimization. No direct rival matches the same marketing-suite breadth.

## When to pick Semrush

- **You run a marketing agency.** Client reports, competitive audits, and multi-channel dashboards all live in one platform.
- **You need SEO plus PPC plus social in one place.** Ad copy research, keyword gap analysis, and organic tracking share data.
- **You track brand visibility across AI search and earned media.** AI Visibility Toolkit plus AI PR Toolkit cover AI answer visibility and the media sources LLMs cite.
- **You run technical audits on large sites.** Business plan limits include 1,000,000 pages to crawl per month.
- **You need Share of Voice and API access.** Business includes SEO Share of Voice tracking and API access.
- **You are already in the Adobe stack.** Post-acquisition, Semrush is the natural visibility layer to watch inside Adobe's CX Enterprise direction.

## When to pick something else

- **Backlink depth and freshness:** [Ahrefs](/tools/ahrefs/). Stronger default for link-building-first teams.
- **Content grading in the base plan:** [Surfer SEO](/tools/surfer-seo/). Content Editor is the core product, not a side workflow.
- **Solo bloggers on a tight budget:** Ahrefs Starter or lower-cost SEO tools are easier to justify than Semrush Pro.
- **Single-channel specialists:** Google Ads, native platform analytics, or dedicated social tools may be enough.
- **AI-first content generation at scale:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or dedicated content tools may move faster than Semrush's marketing-suite workflow.

## Failure modes

- **High entry price.** $139.95/month Pro is a meaningful floor if you only manage one small site.
- **Steep learning curve.** 55+ tools overwhelm new users. Expect weeks to navigate effectively, not days.
- **Backlink-first teams may still prefer Ahrefs.** Semrush is broad, but link-building specialists often choose the deeper specialist workflow.
- **Pro tier limits bite fast.** 500 tracked keywords and 5 monitored sites constrain agencies or multi-site operators.
- **ContentShake AI is not the whole product.** If content generation is the main job, price the content workflow directly.
- **AI visibility and PR packaging needs verification.** AI visibility can be bought through different Semrush surfaces, and AI PR is a separate toolkit.
- **Adobe integration is early.** The acquisition closed April 28, 2026. Buyer-visible Adobe CX Enterprise integration, data-residency, and contracting changes may still evolve.
- **API access starts at Business.** Pro and Guru buyers should not assume custom reporting integrations are included.
- **Dashboard density.** Reports and filters run deep. New users often cannot locate the specific view they need without training.

## FAQ

**Is Semrush worth $139.95/month?**
For agencies and digital marketing pros managing multi-channel campaigns, yes. It consolidates keyword research, backlinks, site audit, PPC, social, and AI visibility into one platform. Solo bloggers or link-building specialists should compare Ahrefs, Surfer SEO, or lower-cost SEO suites first.

**Does Adobe own Semrush?**
Yes. Adobe completed the Semrush acquisition on April 28, 2026. Semrush is now an Adobe company inside Adobe's CX Enterprise brand-visibility strategy. The Semrush product and branding remain in market.

**Semrush vs Ahrefs?**
Semrush wins on breadth: PPC, social, AI visibility tracking, AI PR, and ContentShake. Ahrefs wins for many backlink-first teams. If forced to pick one, match the tool to the dominant workflow: Ahrefs for link building, Semrush for multi-channel marketing operations.

**What is Semrush Copilot?**
Semrush Copilot is an AI advisor layer that surfaces insights, alerts, and recommendations across Semrush projects. Treat it as a monitoring layer on top of the core data, not a replacement for campaign strategy.

**What is the AI Visibility Toolkit?**
It is Semrush's AI search visibility product. Semrush docs say it tracks brand sentiment, share of voice, competitor gaps, prompts, and AI visibility, with pricing starting at $99/month.

**What is the AI PR Toolkit?**
A Semrush toolkit for earned-media visibility in the AI era. The public page says it helps identify media cited by LLMs, pitch with AI, and sync media and AI visibility data.

**Do I need Guru for ContentShake AI?**
If content workflows drive the use case, Guru or a dedicated content-toolkit checkout is usually cleaner than trying to force Pro into a content-production role. Verify the live checkout because Semrush packaging changes by toolkit.
