# Heptabase

Heptabase is a **visual whiteboard-first knowledge base**. Instead of pages in a folder tree or blocks in a database, the core unit is a card note that lives on an infinite whiteboard, and whiteboards can be **nested inside other whiteboards**. That nesting is the product's signature move: a research project can hold a whiteboard for sources, another for arguments, another for an outline, each one a zoomable layer inside the parent canvas.

Card notes carry PDF highlights, YouTube transcripts, and web clips onto the whiteboard directly, so source material and synthesis sit in the same visual space rather than in separate apps. The newer **AI Tutor**, built on Anthropic's Claude Agent SDK and launched March 31, 2026, takes a learning goal, generates a full course syllabus, runs interactive lessons with checkpoints, and converts completed lessons into structured whiteboard notes on request.

Heptabase is built by **Heptabase Labs**, a Y Combinator W22 company founded in 2021 by Alan Chan, Yeefun Lin, and Ethan Wu, backed by Y Combinator, Kleiner Perkins, HOF Capital, and Moving Capital. There is **no permanent free plan**. New users get a trial of up to 7 days or until trial AI credits run out, whichever comes first, then a paid subscription is required to continue.

## What it actually is

Heptabase organizes knowledge around two ideas: **card notes** and **whiteboards**. A card note is a single, atomic piece of content, similar to a Notion page or an Obsidian file. A whiteboard is an infinite canvas where you arrange cards spatially, draw connections between them, and group related cards into sections. The differentiator is that a whiteboard can contain another whiteboard as a nested object, so a large research topic can be broken into layered sub-canvases without leaving the visual metaphor.

PDF highlights and YouTube transcript snippets can be dragged directly onto a whiteboard as cards, keeping source material and your synthesis of it in the same visual space. Readwise and Zotero integrations pull highlights and citations in automatically for research-heavy workflows.

The **AI Tutor**, introduced in 2026, is a separate but integrated feature: tell it a learning goal, it drafts a full syllabus with individual lessons, walks you through them with checkpoints and real-time Q&A, and on request turns completed lesson content into organized whiteboard notes. It runs on Anthropic's Claude Agent SDK and consumes the same AI credit pool as chat and tagging features.

There is no permanent free plan. Every account starts on a trial capped at 7 days or trial AI credit exhaustion, after which a Pro, Premium, or Premium+ subscription is required.

## When to pick Heptabase

- **Spatial thinkers.** If your mental model for a project is "place things, arrange them, connect them," the whiteboard-first design fits better than a database or folder tree.
- **Nested, multi-layered research.** Whiteboards inside whiteboards let a single big topic hold source boards, argument boards, and outline boards as zoomable layers.
- **PDF- and video-heavy study.** Highlights and transcripts drop onto the canvas as cards, so source review and synthesis happen in the same view.
- **Guided AI learning.** AI Tutor's syllabus generation and checkpoint lessons are a genuinely different feature from generic AI chat-with-your-notes.
- **Fast onboarding.** Reviewers consistently describe Heptabase as more intuitive to pick up than Tana's Supertag system, often productive within a day or two.

## When to pick something else

- **You want a free tier first:** [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) is free for personal use with a large plugin ecosystem, including whiteboard-style Canvas.
- **Local-first markdown ownership:** [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) or [Logseq](/tools/logseq/) store notes as plain markdown files you control.
- **Team workspace and docs:** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) is the stronger pick for shared wikis, databases, and company-wide documentation.
- **Structured typed-object knowledge:** [Capacities](/tools/capacities/) models people, books, and projects as objects with properties rather than freeform cards.
- **Database-style querying over notes:** [Tana](/tools/tana/)'s Supertag model rewards users willing to invest weeks learning its query system.
- **Encrypted, minimal linked notes:** [Reflect](/tools/reflect/) is a simpler, cheaper backlinked-notes app for solo users.

## Recent developments

Heptabase launched **AI Tutor** on March 31, 2026, built on Anthropic's Claude Agent SDK. It generates a full personalized course syllabus from a stated learning goal, runs interactive lessons with progress tracking and checkpoints, answers real-time questions during lessons, and converts completed lesson content into structured whiteboard notes on request. As a launch promotion, Heptabase granted all Pro, Premium, and Premium+ subscribers 1,000 free AI Tutor credits, which expired at the end of April 22, 2026 (UTC); AI Tutor usage now draws from each plan's regular monthly AI credit pool, with full access limited to Premium and Premium+.

Pricing itself has been stable through mid-2026: Pro $8.99/month, Premium $17.99/month, and Premium+ $53.99/month on annual billing, all unchanged since at least early in the year based on the live pricing page. The main open question for buyers is the gap between the marketing pricing page and the support-article FAQ's listed non-early-bird monthly rates, which run noticeably higher; this is most likely a monthly-vs-annual framing difference rather than a live price change, but it is worth confirming at checkout.

## Failure modes

- **No free tier.** The trial is capped at 7 days or trial AI-credit exhaustion, so buyers cannot use Heptabase indefinitely for free the way they can with Obsidian or Notion AI's free plan.
- **Pricing page inconsistency.** The live marketing pricing page and Heptabase's own support-article FAQ list different monthly numbers in places; verify at checkout.
- **Not local-first by default.** Notes live in Heptabase's cloud format rather than plain markdown files, which matters for buyers who want full local data ownership.
- **AI credits are metered.** Pro's 100 credits/month is thin if you lean on AI chat or AI Tutor heavily; expect to need Premium for real AI Tutor use.
- **Smaller company, smaller ecosystem.** As a YC-backed startup with roughly 10 employees, Heptabase's plugin and integration ecosystem is far smaller than Obsidian's thousands of community plugins.
- **Learning curve for the spatial model.** The whiteboard-first approach is intuitive for visual thinkers but can feel unstructured to users who prefer databases or strict folder hierarchies.
