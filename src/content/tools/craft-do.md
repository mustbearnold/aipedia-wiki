# Craft

A **design-forward docs and notes app** out of Budapest, built by Craft Docs Ltd. and founded by Balint Orosz in July 2019. Craft's pitch has always been visual polish: block-based documents, card layouts, and native Mac/iOS craftsmanship that won Apple's **2021 Mac App of the Year**. AI arrived later, layered on top as the **Craft Assistant**.

The Assistant runs on cloud **GPT-4o** by default, with an on-device **DeepSeek R1** option (1.5B and 7B variants) added January 31, 2025 for offline or privacy-sensitive work. AI usage is credit-metered on every tier, including paid plans.

Craft has raised roughly **$20.2M** across an $8M Series A (Creandum, April 2021) and a $12.2M Series B (EBRD Venture Capital, with Creandum and InReach Ventures, September 2022). In June 2026 its spun-out open-source agent project, **Craft Agents**, was acquired by prediction-market platform Polymarket, with co-founder Viktor Pali moving over as CEO of the acquired unit while co-founder Balint Orosz stayed on at Craft as VP of Product Engineering.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Craft if you want the best-looking writing surface in the ai-notes category and are fine with credit-metered AI.** The block-based editor, card views, and native app polish are still ahead of most competitors on pure design. The DeepSeek R1 on-device option is a genuine differentiator for anyone who wants offline AI notes without sending text to a cloud model.
>
> **Skip it if you need Notion's database depth, Obsidian's local-first plain-text ownership, or rock-solid roadmap certainty.** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) goes further on relational databases and cross-app search. [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) gives you a local markdown vault and bring-your-own-model flexibility with no vendor lock-in. And the June 2026 sale of Craft Agents to Polymarket, with a co-founder departing to run it, is worth watching if you're betting on Craft's AI roadmap over a multi-year horizon.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for light personal use (1,500 blocks, 15 AI credits/month), Plus at $8-10/mo for unlimited docs and 50 AI credits/month, Family at $15-18/mo for 2-6 people sharing one subscription, Team at $50-60/mo for up to 10 seats needing shared spaces.

## What it actually is

Craft is a block-based document and note-taking app that prioritizes visual design over database complexity. Documents render as cards, pages nest visually, and formatting feels closer to a native word processor than a plain-text markdown editor. It sits in the same broad category as Notion and Obsidian but leans harder into aesthetics and less into structured data or plugin extensibility.

The **AI Assistant** was added on top of the base product and offers writing help, summarization, and Q&A inside documents. It defaults to OpenAI's GPT-4o for cloud requests. Since January 2025, users can switch the model picker to **DeepSeek R1** (1.5B for lightweight tasks, 7B for more detailed reasoning), which runs entirely on-device. That means no network round-trip and no data leaving the machine, a meaningful option for people who want AI note help but do not want their notes touching a third-party cloud model.

All AI use, cloud or on-device, draws from a monthly credit pool: 15 credits on Free, 50 on Plus. Heavy AI users will exhaust that allowance before they exhaust content-block limits.

The company's other notable 2026 event is the sale of **Craft Agents**, an open-source agent project that had been spun out separately from the core docs product. Polymarket, the prediction-market platform, acquired it on June 26, 2026, and installed Craft co-founder Viktor Pali as CEO of the acquired unit. Co-founder Balint Orosz remains at Craft itself as VP of Product Engineering. No financial terms were disclosed.

## When to pick Craft

- **You want the best-looking notes app in the category.** Card-based documents and native platform polish remain a real differentiator versus Notion's denser database-first UI and Obsidian's plain-file minimalism.
- **You want an offline or on-device AI option.** DeepSeek R1 running locally is not something Notion AI or most cloud-first note apps offer natively.
- **You're a small team (2-10 people) that doesn't need enterprise governance.** Family and Team tiers cover shared spaces without forcing an enterprise sales conversation.
- **You're Mac/iOS-first.** The native app quality (2021 Mac App of the Year) still shows, especially on Apple platforms.

## When to pick something else

- **Database-heavy team workspace:** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/). Relational databases, AI Autofill, Enterprise Search, and cross-app connectors go well beyond what Craft offers.
- **Local-first, plain-text, bring-your-own-model:** [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/). Full data ownership as markdown files plus any AI plugin/API key you choose, no vendor credit metering.
- **Free document Q&A without a subscription:** [NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/). Better for source-grounded research than a general writing app.
- **Standalone AI writing and reasoning without a notes app wrapper:** [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/). Cheaper and clearer model access if the notes app itself isn't the point.

## Failure modes

- **Pricing page geo-renders in non-USD currencies.** US buyers checking craft.do/pricing directly may see NZD, EUR, or another local currency depending on region/session. Confirm actual USD billing at checkout or via the help-center article before assuming a price.
- **AI credits run out.** 15 credits/month on Free and 50 on Plus are modest for heavy AI users; expect to hit the ceiling before hitting content-block limits.
- **Database functionality is thin.** Teams that need relational tables, formulas, or complex views should look at Notion instead; Craft optimizes for visual documents, not structured data.
- **No deep enterprise governance.** SSO, audit logs, and formal compliance tooling are not part of the current tier structure; regulated teams should evaluate carefully before standardizing.
- **Roadmap uncertainty after the Craft Agents sale.** With one co-founder now running an acquired unit at Polymarket, buyers making multi-year bets on Craft's AI direction should watch for concrete product signals rather than assume continuity.
- **Smaller ecosystem than Notion or Obsidian.** Fewer integrations, fewer community plugins, and a smaller template marketplace than either larger competitor.
