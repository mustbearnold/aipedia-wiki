# Recraft

Recraft is a **vector-first AI image platform** built around brand-style consistency, on-canvas editing, and native SVG output. The current model family to evaluate is **Recraft V4**, which ships raster, vector, Pro raster, and Pro vector variants for design-grade image generation.

The company is **Recraft AI Limited**, registered in London and operating with a San Francisco presence, founded in December 2022 by Anna Veronika Dorogush. Recraft's differentiators are narrow and real: it outputs true SVG-style vectors with editable paths, and it remains one of the most reliable tools for legible text inside generated marketing graphics.

The Agentic Mode turns Recraft into a conversational design assistant on an infinite canvas. It reads context and updates designs in place. The tradeoff is that Recraft trails Midjourney and Flux on cinematic photorealism, and it does not generate video.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Recraft if your work involves logos, posters, marketing graphics, or any output that needs clean vector paths or readable text.** Recraft V4 is designed for both raster and vector asset generation. The $10/mo Basic tier covers most solo designers, and the API remains useful when a workflow needs Recraft outside the web canvas.
>
> **Skip it if you need cinematic photorealism or video.** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) and [Flux](/tools/flux/) produce richer photoreal output. [Kling](/tools/kling/) and Veo 3 handle video. Recraft's free tier also restricts commercial use and makes generated images public, so commercial workflows need a paid seat.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for trial and personal learning, Basic $10/mo for solo designers, Advanced $27/mo for agencies at mid-volume, Pro $48/mo for high-volume brand systems, Teams $55/seat for multi-designer workflows with shared styles.

## What it actually is

Recraft sits between Midjourney and a traditional design tool. The canvas is infinite, the brand-style system lets designers freeze a visual identity and apply it across generations, and the output can be exported as SVG, PNG, PSD, or PDF.

The vector moat matters for production design work. Most AI image tools claim SVG export but rasterize a bitmap and wrap it in an SVG container. Recraft generates true paths, which means the output scales cleanly, can be color-edited in Illustrator or Figma, and plugs into logo and icon pipelines without a manual trace step.

The other moat is **typography**. Most generalist image models still hallucinate or misspell text inside images. Recraft V3 renders complex multi-line text layouts with accurate spelling, alignment, and typographic style. That single capability carries most of the poster, social-graphic, and packaging-mockup use cases.

## When to pick Recraft

- **Logos, icons, and illustration packs.** True vector output scales cleanly and hands off to Illustrator or Figma without a manual retrace pass.
- **Posters and social graphics with text.** Typography renders legibly on Recraft V3 where Midjourney and Flux still produce garbled or hallucinated characters.
- **Brand-consistent asset runs.** Style Library freezes a look and applies it across hundreds of generations, solving the drift problem that kills brand design on generalist models.
- **Agentic design iteration.** The on-canvas conversational mode shortens the prompt, check, reprompt loop that slows single-shot tools.
- **API-driven production pipelines.** $0.04 per raster image and $0.08 per vector is cost-competitive with Flux and easier to budget than many bundled chatbot image generators at scale.

## When to pick something else

- **Cinematic photorealism:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) still leads on painterly and photoreal output. [Flux](/tools/flux/) is the top open-weight alternative.
- **Integrated chat workflow:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) bundles GPT Image 2 with text chat, which is faster for one-off images.
- **Video generation:** [Kling](/tools/kling/) or Veo 3 via [Gemini](/tools/gemini/). Recraft has none.
- **Maximum model variety on one canvas:** [Krea](/tools/krea/) aggregates Flux, Kling, Sora, and Veo 3 in one interface.
- **Free commercial use:** [Ideogram](/tools/ideogram/) and [Leonardo](/tools/leonardo/) have friendlier free tiers for commercial work.
- **Stock-art workflows:** [Freepik](/tools/freepik/) and [Adobe Firefly](/tools/adobe-firefly/) ship with integrated stock libraries.

## Failure modes

- **Free tier restricts commercial use.** Free images are public and owned by Recraft. Commercial projects need at minimum the $10/mo Basic tier.
- **Credits do not roll over.** Unused Basic-tier credits disappear at month-end. Uneven workloads either leave credits on the table or hit top-up fees.
- **Photorealism trails Midjourney and Flux.** Recraft is strong but not best-in-class for cinematic, painterly, or stock-photo-grade output.
- **No video generation.** Motion work needs a second tool alongside Recraft.
- **Agentic Mode still iterates.** The conversational canvas is powerful but occasionally misinterprets context on longer sessions and needs a manual reset.
- **Vector output is not infinite-detail.** Complex generated SVGs can contain hundreds of paths and slow down downstream editors on older hardware.
- **API rate limits are not publicly documented.** Heavy production pipelines need to request quota from Recraft directly.
- **Smaller community than Midjourney.** Fewer public prompt libraries and style references means more trial-and-error for new users.

## FAQ

**Is Recraft free to use?**
Yes, with significant limits. The free tier gives 50 credits per day, but generated images are public and owned by Recraft, which blocks most commercial use. Paid plans start at [$10/month](https://www.recraft.ai/pricing) for commercial rights and private generation.

**Does Recraft actually generate true SVG?**
Yes. Recraft generates vectors with real paths and anchor points, not a rasterized bitmap wrapped in an SVG container. Output imports cleanly into Illustrator, Figma, and Inkscape and scales without quality loss.

**Which plan should most users pick?**
Basic at $10/month covers most solo designers with 1,000 credits, commercial rights, and private generation. Upgrade to Advanced ($27) or Pro ($48) only when consistent monthly volume justifies the jump.
