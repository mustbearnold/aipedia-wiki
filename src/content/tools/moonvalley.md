# Moonvalley

Moonvalley makes **Marey**, an AI video model the company markets as the first trained exclusively on licensed, high-resolution footage, no scraped web video, no user-generated uploads, no legal gray zone. Roughly 80% of the training data reportedly came from filmmakers and agencies who intentionally licensed footage rather than having it scraped. The pitch is "commercially safe" generative video for professional production, not a consumer toy.

Marey launched publicly on July 8, 2025, after a private beta with studios and agencies. Moonvalley has since raised $154M total (a ~$70M 2024 seed plus an $84M 2025 round led by General Catalyst, with CAA, Comcast Ventures, CoreWeave, Khosla Ventures, and Y Combinator participating), and struck a partnership with Adobe that put Marey inside Firefly Boards and Premiere Pro as a commercially safe partner model.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Moonvalley if licensing risk is the reason you have not shipped AI video yet.** Marey's entire pitch is IP indemnification through licensed-only training data, plus director-oriented controls (camera moves from a single image, motion transfer, trajectory, pose control, keyframing, shot extension). Studios, agencies, and brand teams that cannot risk a scraped-data lawsuit are the core buyer.
>
> **Skip it if you want the cheapest or highest-volume way to make short clips.** There is no free plan, clips cap at 5 seconds, and credits burn at roughly 10 per generation. [Kling](/tools/kling/) and [Seedance 2.0](/tools/seedance/) both output longer multi-shot clips with native audio at competitive or lower cost, and [Runway](/tools/runway/) offers a broader production workspace with a real free tier.
>
> **Pricing confidence caveat:** Moonvalley does not publish pricing on its public marketing site. The numbers below come from Moonvalley's own help-center article, which blocks automated fetching, cross-checked against independent secondary sources reporting the same figures. Confirm current pricing inside the app before budgeting a production against it.

## What it actually is

Marey is a text-to-video and image-to-video model built specifically to avoid the copyright and "trained on stolen art" controversy that follows most of the AI video field. Moonvalley says it licensed B-roll and archival footage directly from filmmakers, agencies, and platform partnerships (Vimeo has been named as a source) rather than scraping the open web. That licensing story is the product's entire differentiator, not an afterthought.

On the creative-control side, Marey targets working directors rather than casual prompt-and-pray users: Camera Control turns a single 2D image into a moving 3D-feeling shot, Motion Transfer applies a reference performance video's movement to a new subject, Trajectory Control moves objects naturally without needing a text prompt to describe the path, Pose Control reads a reference video for timing and body performance, and Keyframing blends multiple reference images into a smooth transition. Shot Extension lets a generated clip run longer without a fresh generation from scratch.

Distribution runs two tracks: Moonvalley's own app (app.moonvalley.com) for subscription users, and inference partners (ComfyUI, fal.ai) for developers who want Marey Realism programmatically before Moonvalley's first-party API leaves its waitlist. The Adobe Firefly and Premiere Pro integrations extend Marey's reach into existing professional editing pipelines rather than asking filmmakers to adopt a new standalone tool.

## When to pick Moonvalley

- **Licensing and IP risk are dealbreakers.** If legal, procurement, or a studio client will not sign off on a model trained on scraped data, Marey's licensed-only claim is the differentiator that gets a deal through review.
- **You need director-grade motion control, not just a text box.** Trajectory, pose, and motion-transfer controls target the same workflow gap that Runway's Act-Two and camera tools address, from a licensing-first vendor.
- **You already work in Adobe Premiere Pro or Firefly Boards.** The native panel and Firefly Boards listing mean Marey slots into an existing edit timeline instead of requiring a round trip to a separate app.
- **Brand and agency work where "commercially safe" is a client requirement.** Moonvalley leans hard into IP indemnification messaging aimed at exactly this buyer.

## When to pick something else

- **Cheapest or highest-volume clip generation:** [Kling](/tools/kling/) or [Hailuo](/tools/hailuo/). Both offer lower per-clip costs and, in Kling's case, longer multi-shot output with native audio.
- **Best raw model quality to test first:** [Seedance 2.0](/tools/seedance/). Multimodal references, 15-second multi-shot audio-video output, and motion stability lead the current field on pure output quality.
- **Full production workspace with editing, teams, and API today:** [Runway](/tools/runway/). Free tier, mature API, Aleph editing, Act-Two performance capture, and third-party model routing in one account.
- **Cinematic camera language as the main hook:** [Luma Dream Machine](/tools/luma/) with Ray3.2, or [Higgsfield](/tools/higgsfield/) for character-consistent social and cinematic creative.
- **Google-stack API and provenance requirements:** [Google Veo 3.1](/tools/veo/).
