# Reflect

Reflect is a networked note-taking application that combines the bidirectional linking approach popularized by Roam Research with a cleaner interface, end-to-end encrypted sync, and an AI writing assistant powered by GPT-4. Built by a small team and launched in early access in 2022, Reflect has carved a niche among professionals who want the conceptual power of networked notes — notes that link to each other and surface connections across your knowledge base — without the steep learning curve of Roam or the privacy concerns of cloud-first tools.

The AI assistant (Reflect AI) is embedded in the editor. It can summarize pages, continue writing from your notes, extract action items, answer questions about your vault, and generate new notes from prompts. Unlike Notion AI, which queries a workspace-wide knowledge base, Reflect AI works at the note and vault level — more suited to personal knowledge work than team knowledge bases.

Reflect's most significant limitation is its pricing model: there is no free tier, only a 14-day trial and a $10/month subscription. For users who decide it fits their workflow, this is reasonable. For those exploring, the absence of a permanent free option is a real barrier compared to Obsidian (free) or Notion (free tier).

## What It Does

Reflect stores notes as markdown with bidirectional wiki-style links (`[[Note Title]]`). When you link one note to another, both notes display a backlinks section showing every note that references them — this creates a web of connections that helps surface related thinking over time. The daily notes feature creates a date-based note automatically each day, encouraging a journaling or log habit. Reflect syncs via end-to-end encryption, meaning even Reflect cannot read your notes.

The AI assistant integrates directly into the writing flow: highlight text and ask AI to rewrite it, expand it, or summarize it. Ask Reflect AI a question about your vault to surface relevant notes.

## Who It's For

- **Professionals with a serious note-taking practice** who want encryption and bidirectional linking in one product
- **Knowledge workers** building a personal knowledge base around their field
- **Writers and researchers** who need to connect ideas across many notes over time
- **Privacy-conscious users** who want end-to-end encryption without building their own sync stack
- **Roam Research users** looking for a cleaner, lower-maintenance alternative

## Key Features

- **Bidirectional links** — link notes with `[[brackets]]`; all backlinks surface automatically
- **Daily notes** — auto-created date-stamped note each day; good for journaling and logs
- **End-to-end encryption** — sync uses E2EE; Reflect cannot read your notes
- **Reflect AI** — embedded GPT-4 assistant; summarize, continue, extract, answer questions about your vault
- **Graph view** — visual map of note connections across your knowledge base
- **Mobile apps** — iOS and Android with full sync
- **Kindle integration** — import highlights from Kindle directly into Reflect notes

## Limitations

- **No free tier** — only a 14-day trial; $10/month required after that, making the entry cost higher than competitors
- **Small team risk** — Reflect is a small independent product; longevity depends on continued funding and development momentum
- **Less powerful than Obsidian for power users** — no plugin ecosystem comparable to Obsidian's 1,000+ community plugins
- **Not a team tool** — no real-time collaboration; Reflect is personal-use software
- **AI limited to vault queries** — Reflect AI cannot browse the web or answer questions outside your vault's content

## FAQ

**Is Reflect better than Obsidian?**
For most PKM users, Obsidian is the stronger choice — it is free, has a vastly larger plugin ecosystem, and stores notes as plain markdown files you own completely. Reflect wins if you want a cleaner out-of-the-box experience without plugin configuration, and if end-to-end encrypted sync is important to you. Obsidian Sync also offers E2EE if that is the deciding factor.

**Does Reflect have an API?**
Reflect has a limited API in beta. It is not as mature as Obsidian's plugin ecosystem or Notion's full API. Most integrations happen through official Zapier actions or the Kindle import feature.
