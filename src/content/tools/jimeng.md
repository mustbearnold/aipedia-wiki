# Jimeng AI

ByteDance's consumer creative app. The Chinese-market brand is **Jimeng (即梦)**, accessed at [jimeng.jianying.com](https://jimeng.jianying.com). The international rebrand, **Dreamina**, launched February 2026 on the same model stack.

The engine is two ByteDance model families. **Seedream 5.0-Preview** handles image generation, with real-time web search and visual reasoning added in the February 2026 release. **Seedance 2.0** handles video, positioned by ByteDance against Sora and Kling. Both pipe directly into **CapCut** desktop and mobile.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Jimeng if work lives inside the ByteDance creative stack.** Douyin creators, CapCut editors, and anyone in a supported Southeast Asia or Latin America market gets credit-based pricing well below Western image tools, plus a live search feature on Seedream 5.0 that grounds generations in current events. Integration with CapCut is tighter than any bolt-on image tool.
>
> **Skip it if compliance, peak aesthetic, or logo text matter most.** US and EU teams should weigh the privacy posture of a Chinese consumer app with uncertain data residency. Midjourney still wins on stylized cinematic output. Ideogram still wins on dense typography. Flux wins on open-weight fine-tuning. Jimeng is a consumer app first, an enterprise tool never.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** China users on 69 RMB/mo (~$9.60) floor, Dreamina international on $18-$84/mo credit plans, free Dreamina tier gives 800 seconds of video per 30 days to test.

## What it actually is

A consumer creative app, not a developer platform. The surface is a web and mobile client where users type a prompt, pick a model, and generate an image or short video. The model stack is shared across Jimeng and Dreamina.

Seedream 5.0-Preview is the image pipeline. The February 2026 update added real-time web search, letting a prompt like "the current Louvre renovation scaffolding at night" pull live reference instead of training-cutoff fiction. Visual reasoning improves composition handling on complex multi-object scenes.

Seedance 2.0 is the video pipeline. ByteDance positions it against Kling 3.0 and Runway Gen-4, with the phased international rollout running through CapCut first.

The moats: CapCut integration is native, not a plugin. A generated image drops into a CapCut timeline without export steps. Real-time web search on Seedream is not in Midjourney or Flux. Pricing floors are well below the Western incumbents. The weakness is access: China account requires Chinese phone and payment; international access routes through Dreamina with phased market availability.

## When to pick Jimeng

- **Workflow is already on CapCut.** Native pipeline beats any export-import loop. Image and video flow into the same editor.
- **China-market creator or Douyin operator.** Jimeng is the default creative tool for the ByteDance ecosystem. Western tools have friction.
- **Budget matters.** China tier at ~$9.60/mo and Dreamina international at $18/mo floor undercut Midjourney Basic and ChatGPT Plus.
- **Prompt needs current reference.** Seedream 5.0-Preview web search grounds images in today, not the model's training cutoff.
- **You are in a supported Dreamina market.** Phased rollout through Brazil, Indonesia, Malaysia, Mexico, Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam means functional access in these regions.

## When to pick something else

- **Peak aesthetic on stylized cinematic shots:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) still leads on mood, lighting, and composition.
- **Open-weight fine-tuning or self-hosting:** [Flux](/tools/flux/). Seedream is closed-weight.
- **Logo text and dense typography:** [Ideogram](/tools/ideogram/). Seedream handles signage-level text but trails Ideogram on poster work.
- **ByteDance's other consumer assistant:** [Doubao](/tools/doubao/). Chat-first, different use case.
- **Pure video generation on the same model stack:** [Seedance](/tools/seedance/) direct. Skip the Jimeng shell if only video is wanted.

## Failure modes

- **China signup requires Chinese phone and payment.** International users cannot use the full Jimeng app directly. Dreamina is the path.
- **Dreamina international rollout is phased.** Supported markets launched first (Brazil, Indonesia, Malaysia, Mexico, Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam); other regions see friction.
- **Compliance posture is a consumer app's posture.** Data residency, training-on-user-content, and prompt logging defaults are not enterprise-aligned. US and EU legal teams will flag this.
- **Credits reset monthly.** Unused credits do not roll over. Batch workflows concentrated in a single week waste capacity.
- **Some features were paused on privacy concerns.** AI-generated voice from facial images was temporarily suspended after public pushback. Feature availability shifts with policy updates.
- **Model churn is fast.** Seedream has moved from 3.0 through 5.0-Preview inside one year; Seedance 2.0 landed in February 2026. Workflows built on a specific version may need re-tuning each cycle.
- **Seedream 5.0-Preview is a preview.** Real-time web search and advanced reasoning ship as beta-flag features; stability varies.
- **Language friction remains.** Jimeng's native prompts are optimized on Chinese; some English prompts produce weaker output than equivalent Chinese phrasing. Dreamina narrows the gap.

## FAQ

**What is the relationship between Jimeng, Dreamina, Seedream, and Seedance?**
Jimeng (即梦) is the Chinese-market consumer app. Dreamina is the international rebrand, launched February 2026. Both run on the same ByteDance model stack: Seedream 5.0-Preview for images and Seedance 2.0 for video. CapCut is the editor where outputs land.

**Can I use Jimeng outside China?**
The full Jimeng app at jimeng.jianying.com requires a Chinese phone number and Alipay or WeChat Pay for a paid subscription. International users access the same model stack through Dreamina, which is available in a growing list of markets led by the Seedance 2.0 phased rollout in Southeast Asia and Latin America.

**What does Seedream 5.0-Preview add over earlier versions?**
Real-time web search and advanced visual reasoning, according to ByteDance's CapCut launch announcement. Prompts can reference current events without hallucinating outdated details, and complex multi-object scenes compose better than in Seedream 4.5.

**How does pricing compare to Midjourney?**
Midjourney Basic is $10/mo for 3.3 Fast GPU hours. Jimeng's Chinese floor is ~$9.60/mo; Dreamina international starts at $18/mo with a free 800-second video tier. Jimeng adds native video via Seedance 2.0, which Midjourney does not have. Midjourney wins on stylized aesthetic depth.

**Does Jimeng have an API?**
A Jimeng AI CLI exists for terminal-driven image and video generation. Full programmatic API access is limited compared to Flux or OpenAI's image endpoints. Most usage is through the web app or CapCut integration.

**Is there a free tier?**
Yes on both sides. Jimeng gives 260 starter credits plus a daily quota in China. Dreamina gives 800 seconds of free video generation per 30 days from first sign-in.
