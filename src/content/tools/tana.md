# Tana

Tana is an AI-native workspace for notes and knowledge. Its core idea is the Supertag: apply a tag to a note and that note becomes a structured object with fields, so your notes behave like a flexible database rather than a flat document. On top of that structure, Tana layers AI chat over your knowledge and voice capture that turns spoken input into organized nodes.

The result sits between an outliner, a notes app, and a lightweight database. It targets people who think in structure and want their information to be queryable and reusable, not just searchable text.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Tana if you want notes that act like data.** Supertags structure information into nodes you can query and reuse, AI chat answers over your own knowledge, and voice capture turns talk into structured notes. For power users and PKM enthusiasts, that combination is genuinely distinctive.
>
> **Skip it if you want a simple notes app.** The Supertag and node model is powerful but has a real learning curve, and AI usage is metered by credits. People who want low-effort capture or a mature all-in-one docs suite may prefer [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) or a simpler tool.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for individuals learning the model and capturing up to 20,000 nodes, Plus at $10/mo for daily users who want AI chat, voice transcription, and unlimited Supertags, and Pro at $18/mo for power users and small teams who need API access, automation, and unlimited storage.

## What it actually is

Tana's differentiator is the data model. In most notes apps a note is text; in Tana, tagging a note with a Supertag gives it a type and fields, so a set of notes becomes a structured collection you can filter, query, and view in different ways. That turns a knowledge base into something closer to a personal database without building one.

The AI layer builds on that structure. Because content is structured, AI chat can reason over your knowledge more usefully, and voice capture can route spoken input into the right shape. Automation and API access on Pro let Tana act as a backend for workflows, not just a place to write.

The cost of this power is the learning curve. Supertags, nodes, and queries reward people who invest in setting up a system, and feel like overhead to people who just want to jot things down. AI features are also metered by monthly credits, so heavy AI use pushes you up the tiers.

## When to pick Tana

- **You want structured, queryable notes.** Supertags make information reusable, not just findable.
- **You want AI over your own knowledge.** Chat answers from your structured base.
- **You capture by voice.** Spoken input becomes organized notes.
- **You build personal systems.** CRMs, trackers, and databases live alongside notes.
- **You are a power user or team that automates.** Pro adds API and automation.

## When to pick something else

- **Simple notes:** a lightweight app is faster if you do not want structure.
- **All-in-one docs and wiki:** [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai/) is more mature for general docs and team wikis.
- **Frictionless meeting notes only:** a dedicated meeting-notes tool may fit better than learning Supertags.
- **Local-first storage:** Tana is cloud-based, so offline-first tools differ here.

## Pricing

Tana is freemium. As of June 2026 the Free plan includes about 500 monthly AI credits, Supertags, and up to 20,000 nodes. Plus at $10 per month adds AI chat, voice transcription, unlimited custom Supertags, and 2,000 monthly AI credits. Pro at $18 per month adds API access, automation, unlimited file storage, and 5,000 monthly AI credits. Paid tiers also unlock integrations like Google Calendar and Readwise and shared workspaces.

Because AI usage is metered by credits and plans change, confirm current limits and prices on the live pricing page before committing.

## Failure modes

- **Learning curve.** Supertags and nodes are powerful but unfamiliar, and onboarding takes effort.
- **Credit metering.** Heavy AI use consumes monthly credits and pushes you up tiers.
- **Overkill for simple needs.** If you just want quick notes, the structure is overhead.
- **Cloud-based.** Not a local-first or offline-first store.
- **Evolving product.** Features and limits change, so verify the current plan.
