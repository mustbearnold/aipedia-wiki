# Perplexity Comet

Perplexity Comet is Perplexity's AI browser. It extends the company's answer-engine workflow into a browsing surface: search, citations, page context, and AI help live inside the browser instead of requiring a separate Perplexity tab. Public search snippets for Perplexity's Comet page describe it as “the AI browser that acts as a personal assistant” for automating tasks, researching the web, and organizing email. Comet launched first for higher-tier Perplexity users in 2025 and is now broadly available as a free browser with paid Perplexity tiers increasing AI capacity.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Comet if your browsing is research-first.** It is strongest when you want cited search, quick source triage, and page-aware follow-up questions.
>
> **Skip it if you just need normal browsing plus occasional chatbot access.** Chrome, Edge, Safari, and Dia may be simpler if Perplexity is not already your default research tool.

## Against alternatives

Comet's advantage is Perplexity's search and citation muscle. Dia's advantage is a broader browser-first product philosophy. ChatGPT browsing is better when the browser is secondary and the assistant conversation is primary.

## What to verify before switching

Comet is compelling if Perplexity is already your answer engine. Before moving primary browsing over, check extension compatibility, bookmark/profile migration, admin controls, and whether the free tier's assistant limits match your workload.

## Failure modes

- It may over-mediate the web for users who prefer direct source navigation.
- Browser switching has high friction.
- Enterprise deployment is less mature than standard managed browsers.
- Heavy assistant usage can still push users toward paid Perplexity tiers.
