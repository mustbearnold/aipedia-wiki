# Pika Labs

Pika Labs is an AI video generation platform that creates short video clips from text prompts, image inputs, or a combination of both. Launched in late 2023 and gaining rapid adoption through its Discord community and web app, Pika has positioned itself as an accessible, creator-oriented alternative to more professional-grade tools like [Runway](../tools/runway.md).

The platform focuses on ease of use and creative experimentation. You can describe a scene in text, upload a reference image, and generate a 3-10 second video clip in under a minute. Pika's style controls — aspect ratio, camera motion, seed control — are intuitive enough for users without video production backgrounds. The free tier with 150 credits per month (roughly 75-150 clips depending on length and quality settings) makes it genuinely usable without paying.

Pika's limitation is output duration and consistency. The clips are short, and complex scenes with multiple subjects or precise motion often produce unexpected results. For polished professional video production, [Runway](../tools/runway.md) or [Google Veo 2](../tools/veo.md) produce more controlled output. Pika's sweet spot is rapid creative iteration, social media content, and short-form visual ideas.

## What It Does

Pika generates video clips from text descriptions or image inputs using its proprietary diffusion model. Users can control aspect ratio (portrait, landscape, square), camera motion (pan, zoom, rotate), and clip duration (up to 10 seconds). Image-to-video mode animates a still image using motion instructions. The Pikaffects feature applies specific visual transformations — melting, exploding, morphing — to uploaded images or prompts. Video-to-video mode lets users re-style existing footage.

## Who It's For

- **Social media creators** — generate short clips for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts without a camera or editing setup
- **Concept artists and designers** — rapidly visualize creative ideas before committing to full production
- **Marketers** — produce eye-catching animated visuals for ads and social posts at low cost
- **Indie game developers** — generate concept cinematics and atmosphere clips for game trailers
- **Experimenters and hobbyists** — explore AI video generation on the free tier without financial commitment

## Key Features

- **Text-to-video** — generate clips from natural language descriptions; supports complex scene descriptions and style references
- **Image-to-video** — animate still images with motion instructions; works well for product photos, illustrations, and portraits
- **Camera motion controls** — specify pan left/right, zoom in/out, rotate, or dolly for cinematic-feeling clips
- **Pikaffects** — visual transformation presets (explode, melt, crush, grow) that apply to uploaded images or generated scenes
- **Video-to-video** — upload existing footage and re-style it in a different visual style or aesthetic
- **Aspect ratio control** — generate natively for 16:9 (landscape), 9:16 (portrait/vertical), and 1:1 (square) without cropping
- **Seed control** — lock a seed to produce consistent variations of the same clip

## Limitations

- **Short clip length** — maximum 10 seconds per generation; creating longer sequences requires stitching multiple clips together manually
- **Consistency challenges** — characters and objects change appearance between clips, making narrative sequences difficult without significant effort
- **Watermark on free tier** — all free outputs include a visible watermark; the Standard plan at $8/month removes it
- **Not suitable for professional broadcast** — output quality is good for social media but not suitable for broadcast or large-screen applications without upscaling
- **Limited API access** — Pika is primarily a consumer and creator product; it doesn't offer the developer-friendly API that Runway or Kling provide

## FAQ

**Is Pika free to use?**
Yes. The free plan includes 150 credits per month, which translates to roughly 75-150 video clips depending on quality settings and length. Free outputs include a watermark. The Standard plan at $8/month removes the watermark and increases credits to 700/month.

**How long can Pika videos be?**
Individual Pika clips are up to 10 seconds long. There is no built-in stitching tool; if you need longer videos, you need to generate multiple clips and combine them in a separate editor.

**How does Pika compare to Runway?**
Runway offers more professional controls, longer clip lengths, and better consistency for cinematic use cases, but at a higher starting price and steeper learning curve. Pika is simpler and more accessible for creators who want results quickly without mastering advanced settings.
