# Boomy

Music generation with training wheels. Pick a genre template, click generate, get a song. Built-in distribution to Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal, and 40+ streaming services means the same tool that generates the track also releases it. Boomy's current distribution terms pay eligible creators up to 80% of Boomy net revenue rather than a simple keep-100% distributor model.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Boomy if you want the lowest-friction path from "I have no idea what I'm doing" to "I have a song on Spotify."** The onboarding is designed for zero musical knowledge. Creator at $9.99/mo gives you 500 song saves and 25 WAV downloads monthly; Pro at $29.99 gives unlimited song saves and 250 WAV downloads monthly.
>
> **Skip it if you care about output quality.** [Suno](/tools/suno/) and [Udio](/tools/udio/) both generate meaningfully better music. Boomy sounds like Boomy: templated, bounded, and often predictable. Fine for background, weak for featured.
>
> **The distribution split matters.** Most indie distributors (DistroKid, TuneCore) take 0-9%. Boomy's "up to 80% of net revenue" language is fine for casual experiments but less attractive for anyone expecting meaningful streams.

## When to pick Boomy

- **Absolute beginners.** Zero music theory, zero DAW experience. Click button, get song, done.
- **Content creators needing background audio.** Quick generation + commercial rights + distribution. Good fit for YouTube channels wanting custom music.
- **Experimenting with release workflows.** Test what it's like to "publish to Spotify" without the friction of production + distribution.
- **High-volume, low-effort releases.** Artists who release 10+ tracks/month for discovery SEO.

## When to pick something else

- **Quality-first music:** [Suno](/tools/suno/) or [Udio](/tools/udio/). Both beat Boomy on output quality meaningfully.
- **Orchestral or cinematic:** [AIVA](/tools/aiva/) specializes here.
- **Ambient / lo-fi for video:** [Mubert](/tools/mubert/) has endless streaming + stem support.
- **Traditional music production:** A DAW (Logic, Ableton, FL Studio) + MIDI is where serious work happens.
- **Royalty-free libraries:** Artlist, Epidemic Sound, or Uppbeat for background music without AI generation.

## Failure modes

- **Output quality ceiling.** AI music gen has advanced dramatically (Suno v4, Udio v2.1); Boomy's internal model hasn't kept pace.
- **Distribution split adds up.** A catalog that starts to earn real streaming revenue should be compared against a normal distributor where you keep a larger share after flat fees.
- **Distribution approval can take weeks.** Spotify and Apple Music moderation isn't instant; Creator's "expedited review" helps but doesn't guarantee speed.
- **Genre templates feel samey.** Easy to end up with output that sounds like other Boomy tracks.
- **Spotify has cracked down on AI-mass-upload.** Streaming services scrutinize AI-generated catalogs more aggressively in 2026. Some Boomy releases have been de-indexed.

## FAQ

**Can I actually earn money with Boomy songs?**
Technically yes, but Boomy's distribution terms pay up to 80% of Boomy net revenue and the output quality ceiling means most creators should expect small payouts. Anyone serious about AI music earnings should evaluate Suno Pro plus a distributor such as DistroKid instead.

**Is Boomy music free to use commercially?**
On paid tiers, yes, with caveats. Check the current license for ad sync, film, or game use which often require higher tiers or additional rights.

**How does Boomy compare to Suno?**
Suno generates better music. Boomy bundles distribution. Different target audiences: Suno for people who care about the song, Boomy for people who care about "a song on Spotify" as an experience.

**Do Spotify and Apple Music block AI-generated music?**
Not all of it, but enforcement has tightened in 2026. Mass-uploaded AI tracks face more scrutiny. Boomy's moderation layer tries to keep releases compliant but delisting still happens.
