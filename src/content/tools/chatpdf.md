# ChatPDF

ChatPDF made "talk to your PDF" simple: upload a document, ask questions, and jump back to cited passages. That core still matters, but the June 23, 2026 recheck keeps the product broader than a single-PDF chat box. The official surface includes multi-file folders, Word/PowerPoint/Markdown/text files, YouTube chat, AI Research, flashcards, slides, AI Writer, AI Detector, mobile apps, desktop app, and a backend API.

## System Verdict

> **Pick ChatPDF if you want the fastest low-friction document chat experience.** It is strongest when the job is "answer questions from this file or folder and show me where the answer came from." The side-by-side document view, page references, no-sign-up trial, and simple API keep it useful even though general chatbots now handle files.
>
> **Skip it if you need a research protocol, enterprise RAG, or deep source-library workflow.** ChatPDF's wider toolkit is useful for study and document triage, but Elicit, Scite, Semantic Scholar, NotebookLM, Humata, and AnythingLLM cover more serious literature review, citation context, source-pack analysis, team document controls, or self-managed data posture.
>
> **Who pays for Plus:** Users who run into daily free limits, need unlimited document analysis, rely on multi-file chats, or want study tools such as flashcards and slides often enough to justify it. Verify web checkout directly. The US App Store listing shows $14.99/month and $89.99/year for mobile Plus, but mobile pricing can differ from web and region-specific checkout.

## What Changed Since The Last Refresh

- **ChatPDF is no longer only a PDF chat page.** The official navigation now promotes AI Writer, AI Detector, YouTube Chat, AI Research, desktop app, mobile app, flashcards, slides, summary, and PDF AI pages.
- **File support broadened in the public copy.** The homepage FAQ now says ChatPDF supports PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Markdown, and text files, not just PDFs.
- **Multi-file folders are a first-class workflow.** The site says users can create folders, add files, and ask questions that reference multiple sources in one conversation.
- **The study toolkit expanded.** AI Flashcards can turn PDFs or text into study cards and export to Anki, Quizlet, Brainscape, or CSV; AI Slides creates presentation material from PDFs; YouTube Chat answers against public transcripts with timestamps.
- **AI Research deserves a caveat.** ChatPDF's research page searches academic papers and cites sources, but it says summaries are based on abstracts and paper chat is available only for open-access papers. That keeps it in the lightweight research lane, not the Elicit or Scite lane.
- **Pricing is clearer on mobile than on the static web page.** The official site still confirms free and Plus positioning but not a public web price. The US App Store listing shows $14.99/month and $89.99/year in-app purchases, so AiPedia should treat exact Plus pricing as checkout-dependent.
- **The API story stayed narrow and useful.** Backend docs still focus on adding PDF sources and stateless chat messages, with 2,000-page or 32MB PDF limits. It is not a full hosted RAG platform.

## When To Pick ChatPDF

- **Single-document study.** Upload a textbook chapter, paper, report, or contract and ask grounded questions with clickable references.
- **Fast multi-file triage.** Use folders when a small set of related PDFs or documents needs one conversation.
- **Lightweight class or research prep.** Flashcards, summaries, slides, and side-by-side citations can turn a reading packet into study material quickly.
- **YouTube transcript lookup.** Paste a video URL and ask questions with timestamp references when the work depends on a public transcript.
- **Simple embedded PDF chat.** The backend API is useful when a product only needs source upload plus stateless Q&A against a PDF source ID.

## When To Pick Something Else

- **Systematic literature review:** [Elicit](/tools/elicit/) for paper discovery, screening, extraction tables, and review workflow.
- **Citation support or contradiction checks:** [Scite](/tools/scite/) for supporting, contrasting, and mentioning context.
- **Source-grounded notebooks:** [NotebookLM](/tools/notebooklm/) for reviewed source packs, reports, Audio/Video Overviews, and study artifacts.
- **Governed team document chat:** [Humata](/tools/humata/) for paid PDF/team workflows, or [AnythingLLM](/tools/anythingllm/) for self-hosted RAG.
- **Already paying for a frontier chatbot:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/), [Claude](/tools/claude/), and [Gemini](/tools/gemini/) all handle document uploads well enough for many casual users.

## Failure Modes

- **Not a complete research workflow.** AI Research cites papers, but its own FAQ says summaries use abstracts and full-paper chat is limited to open-access papers. Use it for discovery and triage, not as the evidence review.
- **Citation links still need inspection.** Page references make checking easier, but they do not prove the answer is complete or correctly interpreted.
- **Scans, charts, tables, and figures can still be weak.** OCR support helps, but data-heavy PDFs need manual review.
- **Multi-file folders are not the same as a governed knowledge base.** Teams still need permissions, retention, audit, and source-quality controls.
- **Privacy depends on policy fit.** The homepage says files are encrypted and deletable, while mobile store pages disclose collected user content and usage data. Review terms before uploading confidential material.
- **General assistants are closing the gap.** Paid ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini subscriptions can handle many document tasks. ChatPDF has to win on speed, references, study tools, and focus.

## FAQ

**Is ChatPDF accurate?**
It is most useful for direct questions whose answers are in the uploaded document. Treat references as a verification aid, not a guarantee. For critical work, open the cited page and inspect the source yourself.

**Does ChatPDF work with scanned PDFs?**
The Google Play listing says the app supports OCR for scanned documents or images. Quality will still depend on scan resolution, layout, handwriting, and table/figure complexity.

**Can I chat with multiple PDFs at once?**
Yes. ChatPDF now promotes folders for multi-file chats. That is useful for a small source set, but NotebookLM is still the better first choice for broader source-pack study.

**Is ChatPDF only for PDFs?**
No. The official FAQ says ChatPDF supports PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Markdown, and text files. The product is still branded around PDFs, so verify non-PDF behavior with your actual files before relying on it.

**How does ChatPDF compare to ChatGPT for PDFs?**
ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini can analyze uploaded documents, especially on paid plans. ChatPDF's advantage is the focused side-by-side interface, clickable references, folders, study tools, YouTube transcript chat, and PDF-specific API. Use a general assistant if you already pay for one and do not need ChatPDF's focused UX.
