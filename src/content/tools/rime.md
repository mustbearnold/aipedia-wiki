# Rime

Enterprise **conversational TTS** built for the phone, not the studio. Rime targets contact centers, IVR, and intelligent virtual assistants (IVA) where correct pronunciation, natural pacing, and low latency on a live call matter more than expressive narration or a large creator voice library.

Founded in 2022 by Lily Clifford, Brooke Larson, and Ares Geovanos in San Francisco. Rime says it now powers 100 million-plus phone conversations a month, with enterprise customers including Domino's and Wingstop. Domino's reported a 20% lift in customer task completion after replacing Microsoft TTS with Rime's voices.

The flagship model is **Coda**, which went live in the dashboard and API on **July 1, 2026**, one day before this page was verified. Coda is positioned as Rime's fastest and most expressive model yet, and Rime's own docs now recommend migrating existing Arcana traffic over to it.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Rime if you are modernizing a phone-based voice system.** IVR, contact centers, and IVAs live or die on pronunciation accuracy and prosody under real call conditions, and Rime is built specifically for that, with on-prem, VPC, or cloud deployment and HIPAA/SOC 2 compliance available.
>
> **Skip it for podcasts, audiobooks, or video narration.** [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/) and Fish Audio have deeper creator tooling, larger voice marketplaces, and no-code editing surfaces that Rime does not try to compete on.
>
> **Who pays what:** Starter is free up to 3,000 minutes, then pay-as-you-go at $0.05 per 1,000 characters with 20 concurrent generations, good for prototyping and small production loads. Enterprise is custom, built for large-scale contact-center deployments that need unlimited concurrency, unlimited voice clones, SLAs, and on-prem/VPC hosting.

## What it actually is

An API-first text-to-speech platform tuned for one job: sounding right on a live phone call, at production scale, without mispronouncing brand names, confirmation codes, or technical terms. Rime is not trying to be a creator studio.

**Coda** is the current flagship, shipped July 1, 2026. Rime describes it as the first TTS model built for enterprise conversations, with a 180-plus voice library, sub-100ms model latency when self-hosted on GPU (cloud adds roughly 25-50ms network latency), word-level timestamps, and multilingual coverage across English, Spanish, French, Portuguese, German, and Japanese.

**Arcana** was the prior flagship, known for emotionally expressive speech (natural laughs, sighs, breathing) and fine prosody control. Rime's docs now point existing Arcana traffic toward Coda, so treat Arcana as a legacy path rather than the model to build new integrations on.

**Mist** (v2 and v3) is the speed-optimized line. Mist v3 targets the fastest time-to-first-audio (~37ms P50) in English only; Mist v2 adds Spanish and custom pronunciation control with ultra-fast on-prem latency (~70ms), useful when brand-name pronunciation needs manual overrides.

Deployment flexibility (cloud, on-prem, or dedicated VPC) plus SOC 2 and HIPAA BAA availability on Enterprise are the enterprise-sales wedge against consumer-first TTS vendors.

## When to pick Rime

- **Replacing legacy IVR or contact-center TTS.** Rime's whole positioning is correct pronunciation and natural pacing under live-call conditions, the exact failure mode of older robotic TTS.
- **Regulated industries needing on-prem or VPC.** Healthcare, finance, and other compliance-heavy buyers can deploy Rime off the public cloud entirely and still get HIPAA BAA and SOC 2 reports.
- **High call volume where latency compounds.** Sub-100ms self-hosted model latency matters when a system is handling large simultaneous call loads.
- **Teams standardizing on a phone-first voice stack.** Rime's roadmap (Coda succeeding Arcana) is aimed squarely at enterprise conversation quality, not creator features.
- **Budget-conscious prototyping.** 3,000 free minutes on Starter is a meaningful runway to test call quality before committing spend.

## When to pick something else

- **Podcast, audiobook, or video narration:** [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs/). Deeper studio tooling, larger voice marketplace, and expressive long-form narration workflows Rime does not target.
- **Real-time voice-agent infrastructure with bundled STT/LLM orchestration:** [Cartesia](/tools/cartesia/). Line platform bundles TTS, STT, and orchestration in one stack with native LiveKit/Daily/Twilio integrations.
- **No-code creator UI:** ElevenLabs or Fish Audio. Rime is API-first with no consumer-facing voiceover editor.
- **Cheapest raw per-character multilingual API:** compare directly against current per-minute or per-character rates on [Cartesia](/tools/cartesia/) and other [AI Voice](/categories/ai-voice/) options before committing to volume pricing.

## Failure modes

- **Pricing pages disagree with each other.** The live rime.ai/pricing page, Rime's own December 2025 blog post, and third-party integration pricing (LiveKit) do not present a single consistent number for per-model rates. Get a written quote before committing budget.
- **Coda just launched.** As of this page's verification date (2026-07-02), Coda has been generally available for one day. Expect rough edges, incomplete documentation, and rapid iteration in the weeks after launch.
- **No creator UI.** Rime is API-first. Solo creators or small teams without engineering resources should look at ElevenLabs or Fish Audio instead.
- **Smaller company, thinner track record than ElevenLabs.** $25.1M total raised is modest next to better-funded voice AI competitors, which matters for longevity risk on multi-year enterprise contracts.
- **Enterprise features are gated behind custom pricing.** Unlimited concurrency, unlimited voice clones, on-prem, and HIPAA BAA all sit on the Enterprise tier, not Starter, so serious production deployments should expect a sales conversation.
- **FAQ section on the live pricing page was not fully populated at verification time,** which suggests the page was mid-update. Re-verify before treating any single number here as final.

## Recent developments

- **Coda shipped July 1, 2026** as Rime's new flagship TTS model, replacing Arcana as the recommended default for new applications. Rime's docs explicitly recommend migrating existing Arcana traffic to Coda.
- **Domino's and Wingstop** are cited as enterprise customers, with Domino's reporting a 20% increase in customer task completion after switching from Microsoft TTS to Rime.
- **Rime's pricing structure has shifted at least twice in the last seven months:** a December 19, 2025 three-tier Starter/Growth/Enterprise revamp with per-character model pricing, followed by a simpler Starter/Enterprise structure visible on the live pricing page in July 2026. Confirm current terms directly before budgeting.
- **Total funding sits at $25.1M** across two seed rounds (2023 and May 2025), with the May 2025 round publicly announced at $5.5M led by Unusual Ventures.

## System verdict

Rime is a credible specialist pick for one job: making phone-based voice systems sound less robotic without breaking on pronunciation. It is not a general-purpose TTS platform and should not be evaluated as one. Enterprises replacing legacy IVR, or building new contact-center voice agents, should shortlist Rime alongside Cartesia. Creators and studios should look elsewhere entirely.
