# Clipdrop

A Swiss-army-knife collection of AI image tools. Originally built on Stability AI's tech (Stable Diffusion variants), acquired by Stability in February 2023, then sold to Jasper in February 2024. Currently operated by Jasper.ai.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Clipdrop for fast, single-action AI image edits.** Background removal is genuinely best-in-class for casual use. Relight, Reimagine, Uncrop, and the text-in-image cleanup tool are all useful one-click operations. Pro pricing remains cheap compared to equivalent per-operation APIs.
>
> **Skip it if you already have Adobe Creative Cloud or a dedicated image pipeline.** Firefly covers background removal, generative fill, text-to-image, and Uncrop-equivalent tools inside Photoshop. If you're paying $4.99/mo for Firefly already, Clipdrop is duplicative.
>
> **Who picks which tier:** Free tier for occasional hobbyist use and testing. Pro for anyone hitting daily quotas or needing HD output, no watermark, and skip queue. Heavier users with API workflows should compare Clipdrop API pricing against Stability AI direct or Fal.ai.

## When to pick Clipdrop

- **One-off image cleanup.** Remove a person from a photo, upscale a thumbnail, change the lighting on a product shot. Fast, focused, cheap.
- **Batch background removal.** Product photography pipelines, marketplace listings, social media assets. The API scales.
- **Non-designers.** No learning curve. Upload image, click tool, download result.
- **Mobile-first workflows.** iOS and Android apps are solid; many competitors are web-first.

## When to pick something else

- **Deep editing needs:** [Photoshop](https://www.adobe.com/products/photoshop.html) with [Firefly](/tools/adobe-firefly/) handles everything Clipdrop does plus layers, masks, and precise control.
- **Text-to-image generation:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/), [Flux](/tools/flux/), or [Ideogram](/tools/ideogram/) produce higher-quality images than Clipdrop's Stable Diffusion integration.
- **High-volume API:** Go direct to [Stability API](https://platform.stability.ai) or [Fal.ai](https://fal.ai) for per-call economics.
- **Free forever + open source:** [Upscayl](https://upscayl.org) for upscaling, [remove.bg](https://www.remove.bg) for background removal. Both have limited free tiers but no paid subscription needed for light use.

## Failure modes

- **Output quality varies by tool.** Background removal is excellent. Relight is solid. Reimagine (full image regeneration) can produce inconsistent results compared to Midjourney or Flux direct.
- **Watermarks on free tier are aggressive.** Not suitable for any deliverable work; Pro is required for unwatermarked output.
- **Owner churn matters.** Stability to Jasper transition shifted roadmap priorities. Expect feature pace to depend on Jasper's strategic focus; under Jasper the product has stayed alive but generative-model upgrades have slowed compared to the Stability era.
- **Free-tier metering is per-tool, per-day.** Users running multiple tools in sequence (background remove then upscale then relight) can hit the 20/24h cap on a single tool while the others stay open. Plan for it.
- **Not a full image editor.** Single-action tools, no layer support, no masks, no brushwork.
- **AI generation quality lags frontier.** Clipdrop's built-in Stable Diffusion XL is older tech than Flux 1.1 Pro, Ideogram 3, or Midjourney V7. New generative work in 2026 happens elsewhere.

## FAQ

**Is Clipdrop still owned by Stability AI?**
No. Stability acquired it in February 2023 from founders InitML, then sold it to Jasper.ai in February 2024. Currently operated by Jasper.

**Does Clipdrop have an API?**
Yes, with per-operation pricing. Best for developers integrating specific tools (background removal, upscaling) into their own products. Check Clipdrop docs for current rates.

**How does Clipdrop compare to Photoshop?**
Clipdrop does 5-10 specific AI-powered operations well. Photoshop does everything but requires learning and costs more ($20.99/mo standalone). For quick one-off edits Clipdrop wins; for deep work Photoshop wins.

**Is free tier good enough?**
20 requests per 24h per tool with a watermark is enough for hobbyist trials. Anyone using Clipdrop for work should be on Pro for unwatermarked HD output and the 1,000/24h cap.
