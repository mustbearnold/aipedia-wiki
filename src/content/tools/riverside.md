# Riverside

Remote recording platform for podcasts and video shows. Every participant records locally in their browser at studio quality (up to 4K video, 48kHz uncompressed audio per speaker), and tracks upload progressively to the cloud. A network hiccup on call never hits the master recording because the master is captured at source.

Shipped 2020 by Riverside.fm, backed by Adam Lisagor's network and funded through a Series B. Now widely used by Marketplace (NPR), Spotify's Gimlet alumni, and most "video podcast" creators on YouTube.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Riverside if remote multi-speaker recording quality is your bottleneck.** Local-track capture solves the classic Zoom-podcast problem (one dropped packet = one unusable minute on the master). Each speaker uploads their own clean track; you mix in post. The Magic Editor ships AI short-form clips, AI transcripts, and captions in the same session.
>
> **Skip it for solo recording or edit-first workflows.** A local DAW or Descript beats Riverside when there are no remote guests. Riverside is capture-first; editing is possible but [Descript](/tools/descript/) owns that step.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for evaluation (2-hour monthly cap, 720p). Standard $15/mo annual for most solo creators. Pro $24/mo annual for creators publishing video podcasts (Magic Clips, transcription). Business $29/mo annual for small teams needing the full Magic Editor and higher hour caps.

## What it actually is

A browser-based multi-participant recorder. Host sends a session link; guests click, pick their mic and camera, record happens locally in their browser; the mixed "monitor" preview is what everyone sees/hears, but the master tracks are separate per speaker and upload in the background.

The moat is the per-speaker local-track guarantee. Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams compress everything for live call fidelity. Riverside records uncompressed at each endpoint and transmits a compressed monitor stream for the live call. Post-production gets studio-quality files.

Added capabilities layered on top:

- **Magic Clips.** AI scans long recordings, extracts candidate short-form clips, suggests captions and titles. Reduces the "1-hour interview to 5 TikToks" workload.
- **Magic Editor.** Transcript-based editor (Business tier). Descript-like but in-Riverside.
- **Live streaming.** Simulcast to YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn on Pro+.
- **Media Board.** Drop sound effects, intros, music beds into the session live.
- **Closed captions.** Auto-generated captions for video output.

## When to pick Riverside

- **Remote podcast interviews.** You record guests in different cities; network quality is unpredictable. Riverside removes the risk that a dropped connection ruins an interview.
- **Video podcast production.** 4K per-speaker video tracks with matched audio make multi-cam edits feasible.
- **Short-form content at scale.** Magic Clips takes a 60-minute episode and proposes 5-10 publishable TikTok/Shorts/Reels candidates.
- **Team productions with simple post pipelines.** Export multitrack to Descript, Adobe Audition, or Premiere.
- **Multi-language transcription needs.** 100+ language support covers global interview shows.

## When to pick something else

- **Solo recording with no remote guests:** a local DAW (Audacity, Audition, Logic) does this simpler and free.
- **Transcript-first editing:** [Descript](/tools/descript/) owns this step. Many creators use Riverside to capture and Descript to edit.
- **Browser-only, no install for any participant:** SquadCast or Zencastr both run entirely in-browser with competitive capture.
- **Live-streamed audio that publishes instantly:** Twitter Spaces, LinkedIn Live, or a local OBS setup. Riverside records; it does not publish for you.
- **YouTube Shorts and TikTok clip generation without the full Riverside stack:** [Captions](/tools/captions/) focuses on the short-form output step.

## Failure modes

- **Uploads fail over flaky connections.** Progressive upload is designed for this, but long sessions on very poor networks can lose some guest track segments. Mitigations: enable "local recording backup" and collect backups post-session.
- **Browser CPU spikes.** 4K recording with multiple guests can overload low-end laptops. Some guests will see their browser slow or tracks degrade.
- **Magic Editor is Business tier.** Transcript-based editing inside Riverside requires the $29/mo plan. Many teams use Standard for capture, then Descript at $24/mo for editing, which costs more in aggregate but gives access to Descript's deeper editor.
- **Not an editor.** Riverside captures beautifully; the actual cut, mix, and master happen elsewhere. Most workflows add Descript or a DAW.
- **Guest onboarding friction.** Guests need Chrome or Edge, a wired internet connection recommended, and 5 minutes of setup. Zoom is "click and join"; Riverside is "click, grant permissions, test mic, test camera, start." Not a blocker for professional shows, occasionally painful for one-off interviews.
- **Storage and retention.** High-quality raw multitracks are large; long-running accounts accumulate terabytes. Export and archive off-platform regularly.

## FAQ

<details>
<summary>Is Riverside free?</summary>

Yes for evaluation. The Free plan offers 2 hours/month of recording at 720p with basic transcription. Most working podcasters upgrade to Standard ($15/mo annual) or Pro ($24/mo annual) for unlimited recording, 4K, and the Magic Clips feature.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why Riverside over Zoom for podcast recording?</summary>

Zoom compresses everything for live-call fidelity; you capture a compressed, heavily-filtered mix. Riverside records each speaker's audio and video locally at studio quality (48kHz uncompressed, up to 4K video) and uploads progressively. You edit from master-quality source tracks, not broadcast-compressed streams.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Do guests need to install anything?</summary>

No. Guests join via browser on Chrome or Edge. They grant mic/camera permissions and click record. Mobile apps exist for iOS and Android if guests prefer, but they are optional.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How does Riverside compare to Descript?</summary>

Different jobs. Riverside captures remote recordings at local source quality; Descript is a transcript-first editor. Many teams use both: Riverside for capture, Descript for edit. If you do solo recording (no remote guests), you can skip Riverside entirely and use Descript end-to-end.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is Magic Clips?</summary>

An AI feature that scans long recordings and proposes short-form clip candidates (15-60 seconds). Generates auto-captions and suggested titles. Available on Pro and Business tiers. Reduces the "turn an interview into 5 TikToks" task from 2 hours of manual review to 15 minutes of selecting from AI suggestions.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Riverside do live streaming?</summary>

Yes, on Pro and above. Simulcast to YouTube, Facebook Live, LinkedIn Live, or custom RTMP destinations while recording. The recorded master tracks stay separate from the live-streamed compressed output.

</details>
