# Mureka

## Recent developments

- **July 2, 2026:** Re-verified pricing directly against the live [mureka.ai/subscribe](https://www.mureka.ai/subscribe) page. Current plan structure is Free / Pro ($9/mo, $7.17/mo billed annually) / Premier ($27/mo, $21.59/mo billed annually). Widely syndicated third-party pricing citing a "Basic $8/mo" tier and "$24/mo Pro" appears stale; the live page shows no Basic plan.
- **June 22, 2026:** Coverage of Mureka repositioning as an "AI-native music platform" spanning creation through listening, with Digital Music News and VentureBeat both framing the move as an attempt to close the loop between generation and consumption, not just ship another generator.
- **March 19, 2026:** Mureka launched **Studio** (a DAW-style production workspace with timeline editing, stem separation, and MIDI export) and **Remix** (structured re-generation of existing tracks), both gated to the Premier tier.
- **March 2026:** **Mureka V9** shipped as the flagship model, using the **MusiCoT** (Music Chain-of-Thought) architecture that plans full song arrangement, verse and chorus placement, and transitions before generating audio, rather than producing audio sequentially.
- **2025:** **Mureka O1**, described by Skywork AI as the first "music reasoning" large model, launched March 26, 2025 alongside V6, with claimed state-of-the-art results against Suno in blind listening tests at the time.

Mureka is built by **Skywork AI**, a subsidiary of Beijing-listed **Kunlun Tech** (market cap roughly $6B as of mid-2026). The platform has grown to roughly **10 million users** across more than 100 countries and reported **annualized revenue near $12 million**, reaching positive gross profit after marketing spend in November 2025.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Mureka if Chinese-language vocals matter, or if you want a credible value alternative to Suno.** MusiCoT's structure-first generation produces coherent full songs, and independent testing consistently shows Mureka's Mandarin pronunciation and tonal accuracy ahead of Suno and Udio. Pro at $9/month undercuts Suno Pro ($10/month) while offering a larger monthly song allowance.
>
> **Skip it if you need a mature, widely-recognized brand with an unambiguous rights and credit-expiry policy, or if Chinese state-linked ownership is a procurement blocker.** Suno and Udio have deeper Western distribution, clearer legal precedent (however contested), and more third-party tooling. Mureka's internal "Gold" credit system also means the advertised song counts can overstate real usable capacity once stems, MIDI, and video exports are factored in.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free is a non-commercial sandbox (4 songs/day). Pro at $9/month ($7.17/month billed annually) is the right default for creators who need commercial rights and MP3 output at volume. Premier at $27/month ($21.59/month billed annually) is for producers who need stems, MIDI, DAW export, voice cloning, and the Mureka Studio workspace.

## What it actually is

A browser-based text-to-song generator. A prompt or set of lyrics goes in; a mixed, mastered track with vocals and instrumentation comes out. The differentiator Skywork AI leans on is **MusiCoT**, a chain-of-thought-style planning step that maps out song structure (verse, chorus, bridge, transitions) before the model renders audio tokens, rather than generating a track sequentially the way earlier autoregressive music models do. The stated goal is more coherent full-song arrangements rather than pretty but structurally loose fragments.

On top of generation, Mureka has been pushing toward a fuller **AI-native music platform**: **Studio** (timeline-based editing, stem separation, DAW session export) and **Remix** (structured re-generation of existing tracks) both shipped in March 2026, and June 2026 coverage frames the company's ambition as closing the loop between music creation and listening, not just shipping another generator.

The most consistently cited strength across independent reviews is **Chinese-language vocal quality**. Multiple testers and comparison pieces describe Mureka's Mandarin pronunciation, tonal handling, and phrasing as clearly ahead of Suno and Udio, which remain optimized primarily for English and other Western languages.

## When to pick Mureka

- **Chinese-language (Mandarin/Cantonese) song projects.** This is Mureka's clearest and most repeatedly verified edge over Western competitors.
- **Budget-conscious creators wanting commercial rights.** Pro at $9/month (or $7.17/month billed annually) undercuts Suno's $10/month Pro tier while advertising a larger monthly song allowance.
- **Producers wanting DAW-adjacent output.** Premier's stems, MIDI export, and Mureka Studio workspace target the same niche Suno's Premier/Studio tier does, at a lower headline price.
- **Voice cloning on a budget.** Premier includes professional voice cloning ("any voice can be the singer"), comparable in concept to Suno's Voices feature.
- **Multilingual coverage beyond English.** V9 was trained with explicit focus on Chinese, Japanese, and Korean alongside English, useful for creators targeting East Asian audiences.

## When to pick something else

- **Deeper brand maturity and Western distribution:** [Suno](/tools/suno/). Larger user base mindshare, Warner Music licensing partnership, and more third-party tooling and community content.
- **Raw audio fidelity and section-level editing:** [Udio](/tools/udio/). Still the producer's pick for inpainting and precise edits to specific parts of a track.
- **Royalty-free background beds without vocals:** [Mubert](/tools/mubert/). Different licensing posture, tag-based generation, no vocal generation to manage.
- **Orchestral, cinematic, or MIDI-first composition:** [AIVA](/tools/aiva/). Purpose-built for scoring workflows rather than pop/vocal song generation.
- **Procurement environments wary of Chinese ownership:** Hold and evaluate data-handling and entity risk before committing budget, especially for regulated or government-adjacent work.

Independent third-party testing has found that songs cost roughly 10-20 Gold depending on model version, while stem/MIDI extraction and music-video export can consume significantly more Gold per asset, so the advertised monthly song counts can overstate real usable capacity for heavier production workflows. ## Failure modes

- **Gold credit metering understates real cost.** Headline song-count allowances look generous, but stem separation, MIDI extraction, and video export consume Gold fast; heavy production workflows can burn a monthly allowance well before the advertised song count is reached.
- **Pricing and plan names have moved during 2026.** Multiple reputable-looking third-party sources still cite an older "Basic $8/Pro $24" structure that no longer matches the live site. Always re-check the official subscribe page before committing budget.
- **Credit-expiry policy is unclear.** Some third-party reviews claim credits never expire; this is not confirmed against Mureka's current official FAQ and should be verified directly with support before relying on it.
- **Ownership risk for regulated buyers.** Kunlun Tech is a Beijing-listed company; procurement teams in defense, finance, or government-adjacent work should evaluate data-handling and entity risk independent of product quality.
- **Smaller ecosystem than Suno or Udio.** Fewer third-party integrations, community tools, and less English-language coverage of edge cases and workarounds.
- **Non-Mandarin niche genres may still lag.** Mureka's most verified strength is Chinese-language vocals; claims of category-leading quality in Western niche genres (metal, jazz, classical) are less consistently supported across independent reviews than the Mandarin-vocal claims are.
- **Watermarking on some exports.** At least one hands-on review found exported music videos carried a visible watermark even on the top paid tier; verify current export behavior before relying on watermark-free video output.
