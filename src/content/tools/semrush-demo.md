# Semrush

Semrush is the broadest SEO and digital marketing platform on the market, and as of **April 28, 2026** it is an **Adobe company**. Adobe completed the roughly $1.9 billion acquisition ($12/share) on that date, so SEMR is no longer publicly traded. The suite spans 55+ tools across keyword research, backlink analysis, site audits, rank tracking, competitor intelligence, PPC research, and social.

Semrush Copilot, AI Visibility Toolkit, Semrush One, ContentShake AI, and the new **AI PR Toolkit** bring generative AI into core workflows. Current Semrush documentation separates the classic SEO Toolkit, Semrush One, AI Visibility Toolkit, AI PR Toolkit, Content Toolkit, and other toolkits, so buyers should price the bundle that matches their actual workflow.

Buyer implication: expect deeper Adobe Experience Cloud distribution and enterprise cross-sell, with Semrush product branding intact for now.

It extends Semrush's "visibility beyond search" thesis into earned media.

## What it actually is

A digital marketing platform, now an Adobe subsidiary, that bundles 55+ tools into a single subscription. The core pitch: stop paying for separate SEO, PPC, social, and content tools. Adobe's intent is to bolt this onto CX Enterprise so brand visibility (search, AI answers, social, PR) lives inside the same cloud as Adobe's analytics and experience tooling.

Core workflows run through Keyword Magic Tool (long-tail discovery), Backlink Analytics, Site Audit (up to 1M pages on Business), Position Tracking, Traffic Analytics, and the SEO Writing Assistant. PPC research spans keyword gap analysis, ad copy review, and display advertising insights across 150+ countries.

AI integration sits across several layers. Semrush One combines SEO and AI visibility for buyers who want one connected workflow. AI Visibility Toolkit can be evaluated separately from the classic SEO Toolkit. The new AI PR Toolkit extends the visibility thesis into earned media by tracking which outlets LLMs cite. Content Toolkit handles topic ideas, briefs, article generation, optimization, the Chrome extension workflow, and CMS publishing. Buyers should verify current pricing and limits before assuming a classic SEO plan includes every AI visibility or PR feature they need.

The moat is surface area plus, now, Adobe's enterprise channel. Ahrefs beats it on backlinks. Surfer beats it on content optimization depth. No competitor matches the total breadth, and none has Adobe's distribution behind it.

## When to pick Semrush

- **You run a marketing agency.** Client reports, competitive audits, and multi-channel dashboards all live in one platform. Saves on tool-stack sprawl.
- **You need SEO plus PPC plus social in one place.** Nothing else ships this breadth. Ad copy research, keyword gap analysis, and organic tracking share data.
- **You track brand visibility across AI search and earned media.** AI Visibility Toolkit ($99/mo) plus the new AI PR Toolkit cover the major AI platforms and the outlets LLMs cite, at a lower combined price than stitching rivals together.
- **You run technical audits on large sites.** Business plan audits up to 1M pages with log-file analysis.
- **You need Share of Voice and brand-level reporting.** Business and Enterprise include competitive share-of-voice tracking across organic and paid channels.
- **You need API access at agency scale.** Business at $499.95/mo unlocks the API; some integrations require Enterprise.
- **You are already in the Adobe stack.** Post-acquisition, Semrush is the natural visibility layer inside Adobe CX Enterprise for teams already paying for Experience Cloud.

## When to pick something else

- **Backlink depth and freshness:** [Ahrefs](/tools/ahrefs/). Deeper index, faster refresh. Starts at $29/mo.
- **Content grading in the base plan:** [Surfer SEO](/tools/surfer-seo/) at $79/mo. Content Editor is the core product, not an upsell.
- **Solo bloggers on a tight budget:** [Ahrefs Starter](/tools/ahrefs/) at $29 or SE Ranking at $52. Semrush Pro at $139.95 is overkill for single-site work.
- **Single-channel specialists:** Google Ads for PPC, native platform analytics for social. Semrush's breadth is overhead you may not need.
- **AI-first content generation at scale:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or dedicated content tools. ContentShake is solid but not best-in-class.

## Failure modes

- **High entry price.** $139.95/mo Pro is the highest floor among mainstream SEO tools. Ahrefs Starter at $29 and SE Ranking at $52 undercut hard for solo users.
- **Steep learning curve.** 55+ tools overwhelm new users. Expect weeks to navigate effectively, not days.
- **Backlink data lags Ahrefs.** Improved over time but still second in completeness and refresh cadence.
- **Pro tier limits bite fast.** 500 keywords tracked and 5 projects constrain agencies or multi-site operators. Move to Guru or Business early.
- **ContentShake AI is Guru+ or $60 add-on.** Pro users miss the full content-generation suite unless they pay extra.
- **AI visibility and PR packaging needs verification.** AI visibility can be evaluated through Semrush One or the AI Visibility Toolkit, and AI PR is a separate surface; confirm the live toolkit mix, prompts, domains, and user limits before buying.
- **Adobe integration is still early.** The acquisition closed April 28, 2026. Buyer-visible Adobe CX Enterprise integration, data-residency, and contracting changes are still being worked out; do not assume current SEMR-era terms carry over unchanged at renewal.
- **API access locked to Business+.** Pro and Guru users cannot integrate data into custom reporting or internal dashboards.
- **Pricing is in constant motion on add-ons.** Classic tier prices have held, but Semrush reshapes toolkit packaging more often than Ahrefs. Long-term budgeting should assume periodic packaging and add-on price shifts.
- **Dashboard density.** Reports and filters run deep. New users often cannot locate the specific view they need without training.

## FAQ

**Is Semrush worth $139.95/month?**
For agencies and digital marketing pros managing multi-channel campaigns, yes. Consolidates keyword research, backlinks, site audit, PPC, social, and AI visibility into one platform. Solo bloggers or link-building specialists should look at [Ahrefs](/tools/ahrefs/) at $29-$129 instead.

**Does Adobe own Semrush?**
Yes. Adobe completed the Semrush acquisition on April 28, 2026 for about $1.9 billion ($12/share). Semrush is now an Adobe company inside the CX Enterprise brand-visibility stack, and SEMR is no longer publicly traded. The Semrush product and branding remain in market.

**Semrush vs Ahrefs?**
Semrush wins on breadth (PPC, social, AI visibility tracking, AI PR, ContentShake) and now on Adobe enterprise distribution. Ahrefs wins on backlink depth and freshness. Many pros pay for both. If forced to pick one, match the tool to the dominant workflow: Ahrefs for link building, Semrush for everything else.

**What is Semrush Copilot?**
A free AI advisor included on every paid tier that proactively surfaces insights, alerts, and recommendations across your projects. Acts as a monitoring layer on top of the core data, not a separate chatbot.

**What is the AI Visibility Toolkit?**
A $99/mo add-on that tracks brand mentions and visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. Priced below Ahrefs Brand Radar's per-platform or bundle pricing, making it the cheaper option for multi-platform AI visibility monitoring ([Semrush AI SEO overview](https://www.semrush.com/ai-seo/overview/)).

**What is the AI PR Toolkit?**
A newer Semrush toolkit ([semrush.com/pr-toolkit](https://www.semrush.com/pr-toolkit/)) for earned-media visibility in the AI era. It finds the outlets and journalists that LLMs cite most, helps draft and send AI-assisted pitches, and syncs PR coverage data with the AI Visibility Toolkit.

**Do I need Guru for ContentShake AI?**
Guru at $249.95/mo includes ContentShake AI natively. Pro users can add it as a $60/mo add-on. If content workflows drive your use case, Guru is the cleaner pick.
