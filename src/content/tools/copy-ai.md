# Copy.ai

Copy.ai is an AI go-to-market automation platform. It started as a template-based copywriting tool in 2020, and the 2025 pivot reframed the product around sales outreach and marketing workflows connected to CRMs, not standalone generation. Ships with 100+ templates, CRM integrations for HubSpot, Salesforce, and Pipedrive, multi-LLM routing across OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google, plus 2,000+ integrations via Zapier.

As of June 23, 2026 the public pricing page still lists Chat at $29/month, then jumps to annual-billed Growth ($1,000/month, 75 seats, 20K workflow credits), Expansion ($2,000/month, 150 seats, 45K credits), Scale ($3,000/month, 200 seats, 75K credits), and custom Enterprise. The previously visible Free trial, Starter ($49/month), and Advanced ($249/month) tiers are no longer surfaced on the pricing page.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Copy.ai if GTM automation is the reason to buy, not copywriting alone.** Growth at $1,000/month (annual) is now the entry workflow tier with CRM chaining at scale: 20,000 workflow credits and 75 seats. Expansion at $2,000/month and Scale at $3,000/month carry larger GTM motions with 45K and 75K workflow credits and 150 to 200 seats. Chat at $29/month covers shared chat with five seats but does not include the workflow runtime.
>
> **Skip it if the workflow is one person generating copy.** A general assistant such as [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) delivers stronger raw drafting breadth, and [Jasper](/tools/jasper/) wins on brand-voice enforcement for pure copywriting shops. For grammar and correction layers, [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) fits better at the individual level.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Chat $29/month for teams that only need shared unlimited-word chat with five seats. Growth $1,000/month (annual) when CRM workflows have to carry a GTM motion across roughly 75 seats. Expansion $2,000/month or Scale $3,000/month when seat counts and workflow-credit volume push past Growth's caps. Enterprise for SSO, custom models, and priority support.

## What it actually is

A workflow platform that treats AI generation as one step in a longer GTM chain. Input flows from a CRM record, through prompt generation, into email or LinkedIn outreach, and back to the CRM for logging. The template library and chat interface are supporting features, not the main product anymore.

Multi-LLM routing picks the model per task: creative copy on one frontier API, factual content on another, analysis on a third. Users do not see the routing layer directly. Brand Voice applies tone account-wide after training on 10 to 50 writing samples.

The 2025 pivot is the story, and the May 2026 pricing reshape pushes it further. Copy.ai is now a GTM AI platform on the homepage and in the pricing structure, and the self-serve lineup has been compressed to one cheap chat tier and three annual-billed workflow tiers. Growth at $1,000/month is the new entry workflow plan; Expansion at $2,000/month and Scale at $3,000/month carry larger team and credit volumes. The retired Starter and Advanced tiers no longer absorb mid-market buyers in between.

The moats are workflow-specific: SOC 2 Type II certification, CRM integration depth, and the 2,000+ Zapier connectors. Raw generation quality tracks whatever frontier API is underneath, which is easy for competitors to replicate.

## When to pick Copy.ai

- **Sales-ops workflow.** Pull a lead from HubSpot, enrich, generate a personalized sequence, ship to Outreach or Apollo, log back to CRM. That chain is now the Growth-tier story at $1,000/month annual.
- **GTM automation at scale.** Growth at $1,000/month gives 75 seats and 20K workflow credits, enough to carry sales plus marketing plus ops on one platform. Expansion ($2,000/month, 150 seats, 45K credits) and Scale ($3,000/month, 200 seats, 75K credits) extend the same shape.
- **Brand voice across a team.** Copy.ai's Brand Voice applies account-wide after 10 to 50 training samples, tighter than relying on individual prompts.
- **Compliance-sensitive buyers.** SOC 2 Type II certification clears most mid-market procurement.
- **Multi-channel content factory.** Ads, emails, social, long-form, and outreach all in one dashboard with shared brand voice.

## When to pick something else

- **Solo copywriting:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) beats Copy.ai on raw drafting breadth when the buyer does not need GTM workflows.
- **Pure brand-voice content:** [Jasper](/tools/jasper/) is still the stronger enterprise-copy suite with deeper brand-voice governance.
- **Grammar and editing layer:** [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) for correction and polish, not generation.
- **Direct frontier-model team use:** [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) if the team wants first-party assistant access instead of a GTM workflow layer.
- **Data enrichment first, copy second:** Clay is the stronger specialist for prospect enrichment; pair with Copy.ai workflows or handle both inside Clay itself.

## Failure modes

- **Mid-market tier gap widened.** With Starter and Advanced retired, the only step below Growth's $1,000/month annual workflow tier is Chat at $29/month, which carries no workflow runtime. Two-to-fifteen-person teams that previously fit the $49 or $249 plans need to either commit to Growth or move to a different platform.
- **Solo users overpay.** Growth at $1,000/month annual makes no sense for one-person shops. Chat at $29/month covers shared chat but does not unlock workflows, and first-party general assistants still beat it on raw drafting breadth.
- **Workflows demand real setup.** CRM chaining requires mapping fields, building approval gates, and testing personalization quality. Not a weekend rollout.
- **Personalization hallucination risk.** AI-generated outreach that cites incorrect company details or misreads role data damages credibility. Monitor accuracy on the first 100 messages before scaling.
- **No proprietary model.** Routed OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google APIs. Output ceiling tracks whatever underlying model handles the call.
- **Template library is commoditized.** Custom GPTs on ChatGPT Teams replicate most Copy.ai templates at lower cost. The defense is the workflow layer, not the templates.
- **Model access trails direct API.** Copy.ai lags behind Anthropic and OpenAI first-party products on new-model availability by days or weeks.

## FAQ

**Does Copy.ai have a free plan?**
Not as of June 23, 2026. The earlier 2-week free trial is no longer surfaced on the [Copy.ai pricing page](https://www.copy.ai/prices). The entry plan is Chat at $29/month (or $24/month on annual billing) with five seats and unlimited words in chat.

**What models power Copy.ai?**
Routed third-party frontier APIs from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google. The platform selects per task; outputs track direct model quality with Copy.ai's formatting and brand-voice layer applied.

**Is the Growth plan worth $1,000/month annual?**
For sales and marketing teams using CRM-connected workflows at scale, yes. 75 seats and 20,000 workflow credits per month cover most mid-market GTM motions and pay the plan back if three to four hours per week of personalized outreach are automated. For content-only teams, ChatGPT Teams remains the stronger fit at $25/user.

**What changed in the May 2026 pricing reshape?**
Copy.ai compressed the self-serve lineup. The Free trial, Starter ($49/month), and Advanced ($249/month) plans are no longer listed. The published tiers are Chat ($29/month, five seats), Growth ($1,000/month annual, 75 seats, 20K workflow credits), Expansion ($2,000/month annual, 150 seats, 45K credits), and Scale ($3,000/month annual, 200 seats, 75K credits), plus custom Enterprise. The change widens the mid-market gap between cheap chat and four-figure workflow tiers.

**Copy.ai or Jasper for content teams?**
[Jasper](/tools/jasper/) wins on brand-voice enforcement and pure long-form copy governance. Copy.ai wins on GTM workflow automation and CRM integration. Pure content shops pick Jasper; sales-aligned content teams pick Copy.ai.
