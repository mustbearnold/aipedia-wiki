# Reve

Reve is a text-to-image generator from **Reve AI, Inc.**, a roughly 50-person Palo Alto startup that has raised about **$390M**. Its differentiator is architectural: **Reve Image 2.0** (launched June 3, 2026) represents every image as an editable, code-based **layout** rather than going straight from a text prompt to pixels. Each element in the scene gets its own position, size, and description that can be edited before or after rendering, which makes iterative changes close to lossless instead of re-rolling the whole image.

Reve Image 1.0 (codenamed "Halfmoon") launched March 25, 2025 and briefly took the #1 spot on the Artificial Analysis Image Arena leaderboard, ahead of Midjourney v6.1, Imagen 3, Recraft V3, and FLUX 1.1 Pro. Reve 2.0 currently sits **#2 on the same leaderboard**, behind OpenAI's GPT Image 2 and narrowly ahead of Google's Gemini 3.1 Flash Image Preview. Reve also added Veo 3.1-powered video generation in October 2025, currently limited to US/Canada accounts.

Pricing is a **free tier plus Lite ($7.99/mo) and Pro ($19.99/mo)**, priced around a "creative energy" pool rather than a flat per-image credit.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Reve if layout precision and text rendering matter more than chasing the single top leaderboard score.** The code-based layout model means you can nudge one element (move a logo, resize a headline, swap a background color) without regenerating the whole composition, and its dedicated typography pass renders in-image text more reliably than most competitors.
>
> **Skip it if you need a mature, high-volume public API today, or want the current #1-ranked image model.** Reve's API is still in beta with separate console pricing, and GPT Image 2 currently outranks Reve 2.0 on the Image Arena leaderboard.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for casual use and testing the layout workflow (daily energy refresh), Lite $7.99/mo for regular creators on small projects (5x Free's energy and storage), Pro $19.99/mo for creators who also want video generation and heavier throughput (100x Free's energy and storage, plus 250 video energy per month).

## What it actually is

Reve is a text-to-image (and now text/image-to-video) generation product built around a **code-based layout** representation instead of the pixel-diffusion-only pipeline most competitors use. When you prompt Reve, the model first constructs a structured layout, an internal, editable description of where each element sits, how big it is, and what it depicts, and only then renders pixels. Because that intermediate layout is addressable, you can go back and adjust one region (resize a product shot, move a headline, restyle a background) without the compounding quality loss that comes from re-diffusing an entire pixel-based image repeatedly.

That architecture pays off most visibly in **in-image text rendering**. Reve runs a dedicated typography pass, so quoted headlines, packaging labels, and small captions come out legible and correctly spelled more consistently than in most pixel-only competitors, a long-standing weak point for diffusion models generally.

Reve 2.0 renders natively at **4K (16 megapixels)**, which removes the separate upscaling step many workflows otherwise need for print or poster output.

Video generation, added October 2025, runs on **Veo 3.1** under the hood rather than a Reve-trained video model: text-to-video, image-to-video (start/end frame), or prompt-plus-image-reference, with 4-8 second base clips extendable to 148 seconds, integrated sound, and landscape/portrait HD output. It is currently gated to US/Canada accounts.

## When to pick Reve

- **Iterative, layout-precise design work.** Marketing assets, packaging mockups, and multi-element compositions where you need to tweak one part without redoing the whole image.
- **In-image text and typography.** Logos, posters, packaging, and thumbnail copy render more reliably than in most pixel-only generators, though production-critical typography should still get a human proofread pass.
- **Native 4K output.** Print or large-format work that would otherwise need a separate upscaler.
- **Budget-conscious creators who still want frontier quality.** Lite at $7.99/mo and Pro at $19.99/mo undercut most other frontier-tier image subscriptions while Reve 2.0 sits at #2 on the leaderboard.
- **Occasional video from a still image.** Free and Pro users in the US/Canada get Veo 3.1-powered animation bundled into the same subscription.

## When to pick something else

- **Top-ranked raw image quality:** GPT Image 2 inside [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) currently ranks #1 on the Image Arena leaderboard, ahead of Reve 2.0.
- **Aesthetic/cinematic composition and moodboards:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/). Still the reference point for stylized, cinematic output and has a purpose-built asset-organization workflow.
- **Open weights, self-hosting, or fine-tuning:** [Flux](/tools/flux/) (Black Forest Labs). Full public API plus open-weight Dev checkpoints.
- **Production typography as the single highest priority:** [Ideogram](/tools/ideogram/) remains the most battle-tested option specifically for dense logo and poster text.
- **Vector-friendly brand and design-system work:** [Recraft](/tools/recraft/). Native vector output and brand-kit tooling that Reve does not offer.
- **Free, no-subscription entry point:** [Leonardo](/tools/leonardo/) or self-hosted [Stable Diffusion](/tools/stable-diffusion/).
- **Mature, high-volume production API:** Flux or GPT Image 2. Reve's API is still in beta.

## Failure modes

- **"Creative energy" pricing is not a simple per-image count.** Free, Lite, and Pro are described as multiples of a shared energy pool (5x and 100x Free) rather than a fixed number of images per month; high-volume users should test actual throughput before committing.
- **Video is US/Canada only.** International users on Free or Pro currently cannot access the Veo 3.1-powered video feature at all.
- **API is beta.** Pricing lives on a separate console page and the product is explicitly labeled beta; teams needing SLA-backed production API access should compare Flux or GPT Image 2 first.
- **Not the #1-ranked model.** GPT Image 2 currently sits ahead of Reve 2.0 on the Image Arena leaderboard; buyers optimizing purely for leaderboard score should account for that gap.
- **Default opt-in to model training on Free.** Free-tier generations are opt-in to Reve's model training by default; Lite and Pro offer an opt-out. Privacy-sensitive users should check their account settings before uploading proprietary material.
- **Newer, smaller ecosystem.** Reve has a fraction of Midjourney's or Flux's plugin, community, and third-party tooling footprint, since the code-based layout architecture and the company itself are both comparatively new.
