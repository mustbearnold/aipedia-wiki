# Loudly

## Recent developments

- **March 12, 2026:** Loudly launched **VEGA-2**, its next-generation in-house AI music model, adding automatic mastering, EQ balancing, dynamic compression and genre-specific production tuning on top of VEGA-1. Included at no extra cost for existing subscribers.
- **2026:** Loudly continues to position its AI training set (reported at roughly 10 million songs) as "ethical AI with rights-safe training principles," distinct from rivals facing major-label litigation.
- **Ongoing:** Loudly Distribution remains bundled into every paid subscription tier, with royalty withdrawal requiring an annual plan.

Loudly is an AI music generator built by **Loudly GmbH** (Berlin, founded 2011 as JAM Just Add Music GmbH, renamed Loudly in 2020), the company behind the earlier **Music Maker Jam** app. The current product generates full tracks, stems and samples from prompts or genre/BPM/key controls using its proprietary **VEGA-2** model, then routes straight into **Loudly Distribution**, which pushes the release to Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, TikTok, Deezer, Tidal and 50+ other platforms.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Loudly if you want generation and distribution in one subscription.** Most AI music tools stop at the download; Loudly ships the track to streaming platforms itself, with artists keeping 100% of streaming royalties (subject to a $10 payout minimum and annual-plan requirement for withdrawals).
>
> **Skip it if you need permanent ownership of your output, or the highest vocal/songwriting fidelity.** Loudly's license runs only while a subscription is active; cancel and you lose the right to publish new work and any pending distribution earnings revert to Loudly. [Suno](/tools/suno/) and [Udio](/tools/udio/) remain ahead on vocal realism and lyrical songwriting.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free is a demo tier (30-second clips, 1 download/month, non-commercial). Personal at $10/mo (about $8/mo billed annually) fits solo creators making full-length tracks for social content, podcasts and small releases. Pro at $30/mo (about $24/mo billed annually) is for business-scale commercial use, higher volume, and larger ad-campaign licensing.

## What it actually is

A browser-based AI music generator paired with a first-party release pipeline. Users pick a genre, mood, BPM and key (or type a prompt), generate two variations in roughly 10 seconds, then refine with an AI Song Remixer, Text-to-Music composer, AI Sample Generator, Stem Splitter and FX/mastering tools. The current generation engine is **VEGA-2**, released March 12, 2026, which layers automatic mastering and genre-specific production tuning on top of composition, so tracks are meant to move from generation straight to release with minimal manual post-processing.

The distinguishing feature versus most AI music competitors is **Loudly Distribution**: a built-in path from generated track to a live Spotify/Apple Music/Amazon Music/YouTube Music release, with artists keeping 100% of streaming royalties. Distribution is included in every paid subscription tier rather than sold as an add-on, though claiming and withdrawing royalties requires an annual plan.

Loudly's roots go back to **Music Maker Jam**, a mobile music-remix app the company (originally registered as JAM Just Add Music GmbH in Berlin in 2011) operated for years before rebuilding around generative AI and renaming to Loudly in 2020.

## When to pick Loudly

- **Creators who want to skip a separate distributor.** Generate a track and release it to major streaming platforms from the same subscription, without signing up for DistroKid, TuneCore or similar separately.
- **Social, podcast and small-brand content needing quick royalty-free beds.** Personal's 300 creations/month and 3.5-minute max cover most short-form and podcast-length needs.
- **Independent artists testing release workflows.** The 100%-royalty-retention pitch and SmartLink promotion tool are aimed squarely at solo artists experimenting with AI-assisted releases.
- **Buyers who value a stated "ethical AI" training story.** Loudly markets its dataset as rights-safe by design, a different posture from rivals facing active major-label litigation.

## When to pick something else

- **Best vocal realism and songwriting fidelity:** [Suno](/tools/suno/). Stronger vocals, lyric handling and voice cloning; no bundled distribution.
- **Best raw audio fidelity and section-level editing:** [Udio](/tools/udio/). Producer-favored for inpainting and precise edits.
- **Ambient/background beds without vocals:** [Mubert](/tools/mubert/). Tag-based generation with a public render API.
- **Simple beginner song generation with paid downloads:** [Boomy](/tools/boomy/). Similar beginner-friendly angle, different licensing and distribution mechanics; verify current terms before comparing.
- **Orchestral/cinematic scoring and MIDI-first composition:** [AIVA](/tools/aiva/). Better fit for composers who need copyright-owned, editable scores.
- **Permanent ownership of generated output regardless of subscription status:** Look elsewhere. Loudly's license lapses when the subscription ends; competitors vary on this and should be checked individually.

## Failure modes

- **License is rental, not ownership.** Cancel the subscription and you lose the right to publish new tracks; unclaimed distribution earnings revert to Loudly. Read the license agreement before building a catalog you expect to own long-term.
- **Commercial scope is tier-gated and capped.** Personal excludes larger venues and bigger ad budgets; Pro caps advertising-campaign use around $100K/year and still excludes TV broadcast, theatrical release and physical products without a separate commercial agreement.
- **No AI-model training or dataset extraction allowed.** The license agreement explicitly bars using Loudly's output to train other AI models or build a music dataset, with immediate termination and no refund for violations.
- **Reselling raw generated files is prohibited.** Standalone resale or repackaging of generated content, in whole or in part, is not permitted under the license terms.
- **Royalty withdrawal is gated behind the annual plan.** Distribution is bundled into all paid tiers, but claiming and withdrawing streaming earnings requires upgrading to annual billing.
- **Pricing page could not be independently scraped.** Loudly's public pricing page is a fully client-rendered SPA; the numbers in this review are corroborated across several third-party trackers rather than read directly off raw HTML, so reverify exact Pro-tier caps at checkout.
- **Vocal and lyric quality trail the genre leaders.** For vocal-forward songwriting, Suno and Udio remain the stronger picks; Loudly is more competitive on instrumental and production-forward tracks.
