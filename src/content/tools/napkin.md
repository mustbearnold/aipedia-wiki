# Napkin AI

Napkin AI turns plain text into visuals. You paste or write notes, an idea, or a document section, and Napkin generates editable diagrams, flowcharts, timelines, and infographics that match the meaning of the text. It is built for people who can write clearly but do not want to open a design tool to illustrate the point.

The core loop is fast: write or paste text, let Napkin suggest several visual treatments, pick one, then edit the icons, colors, fonts, and layout. Output exports to PNG, SVG, PDF, and PowerPoint, and visuals can be shared by link for collaboration.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Napkin AI if you regularly need clean diagrams and infographics from text for slides, documents, and posts, and you do not want to design them by hand.** Its strength is the speed from a sentence to a usable, on-brand visual, with editable elements rather than a flat image.
>
> **Skip it if you need photoreal images, full presentation design, or video.** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) and [Flux](/tools/flux/) lead on images, [Gamma](/tools/gamma/) and [Canva](/tools/canva/) build full decks, and Napkin does not generate video or audio.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for occasional visuals and trial, the Plus tier for individuals who export often and want more AI generations, the Pro tier for heavy users and teams who need collaboration and branding, and Enterprise for organization controls. Confirm the live price before buying, since plans are credit-metered.

## What it actually is

Napkin sits between a notes app and a design tool. Instead of generating a finished picture, it reads the structure of your text, a process, a comparison, a hierarchy, a timeline, and proposes diagram forms that fit. You stay in control of the result because the output is a set of editable shapes, icons, and text rather than a single rasterized image.

That editability is the real differentiator. Many AI image tools produce a flat picture you cannot easily change. Napkin's visuals can be restyled, recolored, and rearranged, and exported as SVG with clean elements, which makes them practical for decks and documents that need to stay on brand and get revised.

The tradeoff is scope. Napkin is focused on explanatory visuals, not photoreal art, not full slide-deck authoring, and not video. It is a fast illustration layer that complements writing tools and presentation tools rather than replacing them.

## When to pick Napkin AI

- **You write first, then illustrate.** If your raw material is text, Napkin removes the step of opening a separate design tool.
- **You need diagrams and infographics, not photos.** Flowcharts, process maps, comparisons, and timelines are its sweet spot.
- **You want editable, on-brand output.** Elements stay editable and export to SVG and PowerPoint for further work.
- **You are not a designer.** The suggestions give a clean starting point without design skill.
- **You produce decks, docs, or posts regularly.** Repeated visual needs are where the speed compounds.

## When to pick something else

- **Photoreal or artistic images:** [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney/) and [Flux](/tools/flux/) lead there. Napkin is explanatory visuals only.
- **Full presentation design:** [Gamma](/tools/gamma/) generates entire decks, and [Canva](/tools/canva/) is a complete design suite.
- **Vector brand assets and logos:** [Recraft](/tools/recraft/) is vector-native with stronger brand-style control.
- **Video:** Napkin has none. Use a dedicated video generator.

## Pricing

Napkin offers a free plan plus paid tiers. As of June 2026 the live pricing page lists a Plus tier at $9 per person per month and a Pro tier at $22 per person per month, both about 25% cheaper on annual billing, with an Enterprise plan on custom terms. Free covers occasional use with weekly AI credits; paid tiers raise the monthly credit allowance and add branding, more export formats, and team features.

Because AI generations are metered by credits and plans can change, confirm the current credit limits and export rights on the live pricing page before committing a team.

## Failure modes

- **Pricing varies by source.** Published numbers are inconsistent across reviews, so verify the live plan before buying.

- **Explanatory visuals only.** No photoreal image generation, no video, no audio.

## FAQ

**What does Napkin AI do?**

It turns written text into editable visuals such as diagrams, flowcharts, timelines, and infographics, which you can restyle and export to PNG, SVG, PDF, or PowerPoint.

**Is Napkin AI free?**

There is a free plan with limited AI generations, enough to try it and make occasional visuals. Heavier use and collaboration move you to a paid tier.

**Does Napkin generate images or video?**

No. Napkin makes explanatory diagrams and infographics, not photoreal images or video. Use Midjourney or Flux for images and a dedicated tool for video.

**How is Napkin different from Canva or Gamma?**

Canva is a full design suite and Gamma builds entire presentations. Napkin is narrower and faster for one job: turning text into editable diagrams and infographics you can drop into decks and documents.
