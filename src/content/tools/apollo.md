# Apollo.io

Apollo.io is a combined B2B data platform and sales engagement tool. The same seat covers a 400M+ contact database, multi-step sequences, a dialer with call recording, CRM sync, and the agentic [AI Assistant launched in March 2026](https://www.apollo.io/magazine/whats-new-aug-2025).

Free tier exists. Paid plans run $49 to $119 per user per month on annual billing.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Apollo when one seat should carry database, sequencing, dialer, and CRM sync.** Professional at $79/user/mo unlocks the built-in US dialer with call recording and is the tier most working outbound teams actually need. The AI Assistant runs GTM workflows from natural-language prompts, not just suggestions.
>
> **Skip it if sending volume is the bottleneck.** [Instantly](/tools/instantly/) wins on unlimited inbox rotation and is cheaper per send at scale. **Skip it if enrichment depth is the bottleneck.** [Clay](/tools/clay/) waterfalls 50+ sources Apollo alone cannot match. **Skip it for enterprise coverage**: ZoomInfo still holds the data lead on mid-market and enterprise accounts, at roughly 3x the cost.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for data sampling, Basic $49/mo for founders on single-channel outbound, Professional $79/mo for most reps (dialer included), Organization $119/mo for teams needing intent data, advanced API, and international dialing (3-seat minimum).

## What it actually is

One seat, four jobs: contact search, sequence sender, dialer, and AI research layer. Apollo imports enriched leads straight into sequences, runs email and LinkedIn steps with A/B tests, and syncs every touch back to the CRM.

The March 2026 [AI Assistant](https://www.apollo.io/magazine/whats-new-aug-2025) executes multi-step GTM workflows from natural-language prompts. Apollo reports users engaging with it book 36% more meetings in their first 14 days.

Meeting Intelligence records calls, generates summaries, and auto-populates CRM fields from transcripts. It ships on Organization and select Professional configurations.

## When to pick Apollo

- **You want database plus sending plus CRM sync in one seat.** No other tool bundles all four at this price.
- **Your reps need a working US dialer with call recording.** Included in Professional at $79/user/mo.
- **You run a small outbound team (2 to 15 reps).** Organization's per-seat features scale without the ZoomInfo contract floor.
- **You are a founder doing sales yourself.** Basic at $49/mo replaces four separate point tools.
- **You use HubSpot or Salesforce heavily.** Bidirectional sync is the standard integration path.

## When to pick something else

- **High-volume cold email across many inboxes:** [Instantly](/tools/instantly/). Unlimited sending accounts on every plan, cheaper per send at scale.
- **Deep multi-source enrichment and custom AI research:** [Clay](/tools/clay/). Waterfalls Apollo plus 49 other sources with Claygent on top.
- **Enterprise data coverage for mid-market and up:** ZoomInfo still wins on account depth, at roughly $20K+ annual minimums.
- **Meeting capture as the primary need:** [Fireflies](/tools/fireflies/) or Gong. Apollo's meeting intelligence is a bolt-on, not a specialty.
- **CRM replacement rather than sync:** HubSpot or Pipedrive directly. Apollo is not a CRM.

## Failure modes

- **Email deliverability depends on your own hygiene.** Bounce rates on Apollo pulls run 4-12% depending on segment. SMB and startup contacts bounce more than enterprise. Verify with NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before large sends.
- **Intent data is Organization-only.** The $119/user/mo floor plus 3-seat minimum pushes the real cost to $357/mo before any reps.
- **Credit accounting is lumpy.** Mobile credits cost 8 each. Power users burn through Professional's 100/mo allocation inside two weeks on phone-heavy outreach.
- **Auto-renewal trap.** Cancellation needs 60 days written notice before renewal. Missing that window locks another annual term. Diarize the date.
- **AI Assistant is newer than the marketing suggests.** Launched March 2026. Results vary by workflow complexity; plain lead scoring works, multi-tool chained prompts still need supervision.
- **Support lag on lower tiers.** Free and Basic users report 48+ hour response times during peak periods.
- **Intent coverage US-heavy.** EMEA and APAC signal strength still trails US by 30-40%.
- **Monthly billing tax.** The posted $49/$79/$119 rates assume annual. Monthly adds 15-25%.

## FAQ

**Is Apollo.io free?**
Yes. The free tier includes limited credits and sequences, enough to test data quality in your market. Most teams upgrade to Basic or Professional within their first weeks of serious use ([Apollo pricing](https://apollo.io/pricing)).

**What is the Apollo AI Assistant?**
An agentic layer launched in March 2026 that executes GTM workflows from natural-language prompts. It handles lead scoring, research summaries, follow-up drafting, and CRM field population. Apollo reports 36% more first-14-day meetings for users who engage it ([Apollo product updates](https://www.apollo.io/magazine/whats-new-aug-2025)).

**Apollo vs ZoomInfo?**
ZoomInfo has deeper mid-market and enterprise data. Apollo delivers roughly 85% of that coverage at a fraction of the price, and it bundles sequencing and dialer ZoomInfo sells separately. Growth-stage teams land on Apollo; enterprises with six-figure budgets often still default to ZoomInfo.

**Apollo vs Clay?**
Apollo is all-in-one: database, sender, dialer, sync. Clay is an enrichment specialist that pulls from 50+ sources including Apollo and runs custom AI research via Claygent. Sophisticated outbound teams often run both: Apollo for base lists and CRM flow, Clay for per-account signal stacking.

**Does Apollo include a dialer?**
Yes on Professional ($79/user/mo annual) and above. US calling with recording is built in. International dialing requires Organization.

**What counts as a credit?**
Email credits cover verified email reveals (1 credit each). Mobile credits cover phone number reveals (8 credits each). Export credits cover CSV and integration pulls. Annual plans grant the full allocation upfront.
