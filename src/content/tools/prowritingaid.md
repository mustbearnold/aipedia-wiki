# ProWritingAid

ProWritingAid is a **manuscript-level writing analysis tool** built by Orpheus Technology Ltd, a London-registered company with its operational base in Oxford, UK. Founder Chris Banks started the project in 2012 while writing his own novel, and it has since grown into a platform the company says over **4 million writers** use.

The core differentiator is depth over speed. Where [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) focuses on inline, sentence-level correction across the browser, ProWritingAid runs **25+ full-document reports** built for authors: pacing, dialogue tags, overused words, sticky sentences, sensory detail, and style consistency across an entire manuscript. It integrates directly with **Scrivener** and **Atticus**, the two dominant long-form drafting and book-formatting tools, so authors edit inside their existing workflow instead of exporting chapters into a separate app.

## System Verdict

> **Pick ProWritingAid if you are editing a full manuscript, not a paragraph.** The pacing, dialogue, and overused-word reports are built for 80,000-word drafts, and native Scrivener/Atticus integration means fiction writers never leave their drafting tool to get manuscript-level feedback.
>
> **Skip it if you want fast, inline browser corrections or quick paraphrasing.** [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/) covers Gmail, Docs, and Slack with lower friction for everyday writing. [QuillBot](/tools/quillbot/) is faster for one-off sentence rewrites and citations. Neither of those tools was built for chapter-length structural editing.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free works for short excerpts (500-word cap) or evaluating the reports before buying. Premium at $30/mo (or $120/yr) is where most serious novelists land, unlocking unlimited word count and rephrases. Premium Pro at $36/mo (or $144/yr) adds higher AI-feature caps and an author-coaching workshop library for writers who want ongoing craft development, not just a checker. Teams at $12/user/mo fits small editorial or co-writing groups.

## What it actually is

ProWritingAid is a writing-analysis engine that plugs into wherever an author already drafts, rather than a standalone editor. It ships as a Chrome/Edge/Firefox extension, a Microsoft Word add-in, a Google Docs add-on, and native integrations with **Scrivener** and **Atticus**, the two most common long-form book-writing and formatting tools.

The report suite is the product. Beyond grammar and spelling, ProWritingAid runs dedicated checks like the **Overused Words Report** (flags weak, vague, or repetitive word choices across five categories), the **Dialogue Tags Report** (catches redundant "she said softly" constructions), the **Sticky Sentences Report** (measures glue-word density per sentence), and a **Pacing Report** that flags slow passages likely to lose reader interest. Chapter Critique and Virtual Beta Reader add AI-generated structural feedback closer to what a human editor or beta reader would flag.

This is deliberately different from Grammarly's model. Grammarly optimizes for low-latency inline suggestions across any web text box. ProWritingAid optimizes for deep, full-manuscript passes that authors run periodically during revision, not every sentence they type.

## When to pick ProWritingAid

- **Editing a full novel or long-form manuscript.** The report suite is designed around chapter- and book-length structure, not single paragraphs.
- **You draft in Scrivener or format in Atticus.** Native integration means no exporting chapters to a separate editor and back.
- **You want pacing and dialogue diagnostics.** No mainstream grammar checker offers a dedicated Pacing Report or Dialogue Tags Report at this depth.
- **You're revising for overused words and sticky sentences.** These reports catch patterns invisible at the sentence level but obvious across a full draft.
- **You want structured craft development, not just corrections.** Premium Pro's workshop library and coaching content target skill-building alongside editing.

## When to pick something else

- **Inline browser grammar checking:** [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/). Better for Gmail, Docs, Slack, and everyday writing that never leaves the browser.
- **Fast paraphrasing and citations:** [QuillBot](/tools/quillbot/). Nine rewrite modes and a citation generator built for students and quick rewrites, not manuscript structure.
- **Voice-preserving sentence rewrites:** [Wordtune](/tools/wordtune/). Better for polishing tone on shorter professional text.
- **Zero-to-one long-form drafting:** [Claude](/tools/claude/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/). ProWritingAid analyzes and improves existing text; it does not draft chapters from a blank page.
- **Fiction-specific AI co-writing:** [Sudowrite](/tools/sudowrite/). Built for generative fiction drafting and brainstorming, which sits upstream of ProWritingAid's editing pass.

## Recent developments

The 2026 pricing restructure introduced **Premium Pro** as a distinct tier above the original Premium plan, bundling higher AI-feature caps with an author-coaching workshop library, live sessions with authors and editors, and cowriting sprints. This shifts ProWritingAid's positioning from a pure editing tool toward a broader author-development platform. The same restructure raised annual Premium pricing from $79/yr to $120/yr.

Atticus added native ProWritingAid integration, extending the tool's reach beyond Scrivener into the newer, faster-growing book-formatting app popular with self-published authors.

## System verdict

ProWritingAid earns its place by specializing where Grammarly and QuillBot do not: full-manuscript structural feedback for fiction and long-form nonfiction. The Scrivener and Atticus integrations remove the export/re-import friction that would otherwise make deep editing tools impractical for novelists. The Free tier's 500-word cap makes it effectively a demo rather than a usable plan for full manuscripts, so serious use requires Premium. The 2026 price increase on annual Premium is worth budgeting for before renewal.

## FAQ

<details><summary>Is ProWritingAid free?</summary>

There is a free tier, but it caps checks at 500 words and 2 report runs per day, which is impractical for a full manuscript. Premium at $30/mo (or $120/yr) removes the word-count cap.
</details>

<details><summary>Does ProWritingAid work with Scrivener?</summary>

Yes. ProWritingAid has a native Scrivener integration for both Windows and Mac, letting authors run reports without exporting chapters to a separate app.
</details>

<details><summary>Does ProWritingAid work with Atticus?</summary>

Yes. Atticus added ProWritingAid integration via the Chrome extension, which works with Atticus's desktop PWA as well as the web app.
</details>

<details><summary>ProWritingAid vs Grammarly, which should I use?</summary>

ProWritingAid is built for manuscript-level structural editing (pacing, dialogue, overused words) and integrates with Scrivener and Atticus. Grammarly is built for fast, inline sentence-level correction across the browser (Gmail, Docs, Slack). Many novelists use ProWritingAid for manuscript passes and a general grammar tool for everyday email and messages.
</details>

<details><summary>What is a Chapter Critique?</summary>

An AI-generated structural critique of a chapter, closer to feedback a human editor or beta reader would give than a line-level grammar check. Premium includes 1 per day; Premium Pro includes 3 per day.
</details>

<details><summary>Is there a lifetime plan?</summary>

Yes. Premium lifetime is $399 one-time and Premium Pro lifetime is $699 one-time, both verified on the current pricing page as of July 2026.
</details>

<details><summary>Does ProWritingAid have a Teams plan?</summary>

Yes. Teams is $12/user/month billed annually for 2 to 100 users, including shared style guides, team analytics, and centralized user management. Enterprise (100+ seats) is custom-priced.
</details>
