# Unbounce

Unbounce is the landing-page specialist that has been in the market since 2009. The 2024-2026 era focus is AI: **Smart Builder** turns a prompt plus campaign details into a full landing page, **Smart Traffic** uses ML trained on 2B+ conversions to route each visitor to the page variant most likely to convert for them, **Smart Copy** generates and rewrites landing-page copy, and industry benchmarking surfaces conversion performance data by vertical.

The platform ships five tiers: Starter $29/mo, Build $99/mo, Experiment $149/mo, Optimize $249/mo, and Concierge / Agency custom. All tiers include unlimited conversions, 100+ templates, free hosting, and Google Analytics, HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Twilio, and Zapier integrations.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Unbounce when paid-campaign landing pages are the bottleneck.** Performance marketers running Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, or programmatic display funnels land here. Smart Traffic is the differentiator: rather than A/B testing and picking one winner, it routes each individual visitor to the variant most likely to convert for them specifically. That capability plus the mature A/B testing and conversion analytics justify the $99-$149 tier for serious marketing teams.
>
> **Skip it for solo blogs or Webflow-first workflows.** [Carrd](https://carrd.co) and [Framer](https://framer.com) cover simple personal or startup landing pages at a fraction of the cost. [Webflow](https://webflow.com) wins if you need the landing pages to also be your marketing site. HubSpot users should stay inside HubSpot's landing-page builder for CRM integration depth.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Starter $29/mo for solo founders with a single campaign. Build $99/mo is the practical floor for any team running multiple campaigns. Experiment $149/mo is the most common choice for marketers who run A/B tests as part of campaign management. Optimize $249/mo unlocks Smart Traffic ML routing. Concierge / Agency custom for high-volume traffic, multi-client agency workflows, and dedicated success management.

## What it actually is

A landing page platform split across two builder paradigms. **Classic Builder** is the original drag-and-drop editor with pixel-level control, multi-step forms, pop-ups, sticky bars, dynamic text replacement, and every integration. **Smart Builder** is the AI-first replacement that generates a full page from a prompt plus campaign context, with AI insights nudging the copy and design based on performance data from 2B+ historical conversions.

**Smart Traffic** is the ML-driven differentiator. Traditional A/B testing picks one winner and sends all traffic there; Smart Traffic looks at the individual visitor (device, location, source, referrer, day, time) and routes them to the variant statistically most likely to convert for someone like them. Unbounce publishes customer case studies showing 20-30% lift vs traditional A/B winners.

**Smart Copy** is the in-app AI copywriting tool. Generate headline variations, expand paragraph copy, rewrite in different tones, summarize long sections, and translate. Trained on Unbounce's own conversion data rather than a generic LLM.

**A/B Testing** is unlimited on Experiment and above, with statistical confidence scoring and automatic winner detection.

## When to pick Unbounce

- **Paid campaigns are your main traffic driver.** Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, programmatic. The ROI math changes when Smart Traffic lifts conversion 15-30% on the same ad spend.
- **You run multiple landing-page tests per month.** Experiment at $149/mo unlocks unlimited A/B testing with statistical significance flagging. Cheaper tools make you build testing infrastructure yourself.
- **Agency workflow with multiple clients.** Concierge / Agency tier with multi-domain support, multi-seat collaboration, and client reporting justifies itself if you manage 5+ client landing-page programs.
- **You want AI inside an established platform.** Smart Builder and Smart Copy are not raw GPT wrappers; they are trained on conversion-specific data and integrated with the platform's analytics.
- **E-commerce promo pages.** Seasonal campaigns, promo landing pages, affiliate-driven funnels all benefit from rapid variant generation and Smart Traffic routing.

## When to pick something else

- **Solo blogs or personal landing pages:** [Carrd](https://carrd.co) at $19/year or Framer's free tier covers simple one-pagers.
- **Your marketing site + landing pages in one tool:** [Webflow](https://webflow.com) or [Framer](https://framer.com). Unbounce is deliberately landing-page-only.
- **HubSpot or Salesforce-first marketing ops:** Stay inside the CRM. Integration depth with native builders beats Unbounce's connectors for lead-routing workflows.
- **Developer-first code-backed pages:** Next.js + Vercel + optimizely for experiments. Lower floor cost if you have the dev team.
- **Sub-$30/mo budget:** Leadpages Standard or Systeme.io free tier covers basics. Unbounce's entry $29 is already above budget-tool alternatives.

## Failure modes

- **Starter tier is marketing-speak.** 5 pages and 500 visitors/mo is essentially a demo allowance. Real use starts at Build $99/mo.
- **Smart Traffic is gated to $249/mo.** The flagship AI differentiator only unlocks on Optimize tier. Teams that land on Experiment for A/B testing have to upgrade to access the ML routing.
- **Smart Builder outputs need editing.** Prompt-to-page generation is usable scaffolding, not production-ready design. Expect to iterate.
- **Visitor caps create surprises.** Going from 20K to 30K traffic/month mid-campaign triggers an upgrade. High-traffic seasonal campaigns need to anticipate this.
- **Classic Builder and Smart Builder are different tools.** Pages built in one cannot be easily migrated to the other. Teams commit to one paradigm.
- **Landing-page-only scope.** Not a replacement for a full marketing site. Requires a separate CMS or website builder for main-site presence.
- **Integration depth varies.** Native integrations with HubSpot and Salesforce are strong; deep two-way sync with CRMs often requires Zapier in the middle.
- **Pricing has crept up.** Mid-2020s tier renaming (Launch / Optimize / Accelerate retired) and price increases put Unbounce at the premium end of landing-page tools.

## FAQ

**What is Unbounce used for?**
Unbounce is a landing-page platform built for marketers running paid campaigns. Customers build campaign-specific landing pages with a drag-and-drop editor or AI-prompt generator, run A/B tests, and use Smart Traffic to route visitors to the best-performing variant for their segment.

**What is Smart Traffic?**
An ML-driven visitor routing feature that learns from 2B+ conversions to send each individual visitor to the landing-page variant statistically most likely to convert for them. Available on Optimize $249/mo and above. Published customer case studies show 20-30% lift vs traditional A/B test winners.

**What is Smart Builder?**
The AI-first landing-page builder. Generate a complete page from a prompt and campaign details (goal, audience, offer). Editor then lets you refine the output. Faster to first draft than Classic Builder for users who know the final design they want.

**What is Smart Copy?**
An AI copywriting tool trained on Unbounce's own conversion-performance data. Generate headlines, rewrite paragraphs, expand short copy, summarize, and translate landing-page copy. Available on all tiers.

**How does Unbounce compare to Leadpages?**
Unbounce is the premium option with stronger AI features (Smart Traffic, Smart Builder, Smart Copy) and higher traffic caps per tier. Leadpages is cheaper for simple landing pages without advanced A/B or AI-routing needs. Unbounce wins for serious paid-campaign marketers; Leadpages wins for small businesses on tighter budgets.

**What's the affiliate commission?**
20% recurring for 12 months per referred paid customer, 90-day cookie, managed through PartnerStack.

**Is there a free trial?**
Yes, 14-day free trial available on all tiers. No credit card required to start.

**Does Unbounce integrate with HubSpot and Salesforce?**
Yes, native integrations for both, plus Mailchimp, Google Analytics, Twilio, Zapier, and webhooks for anything else.
