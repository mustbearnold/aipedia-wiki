# Speechify

Speechify is a text-to-speech application designed to help people consume written content by listening rather than reading. It supports importing PDFs, Google Docs, web articles, emails, physical books (via camera scan), and ePubs, then reads them aloud in AI-generated voices at speeds up to 4.5x. The target audience is people who want to move faster through large reading loads — students, professionals, and people with dyslexia or other reading difficulties.

Speechify is not a voice creation tool. It is a reading and listening tool. That distinction matters: it competes with Audible and read-aloud browser extensions more than it competes with [ElevenLabs](../tools/elevenlabs.md) or [Murf](../tools/murf.md). The core value proposition is speed-reading via audio, not producing voice content for others.

The premium plan at $139/year (roughly $11.58/month) unlocks higher-quality AI voices and speed options above 1x. An optional voice cloning add-on lets users hear their own documents read back in their own voice. The free tier covers standard voices at normal speed, which is functional but limited compared to the premium experience.

## What It Does

Speechify converts text from virtually any source — PDFs, websites, documents, physical pages, ebooks, emails — into AI-generated audio and plays it back through natural-sounding voices at adjustable speeds. The app is available on iOS, Android, Chrome extension, and Mac desktop. It remembers your position across devices and lets you adjust voice, speed, and pitch on the fly. A built-in AI assistant can summarize, explain, or answer questions about the content you're reading. The voice cloning add-on trains on your voice to read content back to you in a familiar sound.

## Who It's For

- **Students** — consume textbooks, research papers, and lecture notes faster via audio
- **Professionals with heavy reading loads** — clear email inboxes, read reports, and catch up on industry content while multitasking
- **People with dyslexia or visual impairments** — accessibility use case where listening removes the friction of text processing
- **Non-native speakers** — hear correct pronunciation of words while reading along
- **Podcast and audio learners** — consume long-form written content in the same way they'd consume a podcast

## Key Features

- **Multi-source import** — paste text, import PDFs, connect Google Drive, use the Chrome extension on any website, or scan a physical page with your phone camera
- **Speed control up to 4.5x** — Speechify trains users to gradually increase listening speed, with most users reaching 2-3x within weeks
- **Cross-device sync** — listen on iPhone, switch to desktop, continue where you left off
- **AI summary and Q&A** — built-in AI can summarize the document you're reading or answer questions about its content
- **Offline mode** — download content to listen without an internet connection (mobile)
- **Dyslexia-friendly fonts** — display options optimized for reading while listening
- **Voice cloning** — hear your own documents in your own voice (add-on feature)

## Limitations

- **Not a content creation tool** — Speechify produces audio only for the user's own listening; it does not export audio files for sharing or publishing. For that, use [ElevenLabs](../tools/elevenlabs.md) or [Murf](../tools/murf.md)
- **Premium price is annual-only** — $139/year with no flexible monthly option makes it a real commitment before you know if speed-listening works for you
- **Free tier voices sound robotic** — the standard voices on the free plan are noticeably synthetic; the quality jump to Premium HD voices is significant
- **Moderate moat** — browser read-aloud extensions (like those built into Edge and Chrome) and iOS accessibility features cover basic use cases for free
- **Voice cloning is personal only** — the cloned voice cannot be exported or used to create content for other people

## Bottom Line

Speechify earns an 8/10 on utility for its core audience: people who want to consume large amounts of written material faster. The cross-device sync, speed controls, and broad import support are well-executed. Value is 7/10 — $139/year is reasonable if speed-listening becomes a daily habit, but the free alternatives are good enough for casual use. Moat is 6/10 because browser and OS-level read-aloud features are free and improving. Longevity is 7/10; the company has strong brand recognition in the accessibility space. Not the right tool if you want to produce voiceover content for others.

## FAQ

**Is Speechify free?**
Yes. Speechify has a free plan that includes standard AI voices and basic importing at normal speed. The free plan covers simple use cases but lacks the HD voices and high-speed playback that make Speechify most valuable. Premium costs $139/year.

**Can Speechify export audio files?**
No. Speechify is designed for personal listening and does not export audio files for sharing or publishing. If you need to produce audio files from text for distribution, use a tool like [ElevenLabs](../tools/elevenlabs.md), [Murf](../tools/murf.md), or [LOVO](../tools/lovo.md).

**Does Speechify work with PDFs?**
Yes. Speechify can import PDFs directly, connect to Google Drive, or read PDFs via the browser extension. It handles academic papers, reports, and ebooks reliably, including basic table and figure navigation.
