# SciSpace

SciSpace is an AI research assistant that began in 2015 as **Typeset.io**, a paper-formatting tool with 40,000+ journal templates, and rebranded to SciSpace in 2022 as it expanded into AI-driven discovery, comprehension, and writing. The product line now spans chat-with-PDF, literature review synthesis, an AI Writer with citation search across a large paper corpus, a paraphraser, an AI detector, and the original formatting engine.

Pricing runs Free through Premium, Teams, Advanced, and custom Enterprise. Premium is the practical entry point at roughly $12/mo billed annually, or about $20/mo on monthly billing.

## System Verdict

> **Pick SciSpace if you want one workspace that covers PDF chat, literature search, and citation-backed drafting.** It is strongest for individual researchers and students who read and write across many papers and want fewer tool switches than stitching together a PDF chat app, a citation manager, and a separate writing assistant.
>
> **Skip it if you need formal systematic-review infrastructure or bulletproof enterprise pricing documentation.** [Elicit](/tools/elicit/) has a purpose-built structured-extraction and PRISMA workflow that SciSpace does not match. SciSpace's own pricing page sits behind a bot-challenge wall, and third-party sources disagree on some plan names and higher-tier pricing, so procurement teams should get a live quote rather than trust any secondhand price sheet, including this one, without confirming at checkout.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free/Basic for casual PDF chat and light literature search, Premium for individual researchers who want unlimited Copilot queries and paper summaries, Teams for small labs sharing a seat pool, Advanced for heavier users who need the Deep Review literature-synthesis model, Enterprise for institutions needing SSO/SAML and SOC 2 Type 2 controls.

## What it actually is

SciSpace is a multi-tool research workspace rather than a single-purpose app. The core surfaces are:

**Chat with PDF (Copilot)**: upload a paper or set of papers and ask questions, get answers sourced from specific sections with citations, and request summaries or related-work suggestions.

**Deep Review / literature review**: given a research question, SciSpace searches its paper index, filters for relevance, extracts key findings, and organizes results into a structured summary across studies. This is the closest SciSpace gets to Elicit's systematic-review workflow, but it is not built around PRISMA-style protocol, screening, and structured-extraction tables the way Elicit is.

**AI Writer**: drafts outlines, introductions, and full sections, pulling citations from the paper corpus and updating references as you write. Supports multiple citation styles.

**Paraphraser and AI Detector**: rewrite text in 20+ preset tones, and check text or PDFs for AI-generated content.

**Formatting engine**: the original Typeset product. Auto-formats a manuscript to a target journal's template from a library of 40,000+ styles.

**SciSpace Agent**: a newer, broader "super agent" positioning that chains discovery, data mining, and drafting from a single prompt, aimed at compressing multi-step research workflows.

## When to pick SciSpace

- **You want PDF chat, search, and writing in one subscription.** Fewer tools to juggle than pairing a separate chat-with-PDF app, reference manager, and writing assistant.
- **You still need journal formatting.** The 40,000+ template library is a genuine differentiator carried over from Typeset and not commonly bundled elsewhere.
- **You write drafts with live citation support.** The AI Writer pulls and updates citations as you go, which is useful for early drafting even if you still need a human pass before submission.
- **You want a lower price floor than Elicit's paid tiers.** SciSpace Premium at roughly $12/mo undercuts Elicit's Pro tier, though the two tools solve different problems (see below).
- **You are an individual researcher or student, not running a funded systematic-review program.** SciSpace's workflow is lighter-weight and easier to start using immediately than a protocol-driven systematic review tool.

## When to pick something else

- **Formal systematic or scoping reviews with structured extraction tables:** [Elicit](/tools/elicit/). Purpose-built protocol, screening, extraction-column, and PRISMA workflow.
- **Free, high-volume paper discovery:** [Semantic Scholar](/tools/semantic-scholar/). No cost, large corpus, citation graph, TLDR summaries.
- **Conversational research answers with a consensus signal:** [Consensus](/tools/consensus/). Consensus Meter shows study agreement directly in the answer.
- **General document chat beyond academic PDFs:** [ChatPDF](/tools/chatpdf/) or [Humata](/tools/humata/). Broader document and format support, simpler scope.
- **General-purpose research chat with web grounding:** [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) or [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/). Faster for casual questions, not paper-corpus-specific.

## Recent developments

SciSpace has pushed toward a broader "AI research super agent" framing in 2026, chaining discovery, data mining, analytics, and drafting from a single prompt. Recent product pages also highlight an enhanced My Library feature with structured per-paper summaries, the ability to query only your uploaded library, and a Zotero integration for reference management. ## System verdict

SciSpace is best understood as a research-workflow bundle rather than a single-purpose tool. Its differentiators, the formatting-template library and the combined chat-plus-write surface, trace back to its Typeset origins and are genuinely useful for individual researchers who want fewer subscriptions. It is not a substitute for Elicit's structured-extraction workflow when the deliverable is a formal systematic review. Buyers should also budget extra time to confirm current pricing directly with SciSpace, since the vendor's own pricing page is bot-walled from automated verification and third-party listings disagree on some higher-tier details.

## Failure modes

- **Pricing page is bot-walled.** scispace.com serves an AWS WAF challenge to non-browser traffic, which means even careful buyers relying on search snippets or cached pages may see stale or inconsistent numbers. Always confirm at checkout.
- **Plan naming is inconsistent across sources.** "Teams" and "Lab" are both used in different places for what may be the same or adjacent offerings; a possible "Max" tier is not consistently documented.
- **Credits do not roll over.** Reported behavior is that unused monthly credits expire, so bursty usage patterns can waste allowance.
- **Deep Review is not a full systematic-review protocol tool.** It synthesizes across papers but does not replace PRISMA-style screening, structured extraction columns, or formal inclusion-criteria tracking the way Elicit does.
- **Corpus-size claims vary by page.** SciSpace's own marketing cites both 280M+ and 200M+ paper figures in different places; do not treat either number as precise.
- **AI-drafted writing still needs a human pass.** The AI Writer accelerates drafting and citation pulling, but original contribution, argument quality, and citation accuracy still require researcher review before submission.
