# Pitch

Pitch is a collaborative presentation software product developed by Pitch Software GmbH, founded in 2019 in Berlin. It provides real-time multi-user editing, AI-assisted writing, and a curated template library for creating professional slide decks, and is primarily used by remote and distributed teams that collaborate on presentations daily. Its key differentiator is the collaboration layer: Pitch is the most fully featured presentation tool for teams that need simultaneous editing, comment threads, approval workflows, and deck management at scale, with unlimited decks on the free tier. As of April 2026, Pitch offers a free plan with unlimited decks, Pro at $22 per month, and Business at $60 per month ([Pitch](https://pitch.com)).

Pitch launched as an explicit challenger to PowerPoint and Google Slides on the collaboration axis. Its founding team came from the design and startup world in Berlin, and the product reflects that: clean interface, strong template quality, and a workflow optimized for teams that iterate on decks across multiple contributors. The AI writing features — introduced in 2023 and expanded since — handle slide text generation, rewriting, and tonal adjustment, but the AI layer is additive to a collaboration-first product rather than central to the pitch.

The unlimited decks free tier is a genuine differentiator for small teams and startups. Most competitors restrict the number of presentations or collaborators on free plans. Pitch's approach is to monetize on the workflow features — real-time collaboration, analytics, and brand controls — rather than on deck count.

## What It Does

Pitch provides a browser-based presentation editor with simultaneous multi-user editing, a library of professionally designed templates, and an AI writing assistant that generates and rewrites slide text from prompts ([Pitch](https://pitch.com)). Teams can work on the same deck simultaneously, leave comments pinned to specific slides, assign slides to team members, and track version history. Presentations are shared as live URLs with view analytics showing who opened the deck and how long they spent on each slide. PPTX and PDF export are available on all tiers. The AI writing feature accepts a topic or context and generates slide outlines, headlines, and body copy that users can edit or regenerate.

## Key Features

- **Real-time collaboration:** Multiple users edit simultaneously with live cursors, comment threads, and @mentions pinned to specific slides
- **Unlimited decks on free tier:** No cap on presentations created or stored; a genuine advantage over most competitors
- **AI writing assistant:** Generate slide outlines and body copy from a topic, rewrite existing text for different tones, and create speaker notes automatically
- **Curated template library:** Professionally designed templates across business categories including investor decks, product launches, quarterly reviews, and sales pitches
- **Version history:** Full revision history with named versions and one-click restore
- **Presentation analytics:** Track opens, views per slide, time on slide, and viewer identity for shared decks
- **Brand kit:** Store brand colors, fonts, and logos for consistent styling across the organization
- **PPTX and PDF export:** Standard format export available on all plans
- **Integrations:** Connects with Slack, Figma, Google Drive, and Notion for workflow embedding

## Limitations

- **AI generation is not prompt-to-deck:** Pitch's AI handles writing assistance; it does not generate a complete designed deck from a single prompt the way Gamma does
- **Template variety is narrower than Canva:** The curated library is high quality but smaller than mass-market template libraries
- **Pro tier is expensive relative to Gamma and Beautiful.ai:** At $22 per month, Pro costs more than Gamma ($10) and Beautiful.ai ($12) for comparable individual use
- **No built-in image generation:** Pitch does not generate AI images for slides; users must source and upload their own visuals
- **Presentation format is conventional:** 16:9 slides, no fluid or card format; suits traditional presentations but not narrative-flow formats
- **Collaboration features require upgrade beyond 3 members:** The free tier caps team size at 3, which limits the collaboration use case for larger teams without paying

## Bottom Line

Pitch is the best AI presentation tool for teams that collaborate on decks daily and need simultaneous editing, approval workflows, and a clean shared library of presentations ([Pitch](https://pitch.com)). The unlimited decks free tier is a genuine competitive advantage for small teams and startups. For individuals or small groups focused on AI generation speed, Gamma at $10 per month delivers more AI horsepower at lower cost. Pitch earns its place when the workflow is inherently collaborative and the presentation quality and process matter as much as the AI.

## FAQ

**Is Pitch free?**
Yes. Pitch offers a free plan with unlimited decks and up to 3 team members. Most AI presentation tools restrict deck counts or collaborators on free plans; Pitch's unlimited decks free tier is a genuine differentiator.

**Does Pitch generate full presentations from a prompt?**
Pitch's AI assistant can generate an outline and slide text from a topic, but it does not produce a fully designed deck from a single prompt the way Gamma or Presentations.AI does. It generates the content layer; users build and adjust the structure.

**How does Pitch compare to Google Slides for teams?**
Pitch and Google Slides both offer real-time collaboration, but Pitch has AI writing features, better template quality, presentation analytics, and a cleaner design interface. Google Slides is free with no user limits and has native Workspace integration. Teams deeply embedded in Google's ecosystem often prefer Slides for cost reasons; teams that prioritize design quality and presentation analytics prefer Pitch.
