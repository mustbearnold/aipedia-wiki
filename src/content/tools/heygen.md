# HeyGen

HeyGen is an AI avatar and talking-head video platform developed by HeyGen, Inc. It generates videos of AI avatars delivering scripts with lip sync and gestures, used for marketing, sales outreach, employee training, and multilingual video translation. Its main strengths include avatar variety and support for 175 languages with lip-sync dubbing. As of April 2026, pricing includes a free tier (1 credit/month, watermarked), Creator at $29/month (15 credits), Pro at $89/month (30 credits), and Enterprise plans with custom quotas ([HeyGen Pricing](https://heygen.com/pricing)).

## What It Does

HeyGen creates talking-head videos using over 500 AI avatars with lip sync, gestures, and voiceovers in 175+ languages. Users input a script, select an avatar or upload footage for cloning, and generate videos in up to 4K resolution ([HeyGen](https://heygen.com)). The platform supports video translation, dubbing uploaded videos into target languages while matching lip movements.

It includes templates for business use cases, an API for automation, and interactive avatars for live conversations. Integration with tools like Zapier enables workflows for personalized video generation at scale ([HeyGen API Docs](https://docs.heygen.com)).

## Who It's For

- **Marketing teams:** personalized outreach videos, product explainers, social media ads
- **Sales teams:** prospecting videos with dynamic personalization, "Hi [Name]" messaging
- **L&D departments:** onboarding modules, compliance training, how-to guides
- **Course creators:** lecture videos without on-camera recording
- **Agencies:** client video production faster than traditional shoots

## Key Features

- **500+ avatars:** diverse stock avatars with realistic lip sync, gestures, and 175+ language voices ([HeyGen](https://heygen.com))
- **Custom avatars:** clone yourself or others from 2-5 minute videos for personalized delivery ([HeyGen Avatar Creator](https://heygen.com/avatars))
- **Video translation:** dub videos into 175+ languages with lip-sync, preserving original style ([HeyGen Translation](https://heygen.com/translation))
- **API access:** generate videos programmatically; supports personalization at scale ([HeyGen API Docs](https://docs.heygen.com))
- **Templates and editing:** 100+ editable templates; add B-roll, music, transitions
- **Interactive avatars:** real-time conversational avatars for support or demos
- **4K export:** available on Pro+ plans for broadcast quality
- **Integrations:** Zapier, HubSpot, Salesforce for automated workflows

## Limitations

- **Talking-head focus:** generates avatar videos only; no scene generation or cinematic content like Kling 3.0 or Runway Gen-4.5 ([HeyGen](https://heygen.com))
- **Credit costs:** heavy users exhaust Creator/Pro limits quickly; Enterprise needed for high volume
- **Custom avatar quality:** results vary with input video lighting, background, audio clarity
- **Detection risks:** AI-generated avatars detectable by careful inspection or tools
- **No free unlimited use:** free tier limited to 1 credit/month with watermark

## Bottom Line

HeyGen suits teams needing scalable talking-head videos for business use, especially with its 175-language translation. It undercuts Synthesia on price while matching quality. For non-avatar video like full scenes, use Kling 3.0 or Runway Gen-4.5 instead.

## FAQ

**Is HeyGen free?**  
Yes, the free tier provides 1 credit per month (1 minute of video) with watermark and 720p resolution. Paid plans start at $29/month for 15 credits without limits on video count ([HeyGen Pricing](https://heygen.com/pricing)).

**How much video does a HeyGen credit buy?**  
1 credit equals 1 minute of generated video. Usage depends on avatar type; Instant Avatars use less than TalkingPhoto or custom clones. Pro plan's 30 credits support moderate monthly volume ([HeyGen Docs](https://docs.heygen.com)).

**What are HeyGen's best alternatives?**  
Synthesia offers comparable avatars with more enterprise tools. D-ID provides lower-cost API access. Kling 3.0 leads for general video generation beyond talking heads.
