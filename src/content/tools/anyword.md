# Anyword

An AI copywriting platform from **Keywee Inc.**, relaunched under the Anyword name in **March 2021**. The product generates ad copy, email subject lines, landing page text, and blog content, then layers a **Predictive Performance Score** on top that forecasts conversion, click-through rate, and engagement for each variant before it goes live.

That scoring layer is the reason Anyword sits apart from generation-only copy tools like [Jasper](/tools/jasper/), [Copy.ai](/tools/copy-ai/), and [Writesonic](/tools/writesonic/). Those platforms write copy; Anyword writes copy and then tells you which version is statistically more likely to convert, trained on a data layer built from historical ad and marketing performance.

The company (formerly Keywee) raised a **$21 million Series B in November 2021**, led by Innovation Endeavors, and has raised roughly $30-32M total to date.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Anyword if you run enough paid ad or email variants that a predicted-performance score changes what you ship.** The Predictive Performance Score is a genuinely different data layer from pure LLM generation, and it is the one feature competitors have not fully matched.
>
> **Skip it if you only need drafting help.** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or [Claude](/tools/claude/) write comparable copy for less, and Anyword has no permanent free tier, only a 7-day, 2,500-word trial. Long-form content and blog-first teams are better served by [Jasper](/tools/jasper/) or [Writesonic](/tools/writesonic/).
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Starter $49/mo ($39 annual) for solo marketers who want the prediction score on a single brand voice; Data-Driven $99/mo ($79 annual) for small teams needing more seats and real-time prediction on manual edits; Business and Enterprise custom for teams needing custom-trained models, larger historical data ingestion, and (Enterprise) a private LLM, SSO, and full API access.

## What it actually is

A copywriting workspace where every generated variant carries a **numeric predicted-performance score** rather than shipping as undifferentiated text. Anyword trains its scoring models on aggregated historical marketing performance data (ad copy, subject lines, landing pages) tied to audience, business goal, and channel, then applies that model to score new copy before it goes live.

The generation side covers ad copy, email, blog posts (Blog Wizard with a built-in plagiarism checker), and landing page copy, plus a Chrome Extension for scoring or drafting copy inline on other platforms. Brand voice controls keep tone consistent across a workspace.

Higher tiers add **custom-built AI models** trained on a brand's own historical campaign data (Business, up to 5,000 performance data rows) and, at Enterprise, a **private language model** plus SSO and full API access for teams that need the prediction layer wired into their own martech stack.

## When to pick Anyword

- **Performance marketing teams running frequent A/B copy tests.** The Predictive Performance Score gives a data point to prioritize which ad or email variant ships first, instead of guessing.
- **Paid ad and email specialists.** Subject lines, ad headlines, and short-form conversion copy are where the scoring model has the most historical data to lean on.
- **Solo marketers or freelancers who want brand-voice-consistent output with a performance signal**, not just a blank-page generator.
- **Teams with enough historical campaign data to feed Business-tier custom models.** The scoring layer gets more accurate the more first-party performance data it can train against.

## When to pick something else

- **Long-form or blog-first content:** [Jasper](/tools/jasper/) or [Writesonic](/tools/writesonic/). Anyword's scoring strength is short-form conversion copy, not long-form editorial workflow.
- **Budget teams that want a generous free tier:** [Copy.ai](/tools/copy-ai/). Anyword has no permanent free plan, only a capped 7-day trial.
- **General-purpose drafting without a scoring layer:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) Plus or [Claude](/tools/claude/) Pro, both cheaper than Anyword Starter for plain copywriting.
- **SEO-first content operations:** Surfer SEO or Clearscope. Anyword's SEO tooling is secondary to its prediction engine.
- **Inline grammar and tone editing:** [Grammarly](/tools/grammarly/). Anyword is a generation-and-scoring platform, not a proofreading layer.

## Failure modes

- **Accuracy claims are self-reported.** The 82% vs. 52% predictive accuracy figure comes from Anyword's own marketing, not a third-party audit. Treat it as a vendor claim, not an independently verified benchmark.
- **No permanent free tier.** The only way to test Anyword is a 7-day trial capped at 2,500 words, a lower ceiling than most competitors' free plans.
- **Metered predictions can bottleneck testing-heavy teams.** Starter's 50 predictions and Data-Driven's 100 predictions per month cap how much A/B variant scoring a busy paid-ads team can run before needing Business or Enterprise.
- **Long-form workflow is secondary.** Blog Wizard exists but the product's data and design investment is clearly weighted toward short-form conversion copy, not editorial content operations.
- **Affiliate program terms are not fully public.** Anyword runs a FirstPromoter-hosted affiliate program, but its own site does not publish an exact commission rate or cookie window; third-party directories disagree on the number.
- **Custom-model tiers require sales conversations.** Business and Enterprise pricing is not published, so teams needing custom-trained models or a private LLM must go through a sales cycle to get a number.
