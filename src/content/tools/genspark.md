# Genspark

Genspark is an agentic AI platform founded in 2024 by former Baidu search leadership. The flagship product is **Super Agent**, a planner that decomposes a single prompt into steps and calls specialist agents for browsing, writing, slide creation, spreadsheet work, image generation, and AI phone calls.

Output is a finished artifact, not a list of links.

## System Verdict

> **Pick Genspark if you want an agent that finishes the task, not just answers the question.** Super Agent plans multi-step work, calls the right sub-agent, and returns a deck, sheet, document, or phone-call transcript in one run. Strong on research-to-deliverable jobs where the output is more than prose.
>
> **Skip it if you need citation-first research, peak-grade reasoning, or a developer-facing API.** [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/) beats it on source-grounded answers. [Claude](/tools/claude/) beats it on long-form reasoning. [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) beats it on ecosystem and custom-agent breadth. Genspark's moat is the orchestration layer, not the underlying models.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Free for casual use (200 daily credits), Plus $24.99/mo for most individuals, Pro $249.99/mo for teams running sustained agent workloads or high-volume AI phone calls.

## What it actually is

One web product with a single prompt box. The user types a goal. Super Agent decides which specialist agent should run, orchestrates the calls, and produces a finished artifact.

The specialists are first-class surfaces, not post-processors. AI Slides generates a full deck with layouts and images. AI Sheets builds spreadsheet tables with formulas. AI Call places an actual phone call from a provided number and returns the recording and transcript.

The moat is the orchestration layer and the specialist surfaces, not the base LLM. Genspark calls frontier models under the hood, so quality tracks whichever provider handles the sub-step. The differentiator is "task finished" versus "chat reply."

## When to pick Genspark

- **Research that ends in a deliverable.** Super Agent hands back a deck, sheet, or doc rather than a prose summary to copy and reformat.
- **Mixed-tool jobs in one run.** Browse a competitor site, pull pricing into a sheet, generate a comparison slide, draft an outreach email. One prompt, one run.
- **AI phone agents for small ops.** AI Call handles restaurant bookings, clinic appointments, and quote requests. No IVR plumbing required.
- **Users who dislike chat-style workflows.** Super Agent is closer to "press go" than "converse." Faster for known tasks.
- **Free-tier evaluation.** 200 daily credits is generous enough to test real workloads before paying.

## When to pick something else

- **Cited, source-grounded research:** [Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/). Better citation surfacing and primary-source discipline.
- **Deep reasoning or long-document analysis:** [Claude](/tools/claude/). Opus 4.7 leads on long-form coherence and 1M-token context.
- **Generalist assistant with the broadest ecosystem:** [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/). Larger custom-GPT marketplace and mature Operator mode.
- **Conversational web search with fewer guardrails:** [Morphic](/tools/morphic/). Lighter, more source-transparent search layer.
- **Social-signal search with community Q&A:** [You.com](/tools/you-com/). Different flavor of search-first assistant.

## Failure modes

- **Model routing is opaque.** Super Agent picks which frontier model handles each step. Users cannot force a specific model end-to-end. Consequence: output quality on identical prompts can vary day to day.
- **Credits burn fast on rich artifacts.** A single slide deck or long AI Call can consume hundreds of credits. The 10,000/mo Plus quota sounds large until heavy use starts.
- **AI Call has jurisdictional risk.** Automated outbound calls are regulated in many regions (GDPR, TCPA in the US, local marketing laws). Review rules before using at scale.
- **Artifact quality lags specialist tools.** AI Slides is usable but trails Gamma or dedicated design tools. AI Sheets trails a human in Excel on complex formulas.
- **No public self-serve API.** Developer integration requires enterprise contact. Teams wanting to script Genspark into existing pipelines have limited options.
- **Citations are present but weaker than Perplexity.** Source grounding is available yet less prominent in the final artifact. Research-heavy users should cross-check.
- **Smaller ecosystem than incumbents.** Fewer third-party plugins, smaller community, thinner documentation than ChatGPT or Claude.
- **Privacy posture is standard, not elevated.** No zero-retention tier advertised for free and Plus users as of April 2026. Treat sensitive prompts accordingly.

## FAQ

**Is Genspark free?**
Yes. The free tier grants 200 credits per day that reset at midnight UTC. Paid plans start at Plus $24.99/mo for 10,000 monthly credits plus the 200 daily bonus ([Genspark pricing](https://www.genspark.ai/pricing)).

**What is Super Agent?**
Super Agent is the top-level planner. It reads a prompt, decomposes it into steps, and calls the right specialist agent for each step (browse, write, slide, sheet, call). The user sees one run and one finished artifact.

**Which models does Genspark use under the hood?**
Super Agent routes across the GPT, Claude, and Gemini families depending on the task. The active provider is typically disclosed per step. Model choice is not user-configurable on standard plans.

**Does Genspark really make phone calls?**
Yes. AI Call places outbound calls from a provided number for bookings, inquiries, or quote requests. The user receives the recording and transcript. Users must comply with local marketing and privacy law.

**How does Genspark compare to Perplexity?**
Different products. Perplexity is search-first with strong citations and prose output. Genspark is agent-first and returns a finished deliverable (slide, sheet, doc, or call). Heavy researchers pick Perplexity. Users who want "task finished" pick Genspark.

**Is there a public API?**
Not self-serve as of April 2026. Enterprise access is available on request. Builders who need scripted access should evaluate [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) or Claude API instead.

**Who founded Genspark?**
Eric Jing and Kay Zhu, both former Baidu search leadership. The company raised funding from Lanchi Ventures and others. Founders and investors are disclosed on the official site and in public coverage.
