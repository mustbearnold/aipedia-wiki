# GetResponse

GetResponse is the 25+ year all-in-one email marketing and automation platform. What started as a single-feature email marketing tool in 1998 is now a full stack: email marketing, autoresponders, landing pages, marketing automation workflows, webinars, ecommerce tools, a website builder, and a course creator with up to 40,000 students on Enterprise.

As verified in June 2026, the pricing ladder runs Starter $19/mo, Marketer $59/mo, Creator $69/mo (Most Popular), and Enterprise custom. All tiers include unlimited monthly email sends. The Creator plan adds webinars and a built-in course creator, making it uniquely positioned for solopreneurs selling digital products via email.

## System Verdict

> **Pick GetResponse when you want one bill for email marketing plus landing pages plus webinars plus courses.** Solopreneurs running newsletters + course sales benefit most. The Creator plan at $69/mo rolls up what would otherwise cost $200+/mo across separate tools (ConvertKit + LeadPages + Zoom webinars + Teachable + Stripe). Course creators selling their own content via email get a single-vendor workflow that competitors structure as multiple integrations.
>
> **Skip it for SaaS-grade transactional email or pure newsletter-first creators.** [Klaviyo](https://klaviyo.com) owns ecommerce email with tighter Shopify integration and deeper segmentation. [Customer.io](https://customer.io) owns B2B SaaS transactional email. [Beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com) or [Substack](https://substack.com) win for newsletter-first creators who do not need automation, webinars, or courses. Enterprise marketing orgs stay inside HubSpot or Marketo.
>
> **Who pays which tier:** Starter $19 for a 1,000-contact list wanting email + landing pages + basic automation. Marketer $59 when automation workflows become the main use case (unlimited workflows + ecommerce + unlimited AI). Creator $69 for creators selling courses and running webinars on top of email. Enterprise for higher-attendee webinars, 40K-student courses, white-label, and dedicated success management.

## What it actually is

An all-in-one marketing platform covering every step of the customer journey from email capture through content nurture through product sale. The historical positioning was "email + autoresponders"; the 2024-2026 evolution added landing pages, conversion funnels, marketing automation workflows, webinars, ecommerce, a website builder, and a course creator.

**Email marketing** is the foundation: visual drag-and-drop email builder, A/B testing, segmentation, automation triggers, transactional and marketing email delivery.

**Landing pages** ship with the platform at every tier. Templates, drag-and-drop editor, A/B testing, dynamic text replacement, form integration.

**Marketing automation** is where GetResponse differentiates vs pure email tools. Visual workflow builder with triggers (email clicks, cart abandonment, webinar registrations, tag adds) and actions (send email, update contact, move segment, notify team).

**Webinars** are native on Creator and Enterprise. Not an integration, not a separate bill. 100-attendee cap on Creator, 500 on Enterprise.

**Course creator** launched as a serious product in 2024. 500-student capacity on Creator, 40,000 on Enterprise. Video hosting, quizzes, certificates, drip content. Sells from the same contact database that runs your email list.

**AI features** are layered throughout: AI email generation, subject-line testing, landing-page content suggestions, automation optimization.

## When to pick GetResponse

- **Solopreneurs building newsletter + course business.** The Creator plan at $69/mo rolls up what would otherwise need ConvertKit + Teachable + Zoom webinars + LeadPages at roughly 3x the cost across separate tools.
- **Small businesses wanting one marketing tool.** Email + landing pages + basic automation on Starter at $19 is a cheaper floor than comparable bundles elsewhere.
- **Webinar-driven funnels.** Native webinars in GetResponse avoid the Zoom + Mailchimp + Zapier three-tool stack. Creator tier covers it in one bill.
- **Ecommerce sellers on Shopify or WooCommerce.** Marketer tier unlocks ecommerce tools, abandoned-cart automation, and post-purchase sequences without jumping to Klaviyo's pricing.
- **Creators monetising with both lead magnets and course sales.** Course creator + email + landing pages under one tier is unusual; most competitors split these across separate products.

## When to pick something else

- **Ecommerce-only with Shopify:** [Klaviyo](https://klaviyo.com) has deeper Shopify integration, better segmentation on purchase data, and dedicated ecommerce automations.
- **B2B SaaS transactional email:** [Customer.io](https://customer.io) or [Mailchimp Transactional (Mandrill)](https://mailchimp.com/) win on developer-first workflows.
- **Newsletter-first creator:** [Beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com) at $0-$39/mo is purpose-built for newsletter monetization without marketing-automation overhead.
- **Enterprise marketing ops:** [HubSpot](https://hubspot.com) or [Marketo](https://marketo.com) win when CRM integration, attribution, and multi-channel orchestration matter more than email.
- **Developer-first transactional delivery:** [Resend](https://resend.com), [Postmark](https://postmarkapp.com), or [SendGrid](https://sendgrid.com) for code-shipped transactional email.

## Failure modes

- **Starter tier's single workflow is a real constraint.** Active lists quickly exhaust 1 workflow; Marketer at $59 is the practical floor for anyone using automation.
- **1,000-contact pricing is the advertised starting line.** Costs scale steeply past 10K contacts; large lists should model full pricing before committing.
- **AI features are layered, not differentiated.** Competitive parity with Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo; GetResponse's AI is functional rather than a reason to buy by itself.
- **Webinar quality caps at decent, not specialist-grade.** Good enough for lead nurture; streaming-first brands still use Zoom or Restream.
- **Course creator is solid but not Kajabi or Teachable.** Covers fundamentals (videos, quizzes, drip, certificates); deep course-platform features (affiliate programs, community, advanced LMS) sit with dedicated course platforms.
- **Legacy UI in places.** 25+ years of platform evolution shows in older settings screens; newer workflows (course creator, website builder) use modernized UI but older email settings feel dated.
- **Enterprise is demo-gated.** No public pricing; requires sales contact for anything above Creator tier.
- **Ecommerce tools are Shopify / WooCommerce-first.** Smaller ecommerce platforms (Wix Stores, Squarespace Commerce) have thinner native integration.

## FAQ

**Is GetResponse free to use?**
GetResponse offers a 14-day free trial (no credit card required) but does not have a permanent free tier. The lowest paid entry is Starter at $19/mo ($15.58 annual) for a 1,000-contact list.

**How does GetResponse compare to Mailchimp?**
GetResponse wins when webinars, courses, or marketing automation beyond basics matter. Mailchimp is cheaper for pure email marketing on small lists and has a permanent free tier up to 500 contacts. Serious creators selling digital products typically land on GetResponse; casual email senders stay with Mailchimp.

**Does GetResponse support transactional email?**
Yes, for GetResponse-native transactional email (confirmation emails, password resets triggered from GetResponse workflows). Not a developer-first transactional API like SendGrid or Postmark.

**What's the affiliate commission?**
40% recurring for 12 months on every referred paid customer, scaling to 60% for higher-performing partners. 120-day cookie. Managed through PartnerStack.

**Is there a free migration from another tool?**
Yes, GetResponse offers free migration services from Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Klaviyo, ConvertKit, and other major platforms. Requested through customer support at any tier.

**Does the course creator compete with Kajabi or Teachable?**
Covers core course creation (video, quizzes, drip content, certificates) but not full-LMS depth. Creators running pure course-sales businesses will want Kajabi or Teachable; creators using courses as one component of an email-first business get the value of GetResponse's bundled approach.

**What's Prezi Video inside GetResponse?**
Not applicable. Prezi Video is a separate Prezi product; GetResponse's video is its own webinar platform, unrelated.
