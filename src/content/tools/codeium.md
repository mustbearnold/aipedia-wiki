# Codeium

Codeium was the AI code completion brand that shipped plugin-based autocomplete across 70+ languages and 40+ editors from December 2022 to April 2025. The company **rebranded to [Windsurf](/tools/windsurf/)** in April 2025; the codeium.com domain now **permanently 301-redirects to windsurf.com**, and Cognition AI acquired the combined entity in December 2025.

Today there is no separately-marketed Codeium product. IDE plugins, the agentic editor (Cascade, Tab), and enterprise autocomplete all ship under the Windsurf name. This page exists to preserve the brand history and route new buyers to the correct evaluation surface.

## What it actually is

One unified product line under one company.

**Codeium** was the autocomplete brand from December 2022 to April 2025. Plugins shipped for VS Code, JetBrains, Neovim, Emacs, and Jupyter with unlimited completions on the free tier. The company changed its name to Windsurf in April 2025 around the agentic-editor launch and consolidated all marketing under that name. The codeium.com domain was kept as a permanent 301-redirect rather than wound down outright.

**Windsurf** is the live product. The agentic editor ships Cascade (multi-file agent runs), Tab (the autocomplete successor to Codeium and Supercomplete), and Windsurf 2.0 (announced May 2026) which introduces coordinated local plus cloud agents. The historical Codeium IDE plugins still exist for VS Code, JetBrains, Neovim, and Emacs but are now released and supported under the Windsurf brand.

**Cognition AI** acquired Windsurf for roughly $250M in December 2025. Roadmap priorities have continued to consolidate around the agentic IDE rather than the legacy plugin line.

## When the Codeium lineage is still the right call (evaluated as Windsurf)

- **Zero-dollar autocomplete across wide language coverage.** Windsurf Free preserves the legacy Codeium plugin coverage. Good on Racket, Fortran, Lisp, Assembly.
- **Neovim or Emacs is the editor.** Plugin support is first-class where Copilot ships thinner integration. The plugins now release under the Windsurf brand but the editor support did not regress.
- **Students and open-source contributors.** Free tier remains; no trial expiration on the autocomplete surface.
- **Enterprises needing self-hosted autocomplete.** VPC deployment and no-training guarantees are part of the Windsurf Enterprise contract today.
- **Testing AI coding before committing to Copilot or Cursor.** Free Windsurf is the lowest-friction on-ramp out of the Codeium lineage.

## When to pick something else

- **Best-in-class completion quality:** [GitHub Copilot](/tools/github-copilot/) at $10/mo. Tighter GitHub integration and stronger multi-file logic. Copilot now hosts Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.7 alongside GPT models.
- **Agentic multi-file editing inside a dedicated IDE:** [Cursor](/tools/cursor/) ($20/mo) is the main direct competitor on Composer-style multi-file edits.
- **Ultra-fast autocomplete with very long context:** [Tabnine](/tools/tabnine/) or Supermaven. Different speed/quality bias.
- **Free autocomplete with open-source control:** Continue (open-source plugin) runs against self-hosted models.

## FAQ

**Is Codeium the same as Windsurf?**
Yes, today. The rename happened in April 2025 and the codeium.com domain now 301-redirects to windsurf.com. Cognition AI acquired the combined company in December 2025. There is no separately-marketed Codeium product in 2026.

**Is Codeium still free?**
The equivalent product (Windsurf Free) is still free for individual evaluation. Editor and language breadth carry over from the Codeium plugin lineage: VS Code, JetBrains, Neovim, Emacs, Jupyter, 70+ languages. No credit card required, no training on user code.

**What changed with the Cognition acquisition?**
Cognition bought Windsurf for roughly $250M in December 2025. At acquisition, Windsurf had $82M ARR and 350+ enterprise customers. Roadmap direction consolidated around the agentic IDE. Windsurf 2.0 (May 2026) is the first major release fully under Cognition.

**Codeium-lineage plugins vs GitHub Copilot?**
Copilot at $10/mo delivers higher completion accuracy, multi-model access (Claude Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.5), and a Copilot Workspace agent. The Windsurf Free tier matches on editor breadth and exceeds on niche-language support. Students and Neovim users pick Windsurf; mainstream VS Code and GitHub-heavy users pick Copilot.

**Which plan gets Cascade?**
Cascade is the Windsurf agentic feature; Windsurf Pro at $20/mo includes it. The Windsurf Free tier (which absorbed the old free Codeium plugin) does not.
