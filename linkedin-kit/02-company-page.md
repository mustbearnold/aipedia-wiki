```
aipedia.wiki is an independent editorial encyclopedia of AI tools. Every tool is reviewed, scored on Utility, Value, Moat, and Longevity, and re-verified monthly against primary sources.

What we cover (April 2026):
- 205 active tool reviews across 14 categories (AI Coding, AI Writing, AI Image, AI Video, AI Voice, AI Search, AI Design, AI Automation, AI Music, AI Presentation, AI Research, AI SEO, AI Notes, AI Chatbots)
- 280 head-to-head comparison pages
- A daily news feed tracking frontier model releases, pricing changes, acquisitions, shutdowns, and platform-level policy shifts
- A dynamic Stack Builder that composes AI tool stacks for 8 role archetypes and 3 budget tiers

Editorial principles:

1. No paid placements. Ever. Affiliate relationships are disclosed transparently on every page and never influence scoring.

2. Every claim needs a source. Every news item needs 2+ independent confirmations before publish.

3. Author byline is always "aipedia.wiki Editorial" — an organization, not a fabricated personal persona. This is Google E-E-A-T alignment and an intentional rejection of the "fake Amy Smith reviewed 47 tools today" pattern.

4. Scoring is a public four-axis rubric (aipedia.wiki/about/scoring/). If a score changes, the history is visible.

5. Pricing is re-verified monthly. The verified date is stamped on every pricing table.

6. Corrections are fixed within 48 hours of being flagged. The site has a visible "Suggest correction" link on every page.

The operation runs on an automated editorial pipeline: primary-source research, scored review generation, cross-reference audit, monthly re-verification. Human oversight at every step. No pretense of personality-driven reviewing; all bylines are organizational.

Built on Astro + Cloudflare. Source of truth is git-tracked Markdown. Transparent methodology at aipedia.wiki/about/.
```
