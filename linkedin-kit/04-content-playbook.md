# Content Playbook — Weeks 2+ Cadence

What to post, when, how to structure it, and how to engage. Built around the realistic 45 min/week budget.

---

## Weekly rhythm (after launch week)

| Day | Type | Source | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Weekly AI news wrap-up | Your /news/ feed from the past 7 days | 15 min |
| **Wednesday** | Tool deep-dive or comparison | Pick one tool page or /compare/ entry | 15 min |
| **Friday** | Commentary / take / thread | Opinion on a recent industry shift | 10 min |
| **Company page** | Reshare your personal post | 1-click reshare to boost impressions | 5 min |

**Total:** 45 min/week. If you can't hit 45 min, do only Monday. Consistency > volume.

## Hook templates (paste-and-fill)

Every post needs a hook in the first 3 lines (LinkedIn truncates at ~210 chars before "see more"). Use one of these frames:

### 1. Contrarian claim

```
[Common belief] is wrong.

Here's what actually happened this week:

[specific evidence]
```

**Example:** "Claude is the best AI coder is wrong. Here's what actually happened on SWE-bench this week: Claude Opus 4.7 scored 80.9%, Codex GPT-5.4 scored 80% — it's a statistical tie."

### 2. Concrete number

```
[Specific number] [specific subject] [specific time window].

[1-line takeaway]

Full story:
```

**Example:** "$2.1B AI coding funding closed in April 2026 alone. Three of the five biggest rounds all went to 'agentic IDE' companies. The category is the most crowded and highest-funded in AI right now."

### 3. Changed my mind

```
I changed my mind about [tool / strategy / category] this week.

Here's what I was wrong about:

[list or paragraph]
```

**Example:** "I changed my mind about Cursor vs Claude Code this week. What I was wrong about: the 'terminal native vs IDE native' distinction isn't the axis that matters. The axis is..."

### 4. Before / after

```
3 months ago [state A].
Today [state B].
What changed: [cause].
```

**Example:** "3 months ago, Anthropic had ~$12B ARR. Today, $30B. What changed: the Claude Opus 4.6 → 4.7 jump, the Claude Code → Claude Design productization, and enterprise-coding customer acquisition doubling in Q1."

### 5. Myth / truth

```
Myth: [common belief]
Truth: [what the data actually shows]
Why it matters: [so-what]
```

**Example:** "Myth: You need enterprise plans for AI coding agents. Truth: Cursor Pro at $20/mo outperforms both GitHub Copilot Enterprise and Claude Code Max 5x on SWE-bench Lite. Why it matters: the $2,000/mo-per-seat enterprise quote is unnecessary for most dev teams."

### 6. Counting / listing

```
[N] [things] every [audience] should know about [topic]:

1. [...]
2. [...]
3. [...]

#[1-2 tags]
```

**Example:** "5 things every SaaS founder should know about AI coding agents in April 2026: 1. Cursor at $2B ARR is now the default. 2. Claude Code is the strongest for long-context refactors. 3. Codex is the cheapest inside ChatGPT Plus. 4. Devin is where you lose money fastest. 5. Aider is the only one that runs locally against any model."

### 7. Thread opener

```
[Declarative one-liner].

[Paragraph explaining].

↓ 1/7
```

Then continue in replies or via a carousel. LinkedIn thread-openers with a "↓ N/M" signal get high engagement in 2026.

## Content sources (what to post about)

**Always-on sources from aipedia.wiki:**

1. **Your /news/ feed** — Repost news item findings. Every news item on the site has enough context for a 3-5 paragraph LinkedIn post.
2. **Tool pages that rank 9+** — "Why [Tool X] scores 9.5 on the rubric" posts.
3. **Comparison pages** — "[Tool A] vs [Tool B] — here's who wins on each axis" is a high-engagement LinkedIn format.
4. **Stack Builder stacks** — "The $50/mo AI stack for a Solo Founder in April 2026" format.
5. **Dead tools** — Retrospectives on shut-down AI tools draw engagement from the "told you so" audience.

**External sources to respond to:**

1. **Vendor announcements** — OpenAI / Anthropic / Google / xAI posts. Your value add: the comparison context they omit.
2. **Funding news** — TechCrunch / Crunchbase updates. Your value add: the scoring-rubric implication.
3. **Benchmark releases** — Artificial Analysis, ARC-AGI, SWE-bench updates. Your value add: what the benchmark misses.

## Engagement strategy

**40% of LinkedIn reach comes from engagement on OTHER people's posts, not your own posts.** Comment more than you post.

**Daily target (10 min/day):**
- 5 substantive comments on posts in your feed from AI operators, founders, vendor teams
- 2 reposts-with-your-own-take of high-signal content
- 0 generic "Great post 💯🔥" comments (the algorithm detects these and down-weights your future posts)

**Who to engage with:**

| Persona | What they post | Why engage |
|---|---|---|
| AI founders (Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Dario Gil, Elon Musk, Alexandr Wang) | Vendor announcements, strategic signals | Your editorial framing adds value |
| Enterprise AI buyers (VPs of Engineering, CTOs, CMOs at ~1000-person co's) | Stack decisions, vendor evaluations | They're your direct audience |
| AI creators (LinkedIn-native creators like Allie K. Miller, Lance Martin, Ethan Mollick) | Daily AI takes, threads | Their followers are your aligned audience |
| Specialized operators (AI coding dev-rels, marketing-ops leads) | Deep tactical posts | High-intent readership per comment |

**How to comment:**
- Add a specific fact or counter-point, never just "Great post"
- Link to a relevant tool page or news item on aipedia.wiki only when it's genuinely additive (1 in 10 comments max)
- Ask a sharp question that extends the original post's thinking

## Posting-time optimization

**Best windows for aipedia.wiki's audience (US-centric):**

- **Tuesday 8-10am ET** — Best engagement window on LinkedIn in 2026 per multiple sources
- **Wednesday 8-10am ET**
- **Thursday 8-10am ET**

**Worst times:** Friday afternoon, weekends, Monday morning (nobody's on LinkedIn on Monday before 10am ET).

**If you're in a different timezone** — schedule via LinkedIn's native scheduler (click the clock icon before posting). Don't use Buffer / Hootsuite / Later — LinkedIn's algorithm down-weights posts from third-party tools.

## Carousel PDFs (2026's highest-reach format)

LinkedIn carousels (multi-page PDFs uploaded as documents) consistently outperform text-only and image-only posts. Structure:

**Optimal carousel:** 8-12 slides, landscape 1920x1080 or square 1080x1080, PDF format.

**Template:**
1. **Slide 1** — hook headline + "Swipe →"
2. **Slide 2** — the setup / question being answered
3. **Slides 3-10** — one point per slide, large font, high contrast
4. **Slide 11** — takeaway / summary
5. **Slide 12** — CTA (follow me / visit aipedia.wiki)

**Tools to build:** Canva (paid for brand consistency), Figma (if you have it), Google Slides + Export as PDF (free).

**Cadence:** Aim for 1 carousel every 2-3 weeks once you're comfortable with the rhythm.

## Native video (2026 growing format)

Short-form native video (30-60 sec) is LinkedIn's fastest-growing surface in 2026. Start with:

- **Text-on-screen** style (no face-camera required)
- **Captions baked in** (LinkedIn auto-captions are rough)
- **Hook in first 2 sec** — same principle as text
- **Square 1:1 or vertical 9:16** for mobile

**Minimum viable video:** 30-second screen recording of you clicking through the Stack Builder or a tool comparison on aipedia.wiki, with text captions explaining what's happening. Record with OBS or QuickTime, export to MP4, upload directly to LinkedIn.

**Cadence:** 1 native video per month if you like the format; skip if you don't.

## Polls (low-effort, decent reach)

LinkedIn polls are the lowest-effort content format that still reaches a crowd. One per 2 weeks max.

**Template poll structure:**

```
Which AI coding tool do you actually use day-to-day?

Cursor
Claude Code
GitHub Copilot
Other (comment)
```

(LinkedIn polls cap at 4 options and 1-2 weeks duration)

**When polls work:** Category / decision questions your audience has opinions on.
**When polls fail:** Anything that looks like a focus group or market research.

## Cross-posting from aipedia.wiki news to LinkedIn

**The shortcut:** every news item on aipedia.wiki is 1500+ words of research. Compressing it to a LinkedIn post takes 5-10 minutes.

**The template:**

1. Open `https://aipedia.wiki/news/[slug]/`
2. Copy the **summary** field (1-3 sentences)
3. Copy the **2-3 strongest paragraphs** from the body
4. Add a 1-line takeaway
5. Link back: "Full breakdown: https://aipedia.wiki/news/[slug]/"
6. Attach `images/post-template.png` or a screenshot from the tool page

5 minutes per post. 3 news items per week = 15 minutes.

**What NOT to do:** paste the full news item body. LinkedIn isn't long-form blog land, and duplicating content hurts your site's SEO.

## Anti-patterns (avoid these)

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Emoji-heavy opening (🚀🔥💯) | Signals low-signal promotional content, algorithm down-weights |
| "Thoughts?" as the close | Generic CTA, no one answers |
| More than 5 hashtags | LinkedIn stopped rewarding hashtag-heavy posts in 2024 |
| Third-party posting tool | Documented reach penalty |
| Posting the same thing on X + LinkedIn verbatim | LinkedIn audiences want different framing (more professional, less hot-take) |
| Editing post within 30 min of publishing | Algorithm resets impressions |
| "Tag 3 people who..." | LinkedIn down-weights this pattern as of 2025 |

## KPIs to track monthly

**Vanity metrics (track but don't over-index):**
- Followers
- Post impressions

**Actual metrics that matter:**
- **Click-through to aipedia.wiki** (check GA / Cloudflare Analytics: LinkedIn as referrer)
- **Engagement rate** (likes + comments + reposts / impressions) — 3%+ is strong, 5%+ is excellent
- **Meaningful comments** (not "great post" — real conversations)
- **Inbound messages** that turn into something (consulting, partnerships, corrections, writer interest)

**Monthly review questions:**
1. Which post format drove the most aipedia.wiki clicks?
2. Which topic drove the most follower growth?
3. Did anyone from the enterprise-buyer audience actually engage, or just other AI operators?
4. Is there a pattern in who's commenting that tells me something about the audience?

Adjust the content playbook every 3 months based on answers.

## First-90-days success looks like

- **500+ personal followers** (from ~0)
- **100+ company page followers**
- **1 inbound opportunity** that's worth your time (consulting gig, partnership, media request)
- **Steady LinkedIn referral traffic** to aipedia.wiki (>100 visits / month is a realistic target)

If you're at those numbers by mid-July 2026, the channel is working. If you're below them, either the content is wrong or LinkedIn isn't the right channel for your audience — revisit before scaling.
