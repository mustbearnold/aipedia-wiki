# Starter Posts — Week 1 Launch Sequence

Five ready-to-post pieces for your first week. Schedule one per day (skip weekends if you want) or batch two per day. Each one is pre-fit to LinkedIn's algorithm: hook in the first 3 lines, scannable body, concrete number in the opener when possible.

**General guidance:**
- Paste the post text as-is
- Where it says `[attach image]`, upload the indicated file from `images/`
- Add the suggested hashtags at the end, not mixed into the prose
- Don't edit after posting unless you spot a real error (every edit resets reach)

## Notes on the 5-post sequence

**Hook structure** — each post opens with a concrete number, a direct statement, or an uncommon claim in the first line. LinkedIn's feed cuts off at ~210 chars before "...see more", so your first 2-3 lines carry the entire click decision.

**Length** — 900-1600 chars per post. LinkedIn's sweet spot is 1200-1800 for engagement; less than 900 reads as low-effort, more than 2000 loses readers.

**Hashtags** — 3-5 per post, placed at the end on their own line. No more. LinkedIn punishes over-tagging.

**Links** — LinkedIn down-ranks posts that link off-platform. Workarounds:
- Put the external link in the **first comment** instead of the post body (most common 2026 tactic)
- Or: put the link in the post body but accept a ~30% reach penalty
- Or: post native LinkedIn articles occasionally that link back to aipedia.wiki

For the 5 launch posts, put the aipedia.wiki link in the post body. You're introducing the site; you want people to click. Post-launch cadence can move to "link in first comment" for ongoing news posts.

**Images** — every post benefits from an image. `post-template.png` is a starting point you can edit in Canva / Figma / any image tool. Add your headline + a takeaway bullet or two.
