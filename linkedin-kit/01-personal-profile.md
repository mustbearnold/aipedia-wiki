```
I run aipedia.wiki — an independent, agentically operated encyclopedia of AI tools.

Every tool gets scored on the same four dimensions: Utility, Value, Moat, Longevity. No sponsorships. No paid placements. No "we tested it for you" without actually testing it. Every claim links to the primary source. Pricing is re-verified monthly and stamped on every page.

As of April 2026:
- 205 active tool reviews across 14 categories
- Daily news feed tracking model releases, funding, shutdowns, and platform-level changes that matter
- 280 head-to-head comparisons
- A Stack Builder that assembles the full AI stack for Solo Founders, Developers, Content Creators, Marketers, Designers, Students, Agencies, and Data Analysts
- Every tool page cites its sources; every news item needs 2+ independent confirmations to publish

Why I built it this way:

Most AI directories are SEO farms with affiliate banners. The reviews are marketing copy with a disguise. The scoring is invented. The "last updated" dates lie.

aipedia.wiki inverts all of that:
- Reviews are written through an automated editorial pipeline that's human-supervised and correction-friendly
- Scoring follows a public four-axis rubric (aipedia.wiki/about/scoring/) so you can see exactly why Claude Opus 4.7 scores 10 on Utility and Grok 4.3 scores 7 on Longevity
- News items track affects → tool-page cross-references so nothing gets stale
- Every page has a "Suggest correction" link; if something's wrong, I fix it within 48 hours

I ship code publicly. The whole site, the scoring rubric, and the editorial policy are documented at aipedia.wiki/about/.

If you're picking AI tools for a team or a client and you want a source that doesn't lie to you, that's what I built.

Currently writing about: AI coding agents (Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, Devin, Windsurf), frontier models (Opus 4.7, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Grok 4.3), and the economics of the 2026 AI stack.

Reach me: editorial@aipedia.wiki
Site: https://aipedia.wiki
```
